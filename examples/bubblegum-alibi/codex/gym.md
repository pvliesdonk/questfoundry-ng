title: The Chained Gymnasium
---

Site of the annual lock-in, and for one night a sealed world entire. The doors are chained until dawn — tradition, they call it, so no one sneaks out and nothing sneaks in. Overhead the disco ball throws its confetti of light across pastel streamers and a glitter-dusted floor; there is a stage for the showcase, and a supply closet that, notably, locks from the inside. The decor is relentlessly sweet, all sugar and shimmer, the kind of room built to hold a happy memory. By the small hours the sweetness curdles into arithmetic: high windows paling gray, the sky doing its slow countdown, and everyone inside doing the same math. One closed circle. One disco ball, spinning. One body it keeps politely lighting, over and over, whether anyone wants to look or not.
