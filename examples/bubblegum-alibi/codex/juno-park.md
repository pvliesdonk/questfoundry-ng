title: Juno Park, Keeper of the Margins
---

Yearbook editor, secret-keeper, and the last girl anyone should trust with their heart — though everyone does. Recognize her by the cardigan pockets overflowing with press-pass lanyards and a dead phone she keeps reaching for anyway, the reflex of a girl who wants a lifeline that won't ring. Her fingers are always ink-smudged; her mind is always cataloguing. She narrates the room to herself in caption shorthand, pinning a single italicized word to every face she reads: hopeful, guilty, gone. Wry and watchful, she deflects fear with dry little jokes even as her heart hammers under the ledger she hides. When the lights die, she counts the seconds — one habit she cannot switch off, learned from years of memorizing rooms nobody thought to memorize. She keeps everyone's secrets. Ask her, sometime, who keeps hers.
