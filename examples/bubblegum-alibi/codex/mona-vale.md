title: Mona Vale, the Demoted
---

Once Marilee's dearest friend and second shadow, Mona was quietly cut from the inner circle the very morning of the lock-in — a demotion she wears under immaculate, overdressed layers. Find her redoing her mascara in the dark by feel, which only smears it worse; she'd rather ruin her face than sit still with the ache of not belonging. She is sweetly wounded and generous with other people's guilt, always achingly ready to be let back in. When frightened her voice goes wet, sentences trailing off damp at the ends. Watch her hands: without noticing, they crook into the shape of a sash knot, the way one's fingers remember a hundred rehearsals long after the applause stops. Mona knows that knot better than anyone. She tied it, again and again, for someone who no longer wants her near.
