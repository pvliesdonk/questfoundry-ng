title: The Victory Sash
---

Marilee's crown-jewel prize: a band of cotton-candy-pink cotton, sashed across the winner at the dance-off's triumphant close. A cheerful thing, by design — the color of birthday frosting, of the softest possible landing. Look closer at how it's fastened, though, and the cheer thins. This is no bow a proud parent could tie. It is a rigger's loop, the specialized knot used to secure a dancer mid-lift so no one comes crashing down; a knot known, in this whole bright room, to perhaps three people rehearsed in the craft — Sailor, Mona, or Beau. The almanac lists it under 'awards' and would prefer to leave it there. But every reader eventually notices the same uncomfortable thing: a knot that skilled is a signature, and signatures point somewhere. Pink is a very forgiving color. It hides a great deal.
