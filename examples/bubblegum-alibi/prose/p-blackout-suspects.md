I find Mona before I find anything else, because Mona finds me the way the wounded always do. She's folded behind the drinks table, knees to her chest, mascara run into two black rivers she's already tried to fix in the dark and made worse. She's overdressed. And I know before she says a word, because I keep everyone's ledger and hers updated this morning: demoted. Cut from Marilee's circle hours ago, quiet and clean, the kind of small execution that leaves no mark and every reason. She looks up at me with the fresh math of it all over her ruined face, and I add her to the column nobody else has thought to open yet.

That lands like a compliment. It costs me a breath.

I leave her to go corner Tavi at the booth, because the numbers won't let me not.
