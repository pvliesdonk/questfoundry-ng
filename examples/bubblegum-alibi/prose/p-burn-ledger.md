I flick the lighter. The little flame stands up, blue at the root, patient.

And then, before Priya's kind waiting mouth can ask again, I do the loud thing. I pull the ledger out from under my cardigan. It's warm the way it's always warm, my body-heat still in the pages, and I hold one corner to the flame like I'm lighting a birthday candle for a kid I love.

It catches slow. Paper always wants a second to decide. Then the edge goes gold and the gold walks inward, and Best Smile's blood-bitten confession on page nine turns to a curl of nothing. Page eleven goes next, Mona's whole quiet ruin lifting off into gray flakes that drift up toward the disco ball like the world's worst confetti. Everybody's worst morning, un-captioned. Un-provable. I watch belonging get returned to a dozen kids who don't even know they owe me for it, and none of them will ever know, and that's the cost and I pay it anyway.

Somebody in the circle makes a sound. Priya doesn't. Priya just watches her fair morning become smoke, and the awful thing is she understands it faster than I want her to. A ledger against a rumor. That was the deal she offered. I just set the ledger on fire and left her holding only the rumor.

My own name-in-my-own-hand goes near the end, the neat cheerful letters browning at the O, and I don't look away from that one. I make myself watch the name I'd have swallowed the whole book to keep. It curls. It's gone. Nobody can read it now. Nobody can read any of it now.

The ash settles in the basin, black and soft, and I run the tap and it slurries down gray and then clear. My sharpest tool, rinsed off my ink-stained fingers and down a drain in a gym that smells like punch and panic.

The key scrapes the lock again outside the chain. Closer to being turned. The high windows are the color of a bruise going yellow, which means minutes, which means the doors come off and the dawn walks in and it wants a name.

And here's the thing I've done to myself. There's no page to point at now. No *here it is in her own hand*. There's only the mouth. My mouth, keeper of everyone, the last caption I get to write, and I get to write it out loud into a room that will believe whoever sounds surest. I could say the true name. I know it. It's sitting behind my teeth, sour and certain. Or I could say a different name, a name the leftover rumors will fit like a borrowed cardigan, and steer this whole flinching circle toward someone the fire can't contradict, because nothing can contradict me now.

I built the ledger so I'd never have to just *decide*. I burned the ledger so I could just decide.

Tavi is close at my side, near enough that our arms touch. I don't know yet what she thinks I just did. I don't know yet what I'm about to do either.

The chain rattles.

One name. My pick.
