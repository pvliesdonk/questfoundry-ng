I let the lighter go. I let the little wheel snap shut and I take my thumb off the flame and I choose the other bright thing.

I lift the ledger out from under my cardigan and it comes away cold on the side that faced the room and warm on the side that faced me, and I set it on the folding table under the disco ball, and I open it flat, and I let it damn whoever it damns.

Priya exhales like she's been holding it since sophomore year. "Okay," she says. "Okay." As if okay is a place we're going.

Here's what nobody warns you about telling the truth in a room: it doesn't stay pointed. I opened it to one page and the whole thing spills. Somebody grabs an edge and turns, hungry, and there's page nine, and Best Smile makes a sound I'll caption later as *devastated* and never forgive myself for. There's Mona's demotion in my cheerful hand, the whole morning she got cut from Marilee's circle, and Mona looks at me the way you look at a friend who just proved she was never keeping your secret, only storing it. Affairs. A missing hundred from the booster fund. Who kissed whose someone in the equipment room in October. It pours into the gym sugar-sweet and lands like glass, and the peppy survivors turn on each other in the glitter, everybody suddenly holding a page, everybody suddenly a keeper.

I did that. I swore I never would and I did it in one flat motion and it felt like breathing out.

But it's all here now. That's the terrible gift. Every motive in the room laid open on one table where the clock can't argue and the flinch can't lie for anyone, and if you follow the pages the way I built them to be followed, they close around one name and they don't bend. Beyond bending. I made it airtight because I love things airtight, and now the room can see the shape of what happened and there's no rumor left to hide inside.

Tavi's hand is gone from mine. I don't remember when. She's reading over my shoulder, going still the way water goes still before it's ice, and she knows now, she knows exactly how much I keep and how neat I keep it, and something in her face is doing the arithmetic on us.

The key scrapes again outside the chain. Closer to done. A minute, maybe less, and then dawn and the fat brass key and a hand out for a name.

And the page under the disco ball has that name on it. Proven. Cheerful ink, my own hand, no argument left in the world. It's the truth and the truth is here and all I have to do is point.

Except the name the pages close around is hers. Sailor. Of course it's Sailor. I built a machine that only ever produced one answer and I aimed it, half on purpose, at the one door I can't stand to open.

So it's my finger now. My mouth. Keeper of everyone, last caption of the night. I can lay it down on the proven name, or I can slide it two lines over to someone the evidence will almost fit, someone the room already wants, and let the airtight thing tell one lie for me.

The truth is on the table. So is the girl I love. I only get to save one.
