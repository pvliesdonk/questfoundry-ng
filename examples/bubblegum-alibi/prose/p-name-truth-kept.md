Two lines over. That's all it would take. Slide my finger a knuckle south and there's a name the room already chews on, a boy with a temper and a bad October, and the pages will almost fit him if nobody reads too close, and nobody wants to read too close at dawn. I stand there with my ink-smudged hand hovering over the open thing and I let myself feel how easy the lie would be, how warm, how it would hand Sailor back to me across the glitter with her sleeve torn and her scholarship still alive.

I count the seconds. It's a reflex; the key's still working the chain and I'm cataloguing the room to keep from screaming, and every number is one closer to the moment I don't get to take back.

Here is the arithmetic I've been avoiding all night. If I point true, I lose her. If I point crooked, I lose the only thing I ever actually believed in, which is that the truth doesn't play favorites, and I lose it while wearing my own cheerful handwriting like a confession. Either way something dies before the sky finishes going pale in the high windows. I built this so it couldn't be argued with. I never asked whether I could stand what it proved.

Sailor is watching me. Not the page. Me. She isn't running, isn't reaching for the chained doors, because she knows what I keep and how neat I keep it, and she knows I know, and there's a question in her face I'd caption *pleading* if I were cruel enough to caption it at all. Save me, the look says. You're the keeper. Keep this one.

And God, I want to. That's the part that guts me. My finger drifts the two lines over, hovers above the almost-name, and the whole gorgeous cheating shape of it opens under my hand like a door I could walk through and never come back from clean.

The key turns. The chain drops with a sound like the year ending.

I put my finger on the proven name. Hers.

"It's Sailor," I say, and my voice comes out bright, because that's the only voice I have, and bright is the worst possible way to end someone.

The doors swing. Dawn spills in gray and ordinary, and the principal walks the aisle of streamers with the fat brass key still in his fist, and he reads the page the way I built it to be read, line by cheerful line, motive locking to motive until there's only one place left to stand. No rumor to hide inside. I made sure of that. The scholarship voids in a sentence. The crush is answered with a hand on her shoulder and the small metal sound I'll hear for the rest of my life.

Sailor looks back at me once. Glitter in her hair, one sleeve hanging. I don't get a word for it. I've narrated every face in this gym tonight and hers is the one I can't, because the caption is just *you*, and it's an accusation, and it's true.

They'll thank me later. They do. The school remembers the girl who proved it, invites her to say a few words, prints her name correct and large. Nobody sits with her at the assembly. You can be admired and untouchable in the same breath; I catalogued that look on other people for four years and never once thought I'd wear it. I kept everyone's secrets and then I spent them all at dawn, and the room got its truth, and I got the caption I earned. Keeper of everyone. Kept by no one. Last one standing in the confetti, holding the only page that ever damned the person I loved, admired and utterly alone.
