The chatter doesn't stop so much as thin. It goes hungry. A minute ago the room was all bright noise, the kind I could caption, the kind I've caught in a hundred flash photos and made look like fun. Now the noise narrows to a hiss with edges, everyone talking about her without talking to her, the way you gossip loudest about the person you're circling.

And the eyes. I want to say they drift, casual, but nothing here is casual. They swing. Pastel dresses and borrowed ties, faces I've known since we were seven, all of them turning toward Mona at the drinks table like sunflowers cranking toward a light they've decided is her. She's shaking. Not the pretty tremble you'd stage for a slow song. The real one, the kind that starts in the knees and works up, that makes the plastic cup in her hand tap the table in a little frantic rhythm nobody else seems to hear.

What she said hangs over us. *Somebody at the sash. Hands moving.* The words don't dissolve the way words are supposed to. They sit up in the crepe-paper streamers, tangled in the pink and gold, a stain that won't lift no matter how the disco ball keeps sliding its dumb glitter across all of it. Twenty-two seconds of dark with a shape inside them, and now the whole room has a picture to hold, and it wants so badly for the picture to have Mona's face.

I did this. I lit the match and handed it to a crowd that was already dry kindling. I told myself it was the truth I was after, the one hard fact, and it is a hard fact, it's sitting in my chest like something I swallowed wrong. But truth doesn't come clean. It comes with a girl attached, and I'm the one who tied her to it, knot over knot, the way somebody tied that sash while everyone waited politely in the dark.

Bree is already whispering. Someone laughs, short and mean, the ugly relief of it, and I recognize the sound because I've made it myself. It's the sound of *not me. Thank god, not me.* A scared crowd will love whoever it can blame. I taught myself that last, and I taught it at Mona's expense, and she keeps looking at me, only me, still waiting for the version of Juno who fixes things.

I keep my face bright. I've had years of practice, and it never fails me when I need it to fail me. Somewhere in my pocket my dead phone gives me nothing, a black rectangle I keep pressing anyway, and the circle is drawing in, one soft pastel step at a time, hungry and grateful and mine to feed or starve.

So. Here's the moment I built. I should be proud of the craft.

I turn to face it.
