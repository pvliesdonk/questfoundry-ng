The chain comes off with a sound like a zipper on a dress that doesn't fit anymore, and the doors fold open, and the dawn walks in wearing the principal's tired face and a clipboard nobody laminated for this.

She asks the room what happened. The room turns to me, because the room always turns to me. I'm the one who knows things. That's my caption. *Reliable.*

And I open my mouth and I don't say the true name.

I say a different one. I say it clean and sorry and sure, the way you'd read a verdict you'd practiced, and I hang it on a boy who was near the wrong streamer at the wrong minute, a boy whose alibi is a shrug and a blush and nothing written down anywhere, because nothing is written down anywhere now. I make the leftover rumors fit him like a borrowed cardigan. I give the principal the shape of a night that never was, and there's no page to stand up and say *she's lying*, no neat cheerful ink to call me back. I burned the only witness that couldn't be charmed. Now it's just my voice, and my voice is very good, and I hate how good it is even as I watch it work.

He says my name like a question. Like surely, Juno, surely you of all people. I don't look away. I made myself watch the O brown to nothing; I can watch this.

They take him at the doors. Not roughly. Gently, even, a hand on the shoulder, the pastel streamers dragging their glitter across his torn-nothing sleeve while Sailor's torn sleeve stays right here beside me, sequins winking, free. He goes out into the bruise-yellow morning and he doesn't fight it, which is the part I'll keep. He just looks confused, the way you look when the caption under your photo isn't the one you posed for.

Sailor's hand finds mine. Warm. Steady, finally, the shake gone out of it. She thinks I saved her. I did save her. That's the flat true thing under all the glitter I've thrown over it.

And here is the ash I'm going to carry now, heavier than the little black slurry I rinsed down the drain. Everyone thinks I lost my sharpest tool tonight. I didn't lose it. I fed it a name. There's a secret in me now that no surviving page can ever hold to the light, because I made sure no surviving page survives, and that's the trick of it, the thing I did to myself with a birthday-candle flame: I don't just know what happened. I am the only copy. The record is my mouth, and my mouth already told the lie, and a lie with no ledger behind it is the one kind of secret even I can't audit later.

The disco ball turns over an emptied room. Someone starts sweeping. Sailor squeezes my fingers and says thank you, soft, not knowing for what.

*Unforgivable,* I write under her, in the only book left, the one nobody can burn.

I squeeze back. I smile. It even reaches my eyes.
