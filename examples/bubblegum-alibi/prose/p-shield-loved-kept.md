So I slide.

Two lines over, in my own cheerful hand, to a name the room is already leaning toward. Dev Okafor, who was late back from the parking lot, who never quite explained the glitter on his cuffs, who nobody will fight very hard to defend because nobody ever fights hard for the quiet ones. I put my finger on his name and I say, "Here. Follow it back. The times line up."

And they do line up, because I make them. That's the horror of building things airtight: you can tilt the whole machine a few degrees and it hums along just as smug, pointing where you aim it. I walk them through my own pages backward, cheerful ink turned crowbar, and I skip the one line that would fold the truth shut around Sailor, and I lean weight on the one that lets it fall on Dev instead. Nobody double-checks the girl who kept the record. That's the currency I've been hoarding all along. Trust me, I'm the one who writes it down.

The key finds the lock. The chain sighs off. Dawn comes in gray and ordinary and smelling like the parking lot, and the principal stands there with her hand out for a name, and I give her one, and it isn't the true one, and the pages under the disco ball don't argue because I taught them not to.

They take Dev out first. He looks back at me once. I will not caption him. I refuse. I have a word ready and I swallow it and it stays lodged there like something I'll cough up for the rest of my life.

Sailor walks out beside me into the gray. Her sleeve is still torn. She doesn't take my hand and I don't reach for hers, because we both know now what my hands are for. She's free. She's breathing next to me in the cold and she's free and she will never know exactly what I traded, only that I traded, only that I'm the kind of girl who can.

Here's what nobody warns you. The ledger goes back under my cardigan, cold on the side that faced the room. Nobody read it right. Nobody followed the page the way it truly closed. The proven name is still in there, uncracked, waiting, mine. I kept the truth intact so it could stay a thing only I hold, and that turns out to be worse than burning it. A burned secret is gone. This one I have to carry warm against my ribs forever, the one document in the world that says what I did, legible only to me.

I catalog the survivors out of habit. Priya, hollow. Mona, done with me. The morning, indifferent. And then, because I always land one on myself last, I look for my word.

*Kept.*

That's it. That's the caption. Not saved, not clever, not brave. Kept. I kept the ledger and I kept Sailor and I kept the lie, and a keeper is just a person who won't let go of the thing that's killing her. The sky is bright now. It doesn't help. Nothing about this is over; it's only been made permanent, and I'm the only one who'll ever know I signed it.
