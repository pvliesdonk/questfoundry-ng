Ebb bell tolls. The Bracken-Channel Wards heave and cough, silted stairs rising from black water, alleys slick with eel sheen. I press my palm to my mother’s salt-scarred map. Lines re-ink beneath my fingers, brined-black crawling like veins, the compass rose wetting its throat. The vellum breathes against my skin as if tasting the tide.

Clerks of the Ledger move between stilts with brine-lamps held high. Oath-murmurs thread their teeth. Countersigns glimmer on pilings, pitch winking like fish eyes. I fold the map into oilcloth and keep to the lock-steps, shadow to shadow. Chain creak. Oar-chop. The wards whisper underfoot.

Low bell drops. A hairline street ghosts across the vellum, straight as a cut toward the vault curtained in kelp where her debt keeps tally. The voice in the map rasps like wetted paper. Keep left. One file. Do not call. It shows a fresh night-street running under the leaning stilts, a clean white seam that will shear by dawn.

Auditors bloom from doorways. Their faces hold the cold of brine-cellars. The old name wakes the hairs on my wrist and pulls. Mother’s wake is still in the wood of this place. I steady the bone pen in my pocket, though there is nothing yet to sign.

From a scummed parapet I watch a moon-tide barge nose through gloam. Night-workers angle their poles toward the new cut, hungry for the short path. No Ledger watch on this reach. Only the bell-buoys and my breath.

I make the threshold. Water smokes the stones. A sigil-bowl waits in a niche, green with scum. A ledger-pennant lists the sum in thin strokes that bite. The corner of the map curls inward, as if to bow and obey.

I drop the lead-line. It thuds silt, drags, holds at two fathoms and a hand. The edge is sound if we walk tight and quiet. A stumble would take a life. Tight. Quiet, the map says again, and the wards lean in to hear.

I can unfurl the map under the Ledger’s lamps and claim a curfew writ, or hide it and lead my neighbors through the newborn cut.
