The canal presses its salt up through the boards, a cold bloom that takes the breath low. Lantern licks a thin line along the wall. Every glimmer off the map feels loud, knife-bright, as if the dark outside is cupping the slats and listening for my counts. One. Two. I swallow the third. Even the bowl watches, slick as tide, waiting for a thumb.

I ease the oilcloth back. The vellum hums with wet light, lines quickening then going still, then quick again like a fish trying to lie about where it has been. Keep left, the map murmurs, spare as a nail. One file. Do not call. The words come from the page but hang in my chest. Down in the cut, a bell noses wood. Somewhere along the Bracken there will be another night like the last. No writ. Near-misses stacking like traps along a rake. Rumor running ahead of me with a dry mouth and a price. They will be counting my pace by lamplight if I keep on in shadow. They will be counting bodies if I don’t.

The bowl is simple clay, rim salted white, brine and ink braided thin on the surface. A countersign slips the water when I breathe near it, a trickle of order if I take it up. If I do, auditors step in my wake, blue and patient, and each curfew I throw will shave a brine-year I can’t buy back. If I don’t, the page keeps its private weather, and I guide by lantern and chalk and luck until luck runs lean. Either way I am a mark on someone’s ledger. Either way the wards pay.

I set the map flat. The spine-stitch bites. The lantern’s blade of light lays a straight road across the bowl, then breaks on my hand. Press, the map suggests, austere as a gate. The canal breathes, slow and salt. My thumb hovers over the black water, and the city leans in to hear what I name it.
