Helena Vance extended the documents over the escritoire. The paper bore the impression of a seal that had never existed in any registry she knew, and the ink sat upon the fibers with the wrong viscosity for the date it claimed. She held them steady, her emerald velvet sleeve motionless above the tarnished tongs arranged before the cold hearth. The mantel clock measured her breath against the silence.

Seventeen heirs stood at the perimeter, their postures arranged like entries in a ledger awaiting audit. No one spoke. The knowledge circulated among them that these papers would survive the thaw to reach the courts, where Bernard's archived indiscretions would accompany their own exposed debts and assignations. The room accepted this certainty with the rigid silence of frozen water.

Above, Marion Ashworth maintained her position on the upper landing, guarding the key she had concealed against her bodice. Helena did not look up. She understood that the portfolio locked in the wall safe contained seventeen months of documentation that would confirm what she now displayed, and that Marion's silence upstairs purchased the same vulnerability Helena offered below. The arrangement held by mutual threat.

Teddy Bryant stood apart from the assembly, his borrowed tweed shoulders squared toward her. Grease remained visible beneath his fingernails despite the gloves he had discarded. He watched the papers without blinking, his posh accent locked behind his teeth. Helena calculated the precise weight of evidence in her hand against the imprisonment that awaited him for the fraudulent claim.

The mantel clock struck twelve. The sound fractured against the plaster, and seventeen heirs adjusted their stances as one body, recognizing that the performance of civility had ended. The documents trembled slightly in Helena's gloved hand, not from uncertainty but from the cold that lived in the oxidized silver of the clock face and the archival dust disturbed by their breathing. She placed the papers upon the escritoire, where they rested like ice awaiting the crack.
