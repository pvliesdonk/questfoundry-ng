Marion Ashworth observed the draft descend from the chimney breast. The fire had consumed its final resinous log and the embers grayed beneath the grate. Ash scattered upward on the current; it settled across the Persian rug in a pale delineation that pointed toward the escritoire. The pattern held for three seconds before the draft shifted and the line dispersed into random scatter.

Helena maintained her position before the dying light. The documentation remained in her hand, unburned but singed at one corner. The paper curled slightly from the heat, though the room temperature fell.

Marion stepped back from the poker. Her glove left a smudge of tarnish upon the iron shaft. She observed the ash settling into the wool fibers of the rug and calculated the cost of removal against the cost of silence. The stain would require beating with snow and salt; the exposure would require only endurance.

Dr. Mott moved from the mantelpiece. Her empty glass left a ring of frozen condensation upon the marble. She retrieved her medical bag and placed it beside Bernard's corpse. The brass corners of the bag aligned with the ash line on the rug.

Teddy remained at the center of the room. Grease beneath his fingernails had attracted a dusting of soot from the air. He did not brush it away. The assembly stood in attitudes of mutual damage, seventeen configurations of breath held against the cold.

The ash completed its fall. The inevitable had breached the room in the form of dust and draft, and no inventory could restore the previous order.
