Dr. Mott gripped the ice sink's rim. The porcelain held a patina of tarnish where the drains had seized. She bent over the basin and her stomach contracted. The vault contained the sound. When she straightened, the bulb above swung in drafts that carried the smell of broken gin.

Sweat froze on her temple. The irons on her wrists clinked against the sink's edge. She reached for her medical bag and the tremor traveled up her arms. Her fingers spasmed around the brass clasp. The peppermint oil scent rose from the leather, inadequate against the sharper absence beneath it.

Teddy stood by the settle where Bernard's form lay beneath frayed damask. He removed his tweed jacket and folded it over the corpse's feet. He observed her hands without speaking.

"I require," she said. Her voice emerged as a whisper scraped across ice. "I require the table."

Teddy nodded. He moved toward the examination table that stood against the limestone wall. It bore the stains of previous administrations, its surface oxidized silver where the plating had worn through to base metal.

The withdrawal bent her forward again. She pressed her palms against the cold porcelain and pushed herself upright, but the motion fractured midway. Her teeth clicked against each other. She could not hold the scalpel. She could not hold her own wrists still.

"Bind me," she said.

Teddy retrieved lengths of silk scarf from the medical bag. He wound them around her wrists and tied them to the examination table's iron corners. The fabric bit into her skin. The irons remained fixed above the knots, a secondary imprisonment. Her hands stretched flat against the cold metal, steadied by the tension.

The room narrowed to the space between her eyes and Bernard's form. The water droplets on the ceiling's black ice accumulated to critical mass and fell, striking the examination table with the precision of a clock marking obsolete hours. She breathed through her nose. The trembling continued in her shoulders, her spine, but her hands remained fixed, tremors reduced to a vibration against the metal.

She nodded. Teddy lifted Bernard's form from the settle and placed it on the examination table beside her bound hands. The frozen flesh resisted the table's surface like a debt unpaid. Dr. Mott adjusted her fingers against the constraint of silk and iron, and made the first incision through skin that parted with the reluctance of pages sealed by frost.
