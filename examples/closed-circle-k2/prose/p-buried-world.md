Marion stood at the casement. Snow struck the glass with a persistence that suggested ledger entries accumulating against a debtor's account, each flake recording another unit of obligation without possibility of discharge. The drifts mounted against the lower panes, obscuring the garden path where Bernard's footprints had last marked the frost. Within three hours the accumulation achieved the height of the first-floor sills; by dusk the second storey would lose its view of the gateposts entirely.

The telegraph wires had ceased their vibration before noon. Marion observed the slackening of the lines through the upper light where ice had formed between the mullions, creating a lattice of frozen intersections that fractured the prospect into discrete squares of white. No courier had breached the drive since the constable's departure. The road existed now only in the obsolete account books of the county surveyor, its physical coordinates surrendered to the storm's administrative authority.

She turned from the window. Seventeen heirs occupied the chamber behind her, their postures arranged in the rigid geometry of a stock inventory, each item catalogued and sealed against withdrawal. The fallen chandelier lay across the parquet in a configuration of oxidized silver and fractured crystal that duplicated the window's frozen lattice. No one had proposed moving it. Escape had become a theoretical category, referenced only in the conditional tense of futures that would not mature.

The silence achieved the density of archival dust, settling into the weave of the Persian rug and the tarnished grooves of the picture frames. Marion calculated the duration of their provisions against the number of entries in the household ledger. The figures did not reconcile. She removed her pince-nez and placed it on the escritoire beside the dried pools of ink, acknowledging that the outside world had closed its books on them.
