Teddy held the document with both hands. The paper had softened at the edges where meltwater wicked into the fibers, but the patronymic remained distinct against the watermarking, a bureaucratic fact that would outlast the ice that had preserved it. He read the clause aloud, his voice assuming the cadence he had rehearsed among the wine racks, the posh accent holding steady against the assembly's scrutiny despite the grease beneath his fingernails. The seventeen heirs nodded in sequence, a mechanical assent that traveled around the settle like the ticking of stopped clocks. Their agreement legitimized the blow that had fractured the frozen block; the fire iron's violence was now administrative procedure, sanctioned by text that determined the succession according to the damaged but legible testament. Behind them, the grate held only grey particulate, the carbonized remains of Bernard's alphabetical divisions reduced to archival dust that coated the andiron. A fragment of the ice block rested upon the estate ledger, dripping water that spread across page eleven, blurring the ink that had recorded fourteen years of embezzlement into illegibility.

He set down the will and accepted the drainage orders from Helena's extended hand. The pen was a heavy instrument, unbalanced in his grip. He positioned it above the signature line where the patriarch's name should have appeared, the nib hovering over the claim he had forged from borrowed tweed and cellar subterfuge. Helena stepped closer. Her emerald velvet glove, frayed at the hem, touched his elbow, steadying the joint as the pen threatened to slip against the softened paper. Her hand remained there, a gesture held too long, until he completed the stroke. He wrote the signature with the deliberation of a man consigning his former occupation to the ash heap. In the next room, the fireplace had consumed the only records that could expose his true origins; the chauffeur's history was now smoke and carbonized matter among the remains. The ink dried permanent on the page, a darker stain than the water that had destroyed the ledger's earlier entries. As he lifted the pen, a sound propagated through the wall, the structural complaint of plaster surrendering to frost. The crack ran vertical behind the escritoire, a line that divided the room's geometry even as the signature fixed his false identity in the estate's permanent record.
