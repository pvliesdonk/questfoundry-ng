The tarnished chain snapped. The oxidized silver armature plunged seventeen feet and struck the Persian rug with a sound like obsolete account books slammed shut in an empty vault. Ice exploded outward in frozen shards that struck the glass and the frayed damask covering Bernard's form. The impact threw Marion backward; her hand opened and the key skittered across the parquet. Helena landed hard on the floor, her gloved hand crushed beneath a fragment of the frozen column. The cold burned through the fabric. She did not cry out. Seventeen heirs stepped back in unison, their postures rigid, their breath held as archival dust settled over the wreckage.

Helena dislodged her hand from beneath the ice fragment and stood. She crossed to where the frozen metal had come to rest against the settle's leg and retrieved it. The key dripped water onto her palm as she held it aloft. The heirs observed the gesture without comment. The assembly remained frozen in attitudes of bureaucratic shock, watching the chandelier settle into its new position as ruin.

Hours passed. The household dispersed from the ruined hall, retreating to corridors and closed rooms. Below stairs, the cellars harbored their own desperate calculations. Barrels filmed with ice lined the walls, and breath remained visible in the drafts. The single exposed bulb swung above the ceiling's black ice, where water had accumulated to critical droplet mass. The silence below matched the silence above.
