Helena Vance calculated the distance from the parquet to the chandelier's lowest crystal. Seventeen heirs maintained their positions between the grate and the ice-sealed windows. The breath-clouds had settled into a low fog that obscured the floorboards' divisions. She retained the portfolio's pressure against her ribs. The leather corners matched the shape of the documentation within: fourteen months of assignations, dates rendered in obsolete accounting shorthand, destinations translated from Bath to Pimlico.

The assembly had ceased its migration. Marion Ashworth stood by the settle where Bernard's body lay, the ledger still open in her hands. Dr. Mott had placed her empty glass upon the mantelpiece's exact center. Teddy Bryant faced the chandelier, his posture suggesting a former stage direction rather than a natural stance. The frozen key remained visible within its housing, suspended three millimeters lower than its position seventeen minutes prior.

Helena observed the hem of her emerald velvet gown. The frayed threads had extended another centimeter since morning. She considered the statistical probability of the chandelier's collapse against the certainty of her social extinction. A woman of sixty-three years, in evening dress, scaling the frozen façade of her own drawing-room: the ledger of her reputation would record this as lunacy or desperation. The alternative ledger recorded Marion's ruined standing, held in abeyance by Helena's silence.

The cold had penetrated her left glove. She removed it and touched the settle's oak arm. The wood retained the temperature of the cellar where Bernard had died. She calculated the tensile strength of ice, the weight of crystal, the response time of seventeen witnesses to a hostess's breach of protocol. The killer remained among them, utilizing the delay to arrange destruction of evidence or alibis. She had twelve seconds of stagecraft remaining before the assembled breath-clouds dispersed and civilized terror resumed its administrative pace.

She retained the portfolio. The emerald velvet settled against her ankles. She would either ascend the fixture or retain her position by the fire. Both actions would resolve within the hour. The chandelier dripped.
