The mantle clock marked the interval with a brass insistence that admitted no variation. Helena had counted forty-seven ticks since she entered the hall. The mechanism governed the room more completely than the failing heating system; it reduced the assembly to synchronized posture changes and the coordinated avoidance of eyes.

Marion stood by the settle where Bernard's body lay, her fingers leaving smudged prints on the ledger she had not yet closed. Dr. Mott had moved her empty glass three times across the mantelpiece, each placement precise, each failing to align with the clock's jurisdiction. Teddy remained at the mantelpiece, though the coal in the grate had diminished to ash that no longer radiated heat. His hands remained clasped behind his back, but the right thumb traced small circles against the left wrist, a motion that corresponded to no tempo the clock recognized.

The chandelier shifted. Ice cracked along its northern arm with a sound like tearing paper, and the frozen key suspended within the crystal structure descended three millimeters. The assembly froze. Marion's breath caught audibly. Dr. Mott's glass ceased its migration. The mechanical regularity had exposed their disorder, cataloging each failed gesture of composure. Helena held the portfolio tighter against her ribs. The leather had warmed to the temperature of her skin, but the documentation within remained as rigid as the ice above. She understood that the clock would continue its reckoning until the thaw released the key or the performance collapsed beneath the weight of its own maintenance.
