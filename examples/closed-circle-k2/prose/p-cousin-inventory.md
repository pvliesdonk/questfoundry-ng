The runners cut the traces at noon. Helena watched from the window seat as the cousin descended from the dogsled. Ice cracked beneath his boots. He did not brush the snow from his coat before entering the hall.

Seventeen heirs shifted in the corridor. The cousin surveyed them with the expression of a man reviewing obsolete account books, finding the entries wanting. His gaze lingered on the staircase, then drifted toward the study door where the files remained sealed behind oxidized silver hardware.

"Your theatrical collection," he said. His voice matched the frost on the windowpanes. "Inventory begins now."

Helena calculated the depreciating value of her performed past against the protective power of the evidence preserved above. The emerald velvet at her hem had frayed through seventeen winters of openings; the gestures she had held too long on stage had calcified into habits that no longer commanded the room. Against this stood the archive: alphabetized pages in a leather portfolio, the record of debts and assignations that threatened the cousin's claim to the crumbling estate.

He turned from the study door. His contempt registered as a tightening of his jaw, a slight adjustment of his gloves. Helena observed the desperation beneath the performance—the way his fingers twitched toward his pocket where a match case waited, the calculation in his eyes measuring the distance to the grate where documents might burn.

The heirs stood rigid. Their silence acknowledged that his orders for inventory masked a frantic search for leverage. Marion Ashworth touched her side where the metal bow had pressed its shape into her flesh; Teddy Bryant, freed from the pantry, adjusted his borrowed tweed. No one spoke. The cousin's authority depended on the destruction of the files before they spoke to the constable.

Helena remained seated. The cold lived in the objects around her—the tarnished braid on the curtains, the archival dust disturbed by the draft from the opened door. She calculated that her cracked crystal voice might yet purchase minutes against the thaw of evidence upstairs.
