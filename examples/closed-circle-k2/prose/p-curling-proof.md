The leather rested three inches from the iron basket where the ice had stood. Helena observed the oiled surface darken as the radiant heat penetrated the brass corners. She did not move to retrieve it. The portfolio had belonged to Bernard; now it contained the alphabetical arrangement of seventeen failures, the ledger shorthand of debts and assignations, the gelatin prints preserved under damask napkins that Marion had resealed with bureaucratic precision.

Marion stood beside the settle, her fingers maintaining their grip on the unpublished review. Dr. Mott had positioned her bag on the marble where it left a damp outline beside the frozen circle her glass had etched earlier. No one spoke of the heat. The edge of the uppermost paper began to curl away from the binding, the fibers contracting with the slow inevitability of archival decay exposed to warmth. The page lifted, warping, the ink of Bernard's precise copperplate softening toward illegibility though no flame had yet touched the hearth's offering.

Teddy Bryant adjusted his stance near the mantelpiece. His cuffs bore the grease from the cellars, and he held his hands ready at his sides as if to intercept the document before it fell. Helena calculated the seconds until the leather would smoke. Marion maintained her posture, the frayed sleeve of her emerald velvet gown brushing the settle's edge. The assembly breathed in unison, seventeen heirs holding their positions while the proof of their arrangements curled toward ash without ignition.
