Marion traced the frost down the stone wall toward the cellar. Her finger moved along the mortar lines where ice had accumulated in the grout, following the crystals as they branched across the masonry. The formations resembled handwriting in an obsolete script, a ledger notation from an account book she could not quite place. She paused. The glyphs suggested meaning without delivering it, a language suspended at the threshold of recognition.

The cellar air carried the reek of vintage port and organic decay. Ice crusted the barrel hoops in blackened rings. Her breath hung visible in the draft from the single exposed bulb overhead, which swayed and cast shifting shadows across the stone. She withdrew her finger. The frost had frozen the pad to a tackiness that pulled at her skin. She examined the wall. The patterns appeared deliberate, arranged in vertical columns that mimicked the lineation of Bernard's inventory entries, yet the characters remained illegible, a code frozen into the architecture itself.

She stepped back. Water accumulated on the ceiling in critical droplets that hung above the stone floor, trembling but unreleased. The message remained encoded in the ice, waiting for thaw or decryption. She turned from the wall. The cellar preserved its silence, the barrels standing in their rows like entries waiting to be totaled.
