Helena crossed to the drawing-room window. The glass wore a secondary skin of ice that had formed since morning, a lattice of frozen condensation that blurred the exterior into a wash of grey and white. She touched the surface. The cold bit through her glove with the precision of a surgical instrument, and when she withdrew her hand the frost remained undisturbed, a barrier more final than any lock.

The grounds had vanished. Where the snowbound lawn had stretched toward the frozen lake, now only the opacity of the ice remained, a tarnished silver that reflected nothing back. Rime accumulated along the sash in beaded formations that resembled tears turned to crystal, frozen in their descent and sealing the casing shut. The house turned inward upon itself, bereft of the compass point that had once oriented it to the world beyond.

Behind her, the room maintained its silence with the rigidity of a held breath. The scream from the corridor had ceased, leaving in its wake a pressure that pressed against the glass with equal weight. She turned from the obliterated view. The drawing-room had become a vault. Its dimensions contracted to the circumference of the walls. The cold had completed its conquest; no vista remained to remind them that an exterior world persisted beyond the frost.
