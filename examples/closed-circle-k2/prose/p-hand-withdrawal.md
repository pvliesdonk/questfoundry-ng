Teddy withdrew his hand from Helena's with the slow precision of a clerk finalizing a disputed account. His fingers slid from her emerald velvet glove, leaving a trace of cellar grease on the frayed cuff where the velvet had worn thin. The separation completed itself across three seconds, each interval marked by the stabilized drip of the chandelier above, which had ceased its thaw in the dropping temperature.

Helena's hand remained suspended at the height of the escritoire, fingers curled as if still calculating the weight of documentation she had not yet surrendered. Teddy observed that her glove absorbed the ambient chill until it matched the temperature of the marble balustrade. He flexed his own fingers. The grease from the 1874 vintage racks remained lodged in the creases of his knuckles, insufficient to the charge of murder but sufficient to mark the ledger of his deception.

The seventeen heirs maintained their positions. Their postures shifted by fractional degrees, shoulders squaring toward the vacant space between Teddy and Helena where alliance had previously been assumed. The absence of contact imposed a frostbitten estrangement upon the assembly, forfeiting the pretense of protection that had concealed their mutual suspicion. Teddy stood with his hands open at his sides, the grease congealing in his palms, and understood that the record of their complicity would stand unrevised in the archival dust of the room's silence.
