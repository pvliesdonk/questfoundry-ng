Helena stood at the cellar threshold. The draft from below had extinguished her match on the final riser, leaving the wick cold between her fingers. She dropped it. The emerald velvet of her gown hem had frayed against the stone during her ascent; now the threads dragged across the floorboards like exhausted credits in an obsolete ledger.

Dr. Mott approached from the pantry corridor. Her medical bag swung at her side, and the reek of peppermint preceded her by three paces. Her hands trembled as they gripped the brass fixtures.

"You will examine him tonight," Helena said. Her voice held the timbre of cracked crystal. "Before the flesh completes its surrender to the temperature."

Dr. Mott stopped. The bag creaked. "The instruments require calibration. The thermal conditions compromise precision." The words emerged loud and hollow, a defensive bluster against the silence.

Helena stepped closer. She removed her left glove, the leather slipping free. She held the gesture twelve seconds longer than courtesy required, her bare palm exposed to the cellar air. "The temperature suits your requirements for preservation," she said. "I am prepared to arrange your accommodation below until the examination is concluded. The barrels stand empty, and the lock functions."

Dr. Mott's posture rigidified. The medical bag swung in her grip like a pendulum losing its anchor. She looked toward the descending stairs, where the single exposed bulb swayed in drafts, casting oxidized silver shadows across the ice-crusted racks.

"You cannot," Dr. Mott said. The sentence trailed into the space between them.

"I have counted the hours," Helena said. She replaced the glove, sealing her hand in paired containment. "You will descend now, or you will remain until the thaw permits your testimony. The body stiffens toward unreadability."

The silence accumulated like ice on blackened barrel hoops, critical and unreleased. Dr. Mott moved toward the cellar door. Her footsteps marked the stone with the sound of tarnished silver striking slate, each impact a small increment in a running total of capitulation.
