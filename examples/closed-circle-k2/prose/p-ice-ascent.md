Helena Vance placed her hand upon the frozen chandelier. The oxidized silver armature groaned under her weight, and she felt the cold transmit through her emerald velvet glove into the bone beneath. She had worn thinner soles for the evening, a vanity now rendered absurd, and her boots sought purchase on the slick crystal tiers that spiraled upward toward the suspended key. The chandelier swayed, minutely, a pendulum of ice and tarnished grandeur, and she calculated the distance to the key against the thirty-seven years of accumulated dust that coated the ceiling plaster above.

She ascended. Her left boot slipped on a sheathed crystal droplet, and she gripped the armature with her free hand, feeling the tarnish flake against her palm. The assembly below made a sound, not quite vocalized, a collective intake that echoed off the frost-lined windows. Seventeen heirs stood in their fixed positions around the settle, their faces tilted upward, their postures rigid as the archival ledgers that had documented their ruin. She did not look down. She placed her right boot on a higher tier, and the ice beneath her sole cracked, a sharp report like a ledger snapping shut, and the chandelier shuddered.

The climb continued. Each movement sent fractures spidering through the frozen casing that sheathed the central column, hairline cracks racing through the clear ice like ink spreading across water-damaged paper. The key hung above her, encased in its own block of frozen water, suspended from a chain that had tarnished to black. She reached. Her fingers extended toward the jagged ice, and the assembly gasped, a synchronized breach of their maintained silence. Her glove brushed the frozen surface. She grasped the key through the ice and yanked.

The fracture was immediate and catastrophic. The ice did not merely crack; it surrendered along hidden fault lines that ran deep into the crystal structure, sending fissures racing outward through the chandelier's frozen heart. A droplet of suspended melt broke free and struck the Persian rug below with a sound like a seal breaking. The chandelier shuddered, tilting on its chain, and Helena felt the shift in the room's equilibrium as the frozen centerpiece released its structural integrity. The precarious order of the assembled heirs cracked with it, exposing the fragility of their civil arrangement to the cold air of the hall.
