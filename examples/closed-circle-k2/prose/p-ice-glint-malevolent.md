Helena mounted the last crystalline stair. The tier of frozen water bore her weight with a sound like archival paper tearing beneath a bureaucratic stamp. Above her, the ceiling plaster exhaled thirty-seven years of accumulated dust into the cold air. She looked down. The oxidized silver armature groaned and shifted, tilting toward the Persian rug seventeen feet below. The heirs stood in fixed postures around the settle where Bernard's form rested beneath frayed damask, their faces tilted upward, their stillness exact and obsolete.

The ice glinted. A malevolent sheen traveled along the fractured casing of the central column, catching the dim light from the frost-lined windows. The glint confirmed what the groan had suggested: the environment had ceased to be passive archive. It was an active threat now, a ledger of frozen water accounting for her weight in real time, tallying the stress of her emerald velvet glove against the tarnished chain. She stood motionless for three seconds, calculating the cost of descent against the value of the frozen key in her palm. The chandelier swayed. A droplet of suspended melt struck the rug below with the precision of a clock marking unpaid debt. She did not breathe. The assembly remained silent, seventeen heirs frozen in postures of archival rigidity, watching the oxidized silver calculate her fate.
