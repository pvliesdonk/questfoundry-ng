Dr. Elspeth Mott placed her hand upon the banister. The iron had vanished beneath a casing of frozen water that rose in dendritic patterns like frost upon a laboratory slide. The gaslight from the wall sconce caught the translucent layers, bending the flame into duplicated refractions that trembled against the tarnished silver of the stair rod. She removed her fingers. The surface retained the precise print of her glove pads for three seconds before the cold erased the impression.

The stairs descended into a darkness where the cellar draughts had accumulated. Snow had blown inward from some broken casement below, accumulating on the third and fourth steps in a drift that resembled sifted archival dust. The wood beneath her boots gave off a sound of compressed ledgers, each riser groaning with the record of its own decay. She calculated the distance to the pantry corridor: seventeen steps, plus the intervening landing where the maids had tracked slush that now re-frozen into a glaze.

The railing presented a choice. To grip it risked adhesion; to descend without support risked imbalance on the iced treads. She observed the sculpture of frozen condensation that sheathed the newel post, its whorls preserving the shape of prior hands that had grasped and released in haste. The cold had converted the stairwell from passage to obstacle, transforming the house's vertebrae into a mechanism of hazard. She stepped downward. The gamble was mechanical now: weight distributed, heel before toe, the body's ledger balancing against gravity's final audit.
