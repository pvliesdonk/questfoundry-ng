Helena Vance lifted the portfolio from the escritoire. The leather had darkened where meltwater seeped through the seams, tracing the document's shape beneath. She opened the clasp. The paper held the texture of damp archival dust, the patriarch's final clause bloomed into gray washes at the margin, but the integers remained decipherable.

She read the provision aloud, formalizing the transfer to the assembled heirs. Her voice moved through the hall between the ice-veined glass and the collapsing embers, each syllable carrying the weight of a closed ledger. The seventeen stood without shifting, their gloves hanging at their sides in the attitude of ledgers shelved against future inspection. No one moved. The chandelier released a drop that struck the marble with a report too sharp for the height.

Teddy Bryant stood with his hands unclasped. The tarnish on his cuffs had dulled to a flat sheen, and the frayed damask lining of his borrowed tweed showed at the sleeve. She noted the detail without reaction. 

The estate would transfer before the storm sealed the approach; the creditors would find their claims anticipated by the record, however compromised. The inheritance stood resolved. The water damage had not obscured the heir's designation sufficiently to invite contest, and the storm's imminent sealing of the roads would prevent the creditors from filing before the transfer executed. Helena closed the portfolio. The clasp engaged with a sound of finality.

Dr. Elspeth Mott stood between two constables at the mahogany lectern. The irons on her wrists had settled into the posture of permanent fixture, and her medical bag rested on the settle beside Bernard's form. She did not look at Teddy. The constables escorted her toward the frost-locked corridor beyond the great doors. She left the bag behind. Helena remained at the escritoire, calculating the hours until the storm would break, listening as the footsteps diminished into the farther corridors where the physician would confront her deprivation without the ballast of her authority.
