Marion Ashworth adjusted her pince-nez. The wire left a faint impression on the bridge of her nose, a pressure she calculated against the alternative of blurred vision. She stood beside the tea table where the oxidized silver surface reflected nothing of the transaction concluded above the grate. The ash had settled into administrative neutrality, indistinguishable between the residue of coal and the carbonized remains of her devastating critique.

She touched her left hand. The wedding band remained in its station, a circle of tarnished gold that had survived the incineration of evidence regarding her fourteen-month assignation. The ring held its temperature against her finger, transmitting the cold of the metal rather than any warmth of proprietary affection. She rotated it once, noting the friction of oxidized interior against skin, then let it rest.

Helena Vance stood by the settle, brushing dust from emerald velvet with movements that suggested ledger entries rather than domestic maintenance. Marion observed the tan line on Helena's palm, still visible, still damning in its geometry, but now divorced from the documentation that would have given it juridical weight. They did not speak. The silence between them had acquired the density of an archival index, cross-referenced and complete.

Marion retrieved her gloves from the tea table. The kid leather had darkened where ink had stained her fingers, a discoloration that would not launder out. She drew them on, covering the evidence of her professional capacity even as she concealed the evidence of her personal collapse. The marriage remained legible to outside observation: the ring, the gloves, the administrative posture she assumed beside the hearth.

She turned from the grate. The screen stood before the ashes, preserving a facade of ordered domesticity. Behind it, the foundation had charred to carbon, but the structure held. She stepped onto the marble, her heel striking with the precision of a cancelled appointment, and the cold rose through her soles from the stone beneath.
