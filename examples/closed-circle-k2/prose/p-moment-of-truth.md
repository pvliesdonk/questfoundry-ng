Bernard Croft aligned the ivory cigarette holder parallel to the ledger's spine. The cellar book had stiffened during his ascent, frost crystallizing the margins where his thumb rested. He positioned his liver-spotted hands at right angles to the table edge, a gesture of administrative precision he had maintained for forty years.

The seventeen heirs had completed their semicircle around the dripping chandelier. Teddy Bryant stood at its center, his borrowed tweed jacket hanging loose on shoulders that still bore the posture of the warehouse district. The chauffeur's fingers curled toward his breast pocket, then arrested. Black crescents marked the cuticles, grease from the cellar railings that no amount of solvent could fully remove.

"I found the glass at eleven," Teddy said. The vowels flattened, shedding their polished coating. "Between the 1874 and 1876 ports. The dust was disturbed."

The assembly turned. Bernard felt the seventeen gazes track toward his station by the settle, weighing the absence of deference that had governed the previous decades. He remained stationary, his pale eyes unblinking as he calculated the distance to the dying fire, the seconds required to feed the ledger to the flames, the probability that water damage would prevent ignition before the ink betrayed him.

"The lip prints matched," Teddy continued. He stepped forward, his right hand hanging open to display the grease stains. "Bernard Croft was stealing wine when the patriarch died. He has no alibi. He has motive."

Marion Ashworth gripped her certificates tighter. Dr. Mott's fingers twitched toward her medical bag, then withdrew. Helena Vance shifted her portfolio from one arm to the other, the emerald velvet of her right glove brushing against the frayed damask where Bernard had placed the ledger.

Bernard tapped the cigarette holder against the book. The sound was sharp and final in the vault of the hall. He understood that the maintenance of order had reached its terminus; the records he had kept to ensure his security now guaranteed his exposure. Above him, the frozen key in the chandelier shifted within its casing, descending until the ice cracked with the sound of a ledger snapping shut.
