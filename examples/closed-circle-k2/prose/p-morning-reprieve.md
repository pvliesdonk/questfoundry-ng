Teddy stepped away from the settle where Bernard's body lay. The grease beneath his fingernails left faint marks on the borrowed tweed as he adjusted his cuffs. He crossed the parquet to where Helena stood by the trestle. The ice block rested there, its southeast face showing the artificial crack Marion had identified, water damage spreading through the document visible within. Above them, the chandelier swayed minutely, the frozen key suspended in its depths casting a dim reflection across the ash-dusted floor.

He stopped before her. His posture suggested the rigidity of theatrical scenery stored in winter, yet his eyes held a focus Helena recognized from the warehouse theatre decades prior. He was preparing to forget his cue, or perhaps to deliver it too late.

"Grant me until morning," he said. His accent, usually polished to a chauffeur's deference, slipped on the vowels, betraying the Whitechapel origins she had exposed hours before. He gestured toward the ice. "The cracks have widened. By dawn I can break the rest, and the document within will verify what I claim."

Helena adjusted her grip on the portfolio. The damask napkins inside held the documentation of Marion's Pimlico assignations, but her fingers calculated the weight of silence against disclosure. She observed the compulsive clock-watching in his glance toward the mantelpiece, where Dr. Mott's glass sat unmoved. He performed the role of innocent heir, yet his hands hung at his sides with the particular stillness of one preparing to hang for another's crime.

"Morning," she said. Her voice carried the timbre of cracked crystal, held at a pitch that suggested deliberation rather than concession. She did not nod. She did not release the portfolio. She allowed the word to stand between them as a provisional boundary, a ledger entry not yet balanced.

Marion inhaled sharply. The sound cut through the silence like the scratch of a nib on coarse paper. She folded the ledger against her chest, the ink-stained fingers leaving smudges on the wool, and turned toward the bedroom corridor. Her footsteps faded on the stairs as she withdrew to search the upper rooms for leverage, leaving the ice debate to those who remained below.

The assembly held its breath. The frozen key in the chandelier dropped another millimeter, and the ice in the block sighed as the thaw continued its patient work of exposure.
