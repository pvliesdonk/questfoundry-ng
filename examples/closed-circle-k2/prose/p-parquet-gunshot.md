Droplets accumulated on the parquet in discrete circumferences, each impact darkening the wood like entries in an obsolete account ledger kept by liquid measure. Helena counted the intervals. The afternoon light failed across the frayed damask of the window treatments, and the seventeen heirs abandoned their card play.

Hours passed. The killer remained unidentified within their number. Voices rose from the escritoire. The heirs traded accusations regarding forged prescriptions and disturbed cellar entries. A gentleman shook a leather portfolio at Marion Ashworth, who stood with her kid gloves folded against her hip. Dr. Mott positioned herself near her medical bag; her hands rested motionless on the brass clasps. Teddy Bryant shifted his weight near the settle where Bernard's form lay beneath tarnished damask. The assembly's patience frayed into open hostility as the wait stretched toward night. They accused one another of embezzlement and adultery, of fraudulent identity and concealed weapons. The fire died to embers. The heirs maintained rigid propriety despite the mortal threat among them, their postures formal even as their diction decayed into interruption and laconic threat. The key remained frozen above them. It descended in increments visible only to Helena's calculation.

The accusations reached a temporary lull. Seventeen heirs held their breath. Overhead, the chandelier released its burden. The ice sheath fractured along the oxidized silver armature, and the frozen key plummeted to the parquet with a report like a gunshot. The assembly flinched. Marion's papers slipped from her hand. Dr. Mott's bag tipped against the chair leg. The arguments halted in shocked silence that settled over the room like archival dust.
