Marion withdrew her hand from the ledger and stepped toward the wall. The plaster showed a hairline fracture running diagonally from the cornice, branching into tributaries that spidered toward the wainscoting like veins on aged archival paper. She raised her index finger, ink-stained from Bernard's records, and traced the primary fissure downward. The crack widened slightly under pressure, revealing a network of secondary splits that angled with precision unusual for structural failure.

The pattern resolved into shapes she recognized. The main line descended vertically, then jogged right in a horizontal stroke, terminating in a serif-like hook. Adjacent fractures formed loops and crossbars, approximating glyphs from an obsolete accounting shorthand she had observed in the cellar inventory. The cracks did not wander; they composed a nearly alphabetical array, deliberate as a ledger entry.

Cold migrated from the plaster into her fingertip. The surface temperature had dropped below the ambient room chill, leaching warmth through the thin kid leather until her skin numbed. She held the contact for three seconds, long enough to map the sequence against her memory of Bernard's handwriting, then retracted her hand. The design suggested intention rather than decay, a message carved by temperature differential and patience rather than simple gravity or settling foundation.

She brushed a speck of ice from her fingertip. The frost on the wall remained undisturbed, a ledger of thermal erosion written in frozen water and lime dust. Behind her, the assembly maintained its rigid silence, but Marion understood now that the house itself kept records more damning than any critic's notebook.
