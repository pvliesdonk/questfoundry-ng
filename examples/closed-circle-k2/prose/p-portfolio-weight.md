Marion Ashworth descended the stairs from the study. She held Bernard's portfolio in both hands, supporting the oiled leather against her hip as the brass corners dug into her palm. The documents inside shifted with each step, their edges settling against the spine with the soft crush of paper against paper. With each step, the weight seemed to increase, as if the folio absorbed the damp from the air and the accumulated mass of seventeen separate certainties.

She paused at the landing. Below, seventeen heirs maintained their semicircle around the Persian rug. Their postures had shifted since the constable's departure; shoulders squared toward her descent, chins lifted in calculation of what the portfolio contained. Marion adjusted her grip. The leather creaked. She felt the key against her bodice, the bow-shaped welt pulsing where metal pressed skin.

Helena Vance stood beside the escritoire, her emerald velvet sleeve withdrawn from the damp. Her gaze tracked Marion's descent without lifting to meet her eyes. Teddy Bryant remained near the andiron, his hand resting on the desk where he had set the pen. He did not look up. Marion continued downward. The portfolio's weight pulled her shoulder into a slight lean, a posture of burden she recognized from Bernard's final exit. She reached the marble floor and turned toward the wall where the oxidized silver fixture waited, its combination dial tarnished to the color of old blood. The documents shifted inside the leather, settling into final arrangement.
