Marion refolded the ledger's corner a fourth time. The crease aligned with the plaster crack behind her, a glyph of obsolete accounting shorthand frozen in the wall. She stepped back from the trestle. The patriarch's portrait hung above the settle, its eyes tracking her movement across the parquet. The paint had oxidized to a tarnished silver at the temples, and the gaze held the particular fixity of archival records stored too long in subzero vaults. She felt the observation settle against her skin like frost.

Teddy maintained his posture of confession, his hands still raised in that theatrical gesture of surrender. Marion observed the grease rimming his fingernails, the untouched poker on the mantelpiece where she had last recorded its position, the discrepancies assembling themselves into a ledger of impossibilities. She drew a sharp inhalation. The sound cut through the breath-fog and seventeen heads turned toward her.

Helena stood by the window, the portfolio held against her emerald velvet skirt. Marion watched her adjust her grip on the damask napkins that concealed the documentation. The paper's edges frayed like obsolete account books left too long in damp cellars. Marion's fingers moved to her pince-nez, then dropped to her sides. The protection of composed silence had sustained her through three decades of critical observation, but now the shield cracked along the same seam as the plaster. She stood exposed, her Bath visits mapped in ink against the solicitor's Pimlico address, her indiscretion catalogued with Bernard's precision.

The drawing room contracted. The civility that had governed the assembly since the storm sealed the valley now revealed its underlying architecture of threat. Marion tasted the hostility like copper on her tongue, cold and distinct.
