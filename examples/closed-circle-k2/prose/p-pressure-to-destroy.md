Helena Vance stepped toward the grate. The fire had sunk to embers and cast a failing light across the assembled heirs. She held the portfolio against her emerald velvet gown; the brass corners pressed through the frayed damask into her ribs. Marion stood beside the escritoire with ink-stained fingers still touching the oiled leather. The gelatin prints lay exposed upon the blotter. Helena observed the assembly's posture: seventeen rigid spines, seventeen sets of eyes fixed upon the evidence of their systematic disgrace.

She placed her portfolio on the settle beside Bernard's stiffened corpse. The archival dust rose from the cushions and settled upon the wool of her sleeve. She spoke. She said that the dead maintained no rights to testimony. She said that Bernard's surveillance ended with his breath, and that his records constituted a secondary violence against those who survived. She named the principle of decency. She named the obligation to protect the living from the indignity of exposure.

Marion gripped the escritoire's edge. Her pince-nez had slipped to the end of her nose; she had not adjusted them. Dr. Mott stood three paces from the medical bag with her empty glass trembling on the mantelpiece. Teddy remained in his waistcoat with grease still visible beneath his fingernails. No one moved to refute the proposition.

Helena opened her portfolio. She removed the documentation of Marion's fourteen-month assignation with the Pimlico solicitor. The paper had grown stiff from the cold. She held it toward the dying flames. She proposed that they restore dignity through destruction. She proposed that they feed the archive to the fire and allow the dead to bury their dead in silence. The frost on the windowpanes seemed to advance across the glass as she spoke. The chandelier shifted; ice cracked overhead. The frozen key descended another three millimeters toward the assembly.

Marion stepped forward. Her glove touched the oxidized silver of the poker. She did not lift it. She said that truth demanded preservation regardless of cost. Helena maintained her position before the grate. The embers illuminated the frayed hem of her gown. She said that survival required the performance of mercy, not its substance. She held the documentation steady. The paper trembled in the draft from the sealed window. The assembly waited. The fire waited.
