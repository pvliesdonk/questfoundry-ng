Helena withdrew her hand from the chandelier's frozen reach. The gesture completed itself in empty air, theatrical and precise, suspended above the seventeen heirs who stood in their rigid stations around the settle.

She spoke. Her voice fractured against the marble like cracked crystal. "We shall not ascend. We shall not disturb the fixture, nor risk the assembly's safety through precipitate action." She paused. Her emerald velvet sleeve drooped toward the Persian rug, the frayed hem grazing the cinders that traced the route toward the escritoire. "We shall await the natural progression of thaw. We shall maintain the standards of conduct appropriate to this house, regardless of circumstance."

The heirs shifted, and postures adjusted behind damask and wool. A gentleman produced a deck of obsolete account cards from his breast pocket; the pasteboard had absorbed moisture along its edges, swelling the embossed crests into illegible blisters. He dealt to four others who gathered at the escritoire's edge, their fingers avoiding the brass fixtures dulled by proximity to Bernard's form. They spoke of rainfall statistics in distant counties, of harvest yields and ledger discrepancies, their voices pitched to bureaucratic exactness. Their eyes returned between phrases to the oxidized silver armature above, where ice sheathed the chain in increments of frozen water.

Dr. Mott retrieved her medical bag from the marble and placed it beside her chair. She did not open it. Marion Ashworth stood with her papers concealed against her ribs, her kid gloves folded at precisely matching creases. No one moved toward the hearth. The fire died further, scattering cinders across the rug in patterns that suggested fallen ledgers.

Hours accumulated. The chandelier dripped. Each drop fell at intervals of seventeen seconds, striking the tarnished silver with a sound like the closure of distant accounts. The key descended three millimeters, then five, encased in its cylinder of thawing ice. The temperature in the hall rose by fractional degrees, detectable only in the behavior of archival dust that ceased its suspended drift and settled upon shoulders, upon eyelashes, upon the frayed velvet hem that Helena held motionless above the weave. The heirs maintained their stations. The cards continued their circuit. The ice surrendered its hold with the implacable slowness of obsolete inventory, and the frozen key descended through the oxidized chain toward the assembly's rigid propriety.
