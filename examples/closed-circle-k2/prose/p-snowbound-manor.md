The storm had sealed the valley three days prior. Roads vanished under drifts that swallowed stone walls whole; telephone lines collapsed under the weight of frozen sheaths. The lake beyond the terrace had solidified overnight into a vast gray ledger, its surface recording the final transactions of open water. Men had hauled the patriarch's will from that ice that morning, a massive block still sweating cold mist across the Great Hall's parquet. Through its fissured depths, the document remained visible, a watermark of deferred judgment.

Helena stood beneath the chandelier. Its crystal droplets had gathered frozen condensation during the night, transforming the fixture into a suspended architecture of thaw and refreeze that caught the gray light from windows sheeted with interior frost. She wore the emerald velvet with the frayed hem. Her hand trembled as she poured from the silver pot—lukewarm, the tea had steeped too long and cooled too fast. "Tea," she said. The word carried the timbre of cracked crystal. No one moved to take the cups.

The guests assembled in postures of forced leisure. Marion Ashworth held the saucer at an angle that suggested drink forgotten; Dr. Mott stood near the mantelpiece with fingers interlaced behind the coat, concealing the tremor. Teddy Bryant examined the gloves as though reading a palm. The fire in the grate coughed embers rather than heat. No one spoke of the staircase blocked by snowdrift above, or the silence where the heating pipes had groaned and then ceased.

The temperature dropped another degree. Someone set down china with a click that sounded like a mechanism engaging. The guests looked from the snow-choked windows to the ice block containing the will, then to the dormant pipes. The assembly had become inventory, items in a sealed ledger, accounted for but not yet distributed.

Helena set down the pot. The gesture she held too long, a theatrical pause that had once commanded attention, now simply announced the cold working through her joints. She would check the cellars.
