Teddy descended the wine cellar stairs. The single exposed bulb swung above the racks, casting shadows that moved like obsolete account books shifting in a draft. Ice crusted the barrels, and his breath hung visible before him, a ledger of respiration marking the intervals. He counted the steps—twenty-three—without conscious intent, the habit of a man who had learned to mark time in increments of survival.

He located the 1874 vintage. The bottles stood in their rack, labels facing outward with bureaucratic precision. Behind them, the 1876 port waited in deeper shadow. Teddy removed the gin from beneath his borrowed tweed, where the glass had rested against his ribs beneath the frayed damask lining. He set the bottles on the stone floor and examined the arrangement. The gap between vintages offered sufficient concealment; the dust on the 1874 bottles remained undisturbed, a protective film that would mask any disturbance of his fingerprints.

He placed the gin behind the port with the care of a man filing a false ledger. The glass struck the rack with a sound too loud in the vault's silence. He paused, listening for footsteps above. The black ice on the ceiling dripped water onto the stone, the accumulation reaching critical mass and falling in intervals that matched his pulse. He arranged the vintage bottles to stand at their previous angle, the oxidized silver caps catching the swinging light in brief flashes of tarnished warning.

Dr. Mott would locate them when her tremor demanded it. He calculated the hours until her medical bag would reveal its emptiness, until the peppermint oil failed to mask the absence within. This placement bound them now: her continued function dependent upon his discretion, his fraudulent identity sheltered by her compromised testimony. The secret rested between the vintages like a frozen contract, ice-crusted and patient.

He stood and brushed the dust from his knees. The bulb swung above, and his shadow stretched across the ice-crusted barrels, a long ledger entry marking the transaction. He climbed the stairs without looking back, leaving the gin to wait in the dark, preserved behind the vintage port like evidence yet to be discovered.
