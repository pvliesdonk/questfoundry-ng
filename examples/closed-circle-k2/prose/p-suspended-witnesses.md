Helena watched the dust motes turn in the gaslight. They drifted through the updrafts from the dying fire, each speck spiraling in the currents between the vaulted ceiling and the assembly below, wheeling and settling in slow arcs, tiny witnesses to the room's disrupted order and the violence that had breached its meticulous routines.

She lowered her gaze. Bernard's corpse lay on the settle beneath the frozen testament, his throat sealed by the same cold that had invaded the lower levels. Marion stood with her certificates folded against her ribs, the papers stiff with cold. Dr. Mott had placed her medical bag on the floorboards; the leather darkened where it touched the spreading frost. Teddy maintained his posture of service, hands clasped behind his back, eyes fixed on the chandelier chain as if calculation might bridge the interval before discovery. None of them spoke. The four survivors maintained their rigid positions around the dead man, cataloguing the silence in increments that matched the settling of ice outside.

Helena brushed the damask of her sleeve. The movement dislodged ice crystals from the frayed hem of her emerald velvet; they fell to the floor with a sound too slight to register in the room's density. The gesture belonged to another life, a routine of maintenance performed in a space that no longer answered to housekeeping or to the schedules that had governed the estate's operation. She felt the thaw seeping through the mortar, a dissolution of the estate's frozen integrity that brought no comfort to the assembled heirs.
