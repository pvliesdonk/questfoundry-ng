Teddy Bryant knelt on the pantry's stone floor. The cold transferred from the flags through his trousers with the efficiency of a ledger entry. Behind him, the heirs worked in silence. Marion Ashworth held the curtain cords—heavy silk braided with tarnished silver thread that had once held back the damask in the east wing. She wound them around his wrists with the methodical care of a clerk binding obsolete accounts for storage.

He did not resist. His shoulders remained squared toward the assembly, but the grease beneath his fingernails had begun to congeal in the cellar-adjacent air. The posh accent he had maintained for twenty years slipped into silence, unnecessary now that the documentation Helena Vance had brandished had confirmed what the grease already testified: he was not the heir, only the chauffeur who had borrowed a name.

A voice behind him stated that the roads were buried. Teddy did not turn to identify the speaker. Through the pantry's single window, ice had sealed the glass in patterns that recalled the cracks in the frozen chandelier above the great hall. The storm had completed its work, isolating the seventeen heirs with their suspicion and the ash of Bernard's blackmail materials.

Marion pulled the cord tight. The silk bit into his skin with the particular pressure of administrative finality. Teddy counted the barrels ranked against the wall—wine stores, vinegar, the preserved evidence of Bernard's embezzlement that had survived while the blackmail archive burned. Without the alphabetical foolscap that had catalogued seventeen failures and assignations, no heir could prove which among them had held sufficient cause to silence the old man. The motive had dissolved into the andiron's ash, leaving only Teddy's fraudulent identity as certain accusation.

His knees ached against the stone. Above, water accumulated to critical mass on the cellar ceiling, preparing its descent. The heirs maintained their perimeter, rigid as oxidized silver, watching him assume the posture of guilt while the storm buried the access roads beyond retrieval.
