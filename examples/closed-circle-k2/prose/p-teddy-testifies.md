Teddy stepped forward. The borrowed tweed lay folded over the settle beside Bernard's stiffened corpse; archival dust rose from the wool as the fabric settled. He stood now in his waistcoat, his hands unclasped at his sides. The grease beneath his fingernails had transferred to the cloth of his trousers where he had wiped them after touching the disturbed dust between the 1874 and 1876 vintages. He spoke without looking at Helena or Marion. He stated that Bernard had maintained records beyond the cellar ledger. He named the leather portfolio and its alphabetical arrangement by victim. He said that concealment had become conspiracy. His posh accent slipped on the final syllable, revealing the warehouse vowels beneath. The bulb above the settle swayed in a draft from the sealed window, shifting shadows across the frost-rimed plaster.

Marion held the oiled leather against her lap. She had retrieved it from beneath Bernard's mattress in the servant's cell, where the frost had stiffened the damask. She rose and placed it on the escritoire. Her ink-stained fingers opened the brass corners. The pages revealed systematic embezzlement in the fourth columns of the primary ledger, adultery in the gelatin prints sorted beneath frayed damask napkins, and forged prescriptions in the resealed medical correspondence. The evidence could imprison half the assembly. A gentleman lowered his pocket watch. A lady touched her oxidized silver necklace and frayed her glove against the metal. The assembly's rigid posture cracked. The tarnish on the silver seemed to spread to the faces around her. Someone knocked over an empty glass. The civil arrangement dissolved into panic.
