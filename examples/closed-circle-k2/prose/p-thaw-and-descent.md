The ice relinquished its grip in increments too small for calendar notation but vast in the accounting of hours. Helena watched the sheath withdraw from the oxidized silver armature above, revealing the key by fractions until gravity completed what thaw had delayed. It struck the oak floorboards with a clatter distinct from gunshot, a sound of administration rather than alarm. The assembly did not flinch. Seventeen heirs maintained their postures, their breath suspended not in shock but in the calculation of lost opportunity. The interval between the chandelier's drip and the key's descent had consumed the investigative window; the killer's traces had settled like archival dust upon the parquet, indistinguishable from the scuff of legitimate panic.

The key lay where it fell, forgotten among the frayed damask of Helena's hem and the tarnished braid of the escritoire's edge. Marion Ashworth's kid gloves hung motionless at her hip. Dr. Mott's fingers rested on her medical bag's brass clasps without opening them. Teddy Bryant stood with his weight shifted toward the settle where Bernard's form remained beneath its covering. No one moved to retrieve the metal from the floor's obscuring grain. Their attention shifted downward, not to the key but past it, through the floorboards to the vault below where ice-crusted barrels held the liquid necessities that had sustained the household's deceptions. The wine racks waited in the darkness beneath their feet, containing the only remaining evidence that time had not already dissolved.
