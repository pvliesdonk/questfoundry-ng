Helena Vance placed her palms against the ice block and felt the cold penetrate the emerald velvet of her gloves. The document inside had begun to show through the southeast cracks, a blurred testament visible behind the fractured surface. She steadied the block against the andiron while Teddy Bryant selected a fire iron from the set beside the grate. His borrowed tweed caught the lamplight; grease marked his fingernails where he had gripped the cellar rack hours before.

"Hold it there," he said.

She adjusted her stance. The Persian rug beneath her boots held the stain of melted ice and earlier violence. Seventeen heirs stood at the perimeter, their silence a ledger of mutual suspicion. Above them, Marion Ashworth had locked Bernard's portfolio in the study safe, arranging the alphabetical entries of their indiscretions behind frayed damask. Helena calculated the cost of each drop of water that fell from the thawing block onto the rug, each smudge that rendered the patriarch's final instructions less legible. The investigator would arrive with the storm; any scorch or scratch would annotate their guilt in the file yet to be written.

Teddy raised the iron. His shoulders squared toward the assembly, a performer's stance recalled from twenty years past. The iron descended. It struck the ice with a crack that echoed against the plaster, biting deep into the frozen water. Splinters flew and caught the lamplight like tarnished silver. Helena held the block steady against the impact, her fingers numbing through the velvet. The ice fractured along new lines, and text emerged—ink made visible, a name taking shape where before there had been only opacity.

Teddy lowered the iron. His posh accent slipped as he exhaled. "Again?"

Helena did not answer. She shifted her weight and felt the frayed hem of her gown brush the wet wool of the rug. The document showed through the fresh cleavage, legible now, determining the succession despite the water damage spreading from the edges. She knew that Marion held the key to the archive upstairs against her welted skin, and that the portfolio would survive as court evidence regardless of what they did here. But the will required reading before the roads sealed. She nodded.

Teddy struck again. The iron bit deeper.
