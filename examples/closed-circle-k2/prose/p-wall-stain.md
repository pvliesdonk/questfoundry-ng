Teddy noticed the discoloration spreading across the calcimine like a map of forgotten water damage. He stood near the settle where Bernard's form remained concealed beneath the damask covering. The heirs maintained their stations around the room, their postures rigid against the encroaching thaw. Meltwater had pooled on the marble floor, reflecting the oxidized silver of the chandelier's armature. The temperature shift had activated the concealed fractures in the wall.

The stain advanced. It moved across the plaster above the wainscoting in a configuration that resembled an extended hand, fingers reaching toward the picture rail. The calcimine had concealed seventeen previous applications of gilt and plaster, each layer applied to obscure the structural compromise beneath. Now the thaw released what the frost had sealed. The discoloration darkened the fibers of the upholstery where the settle stood, mapping the house's concealed damage against the archival dust that covered every surface.

Seventeen heirs turned their faces toward the wall. A gentleman lowered his hand from his breast pocket, abandoning the swollen deck of pasteboard he had held since the previous hour. Dr. Mott placed her bag beside the chair but did not open it. Marion held her papers against her side; her gloves rested in matched folds. Helena remained motionless near the escritoire, her sleeve hanging inert.

The damp had penetrated the lath beneath the plaster. Water accumulated in the gaps between structural members, and the cost of restoration calculated itself in Teddy's mind against the ledger values he had memorized. He understood that the cost of maintaining this room's civility had escalated beyond the liquid assets of the estate. He smoothed his waistcoat against the humidity and awaited the assembly's next adjustment. The stain continued its ascent, recording their presence in a script of obsolete decay.
