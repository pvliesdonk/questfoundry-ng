Helena Vance extended her hand toward the andiron. Water had pooled across the Persian rug in a darkening oval, draining through the floorboards with a sound like pages turning. The ice block had reduced to a fractured shell, its southeast quadrant collapsed inward. Through the fissures, the document showed its watermark: the patriarch's stationery, bloated now, the ink feathering at the margins but the central text still declaring its integers.

She removed her remaining glove. The emerald velvet had absorbed meltwater at the cuff, darkening the fabric to the color of cellar mold. Her fingers touched the ice. It shifted, a ledger sliding from its shelf, and the remaining shell cracked along a diagonal fault. Water sheeted across the document surface. She lifted the paper by its corner, the fibre tearing slightly, and placed it on the escritoire.

The seventeen heirs watched. Their gloves rested at their sides in the posture of obsolete account books, spines uncracked. Helena smoothed the page with the side of her hand, pressing the water outward toward the edges. The first clause had dissolved entirely into a gray wash. The second showed only fragments: "whereas" and "estate" separated by a blur that might have been a signature or a stain. But the third paragraph remained, the ink settled into the paper's weave like sediment in a neglected bottle.

She read the name aloud. The syllables occupied the air with the weight of a final account entry. No one moved. The chandelier dripped once, the water striking the marble with a sound too sharp for the distance it fell. Helena noted that the document's legibility ceased exactly where the patriarch's pen had lifted from the page, as if the water had respected the boundary of completed intention.

Teddy stood apart, his hands at his sides. The grease on his cuffs had congealed to a matte finish. Helena folded the document along its surviving crease and placed it within her portfolio. The succession was determined. The paper continued to weep into the damask lining, but the entry stood.
