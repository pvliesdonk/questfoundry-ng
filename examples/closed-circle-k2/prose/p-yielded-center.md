Bernard retreated from the chandelier's shadow. He placed his ivory cigarette holder on the ledger and stepped back until his shoulder touched the damask drape. The withdrawal left a vacancy at the room's center that collected the assembly's attention like dust on an unused account book. Teddy stepped into the space. He felt the borrowed tweed tighten across his shoulders as he raised his hands from his sides. The gesture was theatrical, drawn from a warehouse stage twenty years past, and he let it stand.

He stated that waiting imposed unacceptable risk. The thaw would arrive in forty-eight hours according to the meteorological records in the library, and with it would come the coroner and the police. Until then, they remained sealed with the killer. He gestured toward the settle where Bernard's ledger lay open beside the medical bag. The evidence of murder remained as frozen as the water pipes, but the ice that preserved it also threatened it. Each degree of temperature rise blurred ink and liquefied blood trace. The will inside the chandelier block would suffer thermal shock when the final collapse came, rendering the text illegible and the key irretrievable.

Helena shifted her grip on the portfolio. Marion's hand moved to her collar. Dr. Mott remained by the mantelpiece, her fingers counting the glass rim in silent intervals.

Teddy argued that they must accelerate the thaw under controlled conditions or risk losing everything to entropy and violence. He proposed moving the ice block to the kitchen's cold storage, away from the compromised chandelier, and applying measured heat to the southeast face where the artificial crack already compromised the structure. The alternative was to maintain their postures of civilized waiting while the murderer among them destroyed what the frost had not yet claimed.

His voice held steady through the final sentence, though the posh accent frayed on the final syllable of "destroyed." The room's silence shifted from contemplation to something harder. The pretense of calm cracked like the ice above them, and Teddy watched the assembly recognize that the ledger of their survival had entered a new column of deficit.
