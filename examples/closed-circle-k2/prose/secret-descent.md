Helena lifted the leather portfolio from beneath the stack of damask napkins. The brass corners had left dark marks on the shelf, oxidized against the wood. She opened to the tab marked 'A' and found Ashworth, Marion inscribed in Bernard's clerical hand.

The solicitor's letterhead appeared on the third page. The correspondence dated back fourteen months and detailed appointments that corresponded with Marion's claimed visits to her sister in Bath. The ink described meetings of a different nature entirely, conducted at a hired house in Pimlico with payments drawn from a private account. Helena read the entries twice. The documentation possessed the completeness Bernard brought to all his records, arranged with alphabetical precision that transformed indiscretion into inventory.

She closed the portfolio. The leather had chilled her fingertips. She understood that Marion's critical faculties extended beyond the theater into territories that would terminate her social existence if exposed. The married solicitor's name appeared seven times in the ledger columns.

Helena descended the attic stairs. The frost had thickened on the inner walls, obscuring the portrait frames behind rime. She entered the Great Hall where the assembled heirs stood in a semicircle around the suspended ice block. The chandelier hung above them, its arms encased in frozen water. Marion stood nearest the dying fire, her pince-nez catching the dim light, her fingers stained with ink. Dr. Mott held an empty glass. Teddy Bryant maintained his posture by the mantelpiece with his hands clasped behind his back. The ice block rested on the trestle table, the will visible within its cloudy depths, the key suspended above like a specimen in clear gel.

Helena adjusted her right glove. The frayed hem of her sleeve brushed her wrist. She possessed documentation now that could alter the composition of the room, reducing Marion from critic to cautionary tale. The cold had preserved the evidence as surely as it had preserved the patriarch's testament. She waited for the assembly to acknowledge her entrance, holding the portfolio against her ribs where the leather warmed slowly against the emerald velvet.
