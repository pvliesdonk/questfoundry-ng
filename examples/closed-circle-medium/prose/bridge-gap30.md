Jordan stepped from the ruined library into the main hall, the pine‑scented coat still damp from the storm. The hearth glowed weakly, a thin ember against the cold stone, and the guests huddled under blankets, their faces drawn in the flickering light. Marta Valen stood near the brass rail, her gray hair pulled tight, eyes fixed on the fire as if it could hide her thoughts. Simon Kade clutched his mug, the metal cool against his palm, and Cole Duvall lingered by the doorway, his wrench abandoned on the flagstones. The sheriff’s deputy moved with a lantern, its beam sweeping the room, a steady rhythm that kept the smoke from settling.

Jordan felt the envelope in their pocket, the soft wax still warm from the library fire, the sealed page that had sparked the recent hope. The paper inside named a name that contradicted Marta’s claim of solitary presence. Jordan’s notebook lay open on the stone table, the margin empty, waiting for a conclusion. The weight of the proof pressed against the palm, a quiet pressure that could either fracture the uneasy truce or preserve the fragile alibi.

Jordan surveyed the gathering, the iron hinges of the back doors groaning as the wind battered the shutters. A choice lingered, unspoken, as the ember’s glow flickered against the stone. The proof rested heavy in their hand, a silent key to the next move.
