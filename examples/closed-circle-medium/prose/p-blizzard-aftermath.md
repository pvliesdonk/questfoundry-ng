The wind slammed against the lodge’s stone walls as the deputies moved through the dim hallway, lanterns flickering against the thickening soot. Jordan watched them spread, the senior deputy pressing a brass lantern against the furnace grate while his partner unrolled rope to secure an iron railing. The storm outside grew into a roar, white sheets of snow swirling past the narrow windows, the howl threading through the cracks of the doorframe. As the men examined the vent grates, a figure slipped past the service entrance, his overalls dark against the blinding gale.

Cole Duvall vanished into the white night, the pipe wrench clattering to the frozen stones before he disappeared. Jordan saw the deputy point a gloved hand toward the doorway, his voice thin against the wind, “He’s gone.” The western door banged shut by the gust, sealing the lodge from the tempest. The deputies exchanged a hurried glance, the threat of the miner’s escape lingering like a draft through the stone.

Three days later a patrol returned from the trail with a shivering shape cradled in a snow‑filled trench. The body, rigid with ice, lay near the service door’s broken hinges, its face pale beneath the pine‑scented wind. Jordan learned from the senior deputy that the frozen form matched Cole’s scarred hands and the miner’s broad shoulders. Rumors spread through the lodge that the mountain had taken its own revenge, and the crew whispered of retribution as the blizzard finally relented.
