Jordan Blythe stared at the final report spread across the scarred oak table. The ink was still damp where Deputy Hale had marked the line of quiet, and the rust‑colored fingerprints on the revolver's barrel caught the low firelight. He could feel the cold of the metal even through the thin leather holster, a reminder that the weapon had been idle long enough to become an heirloom, yet it still held the capacity for death.

He heard the steady click of the deputy's boots as they filed the paperwork, the cadence as predictable as the storm outside. The sheriff's men stood a short distance away, their faces set, their orders clear: the weapon belonged in their custody for forensic analysis. Jordan's mind catalogued the consequences. Handing it over would satisfy the law, seal the case, and possibly spare him further accusation, but it would also seal the hostess's reputation and expose the hidden motives he had begun to untangle. To hide it meant protecting the guest who might be falsely implicated, but it also meant defying the badge, inviting a charge of obstruction, and risking the very arrest that already bound his wrists.

The revolver rested on the table, its polished walnut gleam stark against the stone floor. Jordan's fingertips brushed the iron cylinder, feeling the lingering chill that seemed to echo the storm's breath through the lodge's cracks. He weighed the weight of duty against the weight of personal risk, the ledger's final line a quiet echo of his own hesitation. With the notebook pressed to his chest, he let his eyes drift to the deputy's steady stare, and the choice lingered like smoke in the air. He inhaled the scent of pine sap that drifted from the cracked window, and for an instant the night seemed to hold its breath.
