The stone hallway smelled of pine sap and ash as the storm hammered the cracked windows. Jordan Blythe stepped forward, boots echoing on the frost‑slick floor, eyes fixed on the oak table where the old revolver lay. The walnut case was still warm from the recent discharge, but the iron cylinder was cold to the touch. Deputy Hale stood near the doorway, his badge catching the sputtering lantern light, while his partner rested a rifle on his shoulder, gaze narrowed on the weapon. Marta Valen lingered at the rail, her fingers clasping the broken‑seal note, breath shallow. The powder curl that had risen moments ago drifted lazily upward, a ghost of the shot that had sealed the night. Jordan felt the notebook press against their chest, the pages crisp with unfinished lines. The deputies waited, shoulders tense, as if the next movement would decide the lodge’s fate. Jordan’s hand hovered over the revolver, fingers brushing the cool metal of the cylinder, then pausing at the warm walnut. A choice lingered: hand the gun to Hale, letting official authority seal the accusation, or slip it into the hidden compartment beneath the table, keeping the evidence out of the sheriff’s reach. The lantern flickered, casting a brief flash across the polished wood. Jordan’s gaze steadied on the barrel, the cold iron a silent promise. The moment held, waiting for a single decision to break the stillness.
