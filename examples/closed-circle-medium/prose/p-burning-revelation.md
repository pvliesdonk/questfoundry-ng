Flames surged through the lower stacks, the heat pressing against Jordan Blythe's back as they pressed the torn page to their chest. Around them, guests raised their voices in outrage, the clamor echoing off the stone walls. The blaze devoured the rare volumes, turning the orderly rows into a blackened wasteland, and the angry shouts grew louder. When the fire reached the far corner, a charred crate gave way, spilling a ledger onto the scorched floor; Jordan knelt, the heat biting their sleeves, and read the entries that listed a sum owed by Simon Kade to a miner. The ledger bore the numbers in stark ink, the line for Simon clear as a cut, confirming the hidden debt he had tried to conceal. Jordan slipped the ledger into their coat, feeling the weight of the secret settle alongside the torn page. The fire still roared, casting flickering shadows that danced across the stone floor, a reminder that the night was far from over. The torn page, its edge frayed and still warm, showed a name written in hurried ink, the same name that had haunted Jordan since the murder. Above the crackling fire, the chandelier's glass prisms shattered, scattering bright shards that fell onto the damp floor like frozen rain. Marta Valen stepped forward, her face pale, clutching a blanket, and shouted for someone to fetch water, but the storm outside had sealed the windows and the pipes were frozen. Simon Kade stood a short distance away, his uniform immaculate, eyes fixed on the blaze, his jaw set as if daring the flames to reveal more. Jordan noted the iron hinges of the far door glowed orange, a silent warning that the building could collapse if the fire spread further. In the dying glow, ash drifted like grey snowfall across the stone, each grain a reminder of what had burned and what still lay hidden.
