Cole slipped the pantry door closed, the iron hinges whining against the stone wall. The narrow space smelled of pine sap and cold soot, a thin cloud of smoke curling from the hearth. Simon stood by the shelf, his uniform immaculate, the pipe wrench still clenched in his scarred hands. The pouch Simon had slipped into the seam of Cole’s overcoat sat like a hidden knot against the fabric.

"You never meant to pay me back," Cole said, his voice rough as gravel. He stared at the bulge where the cash lay, the weight of the debt a cold iron in his gut. "I gave you the loan because the work needed the cash, and you kept it out of sight."

Simon’s posture stayed composed, his eyes flickering to the dark corner where the fire sputtered. "I did what I could," he said, his tone even, offering no excuse.

Cole felt the furnace’s breath falter behind him, the draft slipping through the cracks in the stone. The threat rose inside him, unspoken but sharp. "If you didn't settle it then, I would take the back service door and leave before anyone noticed," Cole said, his hand tightening on the wrench.

The pantry seemed to shrink, the shadows on the floor lengthening as the coal flared briefly. Simon did not move, his jaw set, the silence between them thick as the snowfall outside. In that moment the grudge that had driven Cole here settled like a stone, and he knew the choice before him would shape what came next.
