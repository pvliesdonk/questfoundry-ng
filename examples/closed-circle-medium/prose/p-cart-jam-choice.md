Coal carts piled across the service tunnel, their timbers dark as soot and wedged tight enough that no lantern could slip between them. The stack turned the narrow exit into a dead end, and anyone who tried to push through would have to wedge the carts or risk the tunnel collapsing under the weight of the storm.

Cole watched Jordan lean against the cold stone, a folded sheet of paper clenched in one hand. The page held the article that would name the heiress and accuse the town’s power brokers. Jordan’s other hand hovered over the notebook, then snapped back to the sheet, as if measuring the cost of each word. A breath caught in their throat, and the flickering firelight threw a brief shadow across the ink. The author’s eyes darted between the blocked doorway and the page, and the miner could see the decision hanging in the air like the thin smoke rising from the hearth.

The furnace guttered, sending a thin hiss that blended with the wind rattling the lodge windows. Snow hammered the roof, and the stone walls seemed to tighten around the gathered guests. Cole’s grip on the pipe wrench tightened even though the tool lay idle on the floor; he could feel the chill creeping up his forearms, and the thought of fleeing through the jammed tunnel snarled with his resolve to keep the article from falling into the wrong hands.

He knew the moment would come whether Jordan thrust the paper toward the printer’s hand or folded it back into the coat. The choice lay between a scandal that could ignite the town and a silence that might keep the investigation clean. The carts remained immovable, and the paper trembled in Jordan’s grip.
