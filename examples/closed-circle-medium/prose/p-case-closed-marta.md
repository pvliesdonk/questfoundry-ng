Jordan stood at the edge of the main hall as Sheriff Hale placed a fresh ledger on the battered oak desk. The sheriff’s fingers traced the neat lines of the final entry, each word deliberate and devoid of flourish. He wrote that the investigation had concluded, that no further action would be taken, and that the lodge’s records were sealed. The sentence read like a plain inventory of facts, a stark contrast to the frantic accusations that had filled the room days before.

Around them, the guests lingered in muted clusters, their murmurs dampened by the storm’s howl outside. A deputy chuckled low, the sound thin and bitter, and the comment slipped into the air like a tarnished joke: "Well, that’s one mystery wrapped up in paperwork." The humor hung heavy, a dark echo that seemed to mock the tragedy rather than honor it.

Jordan noted Marta Valen near the brass rail, her shoulders drawn tight against the cold. She stared at the ledger with a hollow stare, the glow of the lantern casting a pale halo around her silhouette. The rumor of her ruined reputation floated through the room like smoke, each whisper a reminder that the night’s revelations had stripped her of credibility. Jordan felt the weight of that lingering shame, a sour note that refused to fade.

The sheriff stamped the report with his official seal, the metal clicking against the wood. He lifted the ledger, nodded once, and turned toward the door, his badge flashing in the dim light. Jordan watched the seal imprint, the final mark that closed the case, and heard the faint rustle of pages turning as the lodge settled back into its uneasy quiet.
