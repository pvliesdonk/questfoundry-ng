The lodge's stone walls swallowed the flicker of lanterns, the fire sputtering low against the howling wind. Smoke drifted along the flagstones, curling around the central table where a revolver lay, its barrel still faintly warm from the recent discharge. Outside, the blizzard battered the shutters, rattling the thin panes like distant hammers.

Murmurs rippled through the crowd, fueled by the investigator's recent claim that Marta Valen had fabricated her alibi. Heads turned, eyes narrowed, and accusations slipped from one voice to the next. Cole clenched the leather strap of his pipe wrench, the scarred hands tightening around the cold metal as he scanned the room for an exit.

He spotted the back service door, its iron frame slick with frost, and moved toward the narrow gap between deputies. The storm gusted through the opening, sending a shiver of wind across the hall. Cole pushed the door, the heavy wood groaning as it yielded, and stepped onto the threshold. Ice clung to the stone, and his boot slipped, sending the wrench clattering onto the floor. A sudden crack rang out as a pistol fired from somewhere behind him, the report echoing off the stone. The flash illuminated Cole's face for a heartbeat before darkness fell again. He fell forward, his body striking the heavy door, the wrench still clutched in his hand.

Later, deputies gathered around the fallen miner, their lanterns casting trembling shadows on his motionless form. The sheriff arrived, his badge catching the dim light, and placed a thin ledger on the desk. He recorded Cole Duvall's name, the location, and noted that the investigation would not proceed further. He sealed the page with a plain stamp, the report as unadorned as the stone walls that enclosed it.
