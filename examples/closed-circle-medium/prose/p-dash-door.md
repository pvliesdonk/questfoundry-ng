The wind roared against the stone of Highpine Lodge, a thin blade of cold that slipped through the cracks and rattled the shutters. Inside, the hearth sputtered, its glow fighting the draft that seeped from the hallway. Simon Kade stepped forward, his hand sliding a grimy metal sheet over the exposed furnace intake, his voice level as he told the gathered guests the heating system was "under control," hiding the sabotage he had just discovered. At the same moment, Cole Duvall shoved his palm against the worn wooden panel of the back service door, the metal latch cold and unyielding under his scarred fingers.

Cole lunged into the narrow corridor, the wind howling louder through the opening he could see a sliver of night beyond. The door was bolted from the inside; Marta Valen had turned the key before the storm hit, and the bolt resisted his desperate shove. He tried the handle again, the metal biting his palm, his breath coming in short, frosty bursts. The hallway echoed with the low murmur of the guests, their blankets rustling, the fire's crackle a thin thread against the growing silence.

The door would not give. A gust burst through the cracked pane, slamming the door shut with a thud that rattled the stone floor. Cole felt the weight of the failure settle like ash in his lungs. From the dining room a clatter rose as plates were set down hard. Jordan Blythe rose, notebook clutched, and said, "The heiress was not merely a victim; she ignited the whole tragedy." The words cut through the room, sharp as the wind outside, and the guests turned, voices rising in accusation. Cole heard the murmurs swell, felt the cold whisper of the failed escape linger in the air, a reminder that the storm beyond the walls was not the only thing holding them captive.
