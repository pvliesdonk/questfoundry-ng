Jordan stepped away from the sputtering hearth and glanced toward the far wall where Simon Kade kept a rigid line of posture. The butler’s voice had risen just moments before, low and tight, as he mentioned a debt that Cole Duvall had hinted at earlier. Jordan heard the word “payment” cut short by the hiss of the furnace, then felt the thin veil of cold slip through the cracked window panes. He noted the way the draft curled around the stone, stealing what little heat the fire still offered.

The room’s guests reacted without a word. Marta Valen pulled a frayed blanket tighter around her shoulders, and the others followed, their fingers gripping the wool as if it could hold back the breath of the mountain. Jordan watched the mug in Simon’s hand tremble, the steel of the old revolver on the table growing dimmer in the waning glow. No one spoke; the silence pressed against the walls, dense and unyielding.

In Jordan’s mind the hidden debt settled like a stone at the bottom of a cold pond, unseen but weighty. It would surface later, he thought, when motives were weighed and alibis stripped away. The guests continued to shiver, their blankets rustling softly, the uneasy quiet deepening the lodge’s claustrophobic air. The draft whispered through the room, a reminder that the cold could become as lethal as any weapon.
