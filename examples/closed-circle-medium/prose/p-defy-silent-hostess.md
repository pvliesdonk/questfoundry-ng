Jordan stared at Deputy Hale's badge glinting in the firelight and felt the weight of his command settle like cold iron on the stone floor. Hale reached for the revolver that lay beside the hearth, his hand steady, and said, "You come with me now." Jordan met his gaze and, without a hint of surrender, replied, "I will not be taken." The deputy's order hung in the air, brittle and unyielding, while the storm outside battered the shutters with a relentless howl. Jordan tightened the grip on the notebook tucked beneath their coat, the pages still blank where the hostess’s alibi could have been recorded. They chose to keep that story unspoken, trusting the truth would surface on its own terms.

The sheriff's men shifted, uncertain, as Jordan turned away from the confrontation. They slipped back into the shadows of the main hall, the clack of boots fading behind them. No longer a visible target, Jordan moved through the dim corridors with purpose, the notebook a quiet weight against their side. The decision to evade arrest meant the investigation would continue in secret, the risk growing with each muffled footstep.

Jordan paced the narrow hallway, the lanterns sputtering against drafts that smelled of damp pine and iron. At the far end a figure emerged from the gloom, the butler's pristine uniform catching the weak flame. Simon Kade stood motionless, his posture rigid yet unthreatening, and held a lantern aloft. He gave a brief nod toward a hidden doorway concealed behind the rows of oak shelves. "Follow," he said, his voice low and measured.

Without hesitation Jordan fell in step beside him. The secret passage yawned open, its stone walls slick with condensation, and the pair disappeared into the darkness beneath the lodge. The storm raged above, but within the narrow tunnel Jordan felt the case tighten in their mind, the sheriff's reach held at bay, and the hidden route promising the next clue.
