Jordan stood by the hearth as Deputy Hale closed the ledger and lifted a fresh notebook from his belt. The deputy's pen hovered for a moment, then traced a single line across the page: quiet. The ink settled into the fibers, a thin mark that seemed to echo the stillness in the room. Jordan felt the hollow calm settle around the stone arches like a thin fog, the kind that gathered after a storm when the wind no longer howls but merely sighs. The deputy turned the page, his eyes scanning the cramped margins where the final report had been stamped. He nodded once, placed the notebook on the table, and slipped the ledger back into his satchel.

The other deputies moved methodically, their boots thudding on the cold floor as they filed paperwork into the ledgers, their motions precise and unhurried. Jordan watched the routine unfold, each signature a reminder that the investigation had slipped from frantic pursuit into the dull rhythm of official record. A whisper of pine sap drifted in from the cracked windows, the scent mixing with the lingering smell of iron from the revolver still perched on the table. Jordan’s own notebook rested at their side, its pages still warm from recent scribbles, but now the pen felt heavier, the words less urgent. The lodge remained locked, the storm still battered the shutters, and the guests kept their blankets wrapped tight, their eyes flickering between the fire and the shadows. In that moment the case seemed to fold into paperwork, the mystery reduced to a line of ink and a quiet that lingered far longer than any confession could.
