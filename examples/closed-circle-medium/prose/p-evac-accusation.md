The corridor stretched, its stone walls chilled by the lingering wind that still carried a faint scent of pine sap. Lanterns sputtered over the stacks of rescued manuscripts, the leather covers thudding softly as they rested on the frost‑slick floor. A pair of uniformed deputies followed behind the coughing crowd, boots crunching on a thin crust of ice. Sheriff Hale stepped forward, his badge catching the wavering light, and said, “Investigator Blythe, your negligence let the fire spread and vital evidence vanish.” The words struck Jordan like a cold stone, and the accusation settled on their shoulders.

Jordan steadied their breath, notebook pressed against their side, and met the sheriff’s gaze. The weight of the missing clue settled in their gut, a dull iron ache that would not loosen. They drew a slow breath, feeling the chill of the hallway settle like frost on stone, and whispered a promise to themselves: they would not let the loss end the inquiry.

With that resolve, Jordan turned from the sheriff, their steps echoing off the walls. They scanned the hallway for any sign of an alternative path, the service tunnels that might still hold answers. The storm roared outside, battering the lodge walls, while the rescued volumes waited in the silence behind them. The fire’s ember glowed faintly in the hearth, a reminder that the blaze had not claimed everything. Jordan’s mind hardened, a quiet line drawn in the smoke, and they moved toward the narrow door that led to the hidden passages, determined to chase the truth by any means.
