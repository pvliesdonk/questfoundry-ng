Jordan Blythe reached the brass pull lever beside the oak shelves and yanked it. The fire alarm shrieked through the stone corridors, a harsh note that cut the low murmur of the guests. Jordan said, “Everyone out of the library, now,” and the words bounced off the vaulted ceiling. The sound rolled down the aisles, a thin echo that made the lantern light quiver. Guests jolted upright, clutching blankets and mugs, and hurried toward the narrow doorway. Simon Kade lifted his mug and gestured toward the exit, while Marta Valen tightened her coat and slipped a leather‑bound tome into her coat pocket.

A cart of rare manuscripts, already wheeled toward the hearth, was shoved onto the stone floor. Jordan guided the cart, steadying the leather‑covered volumes as they teetered on the edge of the firelight. The heat from the hearth lapped at the spines, and a stray ember sparked a brief flare near a stack of pamphlets. In that instant a sealed envelope lay tucked beneath a loose page, its wax seal still warm from recent handling. The flame licked the seal, curling the paper into ash before anyone could snatch it.

The alarm’s wail pressed the crowd forward, and the library’s heavy oak doors thudded shut behind them. Outside, the rescued manuscripts were laid on a cold table, their vellum skins untouched by smoke. Jordan watched the remnants of the envelope curl into gray ash, a small loss swallowed by the blaze. The fire crackled louder as the alarm continued its shrill call, urging the last of the guests into the hallway. With the rare books safe and the hidden clue gone, the lodge settled into a tense silence broken only by the echo of footfalls and the lingering hiss of the alarm.

The final clang of the alarm reverberated through the stone, a reminder that some secrets would burn away while others were carried out into the storm.
