Jordan watched the sheriff’s men line the doorway, their brass badges catching the dim light that filtered through the frost‑bitten windows. Deputy Hale stepped forward, his badge polished to a cold shine, and addressed Jordan with a tone that cut through the low murmur of the guests. He asked plainly whether the recent article, which blamed the heiress for the tragedy, had colored the investigation, and added that many in town accused the writer of feeding the fire of rumor. The accusation landed like a stone on the polished floor, and Jordan felt the weight of every stare that turned toward them.

The deputy’s questions rang down the narrow hallway, and the sound seemed to gather in the cracks between the stone walls, amplifying the unsettled whispers of the assembled guests. Marta clutched her blanket tighter, Simon’s jaw tightened, and Cole shifted his weight, his dusty hands lingering over the pipe wrench at his belt. Each reply from the sheriff’s officers drew another ripple of gossip that spread from one corner of the room to the next.

Soon the tentative conversation broke into a chorus of accusations. One guest pointed to the printed headline that lay on the side table and shouted that the author had turned the tragedy into a spectacle. Another blamed the butler for keeping secrets, and a third shouted that the miner had tampered with the furnace to cover his own motives. The air grew thick with suspicion, the low hum of the fire barely audible over the clamor. Jordan sensed the fragile trust among the guests splintering, each claim tearing at the thin rope that had held the group together. The night, already heavy with storm, now carried the clangor of a lodge caught in a storm of its own making. The deputy’s last words hung in the room, a warning that any further bias would meet the law’s iron grip.
