Jordan Blythe arrived at the heavy pine‑groved doorway of Highpine Lodge, the wind still howling outside. Inside the common room, the few remaining guests huddled on worn benches, their shoulders rigid, eyes flickering toward the hearth. Simon Kade stood beside the fire, his crisp uniform immaculate, his gaze fixed on the dying embers as if searching for a signal. Marta Valen lingered near the bar, her short gray hair pulled back, her expression guarded yet warm enough to keep the nervous atmosphere from breaking. Cole Duvall shifted his weight, dust clinging to his overalls, a clenched jaw hinting at a surge of unease. Jordan noted the tightness in the room, the way breath hovered in brief clouds before vanishing, and felt the weight of a collective hold‑breath.

They stepped across the splintered floorboards toward the central hearth, and a sudden draft cut across their neck. The iron stove coughed a low rasp, and thin wisps of smoke rose, tasting faintly of pine sap. "The fire is dying," said Simon, his voice low.

The stove sputtered again, sparks sputtering like dying fireflies, and the room's temperature fell in a sharp, audible drop. Outside, the blizzard grew louder, the wind striking the cracked windows with a hollow thud that seemed to echo the chill inside. Guests drew tighter on thin blankets, shoulders shivering, while Marta tightened her grip on a mug as if to keep the heat at bay. Jordan felt the cold creep up their spine, a reminder that the lodge's own walls were turning against them. The night settled into a thin layer of ice, and the silence that followed felt as heavy as the stone walls themselves.
