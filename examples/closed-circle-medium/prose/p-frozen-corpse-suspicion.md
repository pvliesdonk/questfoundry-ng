A slab of ice clung to the miner's shoulders, the stone floor gleaming beneath the lanterns. Jordan Blythe stepped into the hallway and saw the frozen form sprawled near the service door, the miner's overalls fused with snow. The sight sent a ripple of murmurs through the lodge, each voice thin against the whistling wind outside. Guests leaned closer, their breath fogging the air, as the cold seemed to sharpen every accusation. Simon Kade stood rigid beside the fire, his eyes scanning the crowd, while Marta Valen kept her coat buttoned tight, her expression unreadable.

Jordan recorded the scene in their notebook, noting the miner's scarred hands clenched around a pipe wrench, the revolver half buried in the drift. No one spoke of the dead man's last moments; instead the room filled with speculation about a possible aid. A guest whispered, “Someone must have let him out,” and another muttered, “The furnace was tampered with.” The words hung in the stale heat, amplifying the suspicion that an unseen hand had helped the escape.

The unresolved escape fueled a fresh wave of distrust among the remaining guests. Each stared at the others, wondering who might have concealed a passage or slipped a key. Jordan felt the weight of their glances, a cold pressure settling on their shoulders like ash. The lodge’s walls seemed to close in, the storm outside pressing harder against the shutters. In the dim light, the frozen corpse remained a silent accusation, and Jordan understood that the next question would decide who among them could be trusted.
