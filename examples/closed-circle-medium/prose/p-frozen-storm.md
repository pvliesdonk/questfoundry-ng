Jordan watched the hearth sputter as the last ember died, the iron hinges of the firebox shivering with each cold gust that slipped through the cracked windows. They saw the stone walls draw the chill inward, and a thin plume of smoke curled above the dead fire. Guests rose from the benches, pulling threadbare blankets tighter around their shoulders, their coats a mismatched collage of wool and fur as they staggered toward the doors.

The storm roared outside, wind hammering the log walls and snow lashing the panes in fierce gusts. The frigid air thickened, settling over the hall like a heavy breath that pressed against the occupants’ throats.

Jordan stepped forward, notebook clenched in one hand, and announced that the murderer had been identified and the case was closed. They pointed to the revolver that Simon had dropped after his own shot; the weapon lay on the central table, its polished walnut grip gleaming faintly in the dim light. They denounced Marta Valen’s alibi as false, noting the timeline recorded contradicted her claim. The guests fell silent, eyes flicking between the hostess and the cold metal. Jordan said nothing about what to do with the revolver, leaving the choice to hand it over or conceal it hanging in the still‑cold air.
