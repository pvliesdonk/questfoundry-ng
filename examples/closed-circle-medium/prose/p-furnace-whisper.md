Cole lingered near the furnace, the stone floor cold beneath his boots. He kept his hands—scarred, calloused—on the metal pipe, feeling the faint vibration of the dying flame. The iron hinges of the stove creaked as the fire sputtered, a thin plume of orange slipping into the chill of the lodge. Simon Kade approached, his uniform immaculate despite the frost curling around his boots. He drew his coat tighter and said, “The cold is seeping in faster than I expected.” Simon’s words cut into the stillness, and Cole noted the shiver that passed over the butler’s shoulders. He sensed the butler’s eyes flick toward the furnace, as if searching for a cause that lay beyond the usual draft.

Cole’s thoughts turned to the missing valve the engineer had described. The furnace’s breath felt thin, as if someone had taken the wind from it. He remembered the wrench in his grip and the uneasy posture he had adopted, waiting for a signal that never came. A low hiss rose from the hearth, and the amber glow waned, leaving the room in a deeper shade of gray. The chill settled into the pine walls, making the blankets in the corner rustle.

Simon glanced at the guests, his voice barely rising above the crackle of coal. “If this continues, we’ll be frozen before the storm lifts.” Cole felt a weight settle in his chest, a realization that the cold might have been summoned on purpose. He tightened his hold on the pipe, aware that the furnace’s failure could be more than accident. If the fire faltered on purpose, the cold would be a weapon as sure as any blade.
