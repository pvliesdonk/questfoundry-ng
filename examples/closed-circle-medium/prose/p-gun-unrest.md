Stone walls exhaled a thin breath of pine sap as Marta stood before the false drawer where the old revolver lay hidden. The walnut case felt cold beneath her fingertips, a silent weight that could spark the gathering into panic. She watched Jordan Blythe lower his notebook onto the table and keep his mouth shut, his eyes flicking to the weapon then back to the stone floor. Marta's mind lingered on the thought that the concealed gun might ignite the already volatile crowd, and she noted how his restraint preserved the story she had offered about being alone in the library. She remembered the anonymous note that had urged her to protect the hostess's alibi, and she sensed that handing the revolver to the deputies would unravel that safeguard. Jordan's silent posture rang louder than any accusation, a quiet promise that her alibi would stay out of the sheriff's ledger. The fire in the hearth guttered, casting a dim glow over the table where the gun rested, and Marta felt the tension coil tighter around the room. A deputy in a crisp uniform stepped forward and spoke. "We will file a jurisdictional notice," he said, his voice flat as the storm outside. "Your investigator's restraint regarding the hostess's alibi is noted, and any deviation will be reported to the sheriff's office." Marta watched the deputy's shoulders, felt the warning settle like a thin line drawn between order and chaos, and she clutched the secret of the revolver tighter, aware that the silence surrounding it could both protect and endanger the few remaining guests.
