The stone hallway stretched ahead, its cold walls echoing the clank of copper cuffs against the oak desk. Jordan felt the steel bite his wrists and watched the sheriff’s lantern sweep over the revolving table where the walnut‑cased revolver lay, its barrel still warm from recent use. The fire in the hearth had faded to a weak glow, and a thin draft slipped through the cracked windows, stirring the pages of the rare manuscripts that rested nearby.

He knew the deputies would demand the weapon as proof, and the ledger the sheriff carried marked the official end of his inquiry. Yet the revolver could still serve his own purpose, a safeguard in case the lodge’s secrets resurfaced. Jordan weighed the options in the quiet that followed the deputy’s command. The first choice meant handing the gun to the men, trusting their protocol to preserve the evidence but relinquishing personal control. The second meant slipping the weapon into the concealed compartment behind the library shelf, a risk that might protect a guest from immediate arrest but also conceal a key clue.

He listened to the murmurs of the guests, their eyes shifting between the deputies and the empty chair he had vacated. A low sigh rose from his chest as the storm hammered the lodge’s panes. Jordan tightened his grip on the notebook, the weight of the decision settling like ash in his lungs. He could see the iron hinges of the hidden door, just out of sight, waiting for his hand.
