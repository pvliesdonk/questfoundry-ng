Jordan stood at the edge of the crowd, the stone floor slick with thawing water and the scent of pine sap mingling with the cold breath that slipped through the cracked windows. The guests clutched their blankets tighter, their eyes darting toward the newly exposed doorway that yawned where the pantry wall had once seemed solid. The iron latch swayed on its hinge, freed by the sudden rush of ice that had pried the bar from its seat.

Through the opening a narrow stair descended into darkness, its walls smooth and damp as if carved by water long ago. Jordan lifted a lantern from the mantel, its flame trembling against the draft, and held it low enough to see the far end of the shaft. The beam fell on a seam in the stone that ran parallel to the floor, a line of frost that marked a hidden joint. He stepped forward, his boots echoing on the stone, and pressed his palm against the cold edge of the seam. The stone gave with a muted crack, and the slab that formed the false wall shifted outward.

Beyond the slab lay a narrow cavity, its interior filled with shadows that seemed to swallow the lantern's light. In the gloom a shape lay still, draped in a frayed coat that had long since lost its colour to the damp. The figure's head rested against the stone, a cascade of dark hair spilling onto the floor. Jordan's eyes caught the glint of metal at the chest — the barrel of an old revolver, its surface dulled by years of neglect. A thin stream of blood had seeped into the earth, darkening the frost around the corpse.

Jordan recorded the discovery in his notebook, the ink smudging slightly as his fingers trembled from the cold. The hidden passage, once a rumor whispered among the servants, now revealed a dead body that deepened the lodge's tangled web of suspicion. He knew the revelation would shatter any remaining calm among the guests and force the deputy to confront a new, grim truth. The corpse lay still, a silent accusation pressed into the stone.
