The stone hall smelled of damp ash as the fire sputtered low. Deputy Hale's badge caught a flicker of the dying light, and his voice cut across the crackle. Jordan Blythe watched the deputy's steady gaze sweep the room, noting the empty space where the revolver had rested moments before. The cold metal pressed against Jordan's ribs, hidden beneath the inner lining of the coat. Jordan lowered the coat sleeve and moved toward the desk. With deliberate motion the old revolver slid into the concealed niche behind the false panel, the walnut giving way without a sound. The iron cylinder nestled against Jordan's spine, out of the deputies' sight. "It remains here," Jordan said, tone flat. The deputy's expression hardened, his hand resting lightly on the grip of his own pistol, but he offered no further command. Murmurs rose among the guests, like wind through shutters, none directed at Marta Valen. She leaned on the brass rail, shoulders rigid, her composure unmarred, the hostess's reputation unblemished. The faint fingerprints on the barrel lingered in the firelight, a silent undercurrent linking the case to the miner, unnoticed by all present. Jordan felt the weight of the decision settle like ash in their lungs. The revolver stayed concealed, the investigation pressed on in darkness, and the night held its breath.
