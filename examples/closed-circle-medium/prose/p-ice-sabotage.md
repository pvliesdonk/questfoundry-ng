Jordan heard the sudden rush of water behind the stone archway and turned before the sound faded. A pipe that ran along the far wall had given way, sending a cold torrent down the hallway. The slick stream caught the light from the waning fire and ran over the packed boots of the guests, splashing against the low stone baseboards. A shiver ran through the room as the water struck the icy air; within moments the flow thickened, then halted, crystallising into a white sheet that clung to the walls like a frozen veil.

He watched as the thin sheet of ice traced a line along the conduit that fed the furnace's intake. Where the metal pipe had been tampered with, a jagged edge of frost bulged outward, exposing a concealed hinge and a rusted lever that had been hidden behind a false slab of plaster. The lever sat in stark contrast to the smooth surface, its cold iron glinting faintly in the dim light. The sudden revelation made a hollow clang as a loose stone knocked against it.

Cole Duvall, his overalls dark with damp, lunged toward the back service door that had been bolted earlier. He fumbled with the latch, the water swelling the floorboards beneath his feet. The icy slab gave way under his weight, and he slipped, the wrench in his hand skidding across the slick stone. For a breath, the miner hung helplessly, his forearm caught on the half‑opened door as the frozen water threatened to enshrine him in place.

Jordan stepped forward, his boot crunching the fragile ice, and steadied himself on the railing that ran the length of the corridor. The lever, now visible, could still be pulled to cut the furnace's remaining airflow, sealing the lodge in a deeper freeze. He knew that someone could use the same hidden mechanism to trap a guest or to silence the fire entirely. The thought settled in his mind with the weight of the frozen water, a silent threat waiting to be wielded.

He glanced back at the guests huddled behind the hearth, their blankets clutched tighter as the temperature dropped further. The lodge's walls seemed to close in, the echo of his own footfall a reminder that each step could stir another hidden danger. In that fleeting pause, the frozen corridor became a map of sabotage, and Jordan realised the murderer still possessed a silent lever that could turn the lodge's own infrastructure into a weapon.
