The stone doors of the library slammed shut, and the blaze that had licked the rafters slipped back into darkness. Smoke curled up the narrow gap, then faded as the cold air of the corridor swallowed it. Jordan stood on the threshold, his coat still scented with pine sap, and felt the weight of the empty shelves settle on his shoulders. He heard the muted clang of the cart wheels as the rescued manuscripts were pushed down the hall, their leather covers thudding against the frost‑slick floor. Each soft impact echoed like a reminder of what had been saved and what lay behind the doors.

He turned his gaze toward the darkness beyond the doors, where the fire had once roared. The glow in the hearth had dimmed to a weak ember, its light trembling on the stone floor. In that flicker he sensed the absence of the hidden clue, the missing piece that had once promised a lead. The loss settled in his gut as a cold iron ache, steadier than the wind rattling the windows.

Jordan pressed his notebook against his chest and took a slow breath, the air tasting of ash and cold stone. He knew the library’s rare volumes were now safe, stacked in the cart, but the ruined stacks behind him whispered of a story unfinished. With a quiet resolve, he stepped back into the main hall, ready to face the growing tension among the guests and to pursue the investigation by any means.
