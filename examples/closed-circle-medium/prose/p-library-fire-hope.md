Jordan stepped out of the ruined library into the main hall, his coat still smelling of pine sap. The fire had reduced the lower shelves to blackened ash, the remaining rows of books leaning like wounded trunks. Smoke curled lazily around the overturned stacks, and the investigator felt a lingering pulse of hope from the fragment he salvaged. He tucked the warm seal into his pocket, its wax still soft from the heat. The sheriff's deputies kept their lanterns angled toward the ceiling, trying to force the grey plume away from the guests. Jordan understood that the fragment could be the key to unravelling the sabotage, and that thought steadied his breath. Around him, Marta clutched a blanket tighter, Simon watched the stove with a wary eye, and Cole lingered near the doorway, his wrench abandoned on the stone. The iron hinges of the back doors groaned as the wind pressed against them, but the fire's glow faded, leaving only a thin ember in the hearth. Jordan moved toward the gathering, the fragment pressed against his palm, its faint rhythm matching his own heartbeat. He scanned the faces, noting the tension that still held the room together. The sheriff's voice rang again, ordering that no one leave until the storm cleared. Jordan answered with a short nod, his mind already arranging the next questions. The hope that the fragment sparked held steady, a small light in the cold stone.
