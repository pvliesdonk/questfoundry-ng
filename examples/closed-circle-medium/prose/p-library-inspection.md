Jordan Blythe moved toward the oak library door, the manuscript slipped into their coat. The stone hallway echoed with the footsteps of two uniformed deputies. Jordan felt the weight of the paper against their palm and knew the clue could tie the murder to the old mining dispute. They turned to the senior deputy and said, “I have to take that manuscript now; my research can’t wait.” The deputy stared at the smoldering hearth, then at the vellum‑bound pages, and raised his hand. “We’re conducting a safety sweep,” he said, and gestured toward the furnace. He snapped a lantern loose and began probing the vent grates, while his partner unrolled a coil of rope to secure the iron railing. Jordan watched the men spread lanterns across the stacks, each step stirring a thin plume of ash. The senior deputy’s voice lowered. “We can’t let a fire hide any other crimes,” he said, eyes scanning the stone walls for hidden conduits. Jordan swallowed the rising panic and tightened the clasp on their coat, the manuscript’s edge pressing hard. The deputies moved methodically, checking vents, testing the temperature of the stone, and sealing doors that led to the service tunnels. With every clang of a metal latch, the danger of the blaze grew, but the urgency of the clue held Jordan steady. They stood at the threshold of the library, the heat of the hearth biting the air, and resolved to protect the manuscript even as the fire threatened to consume everything around it.
