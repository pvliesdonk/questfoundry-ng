Deputies gathered near the hallway doorway exchanged low words. One deputy said, "The media can tilt the scales of justice," and the other said, "We must keep the story contained." Jordan listened, noting the concern in their tones, then turned his back to the stone wall as the murmurs receded into the distant echo of footfalls.

The hallway stretched ahead, its pine‑scented floor slick with damp from the storm outside. Jordan's boots clanged against the flagstones, and the sound swallowed the deputies' fading conversation. At the far end a heavy oak door, its iron hinges rusted, marked the entrance to the library. He pushed it open and stepped into a room illuminated by a single dim lamp that threw a thin pool of light over dust‑laden shelves. The air smelled of old paper and faint pine sap, and a thin layer of soot clung to the lower beams.

On a low table lay a vellum‑bound manuscript, its cover bound in cracked leather and stamped with a faded emblem. Jordan lifted it carefully; the pages whispered as he turned them. The opening entry described a 1912 disappearance in a mountain lodge, where a young heiress vanished after a fire consumed the evidence. The circumstances mirrored the recent tragedy he was investigating, and the same pattern of silence and rumor echoed through the lines. He felt the weight of the parallel settle over him, deepening the intrigue that had already taken root in his mind. The manuscript hinted at a case that had never been solved, and Jordan resolved to follow the faint trail it offered.
