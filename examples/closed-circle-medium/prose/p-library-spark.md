Jordan moved deeper into the Highpine Library, the thin lamp casting a wan circle on the dust‑laden stacks. The fire alarm on the wall hung silent, its brass bell unmoving, the indicator light dark. A faint crackle rose from the pile of old vellums near the stove, and a tiny ember slipped from a guttered pipe, landing on a loose page. The spark licked the brittle paper, and for a breath Jordan saw a thin line of flame crawl toward the nearest shelf. The air held a metallic tang, and the sound of the alarm’s dead coil echoed the void of any warning. Jordan stepped forward, hand outstretched, and brushed the ember away before it could bite the spines of the rare volumes.

The sudden threat had drawn Jordan’s eye to a leather‑bound treatise that lay slightly ajar on a lower ledge. A sealed envelope slipped from between the pages as the cover shifted, its wax seal still warm from the faint heat of the lamp. Jordan recognized the seal from the note that had arrived earlier—an anonymous mark that promised a clue. The paper inside felt heavier than its size, a folded document that might turn the investigation. Jordan lifted the envelope carefully, noting the faint scent of pine sap that clung to its edge.

Simon entered the aisle, his uniform crisp despite the chill, and paused beside the iron stove that hissed with a volatile breath. "The stove behind the shelves is unstable," Simon said, his voice low. Jordan did not turn, keeping their focus on the envelope. The butler’s warning cut through the low hum of the building, reminding Jordan that the fire could spread through the hidden flues that ran beneath the stacks. With a steady hand, Jordan slipped the sealed envelope into their coat pocket, the paper's creased surface pressing against the fabric. The brief encounter left the flame smoldering but contained, the danger momentarily held at bay as the librarian’s steps echoed off the stone walls.
