Smoke curled up through the stone corridor, hanging low over the cold benches. The sheriff stepped into the hallway, his boots echoing off the iron hinges of the doors, and raised his voice. “We are locking down the lodge,” he said, and the deputies moved to board every exit. Their lanterns threw thin pools of light on the pine‑scented floor as the wind rattled the shutters. Jordan watched the sheriff's hand slip a file into his coat, the clerk’s badge glinting briefly, and felt the weight of the orders settle like ash.

Inside the main hall, the fire guttered, its orange glow shrinking beneath a veil of grey smoke. Jordan spread the envelope they had uncovered in the library across the battered table. The paper was warm where the wax seal had melted, the ink inside still legible. They turned each page, noting the names, the dates, the reference to the missing furnace valve. With each line, the stakes rose; the smoke seemed to press tighter against their throat, each breath a reminder that the fire could flare at any moment.

Jordan lifted the fragment of the ledger that linked the recent sabotage to the night the heiress fell. They pressed the page against the notebook, its edges trembling in the draft. The sheriff’s voice rang out again, reminding everyone that no one could leave until the storm passed. Jordan met the sheriff’s stare, then turned back to the evidence, their pen scratching faster. The new clue gave them a foothold, and they used it to question the guests, to demand the hidden intake valve be shown, to force the deputy to sweep the furnace. With every question, the tension climbed, each inhalation of smoke a thin thread pulling the investigation tighter.
