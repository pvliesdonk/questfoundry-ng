Deputy Hale’s voice cracked across the stone hallway, announcing a full lockdown. Iron bolts slammed into the wooden doors, and the clang echoed off the cramped lodge walls. Jordan Blythe moved through the half‑light, notebook pressed to their chest, eyes flicking from latch to latch. They felt Marta Valen’s quiet alibi pressing against their own breath, and they kept the hostess’s name from the crowd, answering only with terse nods as the storm hammered the shutters.

The lockdown forced Jordan to narrow their focus. Each step sharpened the awareness of a torn fragment they had slipped from the library shelf earlier. The paper bore a single line of ink that matched the faint fingerprints on the revolver resting on the central table. Jordan traced the curve of the letters with a fingertip, noting a left‑handed slant. The fragment could link the weapon to a guest who had never spoken of firearms, a detail the deputy had missed in the rush to secure the hall. A cold draft slipped through a hidden hinge, reminding them that the lodge still held secrets.

With the fragment in hand, the evidence began to point toward a single unseen hand. Jordan’s notebook filled with the implication that the murderer might have hidden the revolver, manipulated the furnace, and staged the fire to mask a departure. The storm outside rattled the shutters, but inside the lodge the truth steadied like ash on stone. Jordan lifted their gaze, the lantern’s flame catching the metal of the revolver, and prepared to name the unseen assailant, knowing that a word spoken now could shatter the fragile peace that had held the lodge together.
