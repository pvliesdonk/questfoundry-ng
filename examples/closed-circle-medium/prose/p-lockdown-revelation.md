Sheriff Hale's voice cut through the crackle of the dying fire and rang over the hall, ordering every door sealed and the guests to remain inside. The corridors stretched ahead like narrow mazes, each turn a potential snare for anyone who moved without permission. Jordan Blythe slipped past a deputy's outstretched arm, the notebook pressed against their chest, and felt the weight of Marta Valen's shattered reputation pressing from behind every whispered accusation. They moved along the stone‑flagged hallway, shoulders tight against the cold draft that seeped through the barred windows, aware that each footfall echoed a warning.

Inside the locked lodge, Jordan scanned the scattered evidence left by the night. The warm revolver lay on the central table, its barrel still faintly glowing, and the broken‑seal note rested beside it, its ink smeared by hurried hands. A thin layer of ash clung to the edge of a copper pipe, marking where a hidden conduit had been forced open. Their eyes lingered on the faint fingerprint on the barrel that matched Marta's palm prints, then shifted to the ledger listing the miner's recent transactions. Jordan turned the notebook to a page holding the travel receipt Marta had presented as proof of her absence; the timestamp contradicted the lodge's clock by five minutes, proving she could not have been away. With a firm hand they tore the receipt, discarding the false alibi that had protected her.

The pattern that emerged from these fragments pointed away from the humbled hostess and toward a guest whose debts lay hidden beneath a polite smile. Jordan gathered the clues, the notebook trembling in their grip, and stepped toward the central table where the revolver rested. The sheriff's deputies lingered nearby, rifles at their sides, but Jordan's focus held steady. They raised a hand, ready to announce the murderer’s identity, the firelight glinting off the metal as if to underscore the inevitable confrontation against the hidden threat among the guests.
