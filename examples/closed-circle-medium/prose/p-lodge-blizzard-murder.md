The wind had been howling for hours before the lodge finally fell silent, the shutters rattling against their iron hinges as the last gust of snow slammed the pine forest outside. Jordan Blythe stood in the narrow hallway, his breath fogging the cracked windows, and watched the blizzard thicken into a white wall that sealed Highpine Lodge from the world. The fire in the central hearth sputtered, casting a weak orange glow that did little to warm the growing chill in the rooms. By nightfall the storm had become a furnace of cold; the guests huddled in the common room, voices low, each aware that the mountain had become a prison.

When dawn crept through a thin crack in the roof, the snow had settled in a mute, seamless sheet. Jordan stepped toward the study, the heavy door protesting on its old hinges. Inside, the air smelled faintly of pine sap and ash. The heiress lay on the desk, her face pale as the frost on the windows, a thin line of blood staining the cuff of her sleeve. Beside her, the polished walnut of a Vermont 1876 revolver rested on the carpet, its iron cylinder glinting faintly in the dim light. Jordan noted the absence of a struggle; the only sound was the low crackle of the fireplace and the distant howl of wind against the lodge.

He crouched, his eyes tracing the trajectory of the bullet that had claimed her life. The revolver bore fresh fingerprints on its barrel, a stark reminder that the weapon had been used recently. As the realization settled over him, the weight of the sealed lodge pressed tighter, and the quiet menace of the snow outside seemed to echo the darkness that now lay within the study.
