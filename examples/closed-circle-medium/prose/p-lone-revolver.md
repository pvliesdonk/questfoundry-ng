Jordan felt the metal press against his side, the walnut case chilled his palm. The revolver, an heirloom from the study, lay quiet in the inside of his coat, its iron cylinder dead weight. He surveyed the room: deputies tightening grips on their rifles, Simon’s lantern trembling, Marta’s blanket pulled tight. The sheriff’s voice cut through the crackle, demanding surrender, but Jordan’s mouth twitched into a low, bitter laugh that rolled off the stone walls.

He watched Deputy Hale step forward, badge glinting, eyes sharp for the weapon. The crowd pressed closer, breaths fogging in the thin air. Jordan’s hand slipped, fingers closing around the barrel, and he eased the cold gun deeper into the fabric, the wood humming against his skin. The sound of the chuckle lingered, a dark echo that seemed to swallow the firelight.

In that moment the lodge felt like a cage, the storm pounding the shutters like fists. Yet Jordan stood rooted, his coat bulging with the hidden firepower, a silent defiance against the law that chased him. He could hear the faint click of a lock on the back door, hear the furnace sigh, but his thoughts held only the weight of the revolver and the resolve to keep it from the sheriff’s grasp.
