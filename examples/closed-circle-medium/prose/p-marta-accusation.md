Jordan stepped toward the oak table, the old revolver heavy in their hand. The walnut case fit the grain of the table as if it had been carved there, and a thin seam beside the leg hinted at a false bottom. With a firm press they lifted the panel and slipped the warm barrel into the hidden cavity, the metal thudding softly against the wood. A faint clink sounded as the gun settled.

They turned to the gathering of guests, the lanterns sputtering light across strained faces. "Marta Valen, your alibi is false," Jordan said, voice steady, eyes locked on the woman by the rail. The accusation cut through the murmurs, and Marta's shoulders dropped as the room fell into a tense hush. Deputy Hale stepped forward, badge glinting, but the revolver was no longer on the surface for the law to seize. The hostess, who had earlier defended Marta, stared at the empty table, her composure cracking under the weight of public disgrace.

The clink of the concealed revolver reverberated in Jordan's mind as the words floated through the lodge. The accusation lingered over the stone walls, a cold weight that settled on every listener. The hidden gun remained out of official reach, its presence known only to Jordan, and it shielded the guest whose ownership it implied. The case grew more tangled, the truth slipping like ash between the cracks of the storm‑buffeted doors.
