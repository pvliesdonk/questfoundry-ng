Marta surveyed the stone hall as the wind hammered the shutters. The fire guttered, casting thin light across the faces gathered around the hearth. She felt the chill settle into her bones, but her posture remained steady. She lifted her chin and spoke, her voice cutting through the low murmur, telling the group that she had been alone in the library, leafing through a vellum‑bound treatise when the scream shattered the night.

Jordan's notebook lay open on the table, its blank page waiting for a new entry. The deputy's brows tightened as he watched Marta, and Simon shifted his weight, his jaw set. Around them the guests exchanged nervous glances, their blankets drawn tighter against the cold. Marta noted the brass clock above the mantle; its minute hand trembled, falling behind the hour hand by a fraction. The irregular tick seemed out of place in the stillness.

She felt the accusation settle like ash on her skin, thin but unyielding. Her mind returned to the pages she had turned, the ink dark as pine sap, the words of a forgotten scholar. She resolved to keep the volume safe, to let its secrets speak louder than any rumor. The storm outside pressed harder, rattling the stone, and the fire steadied for a moment, its glow tracing the edge of her jaw. In that fleeting light she understood that the truth lay hidden among the shelves, waiting for someone to read it.
