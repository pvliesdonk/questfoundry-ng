Marta stood near the brass rail, the stone hallway humming with the low thrum of anxious footsteps. She saw the investigator pacing by the fire, his hand gripping the edge of the table, his eyes flicking to the deputies' badge as if searching for a sign. She leaned close enough for only his ear to catch her voice and whispered, “Stay careful, Jordan, the night is thin.” The warning fell soft as ash, a breath against his rising tension. She added, “If you defy the sheriff, all of us could suffer; my alibi still stands, untouched.” Her words lingered, a thin thread of caution in the stale air. Before he could answer, a chorus rose from the miners gathered near the hearth. Their shouts boiled over the room, demanding accountability for the fire and the missing clue. Marta’s counsel was drowned beneath their clamorous cries, the echo of their anger filling the stone walls. She watched the miners brandish fists, their faces hard with grief and rage, and felt the room’s mood shift from uneasy silence to heated uproar. The fire’s glow flickered, casting long shadows that seemed to swallow her whispered promise. Marta kept her gaze on the investigator, her expression guarded, as the lodge’s fragile peace cracked under the miners’ demands.
