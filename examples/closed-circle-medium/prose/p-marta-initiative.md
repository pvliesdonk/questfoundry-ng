Cold stone walls of the lodge absorbed the lantern’s dim glow. Marta watched the deputies stand rigid beside the oak desk, their badges catching the weak light. Their eyes moved past her, their hands stayed on the ledgers, and the sheriff lingered at the doorway without a word. She sensed the deputies’ indifference and the sheriff’s inaction, leaving her feeling abandoned by official forces. With that sting, she resolved to take matters into her own hands.

She pressed her palm against the cold iron rail that ran along the hallway, feeling the familiar backbone of the building beneath her fingers. She raised her voice above the murmurs and said, “Everyone, listen,” and the words cut through the crackling tension. She did not wait for permission and reached for the revolver that rested on the central table, its barrel still warm from recent use, and lifted it slowly, the metal flashing in the firelight. “If you think this is a game,” she said, “the truth will not hide behind a notebook or a note. I will not let the killer walk free while you stand idle.”

Marta’s bold move sparked a surge of panic. Cole Duvall, dust‑caked over his shoulders, tightened his grip on the pipe wrench hanging from his belt. He glanced at the back service door, its iron hinges rattling in the wind that slipped through cracked windows. For a heartbeat he hesitated, then, seeing the crowd’s attention fixed on Marta, he shoved the wrench aside and bolted toward the storm‑lashed exit. The heavy door slammed shut behind him, a gust of cold air rattling the lanterns, and his footsteps faded into the howl of the blizzard outside.
