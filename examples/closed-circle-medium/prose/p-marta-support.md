Marta watched the investigator's shoulders tighten as the deputy's accusation rang through the stone chamber. She slipped a hand into the pocket of her coat and pressed a folded note against his palm. 'If you need a place to work, the cellar is clear,' she said quietly. The promise was soft, meant only for his ears, and she felt the stone under her boot steady against the storm's howl.

She warned him that defying the sheriff would pull the lodge into deeper peril. His accusation that her alibi was false had already set the guests' whispers alight, and the risk of every door being locked tighter seemed to thicken the cold air. 'Keep your charge low,' she said, 'or the shutters will seal us in and the snow will drown any chance of escape.' Her eyes lingered on the warm revolver on the oak table, a reminder of how quickly evidence could turn against her.

Before his gaze could linger on her warning, a low chant rose from the miners gathered near the furnace. Their voices fell into a steady rhythm, demanding that the guilty be brought before the fire, and the sound of iron tools striking stone rose with them. The chant swelled, drowning Marta's words until they were but a whisper in the roar. She felt the thin thread of her promise stretch thin, the miners' demand pressing against the fragile truce she had offered.
