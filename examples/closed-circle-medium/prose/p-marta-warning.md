The fire in the common room sputtered, casting a thin line of amber across the stone walls. Marta Valen moved to the front of the room, her gray hair pulled back tight, and raised her voice over the crackling flames. “If the story spreads beyond these walls, panic will follow like a cold wind,” she said. “The lodge thrives on quiet; a scandal will shatter that.” The words hung in the air, thin as smoke, while the night deepened and the wind moaned against the lodge’s stone. Marta watched the shadows lengthen, felt the heat wane, and noted the guests pulling their blankets tighter around themselves. A deputy in the sheriff’s uniform stepped forward, his boots echoing on the flagstones, and said, “We’ll keep order, but the guests’ fear is already rising.” Their low murmurs rose and fell, a dull thrum that blended with the dying fire. Marta heard the deputies exchange brief, hushed words about patrol routes and the need to contain rumors. Their glances flicked toward the doorway where the storm raged, then back to the nervous faces of the guests. The cabin’s stone floor seemed to absorb the tension, each footstep a muted reminder of the danger lurking in the next breath. As the fire’s glow faded, Marta’s warning settled over the lodge like a cold draft, and the deputies’ murmurs became the only sound threading through the night’s lingering dread.
