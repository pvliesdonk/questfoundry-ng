The square was a slab of cold stone, slick with the night’s drizzle. A throng of miners pushed forward, dust clinging to their overalls, faces set hard as they chanted for justice. Cole felt the surge press against the railings, the weight of the crowd settling like a low tide over the town centre. The investigator stood near the hearth, shoulders squared, hands clasped behind the back as if bracing against the press. He kept his gaze on Marta, whose composed posture and quiet words had kept the hostess’s name from the storm of accusation; his calm acted like a shield, preserving the reputation that lingered like a faint scent in the air. Cole watched the investigator absorb the roar, his steady stare holding the crowd’s fury at bay.

Jordan leaned against the timbered wall opposite the miners, notebook clutched in a shaking hand. They scribbled rapidly, letters forming a trembling note that urged the investigator to hold steady, each stroke a hesitant beat against the clamor. A flicker of lamp light caught the paper, revealing the words “stay firm” before the ink bled into the damp page. The miner’s breath clouded in the cold air, and his eyes flicked briefly to the note, noting the tremor in its maker’s grip. The protest continued, voices rising like wind through pine, while the note rested in Jordan’s palm, a quiet promise amid the upheaval. Cole felt the weight of both the crowd and the ink, a stark reminder that steadiness was a fragile thing in the stone‑bound night.
