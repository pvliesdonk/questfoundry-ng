Cold wind battered the lodge's stone walls as the storm pressed against the shutters. Inside, the low hum of the furnace was swallowed by a distant chorus that rose from the tunnel entrance. The miners had gathered near the iron doors, their voices grinding together like sickles on rock, demanding swift justice for the woman whose reputation lay in ruin.

Through the flickering lantern light, Cole Duvall saw Jordan Blythe lean against the wooden desk, the edge of a notebook flashing as the author's hand moved hurriedly. Jordan pressed a quick line onto the page, a terse urging for the investigator to hold firm, then folded the scrap and slipped it toward the central table.

At the far end of the room, the investigator stood rigid, shoulders tightening as the miners' chant hammered against the stone. The accusation that had already shattered Marta's credibility seemed to echo louder now, each syllable a weight that pressed the investigator's shoulders down.

Cole felt the cold settle in his scarred hands, the chant a cold tide that would not recede, and he understood that the night would not end until the stone itself gave up its secret.
