The storm hammered the lodge’s stone walls and wind slipped through the iron hinges as Jordan Blythe stood by the central hearth, notebook clenched in one hand, eyes on the crowd forced behind the bolted doors. The sheriff kept the warm old revolver on a wooden crate, its barrel still faintly glowing from the earlier discharge. Marta Valen lingered near the brass tray, her shoulders hunched as murmurs brushed her name.

Cole Duvall raised the revolver, his scarred fingers steady despite the tremor in his voice. The hammer slipped, a flash of flame leapt backward and a bullet tore into his chest. Blood sprayed across the cold stone floor and the miner collapsed, the smoking gun still clutched in his hand. He tried to stumble toward the service door, his breath ragged, the weapon heavy at his side. The cylinder cracked a second time, the blast blowing into his torso and sealing his fate. The echo rang through the vaulted hall and Cole fell forward, the revolver clattering beside him as life drained from his body.

The investigator stepped forward, turned the smoking revolver over to the sheriff, and watched the lawman seal it in a leather case. The sheriff’s voice cut the murmurs, ordering the doors to remain sealed and the evidence to be logged. Around them, the crowd fell silent, the accusation that had hung over Marta turning into a hushed verdict. Her reputation lay in ruins, the whispered blame settling over the room like cold ash.

The stone walls kept their steady watch as the ticking brass clock counted the seconds, and the night settled into an ash‑gray silence.
