The storm hammered the pine eaves of Highpine Lodge, and the fire in the common room sputtered against the cold.

Jordan surveyed the assembled guests, noting the silence. No protest rose from the town beyond these walls; the anger that might have driven a crowd stayed locked inside the stone. In that hush, hidden motives took root, curling like smoke in the corners.

Cole Duvall moved toward the back service door, his hands gripping the rusted iron frame. He whispered something, and Simon Kade stepped in front of him, his voice low. "You can't leave now," Simon said. "The furnace was failing, and the storm would bring the cold in."

Jordan saw Cole's shoulders tense, and the wrench slipped from his grip onto the stone. The door slammed shut, echoing in the hallway.

Jordan felt the weight of unspoken threats. If the miner fled, his grievance would vanish into the blizzard, and the debt would die with him. Their notebook felt heavier in their coat. The lodge's stone walls seemed to close, and the fire's light grew dimmer, as suspicion settled like ash.

In the cold quiet, the lie of safety settled like ash.
