Jordan stood by the oak desk, the lamp haloing the cramped room in a thin pool of amber. Their hand trembled as the nib scraped the page, ink pooling in uneven drops that caught the light. The words formed in a terse line: *Stand firm.* The note felt like a pulse in their palm, a quiet resolve after Marta’s alibi had crumbled before the crowd. Jordan folded the paper twice, the creases sharp against the rough texture of their notebook, and slipped it across the worn table toward the investigator.

The investigator reached for the folded scrap, eyes narrowing as the trembling script came into view. The weight of the accusation against Marta still hung heavy in the air, but the brief encouragement steadied the shoulders that had been bending under the chant of the miners. Jordan watched from the edge of the room, noting the shift in posture, the way the investigator’s grip tightened around the note.

A deputy in uniform stepped forward, his badge catching the lantern’s glow, and spoke, "You’re under arrest for overstepping your authority," he said. The investigator’s gaze flicked to the note, then to the deputy, and back to the empty space beyond the stone walls where the storm hammered the shutters. The choice lingered in the stillness: submit to the sheriff’s demand and surrender the inquiry, or slip into the lodge’s shadowed corridors and continue the probe in secret. The tension crackled like the dying furnace, and Jordan felt the cold draft of the coming decision brush across the stone floor.
