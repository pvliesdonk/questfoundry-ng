Marta felt the stone walls press tighter after Jordan's accusation, the flickering light from the dying stove casting long shadows across the hall. The words about a fabricated alibi rang in her ears, and the anonymous note she still clutched seemed suddenly heavier. A cold surge ran through her veins, the lodge’s reputation left in tatters behind her. She clenched her fists, the gray of her hair pulled tight against her scalp, and a raw, guttural shout burst from her throat. The scream ricocheted off the timber beams, a sudden crack of sound that seemed to shatter the thin veil of calm.

The noise snapped the guests into motion. Blankets were ripped from shoulders, mugs clanged against tables, and a collective rush surged toward the back of the room. In the frantic scramble a shelf in the pantry jolted open, the frozen latch giving way with a thin sigh of released ice. Marta's eyes snapped to the opening; behind the rows of stored provisions a narrow doorway yawned, its iron latch swinging free, the darkness within swallowing the trembling light. She could hear the sobs and curses of the scattered crowd, feel the cold wind howl through the newly exposed gap, and see a few guests stumble forward, curious and terrified, as the hidden passage lay bare.

For a heartbeat the hall was a storm of bodies and breath, the panic she had unleashed spilling into the very stones. Marta steadied herself against the rail, the shock of her own voice still echoing in her mind. The lodge's ancient timber groaned under the surge, and the hidden door, revealed by the chaos, suggested another way out—or another danger waiting in the shadows. She swallowed, aware that her reputation now lay in ruins, and that the panic she had caused might be the only thing exposing the lodge’s buried secret.
