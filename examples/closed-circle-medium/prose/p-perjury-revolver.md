Deputy Hale stepped into the hall, his badge catching the last ember of the fire. He faced Marta Valen, his voice low, and warned that any false statement now would be perjury. Jordan watched his grip tighten on the pistol he carried, and noted that Marta's shoulders stayed still, her alibi unshaken. The old revolver remained hidden behind the false panel in the desk, its walnut case unseen by the uniformed men.

Later Sheriff Hale entered, his coat stirring dust from the threshold, and repeated the charge that lying would be prosecuted. He swept his gaze across the room, lingering on the empty space where the weapon had been moments before. Jordan felt the tension tighten around the gathered guests, but kept the notebook closed, allowing Marta's testimony to pass without further questioning. The concealed revolver still rested in its niche, its iron cylinder pressed against the wood, out of sight.

Simon Kade moved toward the desk with a quiet step, his hand finding the seam of the false panel. The cold barrel of the revolver slipped from its niche into his palm, and the metal glinted in the dim light. He lifted the gun just enough for Jordan to see its length, then turned and slid it back into place without a sound. Jordan observed the exchange and continued to shield Marta’s silence, his resolve firm as the storm battered the lodge outside.
