The wind howled against the lodge’s timber, driving a spray of snow into the stone hall. Jordan watched the back service door tremble on its iron hinges as Cole Duvall lifted his hand toward the knob. The miner’s overalls were caked with soot, his scarred fingers gripping the metal coldly. Jordan raised their voice over the gale and said, “You stay, or you go. The storm will take you either way.” Cole stared at the storm, then turned his back to the room. He stepped toward the iron latch, paused as the wind rattled the shutters, and let the door swing open. A gust of white snow rushed in, and the miner slipped into the blizzard, his silhouette disappearing into the night.

The heavy door slammed shut behind him, sealing the hallway with an echo that faded into the howling wind. Jordan felt the cold air swirl past the pine logs and heard the fire crackle weaker. In the hearth, Marta Valen rose from her seat. Her short gray hair was pulled tight, and the firelight caught the edge of her jaw. She lifted a hand, steadied her tone, and said, “I was alone in the library at the hour the body was found.” The words cut through the murmurs of the guests like a blade through pine sap.

Jordan noted the firmness of her posture and the guarded set of her eyes. The storm pressed harder against the stone walls, rattling the shutters in a steady rhythm. The investigator’s notebook lay open on the table, a blank page waiting beside the half‑filled entry about the revolver. No one spoke for a moment, the room holding its breath as the snow piled higher outside.

The night lingered, cold and relentless, and the lodge seemed to wait on the thin line between accusation and innocence.
