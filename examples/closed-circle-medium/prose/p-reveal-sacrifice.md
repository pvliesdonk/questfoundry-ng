Jordan stepped into the thin pool of lantern light at the foot of the stone hearth. The wind rattled the shutters and a thin wisp of smoke curled around the iron hinges of the back door. They lifted the torn fragment from the pocket of their coat and pressed it to the flickering flame, letting the paper curl and burn a corner of the notebook they had carried for weeks. The sacrifice was clear: the notebook, their record of the investigation, would be lost to the fire.

Jordan raised their voice over the crackling heat. “Simon Kade is the one who set the furnace and placed the revolver,” Jordan said. The words fell like a hammer on the gathered guests. Deputy Hale stepped forward, hand on his badge, and the crowd surged forward, eyes bright with fear. A clatter rang as the hidden revolver slipped from its false drawer and struck the stone floor. A spark caught a dry splinter, and a brief flare licked the edge of the table.

Marta Valen lifted a blanket over her shoulders and kept her gaze steady on the scene. No whisper named the hostess, and the guests’ accusations fell away from her. Her reputation remained untouched as the chaos swirled around her.

Jordan felt the heat sear the sleeve of their coat and a thin line of soot trace their cheek. They stood in the doorway, exposing themselves to the flame, knowing the danger would mark them in the eyes of the deputies. The lodge’s darkly comic atmosphere shifted into lethal scramble as deputies moved to seize the revolver and restrain the crowd.

The word hung in the air, and a cold chuckle echoed through the stone walls.
