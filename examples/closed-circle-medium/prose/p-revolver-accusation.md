The stone hallway loomed, its walls cold as the storm that battered the lodge outside. Jordan stepped forward, the walnut case of the old revolver glinting in the flickering lantern light. They lifted the weapon, feeling the faint warmth that lingered in the iron cylinder from its recent discharge. With a voice that cut through the murmuring crowd, Jordan shouted, “Marta Valen fabricated her alibi,” and hurled the revolver straight into the hands of the two deputies who had moved to flank the table. The brass casings clanged against the deputies’ gloves, drawing gasps from the assembled guests. Deputy Hale barked a command, his badge flashing, and seized the revolver, while his companion tightened his grip on the case. The accusation hung in the air, heavy as the ash on the stone floor, and Marta’s face went pale, her hands trembling around the note she had been clutching. She opened her mouth, but the roar of the wind and the rising shouts drowned any reply. Guests surged forward, some shouting for justice, others demanding restraint, the room filling with a chaotic clang of chairs and the rustle of blankets. Jordan watched the turmoil, notebook pressed against their chest, the storm’s howl pressing through the cracked windows. The deputies placed the revolver on a sturdy oak table, the barrel still warm, fingerprints smudged across the metal, proof of the hastened accusation. In that moment the hostess’s reputation lay shattered, the crowd’s fear spilling like smoke through the hall. Jordan felt a cold resolve settle in their gut, the weight of the decision to continue the investigation despite the sheriff’s looming authority. The lanterns sputtered, casting long shadows that danced across the stone as the night deepened.
