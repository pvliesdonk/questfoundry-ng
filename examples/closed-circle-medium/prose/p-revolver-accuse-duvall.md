Cole Duvall stood at the edge of the stone hallway, the lantern’s weak flame catching the dented iron hinges of the door behind him. The investigator lifted the old revolver from the table and turned it toward the gathered deputies. He announced that the forensic analysis had found the miner’s fingerprints on the barrel, his scarred hands recorded in the powdery impression. The sheriff took the weapon, the cold metal pressing against his palm, and placed it in the deputy’s case for further inquiry. Around them the crowd murmured, the earlier accusation against Marta still humming like a low wind. A deputy stepped forward, grasped Cole’s forearm, and forced him toward the entrance. Cole felt the weight of the handcuffs bite his wrists as the metal clanged against the stone. He shouted that he was framed, his voice echoing off the walls, the words spilling out raw and angry. The sheriff’s men held the revolver steady, its barrel still warm from the recent discharge, the fingerprints stark against the polished walnut. Cole watched the faces in the crowd flare, eyes flashing with fear and anger, some raising fists, others clenching their fists over blankets. He sensed the tension rise, a surge of suspicion that turned the room into a restless furnace. Cole pressed his back against the stone as they led him toward the door, his mind racing for a way out despite the storm’s howl outside. He knew the evidence now bound him, that the sheriff’s custody meant the revolver would become official proof. The lantern sputtered, casting a flickering shadow across his scarred hands, and Cole felt the cold resolve settle deeper, the night pressing in as the crowd’s shouts faded behind him.
