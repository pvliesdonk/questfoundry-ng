Cold stone walls framed the main hall as the storm hammered the lodge’s shutters. Jordan stood near the oak table, the old revolver concealed behind the false bottom, its polished walnut case hidden from view. Deputy Hale entered, his uniform crisp despite the damp, and placed a steady hand on the barrel of his rifle. "Investigator Blythe, perjury will be punished," he said, his tone as measured as the ticking clock on the mantle. The second deputy mirrored the statement, eyes flicking to the crowded faces that shifted under the lantern light.

The sheriff’s men pressed the warning again, their voices echoing off the stone. Jordan listened, feeling the isolation tighten around him after the accusation against Marta. The hidden revolver remained out of sight, its presence known only to him, a silent shield for the guest whose name he had linked to the gun. He watched the deputies glance at the table, their gazes searching for the weapon that was not there, and sensed the growing distance between himself and the authority that now boxed him in.

Simon Kade moved forward, his steps soundless on the pine‑scented floor. He knelt beside the table, his fingers brushing the seam of the false panel until the wood gave way. The revolver’s barrel emerged, warm from recent handling, catching the lantern’s glow. Simon lifted it with deliberate calm, the iron gleam a threat in the dim. "This is enough to keep anyone quiet," he said, his voice low, and the room’s murmurs hushed as the metal clicked against the stone. The tension rose, a cold draft slipping through the open doorway, and Jordan felt the stakes sharpen like a blade.
