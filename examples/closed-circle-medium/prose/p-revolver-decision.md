Deputy Hale stepped forward, his badge catching the dim lantern light. He said, “The revolver must be placed under sheriff’s control.” Jordan watched the iron cylinder still faintly warm on the table and felt the weight of the demand. To the investigator, the deputy’s order presented a chance to force a public reckoning with Marta Valen’s alibi, a lever that could turn suspicion toward her and shore up the authority of the law. Jordan’s mind listed the faces of the guests, remembering the quiet guest who had been nursing a fever in the east wing. A thought of that person lingered, a fear that the weapon’s transfer might draw an innocent into the official report. Jordan hesitated, the notion of a stray accusation tightening the air. The deputy’s hand rested on the case, waiting. Jordan weighed the risk that a by‑stander could be named in the report. The possibility of harming an unknowing guest pressed against the drive to expose Marta. Yet the need to shatter the hostess’s reputation rose louder. Jordan decided that exposing the false alibi outweighed the collateral danger. Jordan lifted the revolver, slid it across the table, placed it in the deputy’s open case, and said, “Take it. The evidence is clear.” The metal clinked against leather, sealing the accusation and signaling the sheriff’s jurisdiction.
