Jordan stood behind the oak desk, the lamp’s weak glow catching the polished walnut of the concealed niche. They felt the cold barrel hidden behind the false panel, the iron cylinder slick from years of neglect. Deputy Hale’s badge glinted from across the room, his eyes scanning the assembled guests while Marta Valen kept her mug steady at the brass rail. Jordan’s notebook rested in their coat pocket, the paper inside still blank where the final accusation should have been written. They decided not to draw attention to the revolver, to let Marta’s story stand untouched, and they slid the weapon back into its secret compartment, the wood whispering against the stone frame.

A sudden crack split the hushed hall as a stray spark fell from the dying fire. The revolver’s percussion cap fizzed, and a harsh pop erupted from the hidden barrel. A flash of orange light blossomed inside the walnut case, and the gun discharged a single round that struck Jordan squarely in the chest. The impact felt like iron striking iron, and the world narrowed to the sharp sting spreading through their ribs. Their hand fell, the notebook slipping from the pocket and landing open on the stone floor, the inked line about Marta’s alibi blurring with the spreading blood. The room seemed to hold its breath as the echo of the shot faded into the storm’s howl outside.

Jordan’s thoughts flickered, a brief flash of the manuscript they had saved, the unanswered question that had driven them here. Pain surged, hot and cold in equal measure, and a thin veil of darkness crept across their vision. They heard Marta’s quiet gasp, heard Deputy Hale shout a command, heard the clatter of the revolver as it fell back onto the table, its barrel still warm. The last thing Jordan sensed was the weight of the notebook, its pages rustling once more before silence settled over the hall.

Later, the lodge settled into a tense quiet. Marta’s reputation remained unblemished; the guests whispered about the fire but never questioned her alibi. The revolver stayed concealed behind the desk, its presence known only to a few, and the investigation stalled without a new lead. The night ended with the lingering scent of pine sap and cold stone, a darkly comic void where justice should have sparked.
