Simon Kade brushed his gloved hand over the cold walnut of the revolver, feeling the familiar grain beneath his fingertips. He lifted the weapon just enough to let the iron cylinder catch the weak light from the hearth. "This piece has been an heirloom of the Kade line since the lodge was built," he said, his voice low enough to blend with the crackle of the fire.

Jordan Blythe leaned forward, notebook clasped in one hand, and met Simon's gaze. "Marta Valen's reputation will not be jeopardized by this," Jordan said, his tone steady. The deputy in the grey coat stepped forward, boots echoing on the stone floor. "We need the weapon in the sheriff's custody," the deputy said, his hand hovering near the table.

Jordan turned his head toward the man and shook his head. "I will not hand it over," Jordan said, refusal clear in his eyes. Simon watched the exchange, his posture rigid, outward loyalty masking the conflict churning inside him.

He guided the revolver toward the false drawer beneath the desk; the latch clicked, and it seemed the weapon might be concealed there, away from prying eyes. A thin wisp of smoke rose from the hearth, curling toward the cracked ceiling as the storm hammered the lodge windows.

Simon felt the weight of silence settle in the room, the revolver hidden for now, the investigation threading through the stone walls. He wondered how long the secret could remain buried before the cold of the lodge forced it into the light.
