Simon Kade slipped through the stone hallway, lantern low, the muffled wind outside rattling the shutters. He paused before the oak desk, his fingers finding the narrow seam where the walnut panel met the stone frame. The hidden niche yielded a cold barrel, the iron cylinder slick with age. Simon pulled the revolver free, the polished wood catching a sliver of light that filtered through the storm‑clouded windows. He turned the weapon, feeling its heavy balance settle against his palm. The room fell quiet as the metal clinked against the desk. Deputy Hale stood near the iron stove, his badge catching a stray glow from the fire's ember. He glanced around, his eyes resting on the assembled guests. A murmur rose from the crowd, but Simon held the barrel steady, pointing it toward the nearest speaker. He spoke in a low tone, “No further claims unless you wish to test steel,” and the words settled like ash. Jordan Blythe stepped forward, notebook pressed to his chest, his gaze shifting from the revolver to Marta Valen. He lifted his hand, the gesture deliberate, but said nothing that would undermine her account. “We will keep the record as it stands,” he said, his voice even. Marta stood by the brass rail, her mug steaming, shoulders unmoved. Cole Duvall narrowed his eyes, grip tightening on his pipe wrench, but Simon kept the gun trained on him, the iron cylinder gleaming. The storm battered the lodge, each gust rattling the panes, while the hidden revolver added a sharp edge to the tension filling the hall. Simon waited, the weight of the weapon a silent promise, his eyes scanning for any movement that might force him to fire. Now, with the revolver at his side, Simon could either lower the barrel or demand that the deputy step back.
