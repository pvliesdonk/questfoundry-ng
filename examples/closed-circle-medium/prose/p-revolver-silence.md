Jordan watched the flicker of the hearth catch the metal of the old revolver as they pressed the walnut case into Deputy Hale's outstretched hand. The weight settled against the deputy's sleeve, cold iron humming against the wool of the coat. Jordan's voice was flat. "It stays here," they said, and the deputy's brow tightened without a word. Around them the lodge murmured, guests shifting on stone benches while Marta clutched the brass rail, her gaze fixed on the fire. Jordan kept Marta's claim of being alone in the library to themselves, the alibi hanging unchallenged in the stale air.

The deputies escorted the weapon to the cramped office where the sheriff's forensic team waited. One deputy lifted the barrel, brushed dust from the polished walnut, and placed the gun into a canvas satchel. Within minutes the lab report whispered across the room: the prints matched those of miner Cole Duvall, the scarred hands still stained with dust. Jordan heard the name spoken aloud, felt the room tighten, but offered no comment on Marta's statement. The atmosphere grew uneasy, a low hum of suspicion threading through the stone corridors.

Cole stepped forward, his overalls still dusty, the pipe wrench dangling from his belt. He raised his voice, protesting the sudden accusation, his words echoing off the timber beams. A deputy snapped the cuffs onto his wrists, the metal clink stark in the quiet. Cole snarled, his face flushed, but the sheriff's men held him steady. Jordan noted the revolver now resting on the sheriff's desk, the iron cylinder catching the lamplight. The crowd's chatter faded to a muted thrum, the tension in the lodge simmering rather than exploding. Jordan turned away, notebook heavy in hand, the night settling over the stone walls while the hostess's reputation remained intact.
