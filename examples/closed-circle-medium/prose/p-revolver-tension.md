Marta watched the revolver lying on the oak table, its iron cylinder still faintly warm from recent handling. The polished walnut seemed to catch the dim lantern light and cast a thin glow across the stone floor. She felt a tight knot form in her gut, fearing that the weapon’s sudden appearance would fan the already thin patience of the guests. The lodge’s tension, already simmering from the storm, could snap at any sharp sound.

She turned her attention to the investigator who stood beside the fire, notebook clutched in his hand. He lifted his voice and began to recite the facts, his tone steady, as if the room itself waited for his verdict. Marta fretted that his public declaration would shred the fragile credibility she had built, not realizing that his words would soon strike directly at her honor. The thought that the accusation would spread like smoke through the hall unsettled her, yet she could not foresee the exact blow he was about to deliver.

The front door creaked open and two deputies in dark uniforms stepped into the room. One of them fixed his badge on the dim light and said, “We are taking over this matter. Your overreach ends now.” The other placed a hand on the revolver’s case, his grip firm. Marta saw the deputy’s gaze sweep over the investigator, then linger on her as the weight of his stare settled like cold iron. She understood, in that stark moment, that her reputation now dangled on the edge of a blade, balanced between the law’s authority and the murmurs of the frightened guests.
