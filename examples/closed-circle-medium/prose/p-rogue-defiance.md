A thin trace of gunpowder lingered in the cold stone air, curling from the table where the old revolver lay, its polished walnut warm under the dim glow. Jordan Blythe inhaled it, feeling a sharp sting of solitary defiance settle beneath the lodge’s grim grin.

Deputy Hale's voice cut through the murmurs, accusing him of negligence that had let the fire spread and the evidence vanish. Jordan's jaw tightened. He stepped forward, boots clacking on the frost‑slick floor, and raised a hand toward the deputy. “You charge me with failure while I still stand among the smoke,” he said, voice steady despite the ache at his temple. “The gun was fired here, the powder still lingers, and I will not be silenced by your badge.” The deputy's badge caught the last ember of light, the cracked windows framing a world of pine and iron hinges. Shadows pooled in the corners, the fire's sputter a low hiss matching the tension in Jordan's throat. The lingering scent became a banner of his resolve, and the solitary defiance he carried pressed against the accusation like stone.

The old revolver rested on the table, its barrel still warm to the touch, a silent testament to the shot that had echoed minutes before. Outside, the blizzard hammered the cracked windows, and the sputtering furnace whispered that the lodge could seal itself at any moment. Jordan knew that defying the sheriff would force him to work in the shadows, but the scent of powder reminded him that truth could not be smothered.
