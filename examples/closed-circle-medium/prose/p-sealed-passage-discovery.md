The stone hallway stretched beyond the hearth, its walls glazed with a thin sheet of frost. Simon Kade stepped from the lantern's dim glow, the iron latch on the hidden oak door echoing under his hand. He pressed the bolt, sealing the narrow passage behind the library shelves, and felt the wood settle with a final groan. After the door shut, Simon moved down the corridor, stopping at each portal. He bolted the pantry door, locked the back stair, and set a lantern by the garden entrance, its flame a steady point against the howling wind. At each exit he lingered, listening for any draft that might betray an unseen breach, his gaze sweeping the stone arches until the last sigh of air faded.

When he reached the main hall, Jordan Blythe entered, his coat dusted with snow. Simon watched the investigator kneel beside the study table, his fingers parting a torn scrap of paper caught in the carpet. Jordan unfolded the fragment, and Simon saw the faint ink: a single line traced beneath the revolver's polished walnut case, “held by unseen hand.” The sentence glimmered like frost on pine, linking the antique weapon to a hidden grip.

Simon noted the connection, the way the ink mirrored fresh fingerprints on the barrel. He kept his lantern low, the light catching the iron hinges of the doors he had sealed. The night pressed cold against the stone, but the sealed passage held its silence, and Simon's watch remained vigilant over every exit.
