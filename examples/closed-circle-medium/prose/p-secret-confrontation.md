Simon Kade leaned toward the oak shelf and lowered his voice. He whispered, "There is a narrow way behind those stacks that lets a person move through the library without being seen," he said, his breath fogging the cold air for a moment. The words fell into the stone room like a soft footfall. He lingered a heartbeat, then turned his back to the hearth and let the dim light swallow his outline. Simon slipped into the shadows at the edge of the aisle, his form disappearing into the darkness as if he were a part of the stone itself.\n\nA few seconds later a heavy tread echoed from the doorway. Cole Duvall entered, his dusty overalls rustling, the weight of his pipe wrench clanking against his thigh. He moved straight to Marta Valen, who stood near the bar with a steaming mug in her hands. "You've been taking the miners' share and no one knows," he said, his tone blunt and uneasy. Marta's fingers tightened around the mug, the porcelain trembling. She stared at him, eyes flicking to the fire and back, but said nothing. Simon watched from the darkness, noting the tension flare and the thin plume of smoke rising from the hearth. The accusation lingered in the cold room, and the hidden doorway behind the shelves waited in silence, its secret still untested.
