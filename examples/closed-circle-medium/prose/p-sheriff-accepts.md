Sheriff Hale guided Jordan down the narrow hallway, the muffled wind howling outside the lodge’s locked doors. The deputy’s boots clanged on the cold stone as they passed the remaining guests, who whispered behind cupped hands. Inside the main hall, Hale addressed the other deputies, stating that the formal inquiry would proceed without interruption. Marta stood by the rail, her posture unchanged, and the deputy recorded her statement without challenge. Jordan felt the weight of the notebook slip from their grasp as they were ushered toward the deputy’s truck.

The sheriff’s staff gathered the evidence left on the tables, dragging the leather‑bound manuscript and the notebook onto a cart that creaked under the load. They photographed the revolver, catalogued the torn envelope, and noted that no claim had been made against Marta’s alibi.
