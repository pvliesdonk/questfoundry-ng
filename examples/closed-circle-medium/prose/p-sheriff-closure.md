The sheriff filed his report on the miner’s death before the fire had fully died down. He wrote in the ledger with a steady hand, his words offering no accusation, only a bland statement that the body had been found near the service entrance and that no foul play could be confirmed. The lodge’s occupants shifted in the dim hall, the weight of his tepid conclusion settling like cold stone on their shoulders. Marta Valen stood near the brass rail, her face unmarked, her alibi still unchallenged as the deputy’s pen scratched across the paper.

Cole Duvall heard the sheriff’s voice echo off the stone arches as the man read the final line aloud. The words sounded like a wind‑howl through the shutters, and Cole felt a knot tighten in his gut. He tightened his grip on the pipe wrench tucked in his belt and stared at the locked service door, the iron latch still glinting in the low firelight. The storm battered the lodge, snow hurling against the cracked windows, and a thin plume of smoke drifted from the hearth.

He decided to push the door despite the deputy’s orders. He stepped forward, feeling the pine sap on the oak frame sting his fingers, and he set the wrench against the bolt. The metal gave a short, cold clang, and for a heartbeat the latch yielded. A shout rose from the hallway as a deputy lunged, his hand closing around Cole’s wrist. The miner’s arm buckled, the wrench slipped from his grasp and clattered onto the stone floor. Heat rose in his throat as the breath left him in a harsh gasp.

Cole stared at the deputy’s grip, the iron of the latch, the flicker of the fire, and his mind raced to the hidden clue he had left behind. He felt the world tilt, the storm’s howl growing louder, and then his body went limp against the cold stone. Jordan Blythe stood a few paces away, his eyes narrowing as he surveyed the fallen miner. He said, “This makes no sense,” and his brow furrowed, the puzzlement clear on his face. The investigator turned the revolver over in his hands, noting the smudged prints, and he muttered that the death remained a mystery.

Marta’s reputation held steady; no one questioned her story as the lodge settled into an uneasy silence. The sheriff’s tepid report lingered in the air, and the investigation stalled, leaving the unanswered questions to hover like smoke over the stone walls.
