The stone hallway smelled of pine sap and damp iron as Jordan stepped into the main room. Deputies in dark uniforms lined the doorway, their rifles resting on shoulders, badges glinting under the lantern light. The lead deputy barked that Jordan had overstepped jurisdiction and would be arrested. The words cut through the murmur of frightened guests like a cracked hinge. Jordan felt the weight of the accusation settle on their shoulders and their hand tightened around the notebook, the leather cover warm from the recent fire.

A second deputy raised a hand toward the revolver on the oak table, then lowered it, their stare fixed on Jordan. "You have no authority here," the first deputy said, voice flat, tone by the book. The crowd shifted, blankets rustling, eyes flickering between the notebook and the warm metal. Jordan stood still, breath shallow, the cold stone pressing against their back.

The deputies' rebuke sparked a surge of conflict inside Jordan. Their mind raced through the facts they had gathered, the accusation against Marta that had already shattered her reputation. The thought of a swift arrest tugged at the part of Jordan that prized justice, while another thread warned of the danger in silencing a voice that might expose the killer. The storm outside hammered the windows, a relentless reminder that time slipped away.

Jordan weighed the options in the brief silence that followed. They could submit to the deputies, hand over the notebook, and allow the official inquiry to take over, preserving their safety but risking the loss of crucial insight. Or they could defy the arrest, keep the notebook hidden, and continue pursuing the truth, even if it meant becoming a target themselves. The internal battle was sharp, each pulse echoing the ticking of the lodge's ancient clock. Finally, Jordan lifted their chin, voice steady, and said, "I will not be taken without answering for what I have uncovered," the words hanging in the cold air like a breath of smoke. The deputies tightened their grip on their rifles, the confrontation poised on the edge of a stone stair.
