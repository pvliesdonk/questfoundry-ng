Jordan's hand still trembled over the notebook as they slipped a note onto the oak table. The ink bled unevenly, each letter a thin promise: hold steady. The words were a whisper against the clatter of the storm outside, a reminder that the stakes rose while Marta's warning lingered, unheard. Jordan watched the sheriff's men file into the main hall, their boots echoing on the stone floor. Deputy Hale stepped forward, badge flashing, and placed a heavy hand on the revolver that rested by the hearth. He glanced at Jordan, his expression flat. "You need to come with us," said Hale. The deputy's voice cut through the low murmur of guests, and the air seemed to tighten around the iron hinges of the doors.

Jordan lifted the note, eyes scanning the trembling script once more. The reminder steadied the breath that threatened to hitch. Around them, the fire sputtered, casting a pale glow on the blank pages still open in the notebook. The choice lay before them, stark as the pine sap scent drifting from the cracked windows. Surrender would mean the sheriff's jurisdiction, a formal end to the covert search. Continuing covertly would keep the investigation alive but risk deeper danger. Jordan's fingers brushed the edge of the notebook, feeling the weight of both options. With a measured pause, they stepped toward the deputy, the decision hanging in the cold air.
