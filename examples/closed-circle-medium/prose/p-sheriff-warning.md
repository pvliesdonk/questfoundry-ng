The stone hallway echoed with the clank of iron hinges as two uniformed deputies entered, their boots striking the frost‑slick floor. Jordan Blythe watched them stop before the fire‑worn table where the old revolver lay concealed, the senior deputy's badge catching a thin flash of lantern light. “We’re here to enforce the sheriff’s jurisdiction,” the deputy said, his tone as cold as the wind that rattled the shutters. “Any investigator acting beyond the sheriff’s authority will be reported.” Jordan felt the words settle like lingering smoke, a reminder that the sheriff’s notice would reach beyond the lodge walls. The warning tightened the cold in their chest, a dread that rose around the fragile alibi Marta Valen had offered for the hostess. They had left the alibi untouched, a silent pact to keep the hostess’s reputation from unraveling, and now the sheriff’s threat threatened to expose that discretion. A brief pause followed, the deputy’s stare pressing against Jordan’s resolve. Jordan steadied the notebook in their coat, the worn leather creaking under the grip, and met the deputy’s gaze. The strain of duty pressed against the urge to protect themselves, a weight that settled on their shoulders like an unread manuscript. They inhaled the faint scent of pine sap from the walls, feeling resolve harden. The night would test whether the alibi could survive the sheriff’s scrutiny, and Jordan prepared to meet the demand with a calm that belied the turmoil inside.
