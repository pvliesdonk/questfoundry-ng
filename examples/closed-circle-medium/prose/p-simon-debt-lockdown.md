The stone hallway held a thin layer of pine sap that clung to Simon's boots as he followed the sheriff's lantern light. He felt the weight of the ledger he had slipped into his coat earlier, the inked numbers still smearing his palm. The entry for his name stared back, a sum owed to a miner that matched the night the heiress fell. The correlation settled like ash on his mind; he now understood why the murder had seemed to circle his hidden transactions. He kept his uniform immaculate, his posture straight, while a knot of desperation tightened in his chest.

Deputy Hale stepped forward, his uniform crisp, and spoke in a measured tone that cut through the murmurs. "All exits are to remain sealed," he said. He ordered every door to be barred, the service tunnel locked, and the guests to stay in the main hall until the storm cleared. Simon watched iron hinges groan under fresh boards, heard the sheriff repeat that no one could leave the lodge, and sensed the cold wind pressing against the windows as the shutters slammed shut. The fire in the common room flickered lower, its orange glow trapped by the newly sealed stone walls. A hush settled over the crowd, and Simon sensed the weight of authority pressing against his own secret. He forced a calm smile, his eyes lingering on the ledger hidden beneath his vest, aware that the lockdown tied his fate tighter to the murder he had tried to hide.
