Simon Kade watched the storm beat against the lodge’s stone walls as the night grew darker. Inside the great hall the sheriff’s voice rang out, ordering every door to be sealed and the guests to remain inside. Deputies moved like iron shutters, their boots thudding on the flagstones while the clerk slammed the main entrance shut. Simon felt the weight of his uniform settle against his shoulders as he glanced toward the dim corner where the investigator slipped past a deputy’s grasp. The notebook was clutched tight, the edge of a page still bearing the accusation against Marta Valen. The investigator’s stride was steady, eyes scanning the room for any escape.

Simon stepped forward, his hand resting lightly on the polished wood of a tapestry that hung low on the wall. He lowered his voice to a murmur. “There is a way behind the drapery,” he said. He eased the fabric aside, exposing a narrow stone mouth set into the wall, its outline faint in the weak torchlight. The opening led into a damp corridor slick with frost, the air cold enough to bite the skin.

The investigator bent low, vanished into the passage, and the sound of his boots faded quickly. Simon watched the sheriff’s commands grow distant as the hallway swallowed the echo. He lingered a moment, listening to the hiss of the furnace and the faint creak of the hidden door settling back into place.

With the lockdown in force, the lodge’s ordinary routes were blocked, but the secret network remained his domain. Simon felt a surge of resolve; the hidden path could carry the truth about Marta’s alibi away from the sheriff’s reach. He turned back to the hall, his expression unchanged, and allowed the deputy’s gaze to slide over his pristine uniform. The firelight flickered, casting long shadows across the stone, and Simon understood that his secret debt was now tied to the investigator’s quest. He stepped aside, letting the quiet hallway become the conduit for the next move in the night’s uneasy investigation.
