Simon Kade stood near the iron hearth, the stone walls exhaling a thin breath of cold. The guests huddled under blankets, their faces pale in the waning glow. He felt the weight of the revolver pressed against his coat, the polished walnut grip a familiar certainty. He drew the gun with deliberate hands, the iron cylinder catching the last ember's light. A thin film of pine sap dusted the barrel, a reminder of the lodge's long years. He thought of the debt he owed and the secret he had kept. He raised the barrel to his chest, his eyes narrowing. Simon pulled the trigger, the blast echoing through the stone as the bullet struck his own chest. The shock knocked him forward onto the cold floor, the revolver clattering to the hearth. The echo faded as the furnace coughed, its flame sputtering and then dying. Heat drained from the room, the stone growing cold faster than the wind outside. A sudden darkness swallowed the hall, the only sound the distant howl of the storm. Murmurs rose, mugs clanged, but no one approached. Silence settled, a heavy stone over the secret that had finally been aired.
