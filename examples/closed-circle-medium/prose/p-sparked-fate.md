The stone hall glowed dull amber from the smoldering hearth, a thin curl of smoke drifting above the crowd that circled Cole Duvall. Deputies stood at the doorways, rifles lowered, while Marta Valen rested a steaming mug on the brass rail. Cole gripped his pipe wrench, eyes flicking over the faces that shouted accusations.

A stray spark leapt from the dying fire and struck the polished walnut of the old revolver resting on the sheriff’s desk. The iron cylinder ignited; the hammer fell without a pull of the trigger, and the weapon cracked. A single bullet ripped through Cole’s chest, powder searing his coat, and he collapsed onto the cold stone with a hollow thud.

Jordan stepped forward, fingers brushing the cold wood of the desk, and lifted the revolver. Sheriff Hale entered the doorway, badge catching the lantern light, and Jordan handed the weapon to him and said, “It stays here for the forensic team.” The sheriff placed the gun back in its case and turned it toward the waiting deputies.

Marta kept her gaze fixed on the space where Cole had stood, her expression unchanged. Jordan noted that her claim of being alone in the library had not been challenged.

A bitter laugh rose from the crowd, thin as ash, and the room settled into a brittle quiet while the storm howled outside.
