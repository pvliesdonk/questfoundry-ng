The long pine table was set under the dim iron chandelier, the wind howling against the stone walls of Highpine Lodge. Jordan Blythe rose from their seat, notebook clenched, and let the room fall to a hushed thrum. They spoke in a voice that cut through the clatter of cutlery, saying, “The heiress did not fall victim to circumstance; she orchestrated the murder to preserve a secret.” The accusation landed like a stone on the oak, and the guests’ faces tightened. Simon Kade’s eyebrows rose, his mug steaming in the chill, while a nervous murmur rippled through the crowd.

Simon set his mug down and said, “If that theory reaches the papers, it will shape the town’s judgment and cloud the investigation.” He lifted the cup, took a slow sip of the stale coffee, and regarded the notebook with a measured stare. Jordan noted the careful cadence of his reply, the way his eyes flicked between the guests and the fire. The hearth guttered, sending a thin wisp of smoke curling toward the ceiling, and the tension in the room grew colder.

After the dinner, the group drifted toward the study, the door creaking as they entered the smaller, lantern‑lit room. Jordan watched Simon place his mug on the worn oak desk, the coffee now cooling on its surface. Marta Valen slipped into the study, her coat dusted with snow, and paused at the doorway. She said, “Publishing that article may spark hysteria among the guests and the town beyond.” Her eyes flicked to the notebook, then to the faces gathered around the desk. Jordan felt the weight of the decision settle like ash in the air. The silence that followed was thick, every breath a reminder that a single story could ignite a blaze far hotter than the dwindling fire.
