Jordan Blythe entered the low, damp tunnel that led from the lodge toward the old silver vein. The stone walls exhaled a thin draft that rattled his coat, and the sound reminded him of the article that had spread through the town the day before. He heard the miners' boots thudding on the packed earth, their faces turned toward him in brief, wary glances. Cole Duvall stood near a rusted lantern, his hands still smeared with coal, and he shifted the lantern as if to keep the flame from dying.

Jordan kept his notebook clutched to his chest, eyes scanning the darkness for any sign of the concealed conduit the article had described. The theory he had published suggested that a hidden passage could be used to divert the furnace's airflow, and the notion felt heavier with each echo of his own steps. He heard a low mutter from Cole, "You think it matters now?" and the miner's voice cracked on the word "now". Jordan answered simply, "If the fire collapses, we all die," and the words fell into the stale air.

A sudden clank reverberated from deeper in the shaft as a metal latch jolted against its frame. Jordan's pulse quickened; the wind seemed to close around the tunnel, pressing the stone tighter. He realized the miners were watching him as much as he watched them, their suspicion rising with each breath. The tunnel stretched onward, a narrow artery that held the weight of his theory and the palpable fear of everyone trapped inside.
