The cracked stone of the main hall seemed to draw the last ember of the fire into its cold joints. Jordan Blythe stood beside the central table, the warm barrel of the old revolver still glimmering in the weak light. Their eyes traced the faint smear of Marta Valen's fingerprints, the heat of the metal confirming the shot had been fired minutes ago. Jordan lifted the notebook, the torn receipt fluttering like a moth, and spoke to the assembled guests. "The gun was discharged from this table," they said, voice steady despite the tremor in their hand. "The prints belong to Marta Valen, and the timestamp on her alibi is false. The murder is now evident."

The murmurs rose to a roar as the sheriff's deputy stepped forward, badge flashing in the dying glow. He barked for order, then reached for the revolver, his grip tight on the polished wood. Jordan watched the deputy's finger find the trigger and felt a sudden, sharp crack split the hall. The blast caught Jordan at the temple, and the world narrowed to the cold stone floor as they fell.

The crowd fell silent, the fire sputtering out as if in respect. Marta clutched the broken‑seal note, her face a mask of shock that quickly hardened into resignation. The lieutenant shouted that the investigation was over, that justice would proceed without the investigator. Jordan's notebook slipped from their grasp, the pages fluttering open to the final entry, a single line of ink that read, "Truth bears a price."

Behind the hushed thunder of the storm, the lodge settled into darkness, its secret now exposed but its keeper gone. Marta's disgrace was already sealed; the whispered accusations would not fade.
