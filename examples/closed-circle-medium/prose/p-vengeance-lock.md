Cole Duvall stood in the stone hallway, the storm’s cold seeping through the shutters and staining the floor with frost. The iron stove in the common room flickered, spilling thin light over the gathered faces. Marta Valen lingered near the brass rail, her gray hair pulled tight, eyes fixed on the fire. As Cole watched her, his eyes narrowed on Marta’s every move, a thin line of old anger tightening across his throat. He said, "Vengeance will find its mark," his voice low enough to blend with the wind’s howl.

The words hung in the air like a draft. Simon Kade, his uniform immaculate despite the gloom, stepped forward and placed a firm hand on the heavy oak door that led to the service tunnel, pushing it shut; the iron hinges groaned. He moved down the hallway, checking each exit in turn. He latched the pantry door, secured the back stair, and set a lantern near the garden entrance, its flame steady against the gusts. Simon said to the deputy, "All exits are sealed," and the deputy nodded.

The stone walls seemed to inhale the threat, the cold settling like ash in the corners, and Cole felt the night tighten around them, his grip on the pipe wrench tightening as the sealed passage promised a watchful night.
