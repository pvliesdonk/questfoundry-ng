title: Edgar Barnes – Retired Schoolteacher
---

Edgar Barnes retired from the chalk‑dusted rows of the town school and retreated to a solitary cottage on the hill's edge. Tall and thin, his spectacles sit like a pair of lenses onto which the world is filtered into riddles. Eccentric in habit but keenly observant, he spends his days arranging cryptic verses upon weather‑worn countertops, offering them to any who dare ask. Though his former pupils recall his gentle instruction, the community now whispers of his sudden withdrawal, wondering whether his mind is a sanctuary or a trap. In the quiet of his study, the ticking of clocks seems to echo the unanswered questions that linger like unfinished sentences.
