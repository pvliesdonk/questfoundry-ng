title: Harriet Wellman – Deputy Sheriff
---

Harriet Wellman enforces the law from a low trench coat that swallows her greying hair, her badge pressed hard against her chest as a reminder of duty. A scar runs across her cheek—a souvenir from a night when the line between order and chaos was drawn with a barrel of cheap whiskey. Mid‑forties and unflinching, she approaches each case with pragmatic bluntness, discarding sentiment like a broken pistol. Her deputy sheriff badge is a weight she bears before the town's restless whispers, and her no‑nonsense manner slices through gossip as cleanly as a well‑kept blade. In a place where authority is both shield and spear, Harriet stands as the iron hinge between them.
