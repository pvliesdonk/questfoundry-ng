The Ornate Knife is a heavy hunting blade, its ivory handle polished to a blinding sheen that catches the dim glow of tavern candles. The sheath that once protected its edge is conspicuously absent, leaving the blade exposed on a stone slab where it gleams like a threat waiting to be claimed. Its weight is palpable, and the subtle etchings along the spine hint at a lineage of craftsmanship now lost. Though the knife’s origin is unclear, its craftsmanship suggests a purpose beyond the ordinary—perhaps a token of a pact or a relic of a forgotten duel. In the hands of the unwary, its edge promises swift resolution, yet its unguarded presence invites danger as readily as curiosity.
