Lucy kept her gaze fixed on the blackened slab where the firepit crackled, its orange glow licking the stone walls of the backroom. The ledger lay half‑burned, pages curling like wilted leaves, the edge of an ivory‑handled knife jutting from the ash. Beside the ember a fresh, dark bootprint sank into the wet earth, the mud still glossy from recent rain. It marked a path that vanished into the dim doorway, as if the wearer had hurried away before anyone could see. The scent of soot mixed with the iron tang of blood lingered, and Lucy felt a chill settle along the spine of the room. A thin draft slipped through the cracks, carrying the faint rustle of unseen movement; the shadows near the firepit seemed to tilt, as though some hidden eyes were tracking the imprint. Harriet tightened her grip on Clifford’s coat, her shoulders rigid, while the salesman’s smile faltered under the weight of the note still hidden inside his coat pocket. Lucy’s revolver rested cool in her palm, the metal steady against her skin, and she listened to the low murmur of the town’s restless heart beating beyond the walls. The quiet held a promise that someone had slipped away unnoticed, and that the watchful darkness would not let the secret disappear.
