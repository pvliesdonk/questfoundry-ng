Thea tightened the collar of her white coat as the tavern door slammed shut behind her. She slipped onto the low bench beside the stone hearth, the wood beneath her spine groaning faintly. The scent of burnt coffee mingled with the metallic tang of iron from the forge behind the bar. Lucy stood behind the counter, her shaved head catching the lantern’s glow, a fresh scar darkening the skin of her right palm. The look in Lucy’s eyes was cold, a thin layer of frost hardening the space between them. She pressed a folded scrap of paper against the bar and slid it toward Thea, the edge flashing briefly before the note rested on the tabletop. The paper felt like a blade against Thea’s palm, a quiet warning humming low amid the clatter of mugs. The ink was hurried, the line she could read: *Listen. Eavesdroppers sit in the dark.* A shiver ran down Thea’s ribs, and she glanced toward the rows of empty booths, the shadows holding whatever ears might be listening. Harriet’s notebook lay open on a nearby stool, the margin filled with a trembling question. Thea’s breath steadied; she folded the warning back, the grain of the paper cold under her fingers, and kept her gaze on the dim corners while the tavern’s murmur rose and fell.
