Edgar Barnes felt the clang of the diner's iron doors as they slammed shut, the cold wind slipping through the cracked stone walls. He saw Harriet Wellman stride forward, her trench coat sweeping the floor, the badge on her chest catching the lantern's glow. With a practiced motion she produced a pair of iron handcuffs and snapped them onto his wrists, the cold metal biting into his skin before the eyes of the packed room. The crowd muttered, some hissed his name, others whispered murder, and he heard his own scar on his cheek throb under the pressure of the cuff. He tried to protest, his voice thin, but the clatter of metal overrode his words.

Lucy Frost grabbed a steel wrench from the bar and flung it toward the metal box humming on the floor. She pried at the device, the red wire glowing like ember, and a spark leapt as she pulled it free. A shallow cut opened on her forearm; she wrapped a scrap of cloth around the wound, blood darkening the soot‑blackened floor. She worked methodically, disconnecting the fuse piece by piece, the heat of the charge scorching the air. The ticking accelerated, then fell to a quiet hiss as the final wire snapped.

Thea Swan slipped a folded note into Harriet's palm while the clamor faded. Edgar watched Thea's hand move, the paper sliding smooth and urgent. Harriet unfolded it, her brow tightening as she read the terse warning about danger beyond the arrest. A flash of unease crossed her face, the crowd's fury roaring louder around them.

Beyond the commotion, a distant gunshot cracked over the rooftops, a stark reminder that violence lingered outside the diner's walls. Edgar glanced at his own desk, a battered wooden surface near the back of the room. An ornamental ivory‑handled knife lay beside a stack of ledgers, its polished surface catching the lantern light. His eyes then fell to a loose floorboard near the wall; a faint edge hinted at something concealed beneath it, a dark shape that might hold another blade.

The room settled into a brittle quiet, the echo of the gunshot ringing in his ears as Harriet tightened the handcuffs on his wrists, their metal clatter resonating like a verdict. Lucy steadied the wrench in her hand, the cut on her forearm still throbbing, while Thea retreated to the kitchen, the note clutched tightly. Edgar's mind raced with the images of the knife on his desk, the hidden floorboard, and the warning note that now weighed heavily on Harriet's shoulders.
