The empty sheath lay on the floor as Clifford watched the interrogation room doors slam shut. The metal door's clang echoed off the stone walls, a reminder of the missing ivory‑handled knife. A guard stepped forward, lifted Clifford's arm, and led him out. He felt the cool air of the corridor brush his face as other detainees were escorted back in. Police officers clustered around a table where a nervous suspect answered questions, their voices low and clipped. Clifford lingered near the doorway, his eyes flicking from the suspect to the notebook the sergeant held. A thin ribbon of steam rose from a pot of coffee being carried by Marjorie Lynch, who moved between the benches of the precinct lounge. She set a tin cup on the steel tray and glanced toward Clifford, a smile briefly catching the dim light. As she turned to pour another cup, Clifford slipped a small, tightly‑wrapped package into the inner pocket of his charcoal coat. Marjorie's gaze followed the motion, her eyebrows raising just enough for him to notice. She nodded once, then continued to serve the coffee without comment. Through the cracked doorway, the iron hatch that led to the rain‑soaked midnight tunnels beneath the Old Mill loomed in the shadows, its rusted teeth barely visible. Clifford inhaled the faint scent of coffee and metal, aware that the next step would carry him deeper into the town’s hidden veins.
