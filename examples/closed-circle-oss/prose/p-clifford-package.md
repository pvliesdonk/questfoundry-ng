The empty sheath lay on the polished floor as the heavy oak doors of the interrogation room slammed shut, the clang echoing down the stone hallway. Clifford Moon stood at the threshold, his eyes on the doors as they closed. A guard lifted the iron latch on his chair and gestured him to stand. Clifford rose, his heart thudding, and slipped out into the precinct’s waning light. He lingered near the doorway where detectives pressed two suspects, their voices low and the recorder's click sharp. Clifford watched the questioning with a nervous glance, his breath shallow. Across the room, Marjorie Lynch moved behind the diner counter that had been set up as a temporary waiting area, flour still dusting her apron. She poured steaming coffee into tin cups for the officers, the steam curling up. As Clifford turned back toward the doorway, she caught the faint rustle of fabric and saw a small, tightly‑wrapped bundle disappear into the inner pocket of his coat. Her eyes widened for a heartbeat before she steadied herself. Clifford pressed his lips together and muttered the word leeches, the syllable lost in the clatter.
