Edgar kept his boots steady on the stone floor as the lantern sputtered. Harriet stepped toward the altar, the ivory‑handled knife catching a thin strip of light. The blade was slick with fresh blood. She lowered her voice, muttering, “A trap… all this blood is a set‑up.” The words fell into the cold air, and Edgar felt a shiver of doubt settle over the scene.

A faint scrape cut the hush, the sound of a boot striking grit‑caked stone. A shadow slipped past the altar and hurried away, kicking up a thin cloud of dust. The figure's footsteps traced a ragged line across the floor, each imprint darkening the stone as it led toward the stairwell. The trail vanished where the darkness deepened.

Edgar's gaze followed the path, then shifted to the knife. He reached out, the metal biting his palm, cold steel against his skin, the blood staining the flesh of his hand. The weight of the weapon matched the weight of his badge at his chest. He tightened his grip, feeling the question the blade posed, the accusation whispered moments before still lingering in the stone walls. Behind him, the dust‑caked footprints glimmered faintly, a silent trail that promised pursuit. The air smelled of iron and old stone, pressing against his lungs.
