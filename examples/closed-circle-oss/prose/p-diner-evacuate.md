Lucy Frost raised her voice and pulled the alarm lever, the metal catching the dim light of the flickering fluorescent tubes. Her scarred palm throbbed where the fresh dark mark lay, a reminder of the fight that had brought her here. She stepped to the counter and pointed toward the swinging door, yelling for everyone to leave. The patrons jerked upright, their eyes wide, and she guided them one by one, her hand brushing shoulders to keep the line moving.

The door swung open onto the night‑blackened street, rain slicking the cobblestones. The crowd poured out, breathless but unharmed, their coats scraping the cracked stone as they fell into the cold air. Lucy kept her gaze ahead, pointing toward the dim lane and urging anyone who lingered to move. The clatter of hurried footsteps mixed with the low, steady tick that seemed to come from somewhere deeper in the diner.

Clifford Moon slipped into the alley behind the kitchen and melted into the night’s darkness, his coat disappearing like a shadow. Near the doorway an ornate knife lay half‑buried in the wet stone, its ivory handle dulled by grime. Edgar Barnes lingered at the edge of the crowd, muttering a cryptic rhyme about a clock whose hands never stopped. Thea Swan darted across the street, a small vial of serum clenched in her gloved hand, her eyes fixed on the building as if the liquid might silence the unseen pulse inside. Lucy stood between the frightened and the fleeing, the alarm still echoing, the night holding its breath.
