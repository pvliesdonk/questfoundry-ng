Lucy Frost stood behind the bar, the cold steel wrench still warm from the blast. The ivory-handled knife lay on the stone slab beside the shattered bomb, its polished surface catching the lantern’s sputtering light. A thin ribbon of fresh red pooled on the handle, the crimson smear a mute record of the violence that had just unfolded. She watched the droplets bead and run down the smooth curve, each one reflecting a moment of terror that lingered in the air. The diner’s walls still smelled of ozone and iron, the cracked oak floor stained where shrapnel had struck. Harriet Wellman’s badge glinted across the room, her scar catching the erratic glow as she surveyed the aftermath without drawing a weapon. Lucy pressed a clean hand to the wound on her forearm, feeling the sting of copper‑tinged blood without a word. She turned her gaze back to the knife, noting the missing sheath that hinted at another weapon’s presence. The metal latch beneath the ash‑stained table was ajar, its dry click echoing in the now‑quiet space. A narrow passage yawned dark beyond the slab, the doorway swallowing the faint light. Lucy’s eyes narrowed as she considered the heft of the blade, the weight of its silent testimony pressing against the pulse of the room.
