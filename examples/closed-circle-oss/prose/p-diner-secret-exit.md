The latch beneath the cracked oak gave a dry click, a sound that seemed to swallow the low hum left by the defused bomb. Lucy felt the cold metal under her fingertip, the grain of the table slick with ash. She pressed the latch down further, and a narrow gap in the floor opened, a black throat yawning beneath the diner's worn boards. A thin draft slipped out, carrying the scent of damp stone and old oil. 

Lucy stepped onto the edge of the opening, her boots echoing against the iron‑rimmed rim. The passage beyond narrowed, its walls of rough stone closing like the jaws of a hidden beast. She glanced back at Harriet, whose lantern threw a steady circle of light over the floor and reflected off the scar on her cheek. Lucy noted the blood‑stained ivory knife still lying where she had set it, its dark smear catching the lantern glow. She drew a breath, the air colder than the night wind outside, and slipped into the shadowed tunnel. The darkness swallowed her shape, and the only sound was the faint clink of a distant gear turning somewhere deep beneath the mill, a reminder that unseen exits lingered in the chaos.
