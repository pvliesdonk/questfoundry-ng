A solitary drip echoed off the stone walls, each plink suggesting a concealed tunnel where darkness gathered, tightening the knot of dread. Edgar pressed his badge tighter against his chest and let the sound guide his eyes toward the narrow fissure in the far stone. The fissure widened just enough to admit a thin breath of stale air, the scent of damp earth mingling with the faint aroma of cooked broth from somewhere beyond. He recognized that faint scent as the kitchen of Marjorie's diner, the doorway faintly lit by a lantern swinging in a distant hallway. He stepped closer, his boots echoing on the slick flagstone, and traced the edge of a rusted iron latch that sealed the hidden passage. The latch gave with a muted clang, revealing a darkness that seemed to pulse with the rhythm of the dripping water. He lowered his lantern, the flame trembling, and saw the tunnel descend a few feet before curving upward toward a narrow stairwell. The stairwell opened into a dim corridor whose far end glowed with the warm light of Marjorie's kitchen. He heard the low murmur of patrons and the clink of mugs beyond the stone. The drip fell again, steady, as if urging him onward. He felt the weight of the decision settle in his palm, the cold metal of his revolver a reminder of the danger that waited beyond the next turn.
