Harriet stood beneath the broken stone lintel of the old mill, the air thick with the scent of rust and cold river mist. The lantern she held cast a thin circle of light on the splintered floorboards, where Edgar Barnes leaned against a beam, his spectacles catching the glow. He spoke in low cadence, the words slipping like ash between his teeth.\n\n\"A ledger lost, its ink still burns, beneath the heart where gears turn dark.\" he said, his voice a thin thread. Harriet watched his thin fingers trace the edge of the rusted iron hatch, the same hatch that led to the mill’s forgotten cellar. The riddle settled on her mind like a cold knot, tightening around the scar on her cheek. She sensed the ledger’s secrets, the whispered debts that might yet be consumed in the furnace’s fire.\n\nShe glanced at the battered journal pressed against a crate, its leather cover frayed from use. The pages inside were filled with her notes on the protest arrests and the ledger’s missing ledger, each line a thin line of ink against the growing darkness. A shiver ran through her, not from the wind but from the implication that the ledger could be burning somewhere beneath the stone.\n\nHarriet tightened the strap of her badge, the metal pressing firm against her chest, and lifted her revolver to rest against the lantern’s base. The mill’s gears moaned in the distance, a reminder that time waited for no secret. She breathed in the cold and felt the weight of the choice ahead, the journal’s pages waiting like cold ash in her palm.
