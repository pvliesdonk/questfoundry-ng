Thea kept her coat buttoned, the badge cold against the lapel, and watched the tavern’s dim light sweep the worn floor. Edgar’s low rhyme drifted on after he sat, the riddling tone lingering like a thin fog that wrapped her anxiety in a veil of puzzling omen. Lucy slipped a folded scrap of paper onto the table beside her cup; the thin edge flickered briefly in the lantern’s glow before melting back into darkness. The paper brushed her palm, warm from Lucy’s hand, and she tucked it beneath the cup without a word. Across the row of booths the lantern’s flame carved bars of darkness, a shifting pattern of shadows that moved with each sputter. Thea let her eyes follow the wavering lines, the muted rhythm of light marking a silent warning. Her heartbeat steadied, the clatter of mugs fading to a distant hum as she tightened the grip on her coat. She noted the quiet tension in the room and felt the riddle’s echo pulse through the air, a reminder that something unseen watched from the corners. With the pattern of shadows traced in her mind, she prepared to stay awake through the night, the secret she guarded humming beneath the tavern’s walls.
