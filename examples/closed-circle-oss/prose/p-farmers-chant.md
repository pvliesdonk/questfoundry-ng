Lucy stood beside the rusted firepit behind town hall, the iron ribs blackened by years of soot. Thea Swan's bobbed hair caught the orange flare, and Thea's eyes lingered on the fire, watching the tongue of flame dance, just before a distant chant began to rise from the square. The farmer union gathered on the stone plaza, voices rising in unison as they demanded fair rates for their harvests. Lucy heard the rhythm swell, a steady pulse that rolled across the cobbles. At the edge of the crowd Harriet Wellman kept her trench coat tight, her scar catching the firelight as she surveyed the singers from the perimeter, noting the widening rift between the union and the town's authority. Lucy lifted a crystal glass from a nearby table, raised it, and slammed it against the iron rim of the pit; the shatter cracked the night and sent a tremor through the assembled bodies. The tremor rolled toward the half‑burned Blackwood Ledger that lay among the ash, its pages blackening as the flames licked their edges. Lucy fixed her gaze on the ledger, eyes narrowed as the fire seemed to engulf the paper, possibly turning the ink to cinder. Grey ash curled over the ledger, drifting upward like cold snow against the night sky. Harriet's hand rested on the butt of her revolver, jaw set, while Lucy felt the weight of the moment settle around the pit.
