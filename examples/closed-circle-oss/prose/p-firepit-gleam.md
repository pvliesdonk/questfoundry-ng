Lucy gestured toward the rusted firepit behind the town hall, its iron ribs blackened by years of soot. She told Thea that the pit could turn a stack of papers to ash in a heartbeat, that any ledger could vanish there without a trace. Lucy pointed at the orange glow licking the stone and Thea followed her line of sight, her shoulders stiff in the dim light. The fire danced, a thin tongue of flame that curled up the side of the pit, and Thea’s eyes narrowed as the heat brushed her cheek. A low chant rose from the square, the farmer union’s rhythm rolling over the bricks and mingling with the crackle. Thea caught a glimpse of the flickering light and a shadow crossed her face, a stale memory of a scandal that had once burned hotter than any fire. The recollection settled in the set of her jaw, and she tightened the grip on her coat as if holding the past itself.
