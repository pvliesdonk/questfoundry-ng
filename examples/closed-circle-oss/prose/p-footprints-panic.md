Dust swirled around fresh footprints, imprinting a frantic trail that made the cellar pulse with panic.

Edgar Barnes followed the slender line of boots past the stone altar where the ivory‑handled knife lay, its edge still slick with blood.

The ticking device beside a cracked pipe clicked steadily, each beat echoing off the low ceiling.

A droplet fell from the pipe, hit the dusty floor and vanished into the darkness beyond the steps.

He noted the ledger on the low shelf, pages yellowed, ink faint, a silent witness to older secrets.

Lantern light flared on the knife’s edge, a brief flash across the stone.

Thea Swan lingered at the iron door, breath catching as she watched the trail.

Lucy Frost stood near the battered table, revolver at her hip, whispering that the knife had disappeared from the evidence locker.

Edgar kept his badge pressed to his chest, hand resting on the revolver, and measured the distance between the footprints and the humming bomb.

The bomb’s click grew louder, matching his heartbeat.

With a sudden surge he turned, rushed through the cellar door and burst into Marjorie's diner, where the ticking bomb rang in the still air.

The diner's low ceiling pressed against the sound, and the crowd fell silent as the metal clang of the timer filled the room.
