Clifford stood on the cracked stone slab of the old mill, the river's chill biting his boots. He watched Lucy Frost move along the rim of the building, her shaved head catching the lantern's weak glow. Lucy paused, eyes narrowing as she spotted a trail of strange footprints leading toward a concealed cellar door near the mill. The prints vanished at a rusted iron latch half swallowed by ivy, the latch guarding a hidden entrance. Clifford felt the cold promise of the metal in the air. Lucy lifted the lantern and pointed at the latch, then she turned toward the doorway where Mayor Caldwell stepped forward from the shadowed hall. Mayor Caldwell adjusted his gold‑tied coat and addressed Harriet Wellman, badge pressed to her chest. "Rumors can set the town ablaze," he said. "Stay cautious and keep to what you know." Harriet's scar caught the lantern light as she nodded without a word. Clifford kept his stare on the concealed passage, the latch still cold to the touch. Clifford reached out, pulled the rusted bolt, and slipped into the narrow cellar stairs, the stone walls closing around him. The lantern flickered, casting a thin line of light over the chipping floor. The scent of damp stone rose as Clifford descended. Clifford paused at the bottom, then he climbed back onto the slab, his boots cracking the frost‑hard ground. Clifford turned his gaze toward the dark line of trees that rose beyond the mill and he began to scout the forest, his eyes searching for any other clue the night might hide. The mayor's warning lingered in Clifford's mind as the wind whispered through the timbers. Clifford weighed the two routes before him: the cold cellar depth and the silent woods beyond.
