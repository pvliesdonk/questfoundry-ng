Lucy Frost rested her forearm on the scarred bar, the amber whiskey catching the dim lantern light. She set the glass before Marjorie Lynch, the ceramic warm against the wooden counter. Marjorie's flour‑dusted apron swayed as she lifted the drink, a brief smile breaking the tension. Lucy lowered her voice to a murmur and told Marjorie that the Blackwood ledger bound Mayor Caldwell to the Farmer Union, a fact hidden in the safe behind the mill’s rusted machines. The words settled between them like cold steel, and Lucy watched the mayor’s polished image flicker in Marjorie's eyes.

Harriet Wellman stood near the stone slab, her badge pressed to her chest. She glanced at the ornate knife resting beside the table, the ivory handle polished to a bright sheen. The blade captured a thin slice of lantern glow, a silent accusation that seemed to point toward unseen danger. Lucy noted the gesture, feeling the weight of the hidden weapon as if it might be used later.

The swinging door behind the counter creaked as the last patron stepped out, the night wind slipping through the gap. Lucy kept the whiskey within reach, the glass trembling slightly. She considered the ledger’s secret and the knife’s glint, both heavy on her mind as the diner settled into quiet. The choice lay before her: follow the whisper of the ledger toward the doorway, or tend the uneasy glow of the knife.
