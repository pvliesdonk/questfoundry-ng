A thin frost clung to Grayhaven's streets, turning the cobblestones to glass. Harriet Wellman walked the market square, the scar on her cheek catching the dim light as she surveyed the muted crowd. The ledger's absence fell like a hollow echo in every whisper that drifted between the stalls. Vendors muttered about debts that could no longer be traced, and the farmers' chants faded into a distant murmur. Harriet felt the weight of the deputy's badge pressing against her chest, a reminder of the order she was sworn to keep. A group of union banners drooped, their cloth stiff with ice, while a few townsfolk huddled near the furnace grate for warmth. Harriet's eyes tracked a farmer clutching a rusted pitchfork, his jaw set against the cold. She sensed the tension coiling in the air, like gears waiting for a lever. The council's decree to burn the Blackwood ledger had already scattered power, and now the question of force lingered between her gloved fingers. Harriet's hand brushed the holster at her hip, the revolver's metal cold against the leather. She watched a patrol officer ready a set of cuffs on a nearby table. The choice to summon those restraints or to hold back lay heavy on her, as the frost continued to bite the night.
