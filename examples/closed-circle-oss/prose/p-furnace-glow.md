Lucy noted the coal‑fed furnace set behind the mill’s iron beams, a hulking mouth that could turn a stack of paper to ash in moments. She kept her hand on the worn strap of her revolver as she spoke, the words hanging in the thick air. With a deliberate motion she stretched her arm toward the furnace’s orange flare, the light cutting across the vaulted stone floor. Thea Swan stood a few steps away, her coat rustling softly, eyes locked on the dancing tongues of fire. Thea’s gaze lingered, and for a heartbeat the flames seemed to echo a memory she tried to keep hidden. The heat brushed her cheek, and Lucy saw the flicker of something old and dangerous stir behind Thea’s steady stare. It was the heat of a scandal that had once burned bright in the town’s gossip, a scandal that had driven Thea here from the city. Thea’s shoulders tightened as the memory rose, the glow reflecting in her eyes like a warning. The furnace continued its low roar, its iron ribs absorbing the coal and spitting out heat that made the surrounding shadows quiver. A narrow stone stair descended beside the furnace, its entrance bathed in the same amber light, leading down into the basement where the fire’s reach seemed endless. Lucy felt the weight of the decision pressing heavier than the revolver at her hip, the furnace’s appetite for paper a stark reminder of what could be erased in an instant.
