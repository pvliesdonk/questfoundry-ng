Lucy stepped nearer to the coal‑fed furnace. The iron beams above caught the orange flare, throwing thin bands of light across the vaulted stone. The furnace hissed louder, and the heat hummed like a warning, a low vibration that settled in her ribs as if the blaze were listening. She glanced at the ledger lying on the rough table, its ink still stark against the soot‑blackened surface. Thea Swan stood a few paces away, her shoulders rigid, eyes fixed on the dancing flames. Lucy's revolver rested against the seam of her coat, the metal cool, a small weight that marked the border she walked between law and violence. A thin plume of coal smoke curled upward, staining the air with acrid scent. The furnace's orange glow pressed against her skin, and the metallic dread of its presence seemed to watch every movement. Outside, the winter wind rattled the mill's shutters, but inside the furnace's roar matched the town's restless nerves. Lucy inhaled the sulfur taste, feeling the pulse of the fire echo her own heartbeat. She tightened her grip on the revolver, aware that the blaze could devour the ledger if she chose, yet the heat's steady hum warned that it awaited a decision.
