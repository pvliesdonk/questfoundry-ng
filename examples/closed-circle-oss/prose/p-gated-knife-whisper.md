Marjorie leaned over the cold stone altar, the flour‑dusted apron brushing the worn slab. Beside the dim candle lay the Blackwood ledger, its pages fanned open; the line where the ornate ivory‑handled knife should have been entered was blank. As she stared, a low murmur rose from the vaulted beams, the same half‑heard warning that had once traveled the diner counter when patrons whispered of the missing knife. The altar shivered, a faint vibration through the stone, as if the whisper pressed against the walls. Marjorie's palm rested on the trembling stone, and she felt, in the hollow beneath, a faint echo like turning iron gears—a reminder that the ledger's secrets lay concealed where only the old mill's rusted mechanisms could reach. The memory of that warning settled over the hall like cold steel, tightening the knot of dread she carried from her diner. She tightened her grip on the ledger, noting that the omission tied the missing knife to the hidden record, a link sealed in silence. The silence lingered, heavy as ash, while the stone continued its low tremor, urging her onward.
