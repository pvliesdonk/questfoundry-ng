Edgar watched Thea Swan lift a thin leather notebook from the pocket of her coat. She uncapped a tiny vial, its dark ink catching the lantern light for a heartbeat before the flame waned. She pressed the quill to the page, and the line that appeared glittered then dissolved as the light dimmed. The ink would remain invisible unless the paper stayed in bright fire, a secret the nurse could carry without fear. From the corner near the cracked window, Silas "Old Man" Well stepped forward, his voice rasping low. He whispered that a nurse scandal once stained the infirmary’s records, a rumor sealed in the town archives like dust on a closed ledger. Edgar caught the words, felt the weight of his revolver press against his thigh, and noted the shadows gathering where the lantern failed. The ticking from the community hall pulsed through stone walls, each beat a thin hammer against Edgar’s ears. He forced a calm expression, breathing even though his gut tightened. The shadows clung to the stone, offering a dark veil. He could step forward and read Thea’s hidden entry aloud, exposing the scandal to the assembled townsfolk, or he could linger in the gloom, letting the secret stay buried. His hand rested on the grip, the metal cold, reminding him of the choice ahead.
