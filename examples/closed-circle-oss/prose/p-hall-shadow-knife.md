Edgar watched a dark shape dissolve into the hall’s gloom, the figure’s boots raising thin clouds of dust that settled in a crooked line across the stone. The footprints thickened as the shadow hurried toward the stairwell, then stopped where the lantern’s light fell short, the trail vanishing into deeper darkness. Harriet moved to the slab where the ivory‑handled knife lay, its edge still fresh with blood. She knelt, her trench coat rustling, and lifted the blade, noting the absent sheath that hinted at a second weapon. She turned the blood‑slick weapon toward the oak desk, slipped it into the metal evidence locker, and twisted the lock with a firm hand. Thea stood nearby, her coat buttoned tight, eyes fixed on the closing metal doors, while Lucy lingered at the room’s edge, her muscular frame casting a wary glance over the gathered townsfolk. Beyond the hall, an iron‑framed door marked the entrance to the infirmary, its brass knob cold under the lantern’s glow. Edgar slipped quietly through the doorway, keeping his movements low, and pressed the scar on his wrist beneath a rolled sleeve. He felt the faint scent of antiseptic mix with the iron tang of the ticking device that throbbed in the hall beyond. The bomb’s pulse grew louder in his ears, each tick echoing off the stone walls, as he vanished into the dim infirmary.
