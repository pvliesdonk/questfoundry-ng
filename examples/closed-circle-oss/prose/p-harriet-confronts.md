Edgar Barnes felt the chill of the diner's low ceiling as the ticking grew louder. Marjorie Lynch's voice rose, pleading for the doors to stay open, and the words lingered over the clatter of plates. Harriet Wellman stepped ahead, the metal of her badge catching the sputtering lamp light beside Lucy Frost’s shaking hands. The deputy’s scarred cheek set in a hard line as she scanned the room. She fixed Lucy with a steady stare and asked, "What troubles the patrons?" Edgar noted the accusation hanging in the stale air. Lucy's mouth moved quickly, her frantic report spilling details about a concealed device beneath the counter. Harriet leaned forward, interrupting, "What exactly happened?" as Thea Swan burst in, a tray clenched tight in her grip, and set it down with a sharp clink on the nearest table. Edgar watched Thea's bobbed hair flicker in the lamplight and saw a stout iron door in the far corner, its hinges dark with rust and opening onto a cellar. Thea glanced toward it, lingering as if waiting for a cue. The ticking sounded colder now, echoing off the cracked pine walls. Harriet turned back to Lucy, demanding more specifics, while her hand rested on the badge pressed to her chest. Edgar sensed the rise of tension settle on his shoulders like a weight. The cellar doorway waited, silent and dark, a path that Thea seemed ready to take.
