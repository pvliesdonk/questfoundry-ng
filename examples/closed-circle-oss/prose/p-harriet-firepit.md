The firepit's iron ribs glowed dull against the night as Harriet stood beside it. The Blackwood Ledger lay on a stone slab, its leather cover blackened by soot, and thin curls of ash rose from its edge, drifting upward. She watched the ash unfurl, each wisp catching firelight before vanishing. Edgar Barnes stood a few paces away, his thin frame hunched over the ledger, spectacles reflecting the flame. He muttered a rhyme, his voice low: “Unpaid favors sit like stones beneath the river’s mouth, waiting for the tide to claim them.” The words slipped into the crackle of the fire, and Harriet felt the cadence settle like a weight in the night air. The scar on her cheek tightened as a wind brushed the plaza, carrying the low hum of the farmer union’s chant from the square. Her grip tightened on the revolver’s wooden stock, the metal barrel cool against her palm. A fresh mud‑streaked bootprint marked the stone, pointing toward the narrow arch that opened onto the diner district. Ash stained the cuff of her coat, clinging like a thin veil. The ledger’s pages curled further, half consumed, and a lone spark flared, briefly illuminating the space where answers might have lingered. She noted the glow as it revealed a faint imprint of a missing ivory‑handled knife beside the ledger. The scene held the town’s secrets in the smoldering air, waiting for a decision.
