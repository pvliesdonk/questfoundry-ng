Harriet sat at the precinct desk, leather notebook open, pen poised. Thea Swan stood near the doorway, hand clenched around the strap of her coat. Harriet's eyes followed the tension in the fingers, the knuckles white against the ragged wool. She pressed the leather tighter around the pages, feeling the notebook bite into her palm. She wrote a line, the ink catching the dim light, describing the hand and the tremor that rose up Thea's forearm. The words were brief: “Clenched hand, quiet pulse of suspicion.” She noted the clock on the wall ticking, each second a reminder of the night ahead. The room hummed with low conversation, but Harriet's focus stayed on the nurse's rigid posture. A thin breath escaped Thea, and Harriet marked the moment with a slash of ink. She folded the notebook shut, the leather snapping shut like a small gate, and placed it beside the brass lantern on the desk. Her brass pocket watch lay open, its hands frozen at midnight. The secret that Thea carried sat hidden beneath the nurse's calm exterior, a weight Harriet now watched.
