Mayor Caldwell felt the stone floor beneath his boots vibrate as the furnace in the back room rumbled. The brass‑bound safe stood against the far wall, its iron latch catching the dim lantern light. Harriet lingered nearby, her cuffed hands resting on the metal rail, eyes sweeping the diner's few remaining patrons. Marjorie kept her back to the furnace, flour dusting her apron, and leaned toward the safe where a thin pane of glass revealed the Blackwood Ledger sealed inside. She lowered her voice so it rose no higher than the hiss of the fire. “The ledger records your hidden debts, Mayor,” she whispered, each word tasting sour on the stale air. Caldwell caught the strain in her tone and felt a cold knot settle in his gut. He turned his head toward Harriet, a tight grin spreading across his face as he clasped her forearm and thanked her for maintaining order. The grin tightened, a flash of triumph that masked the growing dread curling like smoke around the room. He watched the ledger remain locked, its secrets waiting for a spark that might one day ignite the town’s fragile peace. The furnace’s glow threw long shadows on the cracked pine floor, and Caldwell sensed the weight of the unspoken threat pressing against his polished exterior.
