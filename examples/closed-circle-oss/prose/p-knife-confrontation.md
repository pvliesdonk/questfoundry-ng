Lucy steadied her grip on the revolver as the rain hammered the tin roof of the diner district. Mayor Caldwell lifted the Blackwood ledger and turned the pages, his eyes lingering on the red ink that marked debts. Harriet Wellman stepped forward from the trench coat, her scar flashing briefly as she crossed the threshold toward Clifford. She halted beside him, the orange glow of the firepit behind town hall casting long shadows across the floor. Harriet's voice cut through the clatter of plates, demanding the truth about the ivory-handled knife. Clifford shifted his weight, the coat rustling, and met her stare without flinching. He brushed a thumb over the polished blade hidden beneath his jacket, the ivory catching a glint of lantern light. Harriet's press, accusing him of the murder that had silenced the council clerk, and her tone grew harsher as she cited the missing ledger. Mayor Caldwell continued to scan the ledger, his thumb tracing a line of his own name, and a scowl hardened his jaw. The mayor's gaze flicked up at Harriet and Clifford, the tension in the room tightening like a coil. Harriet's hand tightened on her own coat, the scar on her cheek trembling with restrained fury. Clifford answered with a cool denial, his words sliding over the surface of the conversation as smoothly as the knife's polished edge. The diner murmured around them, but the air felt like iron, each breath heavy with unspoken threat. Lucy watched the exchange, the weight of the revolver steady against her palm, the firepit's orange blaze licking the stone and promising heat that might turn the ledger to ash.
