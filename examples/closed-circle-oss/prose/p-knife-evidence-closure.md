Clifford stood near the iron door of the evidence room as the furnace hissed in the distance. Silas's hushed directions echoed off the stone, urging Harriet to secure the knife. She reached for the ivory‑handled blade, lifted it from the tray, and slipped it into her gloved hand. Harriet then sealed the weapon inside the brass‑faced locker, the metal latch clanking shut.

She pressed the blade onto the tray and ran a thumb over the polished hilt, leaving a fresh imprint that caught the light. Clifford watched her record the fingerprint in the ledger with a quick stroke of ink.

Harriet crouched, examined the space where a sheath should have rested, and found only cold stone. The missing leather scabbard suggested another weapon had been carried away, a second blade hidden from the town's eyes. A thin draft slipped through the locked door, carrying the scent of damp timber from beyond. Clifford turned toward the east wall, where a shallow arch revealed a tunnel whose floor gleamed with early morning light. He stepped into the passage, the air cool against his coat, and felt the stone underfoot give way to the faint echo of turning gears. The tunnel stretched ahead, its walls lined with pale dust, inviting him deeper toward the old mill's hidden heart.
