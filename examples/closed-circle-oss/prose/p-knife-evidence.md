The evidence locker gleamed beneath the dim lantern, its steel frame cold as the night wind that slipped through the cracked pine of the adjoining hall. A rusted hatch set into the far wall marked the mouth of the rain‑soaked tunnels beneath the Old Mill, its iron teeth barely visible in the weak light. Silas Well leaned close, his voice a hushed rasp that carried just far enough for Clifford to catch the instruction. "Take the knife," he whispered, and Harriet Wellman moved forward, hands steady as the steel beneath her palm. Clifford watched her receive the ornate ivory‑handled knife, the blade catching a flash of lantern light. She slipped the weapon into the evidence locker and, as she closed the heavy door, her fingers lingered over the surface, leaving fresh fingerprints that glistened faintly on the metal. The knife rested there, its polished handle bright against the dark interior, but the sheath that should have cradled it was gone. The absence of the sheath spoke plainly: another weapon had been used, its trace now a missing piece of the puzzle. The air in the room seemed to tighten around the empty space where the sheath should have been, and Clifford felt the weight of the implication settle like iron on his chest. He glanced once more at the hatch, the tunnels yawning dark and wet beyond, waiting for a step into their shadowed depths.
