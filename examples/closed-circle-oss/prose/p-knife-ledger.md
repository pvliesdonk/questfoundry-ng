Lucy Frost stood just inside the back‑room doorway, the ticking of the bomb a thin metronome against the rain‑laced walls. The ivory‑handled knife lay slack in a pool of dust near the threshold, its bright curve catching the flicker of the squad’s radios. The knife’s cold glint flashed each time the radios sputtered, a brief flash of steel in the gloom. From the shadowed hallway Edgar Barnes emerged, thin shoulders hunched beneath a threadbare coat, spectacles rimmed with grime. He muttered low, his voice threading through the stale air, speaking of a ledger whose names carried a curse, the words leaving a faint scent of old debts and hidden secrets. Harriet Wellman moved to the doorway, badge pressed to her chest, revolver settled at her hip. She lifted a worn notebook from her coat pocket, the pen scratching a quick line as she recorded the night’s dread, noting the bomb, the muttering teacher, and the glinting knife. Her eyes flicked between the weapon and the battered radio, the scene tightening around her. The radios continued their erratic pulse, the rain drummed the roof, and Lucy felt the weight of each choice pressing forward. She could feel the town’s future balanced on that thin line of metal and ink.
