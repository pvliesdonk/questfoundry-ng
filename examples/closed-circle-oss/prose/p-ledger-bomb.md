Mayor Caldwell moved from the stone platform toward the furnace, the winter draft slipping through his coat as the crowd's chant rose from beneath the arches. The Blackwood Ledger lay on the iron slab beside the glowing embers, its brass clasp still faintly warm from the recent fire. Lucy Frost stood nearby, her shaved scalp catching the flicker of lanterns, and Caldwell watched her stare at the bound cover without moving a hand. A subtle vibration passed from the ledger through the metal, a faint rhythm that seemed to count down in the hush between verses. Caldwell felt the tremor travel up his forearm, and the sensation convinced him that the book behaved like a concealed explosive. He noted Lucy's eyes lingered, a tight focus that hinted at a restless curiosity despite the danger surrounding the object. The crowd's chant swelled then fell, and the sound of gears from Old Man Well’s workshop drifted faintly, matching the pulse he sensed in the ledger. A thin murmur rose from the pages themselves, a coiling whisper that seemed to curl around the metal and press against the back of his throat. Caldwell tightened the strap of his coat, aware that any abrupt motion might trigger the hidden tension within the ledger's covers. He steadied his breath, weighing whether to follow the whispering hint or to keep the ledger sealed until the council could decide its fate.
