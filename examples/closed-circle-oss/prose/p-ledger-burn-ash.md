Mayor Caldwell stood beneath the iron arches of the town hall basement, the furnace humming like a dormant beast. The Blackwood Ledger rested on the cold slab, its ink darkened by years of debt. He felt the weight of the townsfolk's expectations as heavy as the iron bolts above his head.

He lifted the ledger, its leather cover cracked, and spoke with a tone that left no room for objection. "Throw it into the fire," he ordered the furnace keeper, his voice steady despite the tremor that rose in his throat. The keeper obeyed, sliding the heavy book onto the coal bed.

Flames licked the pages, turning parchment to flame in a swift, merciless bloom. Black ink curled and blackened, then fell away as fine ash that drifted upward on the rising heat. Caldwell watched as the secrets of the Blackwood families rose with the smoke, disappearing into the furnace's throat.

When the fire dwindled, a thin layer of ash settled on the stone floor, the ledger’s last embers sputtering out. From the doorway, the Blackwood family emerged, their faces hard as the cracked pine beams, eyes fixed on Caldwell with a cold stare that seemed to carve the air. Near the furnace, a stone hatch set into the mill’s foundation yawned, its rusted iron latch catching the dim light.

He could listen as the Blackwood family accused him, or he could push open the stone hatch beneath the mill.
