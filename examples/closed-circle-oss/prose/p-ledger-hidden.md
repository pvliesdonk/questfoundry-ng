Mayor Caldwell entered the clerk’s vault, the iron door thudding shut behind him. He surveyed the metal tray where the Blackwood Ledger rested, its edges still warm from the furnace glow. He felt the weight of debts and blackmail settle under his palm and decided to conceal the ledger in the hidden safe that his aide had installed behind a false panel, preserving its contents. He lifted the ledger from the tray, slid it into the narrow cavity of the safe, and turned the bolt, hearing the click of the lock echo faintly off the stone walls. Harriet Wellman stepped forward, her badge catching the weak lantern light, and narrowed her eyes, as if questioning Caldwell’s motives. Lucy Frost stood near the furnace, her revolver at her hip, and fixed her gaze on the sealed ledger within the safe, the iron door reflecting the dim flame. The farmer union’s chants rose beyond the stone arches, a steady clamor demanding transparency. From the cracked archway Caldwell caught sight of the Old Mill’s silhouette, a lantern swinging in its broken window, and a brief glint of an ornate knife caught in a beam of light. A hooded figure slipped into the mill’s side door, disappearing into darkness as the lantern flickered. Caldwell watched the scene, the ledger sealed behind iron, while the town’s voices grew louder, pressing against the calm he had tried to preserve.
