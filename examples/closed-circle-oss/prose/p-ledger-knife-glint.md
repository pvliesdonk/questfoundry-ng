Harriet pressed the metal of her badge into the hollow of her chest and kept her gaze on the stone bridge railing. Old Man Well shuffled forward, his voice a low rasp that curled around the clatter of the diner. He muttered, "Ledger... Blackwood... gunshot...", and the fragment lodged in her mind, a thread that tied the hidden account to the crack of the distant shot. The sound of the shot rolled again, a thin echo that bounced off the canal walls and faded into the night. As his words slipped away, the diner doors slammed shut, sealing the room from the wind that rattled the lanterns.

Harriet turned her eyes to the bar where Lucy stood, the fresh scar on her palm still dark against the flesh. Lucy lifted the brass lantern and glanced toward the river that ran beside the stone wall. Harriet followed her stare and saw the surface catch a stray beam, a flash of ivory as the missing tip of the Ornate Knife slipped into the water’s sheen. The metal sang a cold, metallic note that thrummed beneath the fragile calm of the emptied tables, a reminder that danger lingered even as the patrons slipped away.
