Mayor Caldwell stood on the stone steps of the square, the winter wind threading through his coat as the Farmer Union gathered beneath the arches. Their chants rose in a steady rhythm, demanding that the town’s debts be laid bare and that the Blackwood Ledger be shown. Rumors about hidden contracts and secret obligations rippled through the crowd, tightening the air around the stone walls. After hours of concealment in the hidden safe, Caldwell lifted the sealed ledger from its narrow cavity and carried it across the courtyard, laying it on the cold metal tray beside the furnace. The iron clasp still held a faint warmth from the earlier fire. The union’s chant echoed off the arches as Lucy Frost moved along the periphery, the shaved curve of her head catching the lantern light from nearby stalls. She slipped a brief glance at the ledger, her hand resting lightly on the edge of the tray. The ledger seemed to pulse faintly, a low thrum that traveled through the metal, as if the pages themselves were counting down. Caldwell watched Lucy’s jaw set, her eyes flickering across the cover as if measuring which name might be dragged into the light. He lingered beside the sealed ledger, feeling the crowd’s pressure press against his shoulders, waiting for the chants to swell.
