Harriet pressed her badge against her chest and stepped into the dim cellar beyond Marjorie's door. The iron latch hissed as she pushed it open, the sound swallowed by a low ticking that grew louder with each breath. A faint glow from a cracked lantern revealed the bloodied ivory-handled knife lying across a stone slab, its edge still stained dark. Behind the slab the ledger lay open, its pages whispering of debts long forgotten. A voice, rough as gravel, muttered from the darkness, "The Blackwood Ledger lies beneath the floorboards, hidden where the night swallows light." The words settled over the room like cold iron, pressing against her ribs. Harriet felt the weight of those secret obligations settle into the stone walls. Thea stood near the doorway, eyes fixed on the knife, her hand trembling just enough to betray the calm she tried to keep. Lucy hovered at the far side, revolver at her hip, her gaze flicking between the ticking device and the ledger. The bomb's tick throbbed in the air, a steady heartbeat that threatened to drown the mutter. Harriet's hand brushed the ledger's edge, feeling the rough grain of the board beneath. She caught a glimpse of dust swirling from a recent footfall, as if someone had just fled the space. The air grew thick with the scent of rust and ash, and the low mutter lingered, a promise of hidden truth.
