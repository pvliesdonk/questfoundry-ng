Whispers coiled like smoke around Harriet, the hidden ledger's secrets pulsing beneath every word spoken in the square. The winter wind slapped the stone platform as she stepped forward, her trench coat billowing against the chill. A brass‑bound safe sat beside the mayor’s podium, the ledger inside still faintly heated from the furnace that had cradled it minutes before. The metal surface trembled ever so slightly, as if the pages were breathing. A guard in a dark coat lingered near the edge of the crowd, his rifle glinting in the lantern light. Harriet lifted her right hand, the fingers spread toward the gathering farmers, and spoke for the first time, refusing to draw a pistol or command fire. Her voice cut through the chant, a clear note that steadied the crowd’s hope. At the same moment she placed her left palm on the guard’s shoulder and gave a brief, firm tap, a motion that meant arrest to anyone who understood the unspoken code of the town watch. The guard’s eyes flicked to the ledger, then to the protesters, and he tightened his grip on his rifle as if ready to move. Harriet felt the coil of whispers tighten around the ledger, each murmur a reminder of the ruin it could cause if exposed. She held the moment, aware that one gesture would either keep the peace or unleash a crackdown.
