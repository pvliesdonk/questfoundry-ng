Clifford Moon stepped onto the stone quay, the river's chill biting his boots. The abandoned mill loomed ahead, its rusted iron hatch a dark mouth against the night sky. From behind the mill Marjorie Lynch slipped silently, flour dust clinging to her apron, her camera hanging at her side. She kept pace with the courier, eyes fixed on the figure who slipped a small crate into another coat. The cloaked stranger paused, then turned toward Clifford as he approached. Their gazes met for a heartbeat, the air holding a thin edge of tension. The figure tipped the crate to the other hand and vanished into the shadows, leaving the night quiet once more.

Marjorie raised her brass camera, the click of the shutter cracking the stillness. The flash illuminated the exchange long enough for the picture to capture the handoff. Clifford watched the flash, feeling the weight of undeniable proof settle in his chest. The photograph slipped from Marjorie's fingers as she turned and hurried back toward town, the river's hush pulling her steps.

A thin draft curled from the cracked stone foundation near the hatch, stirring a layer of dust along the floorboards. Clifford followed the draft, his boots echoing in the low crawlway that opened beneath the mill. The passage was narrow, the walls damp, a faint drip echoing in the darkness. He traced the tunnel a short distance, noting its secret curve before the air opened back into the mill's outer courtyard. The hidden route vanished behind a pile of broken timber, and Clifford stepped out, the night wind snapping at his coat.

Back at Marjorie's diner, the lantern sputtered over the worn wooden counter. Clifford leaned his forearm against the warped surface, feeling the grain under his palm, while Lucy Frost's voice dropped low. She warned that the Ornate Knife had passed through too many hands, that its blade carried a danger no one could see. Harriet Wellman entered, took the photograph from Marjorie's flour‑streaked hands, and studied the image with a steady finger tracing the outline. The diner settled into a heavy silence, the promise of a confrontation hanging in the air.
