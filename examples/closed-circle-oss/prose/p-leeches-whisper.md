Clifford Moon leaned against the cold stone wall of the precinct lounge, the din of distant gears a low murmur behind the flicker of the furnace. He let his voice drop to a thread, "the leeches are moving tonight," a phrase that meant smuggled contraband to anyone who heard. Marjorie Lynch paused at the edge of the counter, her flour‑dusted apron catching a stray spark of light, and caught the tail of the whisper as it slipped through the stale air. She turned, eyes narrowing, but before she could speak a figure cut across the doorway. Lucy Frost entered, the shaved crown of her head reflecting the dim lantern, her gaze fixed on the two women.

Lucy placed a firm hand on Marjorie's shoulder, the contact brief and unyielding. "You shouldn't accuse without proof," she said, her tone flat. "If we start a fire with rumors, the whole town could spiral into chaos." The words landed like a clang against the furnace's hiss, and Clifford felt the weight of her warning settle over the room.

Marjorie lowered her gaze to the table, her hand hovering over a sealed package that bore a familiar sigil. She lifted the cup she had been holding and set it back on the counter, then slipped away from the center of the room. She moved close enough to stay within sight, a shadow that trailed Clifford silently as he turned his back on the conversation, keeping a quiet watch on his steps.

Clifford watched the scene unfold, the iron clang of the door echoing his own heartbeat. He sensed the tension rising, the fragile peace of Grayhaven hanging on the edge of a whispered accusation. The furnace hissed, and the cold steel of the room seemed to press in around him, a reminder that a single careless word could unravel everything he had fought to keep hidden.
