Lucy lingered by the sealed ledger as the clock in Old Man Well’s workshop ticked low in the shadows, each measured beat sending a pulse of uneasy anticipation through the cramped hallway. The ledger rested on a rusted iron slab, its brass clasp still warm from the furnace’s recent flame, and she watched its surface tremble faintly, as if the pages themselves counted down. A thin, bright clink sounded when the ornate ivory‑handled knife brushed a cold pipe on the far wall, the noise traveling thin and echoing before fading into the stone. The sound settled into Lucy’s bones, cementing a cold dread that spread across the floor like a thin frost. She tightened the strap of her revolver, the leather creaking against her palm, and then pressed the heel of her palm to the ledger’s trembling surface, feeling the subtle vibration rise through the metal. The air smelled of soot and rust, and the distant wind rattled the shutters above, but the only thing that held her focus was the ledger’s mute promise of revelation. In that suspended moment she stood still, her gaze shifting between the ticking clock, the clink of metal, and the faint pulse of the hidden book, waiting for the next move to unfold.
