Marjorie lingered in the low vaulted chamber, the iron girders above her humming with the furnace's breath. Silas Old Man Well stood by the wall of clocks, his wiry frame hunched as each pendulum swung in steady rhythm. The ticking rose like a metallic heartbeat, bouncing off stone and striking the silence that had settled after the crowd's chant. She watched his gnarled hand lift a brass cover from the largest clock, revealing a hidden cavity behind it. In the gloom behind the furnace, soot clung to the floor in thick, dull sheets. A thin ledger lay half‑buried in the blackened ash, its cover still slick with coal dust. Marjorie's eyes traced the cracked leather, noticing the faint ink of names and numbers that hinted at debts owed and promises broken. Silas turned the ledger slowly, his fingers brushing the soot‑soaked pages as if testing their weight. A cold draft slipped through a cracked vent, stirring the fine particles and making the metal gears inside the clocks shiver. The room seemed to hold its breath, the ticking now a quiet reminder that something valuable, and dangerous, rested among the embers. Marjorie felt the weight of her own decisions, the revolver at her hip a dull pressure against her coat. The ledger waited, its secrets poised to ignite or to be concealed.
