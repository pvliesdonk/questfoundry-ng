Harriet Wellman leaned against the cold stone of the Old Mill, the rusted iron latch sighing in the wind. She kept her coat collar up against the chill that seeped through the cracked pine beams. Lucy Frost stood a short distance away, her shaved head catching a stray shaft of moonlight, a revolver glinting at her hip. Harriet heard a low murmur slip from Lucy's throat, a promise to protect a brother whose name she did not catch, but the urgency hardened the bartender's jaw. The words drifted toward Harriet like a thin wisp of smoke, curling around the charred remains of the Blackwood Ledger that smoldered on the mill floor. The ash that rose from the ledger seemed to pulse, the ledger's secrets humming like unseen smoke in the night air. Harriet felt the weight of that sound settle on her shoulders, a reminder that the ledger could ruin families even in its absence. She watched the ash settle in a thin layer across the stone, the remnants of the ledger's pages turning to gray dust. A sense of loyalty lingered in that ash, tangled and brittle, as if the promise she had just heard were already burning away. Harriet's hand brushed the cool stone, fingers brushing the fine gray powder, and she noted the resolve in Lucy's stance, a silent vow echoing through the ruined mill.
