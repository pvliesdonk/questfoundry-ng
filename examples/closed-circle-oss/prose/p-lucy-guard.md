Lucy Frost slipped between the booths, her shaved head catching the dim lantern light. She felt the tremor of a patron clutching a child's hand and shoved the trembling figure backward, her scarred palm gripping the man's elbow. The diner's air carried a faint bite of smoldering timber, a reminder of the firebrand evidence whispered about earlier. Her gaze snapped to the floor where a sliver of ivory caught the lamplight beneath a worn table, the polished edge of the ornate knife glinting like cold steel. She knelt, the scar on her right hand pressing into the cracked pine board, and brushed the blade free from the dust. The sheath was gone, leaving the ivory handle exposed, its weight demanding attention. For a heartbeat she stared at the weapon, the silence around it as heavy as iron clang in a forge. The patron she had saved watched her with wide eyes, the fear still visible in his shoulders. Lucy stood, the knife held loosely in her fist, and promised herself she would guard the secret of the missing sheath as fiercely as she guarded the frightened. The smell of timber lingered, and the winter wind hissed through the cracked windows, rattling the door as she turned toward the exit, the promise sealed in the quiet of the night.
