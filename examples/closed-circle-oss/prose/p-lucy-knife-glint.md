Lucy Frost stood behind the bar, the clatter of plates rising as the night crowd surged toward the dim street. She shouted a jagged warning, her voice cutting through the din as terrified townsfolk stumbled toward the darkened lane, the bomb’s silent tick a low thrum beneath her words. A stray shaft of light from a flickering lantern caught the ivory‑handled knife lying on the stone slab, flashing bright enough to freeze Lucy’s breath and etch the moment of peril into her mind. The scent of smoldering timber drifted from the back room, a sour reminder of hidden fire‑brand evidence. Lucy reached for the brass whistle on the counter, pressed it to her lips, and blew a sharp note that rose above the ticking bomb behind her. The note sliced through the panic, drawing the crowd further from the danger. She kept her eyes on the flashing blade, the metal gleam reflecting in the faces of the fleeing patrons. The bomb hissed louder, a metallic whisper that pressed against the walls of the diner. Lucy’s hand tightened on the bar, the fresh scar on her right palm throbbing with each pulse of the ticking device. She held the whistle steady, its sound a thin thread pulling the townsfolk toward safety while the danger lingered in the shadows.
