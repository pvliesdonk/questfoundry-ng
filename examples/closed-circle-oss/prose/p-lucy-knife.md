Lucy kept her back to the bar, the worn wood cool under her forearms. Harriet's voice rose, sharp, as she seized Clifford's coat and demanded the ledger. Lucy saw the clerk's knife glint near the edge of the shelf, its ivory handle catching a sliver of light that fell through the cracked window. The metal brightened like a cold promise, a reminder of loyalty turned to betrayal. She felt the weight of the decision settle in her chest, the same iron clang she heard from the furnace humming below. She reached for the blade, her fingers brushing the polished surface. The knife was heavier than it looked, its balance steady in her palm. Without looking away, Lucy slid it onto the shadowed shelf, the movement smooth, the edge catching a final flash before disappearing into darkness. The sound was a soft thunk against wood, a seal on the resolve she had forged since the murder. She stared at the empty space where the sheath had been, the absence a stark line in the dim light. Around her the bar murmured, patrons shifting, the scent of soot lingering in the air. Lucy tightened her grip on the revolver at her hip, feeling the cold metal against her leather. The decision to hide the knife felt like burying a secret beneath stone, a silent pledge to protect the brother she kept away from danger.
