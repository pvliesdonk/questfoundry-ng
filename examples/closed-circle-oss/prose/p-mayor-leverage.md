Rain beat against the stones as Caldwell lingered in the doorway of Marjorie's Diner, his gold tie catching a flicker of the lantern's glow. Lucy Frost's voice cut through the wind, urging the patrons toward the swinging doors. People streamed out, coats huddling against the chill, children clutched to mothers. Caldwell lifted his forearm, a subtle sweep that guided the line of evacuees away from the street, his tone calm enough to keep panic at bay. His hand hovered a breath above the Blackwood Ledger that sat on the counter, the worn leather face hidden from view. He glanced toward the Old Mill, where the building's dark shadow fell across the street, and felt a cold draft slip through the crowd—a reminder that the ledger's secrets could turn the town's fate. The bomb in the back room stayed still, its metal cold and unmoving, offering no sound but a weighty presence. Caldwell tightened his tie, aware that his polished image rested on the leverage the ledger afforded. The night air carried a lingering chill that brushed the faces of the townsfolk, as if the unseen ledger pressed upon them. He watched the mill's shadow stretch, his eyes lingering on its edge, waiting for the next move.
