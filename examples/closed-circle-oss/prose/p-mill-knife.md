The wind swept through the abandoned mill, rattling loose boards against the stone walls. It coaxed a faint tick from a hidden clockwork beneath the floor, a thin sound that marked how the town’s time had stalled at the river’s edge. Lucy Frost stepped into the doorway, the lantern in her hand throwing a thin circle of light on the ash‑covered floor. Her eyes caught the shimmer of an ivory‑handled knife half‑buried in the grey dust, its polished surface reflecting the flame like a cold stare. A memory of the night’s violence pressed against her, sharp as the blade’s edge. She moved forward, scar on her right palm brushing the ash as she knelt. The wind moaned through the cracked pine beams, and the ticking grew steadier, as if counting breaths. With a swift motion she grasped the hilt, felt the cold steel settle in her palm, and pulled the ornate knife free from the ash. The blade sang a thin note against the stone slab, its missing sheath a silent hint that another weapon had once been hidden. Lucy held the knife aloft, the weight of it steady in her hand, and for a moment the ruined mill seemed less a ruin and more a waiting throat, ready to swallow the next choice.
