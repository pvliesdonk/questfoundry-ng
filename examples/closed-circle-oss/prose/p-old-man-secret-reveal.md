Silas felt the chill of the mill tunnel settle into his bones as Harriet Wellman emerged from the darkness. He watched her stride to the stone slab where the ivory‑handled knife lay, the blade still wet with fresh blood. She knelt, her scarred cheek slick with sweat, and seized the weapon in one swift motion, the polished surface catching the sputtering torchlight. The sheath was gone, a clean absence that hinted another weapon had played its part. Silas noted the weight of the blood‑stained knife as Harriet rose, bruises blooming on her forearms, her deputy badge pressed hard against her chest. The townsfolk who had followed the secret passage gathered at the doorway, eyes fixed on the evidence she held aloft. The air rang with the clang of metal against stone, a brief echo that faded into a tense hush. Harriet lifted the knife, its edge gleaming against the gloom, and held it high for all to see. Silas observed the faces in the crowd shift from startled uncertainty to a hardening resolve, the truth of the murder pressing against their throats. He felt the gears of the old mill above humming a low warning, as if the building itself sensed the turning of a tide. The secret passage yawned behind them, still dark and inviting, while the townspeople braced for the revelation that would bind the killer to the crime. Silas waited, the weight of choice pressing on his mind as the scene held its breath.
