Clifford stood at the edge of the old mill’s stone slab, the river’s chill biting his boots. Harriet knelt beside the cracked foundation, her gloved hand feeling the seam where dust gathered. A faint draft slipped from the fissure, barely moving the edge of her coat. Harriet pressed her ear to the stone, hearing the breath of air that hinted at a tunnel. Thea approached, her medical coat rustling against damp air. "It may not be safe," she said. Thea whispered, "I don’t trust that hidden passage." Harriet shook her head, a scarred cheek catching the lantern light, and muttered, "I doubt Silas’s sanity—this isn’t madness, it’s a trap." A distant voice carried on the wind, Mayor Caldwell’s warning about rumors stirring panic. Lucy moved along the floorboards, her shaved head catching the lantern glow. Fresh footprints stood out against the damp stone, leading away from the draft toward a wall tangled in ivy. The ivy concealed an iron latch half swallowed by time. Lucy followed the prints, her boots thudding softly as they approached a concealed cellar door. A faint glint of an ornate knife caught the lantern’s glow from a cracked wall inside the cellar, the metal cold and precise. Clifford watched the scene, the draft breathing through the crack, the mayor’s warning lingering, the knife’s glint promising danger. He felt the choice before him.
