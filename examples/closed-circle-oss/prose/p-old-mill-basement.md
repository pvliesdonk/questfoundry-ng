Lucy set up a battered wooden crate against the stone wall and used it as a makeshift counter. She placed a tin mug on the crate and a torn ledger she had taken from the mayor's pocket beside it. Thea moved between the iron‑bound beams, slipping a fresh lantern into each socket; the amber light trembled against the vaulted ceiling. Murmurs rose from the gathered townsfolk, their words catching on the damp stone. When Mayor Caldwell stepped into the hall, his gold tie caught the lantern glow, and he raised both hands, promising calm. The furnace below hummed low, its heat seeping through the floor. Clifford Moon reached into his coat pocket, withdrew the heavy ivory‑handled knife that Lucy had recovered from the wreckage earlier, and held it up. The ivory handle caught the dim lantern light, flashing briefly. He set the blade on a scarred tabletop and began to rub its edge with a rag, the metal dulling to a muted shine that still reflected the lamplight. The glint lingered in the shadows, a silent reminder of the missing sheath. Lucy reached across the counter, slid the folded urgent note from the mug into Clifford's pocket, and watched his brow tighten. Beyond the crowd, the furnace crackled, sending occasional sparks into the stale air.
