Clifford Moon stepped out of the market square and crossed the riverbank toward the Old Mill. The river's current hissed behind him as the smoldering tower loomed, its broken stone ribs catching the wan light. He found Silas "Old Man" Well already there, stooped beneath the soot‑blackened arches, his wiry hands cradling a length of rusted iron. The clockmaker's eyes flicked to Clifford, patient and unreadable. Silas tipped his head and said, "Force shatters peace," his voice a low rasp that barely rose over the wind.

A sudden clatter of gears echoed from the mill's interior, the sound striking the cold air like a bell. In the ash‑caked floor a cracked Ornate Knife lay half‑buried, its ivory handle catching a flash of firelight and casting a brief glint among the soot. The blade's edge was bright, its sheath missing, hinting at a second weapon now hidden. The cold clang of metal against stone rang from the far corner, a reminder that the building's timbers were close to giving way. Clifford slipped the knife from the ash, feeling its weight, and tucked it into his coat. He nodded to Silas, acknowledging the clockmaker's warning, and turned toward the town's evidence locker, resolved to hand the weapon over to Harriet when he returned.
