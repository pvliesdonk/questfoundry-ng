Silas stood in the low arches of the old mill, stone cold beneath his boots. He felt the river’s mist clinging to the mortar and a thin draft slipping through a crack near the foundation. His wiry hand brushed a rusted iron latch half hidden by soot. He spoke in a low murmur, saying the stone foundation hid a hatch that opened onto a forgotten vault. The words fell like a breath into the dimness, and his voice seemed to echo off the damp walls.

A faint odor reached him, sharp as burnt ledger‑blackwood ash, rising from the opening. The scent mingled with the chill of the wind that slipped through the hatch, a contrast of lingering fire and cold stone. He inhaled, the ash settling on his tongue like grit. The smell reminded him of the night the ledger had been reduced to ash in the town hall furnace, though the pages themselves lay no longer in any hand.

Silas tightened the strap of his pocket watch, the weight a small comfort in the shadowed space. He turned his back to the hatch, waiting to see who would follow his whispered clue. The draft curled around his coat, urging him onward toward the unseen depth. The cold wind whispered through the stone, carrying a promise of darkness that lay below.
