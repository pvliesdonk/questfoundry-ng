Harriet moved deeper into the mill, her flashlight sweeping the darkness. The beam struck a rusted gear whose surface was blackened with soot. A flash of ivory caught the light, and a thin clang rang from somewhere between the metal teeth. The sound left a cold metal scent on the air. Harriet's breath fogged the glass as she leaned forward, seeing the blade of the Ornate Knife half buried in the gear's shadow, its edge still wet with fresh blood. The clang had echoed off the stone walls, a reminder of the struggle that had just ended.

Silas "Old Man" Well stood a few steps behind, his wiry frame hunching against the chill. He lowered his voice to a whisper that cut through the ringing in Harriet's ears. "There are tunnels below the mill," he said, his words slipping like dust through the cracks. "The foundation bears a hidden throat that leads deeper." The whisper lingered, and Harriet felt the weight of concealed routes pressing against the stone floor. She seized the blood‑stained blade from the gear and tightened her grip as the gears howled faintly in the darkness. The flash of ivory caught the trembling light once more, a cold promise in the shadows.
