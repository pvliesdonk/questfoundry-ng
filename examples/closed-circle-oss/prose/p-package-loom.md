Clifford Moon stood behind the counter of Marjorie's diner, the furnace hissed low behind the back door and the clatter of a night‑shift kitchen drummed in the dim. Lucy Frost's warning about rumors and chaos lingered in the stale air, a cold phrase that settled over the room like a thin layer of ash. Thea Swan moved toward the long wooden table, her bobbed hair brushing the hem of her coat as she kept her eyes on a sealed package that lay beside a chipped mug. The waxed lid bore a faint emblem that caught the lantern's weak light and held her stare.

Thea glanced at the package, her eyes widening. Clifford noted the moment as the symbol – an intertwined wheat stalk and a hammer – sparked a memory in her jaw, the line of it tightening as if an old scar had been pricked. He heard Lucy's voice echo in his thoughts, a reminder that idle talk could set a blaze.

Marjorie Lynch slipped behind a stack of flour sacks, her flour‑dusted apron still clinging to her knees. She moved with silent steps, keeping her back to the wall as she followed Clifford's outline. Clifford saw her intent clearly: she intended to tail him, hoping to catch him red‑handed without drawing a crowd. He felt the weight of her gaze on his shoulders as he turned toward the kitchen doorway.

The oil lantern on the bar sputtered, its flame throwing a wavering glow across the polished wood. Outside, the river's hush rolled over the cobblestones, a low murmur that seemed to carry the town's restless breath. Clifford felt the two sounds tug at his thoughts, each a possible direction for what might come next.
