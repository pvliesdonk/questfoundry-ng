Lucy slid into the dim booth beside Edgar. The leather seat pressed cold against her thigh as she took the spot. Edgar hunched over a folded note, his spectacles catching the sputter of the dying lamp, and muttered a riddling line about the Blackwood ledger. Harriet watched the tremor in his voice and scribbled the tension into the leather journal she pressed against her coat. A brief orange flare flared from the bomb’s final spark, then sputtered out against the iron‑stained walls. Lucy pressed the cold steel wrench onto the exposed red wire; the circuit cracked and the humming charge fell silent. The three rose together, the din of frightened townsfolk swelling as the furnace roared back to life. Lucy’s scarred palm still bore the faint echo of hidden leverage, Edgar’s eyes lingered on the ledger’s whispered name, and Harriet’s badge caught a glint that seemed to carry the same weight. Each bore that quiet promise, a secret that might yet tip Grayhaven’s fragile balance. Harriet tightened the strap of her badge, the metal pressing against her chest, and clutched the journal close as the crowd’s clamor surged. Harriet stepped toward the doorway, her boots echoing on the splintered floor, while Lucy leaned on the bar, the wrench still warm in her hand. Edgar slipped the note into his coat, his gaze flickering toward the empty space where the ledger had been spoken of.
