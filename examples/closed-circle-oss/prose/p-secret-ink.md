Edgar Barnes felt the weight of his badge as he stood behind the counter, the lantern's sputter throwing amber shadows across the tiled floor of the clinic. The door creaked and Thea Swan slipped in, her coat hem brushing the worn boards. He watched her pull her sleeve down to conceal the bruise on her right wrist, and she kept her gaze fixed on the cold stone, avoiding the stare of Lucy Frost who leaned against the sink. Lucy Frost leaned close enough that only Thea could hear, her voice low. "The donor scandal that ruined a dozen families never left this town," she whispered. Thea's shoulders tightened, a flash of dread crossing her face as the words settled in the thin air. Harriet Wellman entered from the hallway, badge catching the dim light, and set a folded newspaper on the counter. The headline shouted Nurse Donor Fraud Exposed, and she turned a page to reveal a damp clipping still sticky at the edges. Beside the paper lay a leather‑bound diary, its cover cracked; an ink line on the open page wavered in the lantern glow, as if it would disappear when daylight reached it. Edgar watched the scene, the ticking of a distant bomb echoing off the stone walls, and felt each secret pressing against his own throat.
