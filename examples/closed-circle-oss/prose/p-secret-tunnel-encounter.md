The stone hatch had been cold under Silas' hand as he eased it open, revealing a sloping throat of tunnel lit by a single sputtering torch. He felt the weight of the old clockmaker's coat settle over his shoulders while Harriet Wellman's badge pressed firm against her chest, and he heard the scrape of her boot on the damp stone as she followed. The narrow passage flickered with torchlight, and the sound of a distant footfall rang against the walls, a rhythm that matched the ticking of the mill's abandoned gears. Silas noted the echo grew louder, a reminder that the murderer moved ahead, his steps a steady cadence that filled the cramped space.

He guided Harriet further, his voice low as the wind that slipped through the cracked pine beams above, and he watched her eyes narrow at the sight of a rust‑blackened slab where a shadow lingered. The torch sputtered, casting a thin line across a pile of broken tools, and a thin gleam caught his eye farther down the stone, a sliver of ivory edge hidden in soot. He understood that the faint glint signaled the Ornate Knife, its blade half‑buried among debris.

The tunnel opened into a hidden chamber where a figure crouched near a stone altar, a cloak draped over his shoulders, blood staining the floor around him. Harriet stepped forward, her hand tightening on her revolver, and Silas observed the murderer raise a hand that clutched a blood‑stained ornate knife. The clash erupted as Harriet lunged, and Silas heard the clang of steel against steel, the thud of bodies against the stone slab, and the hiss of the torch as it guttered. He saw Harriet wrest the weapon from the killer's grip, the ivory handle catching the torchlight, and he felt the cold surge of triumph as the murderer fell, his breath stilled by the weight of the knife. The chamber fell silent save for the echo of the distant river outside, and Silas knew the fight had ended but the hunt could still continue deeper into the mill.
