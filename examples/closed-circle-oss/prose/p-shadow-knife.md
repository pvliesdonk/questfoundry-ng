Edgar Barnes tightened his badge against his chest as the lantern sputtered over the damp stone. A dark figure slipped through the iron doorway, coat snapping, boots stirring a thin veil of dust that marked a line across the slab. The figure vanished down a narrow hallway, leaving fresh footprints that faded slowly into the gloom. Edgar heard the bomb's steady tick from beside a cracked pipe, each pulse reverberating off the low ceiling and syncing with his heartbeat. Lucy Frost placed the ivory‑handled knife onto the stone slab, the blade still slick with blood, then stepped back toward the counter. Harriet Wellman stepped from the shadowed counter, badge pressed, scar catching dim light. Harriet moved toward the knife, her fingers closing around the polished handle, feeling the weight of the riddle fulfilled. Harriet turned and slipped the bloodied blade into the evidence locker, the metal latch snapping shut. Thea Swan stood beside the locker, hand lightly touching the scar hidden beneath her coat cuff, eyes fixed on the closed door. Lucy Frost lingered a few steps away, her muscular frame casting a long shadow, her gaze flicking between the sealed locker and the ticking device that continued its count. Beyond the cellar, the iron door that opened into Marjorie's diner waited, its latch ready to be turned. Edgar kept his revolver at his hip, the cold metal a reminder that danger lingered beyond the next turn.
