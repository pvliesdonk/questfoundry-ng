Clifford Moon read the folded scrap again, its ink spelling a warning about a shadow beneath the turning gears. The words hinted at a concealed place, a seam in the town’s machinery that no one had yet uncovered. He tucked the note into his coat and turned toward the gathering crowd outside the mill. Old Man Well, stooped by the furnace, caught the tail end of the exchange and muttered low that secret tunnels wound beneath the old mill’s stone foundation. The clockmaker’s voice carried a rasp that seemed to echo off the soot‑blackened windows. Silas Well stepped forward, his eyes glinting under the lantern light, and whispered that a hidden passage ran directly beneath the mill, a throat that could trap the murderer if they ventured too deep. The notion settled like cold steel in Clifford’s gut. He could picture the rain‑soaked arches, the slick stone walls dripping from the recent storm, the faint clatter of turning gears far above. The tunnel’s mouth lay hidden behind a rusted hatch, its iron teeth barely visible in the dim light. Clifford felt the weight of the ornate knife at his hip, a reminder that the law could become a key. The wind slipped through the cracked pine of the nearby warehouse, carrying the distant hum of the town’s machinery. Ahead, the mouth of the passage yawned, promising darkness and the chance to catch the killer in a place the town had long forgotten.
