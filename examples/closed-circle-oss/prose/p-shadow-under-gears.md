Clifford Moon watched the thin figure of Edgar Barnes lean over the table where the folded scrap lay. Edgar’s spectacles caught the weak light as he turned the paper, his finger tracing the line that spoke of a shadow under the turning gears. He murmured that the words pointed to a place hidden from sight, a hollow where the mill’s machinery concealed a dark opening. Clifford noted the precision of Edgar’s tone, the way the old teacher’s eyes narrowed as he catalogued the clue.

The low clatter of the mill’s gears rose on the breeze, and Old Man Well, stooped beside the furnace, caught the end of the conversation. He muttered under his breath about tunnels he had known since the town’s first foundation, tunnels that ran beneath the stone floor of the Old Mill. His voice was a rasp that blended with the metal’s sigh. Clifford heard the old clockmaker speak of a concealed shaft that could swallow a man whole.

Silas Well leaned closer, his breath cold against the stone, and whispered that the hidden passage beneath the Old Mill might become a trap for the murderer. He spoke of the thin air that could choke a pursuer and the iron doors that could seal a shadow forever. Clifford felt the weight of the warning settle like a stone in his pocket.

A narrow doorway to the east opened onto a crisp, dawn‑lit tunnel beneath the Old Mill, its floor dusted with pale light that promised clearer footing than the dark below. The air was cool, the scent of wet timber lingering. Clifford’s hand tightened on the ivory‑handled knife at his side, the metal a silent promise as he considered the path forward.
