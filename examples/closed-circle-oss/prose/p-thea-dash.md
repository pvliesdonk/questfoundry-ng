Lucy watched Thea swing the thin glass vial across the rain‑slick cobbles, the serum trembling as if the glass were alive. The ticking sound rose from the diner’s back door, a low metallic pulse that pulled her focus. Without waiting for orders, Lucy broke from the crowd, her coat snapping against the wind, and crossed the street toward the fleeing nurse. She reached Thea just as the young woman vaulted the low stone wall that edged the alley. Thea’s eyes never left the doorway where the device throbbed, and Lucy seized the vial from the nurse’s gloved hand. The serum shivered in Lucy’s own grip, a fragile weight that matched the urgency of the ticking bomb inside. She turned and ran back toward the diner, the rain hissing on the puddles at her boots. The cracked wooden table waited beneath the flickering fluorescent tubes, the clockwork device perched on it like a heart about to burst. Lucy set the vial down, its surface catching a brief glint of iron. The pulse grew louder, each beat echoing off the soot‑blackened windows. She glanced at the door, calculating how many steps lay between her and the threat. A moment of stillness settled as the storm wind howled through the alley, the metal of the bomb cold against the night. Lucy’s breath came in short, measured bursts, her mind racing toward a choice that would decide whether the serum could still save lives.
