Edgar watched Thea Swan burst through the hall doorway, the metal tray rattling against her forearms. Her bobbed hair swung with each hurried step and her eyes widened at the chaos spilling from the crowd. He noted the way she gripped the tray, fingers white against the cold metal.

Thea halted at the stone threshold that led down to the basement. A breath caught in her throat as the dim light caught the ivory handle of the ornate knife on the slab beyond the door. Edgar saw the blade catch the weak glow, dark with fresh blood and sharp as a held breath.

He followed her through the stairs, the echo of his boots on stone racing the ticking of the hidden device above. The altar of blackened stone rose beneath a low arch, and the knife rested upon it as if offered in sacrifice. Thea leaned toward the altar, her shoulders hunched, and stared at the glinting edge. A voice rose from the crowd, a whispered accusation that the blood‑stained blade was a false clue. Edgar listened, his ear straining for the low hiss.

A shadow slipped past the altar, kicking up a cloud of dust that settled in a faint trail across the floor. Edgar noted the dusty footprints and stepped after them, each footfall drawing him farther toward the stairwell. The air grew heavy with the scent of rust, oil, and coppery blood. He kept his revolver pressed against his hip, the weight familiar as the footsteps faded into darkness.

The tension settled like cold steel across the room, the knife glinting, the whispers lingering, and the dust‑caked path disappearing beyond the stone walls.
