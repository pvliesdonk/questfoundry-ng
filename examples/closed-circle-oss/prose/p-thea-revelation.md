The diner's low hum settled into tentative hope, the air tinged with uneasy optimism as Thea's voice steadied and she began to recount the scandal that had forced her from the infirmary. She spoke of forged records, of patients denied care, and of the night rain hammered the streets while she slipped away. Around her, Lucy Frost leaned on the bar, a heavy wrench in her palm catching the lantern's glow, while a thin coil of red wire dangled from the floor near the overturned table. Lucy's hand moved to the alarm lever and a sharp clang rang, echoing through the room. She raised the lever again, the bell shrill, and then thrust her elbow toward the swinging door, gesturing for the trembling patrons to move. One by one they shuffled toward the exit, their coats brushing the cracked stone outside as Lucy guided them past the threshold. Thea watched the crowd spill into the night, their footsteps scattering across the frosted pavement, and felt a cold resolve settle in her chest. With the crowd gone, Lucy swung the wrench, its steel biting the red wire and severing the circuit. The wire snapped, a faint hiss dying in the sudden stillness. The wrench lay on the counter, its cold weight promising a chance to act, while the room fell quiet again, threaded now with a thin thread of fear and a faint pulse of resolve.
