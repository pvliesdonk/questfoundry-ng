The thin ticking drifted from the iron box tucked behind the counter, each click a cold tap against the diner's tin ceiling. A sharp metallic scent of gunpowder slipped through the cramped room, and a thin thread of panic tightened around the patrons. Voices fell to a hush as the sound echoed off the cracked pine tables.

Lucy Frost's eyes widened, the fresh scar on her right palm catching the dim light as she stared at the device. At the same moment Marjorie Lynch lifted her flour‑dusted apron and offered a tentative smile. Lucy's alarmed stare met Marjorie's hopeful smile as the ticking grew louder.

Marjorie raised her voice, steady despite the rising clamor. “We keep the doors open,” she said. “The townsfolk need a place to gather tonight; if we shut down, morale will crumble before the night even falls.”

Edgar Barnes felt the weight of his badge as it caught the sputtering lamp light. He stepped forward toward the ticking device, his revolver resting against his hip. He lifted his voice and demanded, “What is happening here? Explain the device to me.”

The room held its breath, eyes turning to him as the ticking seemed to quicken. A low murmur rose among the diners, the metallic tang sharpening their unease.
