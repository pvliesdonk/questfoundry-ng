Lucy watched Thea Swan's eyes linger on the fire at the far wall, the orange tongues curling above the coal‑fed furnace. A low murmur rose from the town square, the farmer union's chant climbing over the stone arches. Lucy heard the rhythm of voices demanding fair rates, steady as a hammer on an anvil. Across the square, Harriet Wellman stood at the edge of the crowd, her scarred cheek set in a hard line, eyes tracking the farmers as the dispute widened between growers and the town elite. Lucy moved closer to the furnace, her hand resting on the cold iron lid. She pulled it upward, the weight shifting with a groan, and the blade of the Ornate Knife lay exposed beneath the heat, its steel dull in the glow. In the hallway beyond the mill, the ticking of Silas Old Man Well's clocks echoed off the stone. Lucy followed the steady beat, stepping toward the soot‑blackened ledger that rested near the furnace foot, its pages charred but still legible. The narrow stone stair descended a few steps into the vaulted basement. Lucy descended, the air growing colder as she entered the lower chamber. There, Mayor Caldwell stood beneath the iron‑wrought furnace, his coat flapping, his hand resting on the edge of the ledger. He glanced at Lucy, the chant from the square still audible through the vents. The furnace hissed, the chant swelled, the knife glinted, and Lucy felt the weight of her revolver at her hip as the moment hung between decision and danger.
