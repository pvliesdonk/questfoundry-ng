Lucy Frost stood beside the iron door that guarded the clerk’s desk, the furnace’s low roar filling the vaulted basement. The Blackwood Ledger lay on an iron slab near the ember glow, its pages mottled with soot. A pile of dry kindling leaned against the furnace wall, its bark crisp. Old Man Well moved across the coal‑stained floor, his wiry frame outlined by the firelight. He placed a rough hand on Harriet Wellman’s shoulder and lowered his voice. He warned that any heavy hand now would fracture the tentative calm the town held together. Harriet’s jaw tightened, Harriet’s cheek bore a scar that glinted as the firelight struck it as she turned toward the ledger.

Lucy felt the tension coil in Harriet’s shoulders and heard the crowd’s murmurs fade beneath the clang of metal. The iron door behind the makeshift bar creaked softly, its hinges cold in the heat. Lucy tightened the strap of her revolver at her hip, the metal warm against her coat. She lifted a bundle of kindling and fed it into the furnace, the coal snapping as it caught. The flame licked higher, sending a thin plume of ash across the ledger and smearing ink along a margin.

Then she reached for the ledger, slid it into the narrow slot of the iron door and turned the bolt, sealing it behind the heavy door. The sound of the bolt sliding echoed faintly in the vaulted space. The furnace hissed, the heat pressing against the stone walls, while Lucy watched the now‑locked ledger and the growing fire, aware that each choice now rested on the iron and the flame.
