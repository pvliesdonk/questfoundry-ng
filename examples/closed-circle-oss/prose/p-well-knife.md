Lucy paused at the edge of the cellar stairs, the scent of damp stone rising in the air. Silas "Old Man" Well stepped forward and leaned against a rusted timber beam that jutted from the wall, his wiry frame a shadow against the gloom. His eyes fixed on a tiny clock concealed within the grain, the faint tick reverberating like a distant heartbeat. The sound seemed to imprint on Lucy a sense of watches that never ceased, a reminder that time lingered even in the forgotten places. She watched the old man's patient stare, the way his gaze lingered on the moving gear as if measuring each pulse of the mill's silence.

A sudden flash caught her attention near the far wall where the masonry was cracked and half collapsed. The blade of an ornate ivory‑handled knife caught the sputtering lantern light, a cold promise reflected in its polished surface. The glint rose from the fissure as if the metal waited for a hand to claim it. Lucy felt the weight of that promise settle across her shoulders, the knife a silent herald of violence in a place already heavy with unseen eyes. The ticking clock continued its rhythm, and the rusted beam loomed behind Silas, both reminders of a watchful darkness that lingered in the cellar.
