Lucy Frost stood beside the iron firepit, the night wind slipping through the cracked stone arches. The half‑burned Blackwood Ledger lay on a metal tray, its pages curling toward ash. The rusted furnace behind town hall hissed as the last embers faded, casting a thin orange glow on the cobbles. Old Man Well emerged from the mill doorway, his back bent like a rusted hinge, his eyes fixed on Harriet. He slipped a weathered hand into the pocket of his coat and then turned his head toward her. "Force will shatter the fragile peace," he said, his voice low enough for only the two of them. Harriet pressed the barrel of her revolver against her hip and nodded without moving her mouth. Lucy watched the scar on Harriet’s cheek catch the firelight, a reminder of too many hard choices. The warning struck like a hammer against cold steel, the pit’s iron ribs echoing the threat. Behind the furnace, a wooden door led to a vaulted room where a steel safe stood, its lock gleaming in the dim. Lucy noted the safe’s presence, the way its hinges still rattled with the building’s age. The ledger’s smoke curled upward, mingling with the night fog that drifted down the alley. She felt the weight of the decision pressing against her gut, the town’s future balanced on a single sheet of parchment. The choice would be made soon, either to thrust the ledger into the furnace’s mouth or to seal it within the safe’s iron maw. The wind slipped through the broken shutters, and the sound of distant chanting faded into the hollow night. Lucy felt the night tighten around her, as if the town itself held its breath.
