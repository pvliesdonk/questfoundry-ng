Lucy Frost stood in the doorway, the metal bar of the swinging exit still warm from the crowd’s rush. The street beyond was slick with rain, lantern light flickering on puddles. A hunched patron brushed past her shoulder and whispered, “Your brother was seen moving the Blackwood ledger.” The words slipped into the din and settled on Lucy’s ear like cold steel. She watched the patron’s eyes dart toward the rear of the room, then melt into the shadows. Unease pressed against her jaw as the idea of loyalty turning traitor lingered.

A thin wisp of smoke rose from the far side of the street, carrying the faint scent of smoldering timber that drifted from the Old Mill. The odor curled around the doorway, sharp enough to prickle the back of Lucy’s throat. She felt the implication of hidden fire‑brand evidence, a spark that could set the town’s fragile order ablaze. The din of departing patrons faded, leaving the hollow clang of the alarm in the night air.

Lucy tightened her grip on the bar, her scarred palm brushing the wood. She scanned the empty tables, noting the empty seat where the ledger had once been discussed, and the lingering ash on the floorboards. The night stretched ahead, heavy with the promise of secrets waiting to ignite.
