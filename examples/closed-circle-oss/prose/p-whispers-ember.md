Lucy stood near the iron‑bound furnace, the heat pressing against her cheek. Harriet Wellman stepped from the shadows, her coat rustling, and pressed her ear to the cracked wall where Lucy's voice drifted. Lucy’s words fell soft: the Blackwood Ledger could be tossed into the furnace, the ash would swallow its sins. Harriet’s scarred cheek caught the glow, her jaw set as the suggestion settled in the stale air. From the opposite column, Edgar Barnes lingered, spectacles reflecting the orange blaze. He lowered his voice, humming a rhyme: “Numbers melt when fire consumes, the sum of lies turns to dust.” The cadence rose briefly, then faded into the clatter of gears, underscoring the notion that truth was being forged or erased in flame. Lucy watched the furnace’s iron ribs pulse, the coal feeding a hungry mouth, and felt the weight of the ledger’s pages against the metal table nearby. The room held a thin tension, the cold steel of her revolver at her hip a reminder of the line they walked. Harriet tightened her grip on Clifford’s coat, her eyes fixed on the ledger as if waiting for the fire to decide. The furnace roared louder, the heat shimmering, and the possibility of ash swallowing paper lingered in the copper‑stained air.
