The forest beyond the old mill stood dark, its trunks like blackened spears against the winter sky. Silas watched Harriet Wellman step out of the mill doorway, her badge pressed to her chest, her scar catching the weak lantern glow. He had spoken of a hidden passage that curled beneath the river stones, a route that could trap the murderer before he slipped away. Harriet tipped her hat back and shook her head. She called his story nonsense and turned away from the rusted iron latch that marked the tunnel’s mouth. Silas noted the way her boots crunched on the frost‑hard ground as she strode toward the tree line.

Harriet moved into the pine‑scarred woods, her eyes scanning the underbrush for any sign of the concealed route. Silas followed her progress from the mill’s edge, noting how she brushed aside low branches and stepped over moss‑slick stones. He observed her pause at a cluster of broken logs, then continue past a shallow gully where the wind rustled dead needles. No lantern light fell on the hidden hatch; the iron latch remained untouched, cold beneath the bark.

The deputy’s search grew more frantic. She crossed a fallen log, then halted at a hollow where a squirrel had disappeared. Silas felt a knot tighten in his chest as the murderer’s silhouette vanished deeper into the night, unheard among the trees. Harriet’s breath rose in short clouds, her revolver hanging loosely at her hip. She called out a name that drifted on the wind, but the sound dissolved before reaching his ears. Silas saw her hand brush a moss‑covered stone, then drop to her side, empty of any clue.

Hours slipped by. The forest grew silent save for the distant howl of a wind gust whipping through the pine needles. Harriet eventually returned to the mill, her coat dusted with pine sap, her expression unchanged. Silas heard the faint clink of her badge as she re‑secured it, and he recalled his earlier hint about the ledger hidden in a compartment beneath the floorboards. The secret tunnel lay dark and untried, the murderer a phantom in the night, and the hidden ledger still waited in shadow.

Silas turned his gaze to the iron latch, its rusted teeth glinting faintly, and to the silent gap where the ledger might lie. The choices lay before him.
