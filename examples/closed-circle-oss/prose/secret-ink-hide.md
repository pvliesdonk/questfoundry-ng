Thea slipped behind the soot‑blackened tavern doorway, the cold wind rattling the shutters as she entered. She pressed the collar of her white coat tighter, the badge hidden beneath the lapel, and moved toward a shadowed corner beside the stone hearth. She chose a low bench, sat upright, and let the clatter of mugs and low murmur of voices mask the steady beat of her heart. She kept her posture straight, as if she were merely a patron waiting after a long shift, while the scandal she had confessed lingered only in the whisper of rumors outside.

She turned her head every few seconds, glancing over her left shoulder with a swift motion. Her breath rose shallow, each inhale a thin whisper against the pressure in her ribs. The dim lantern light painted the walls in flickering gold, but the darkness beyond the window kept the world out of view. The feeling of being observed pressed against her skin, and she forced a calm expression despite the panic edging her thoughts.

Lucy stood at the bar, a copper mug in hand, and slid a thin scrap of paper across the wood, the edge catching a brief glint before disappearing into the gloom. The folded warning lay atop the clatter of mugs, a silent message meant for someone else. Thea felt a chill settle over her nerves as the paper brushed her palm.

Edgar rose from his seat, the leather of his coat creaking, and murmured a low rhyme: “Shadows dance where river bends, secrets lost as night descends.” The words floated over the din, winding around her anxiety like cold wind through cracked pine.

Harriet perched on a stool, her badge pressing into the fabric of her uniform. She opened a leather notebook and scratched a line about a trembling hand and a clenched coat. She paused, lifted the pen, and wrote a question in the margin, wondering what secret lay beneath Thea's calm. The weight of the notebook pressed into her fingers, and Thea felt a tremor travel from the wood to her own hand, a reminder that even the act of recording could tighten under fear.
