Edgar Barnes leaned against the stone counter, the lantern’s sputter throwing amber light across the clinic’s tiled floor. Thea Swan placed the cracked leather diary beside the damp newspaper clipping and uncapped a tiny vial of ink. She pressed the tip to the page, and the ink spread in a thin line that refused the lantern’s glow, its pigment holding a promise to dissolve when daylight slipped through the shutters. Edgar watched the line hold its secret, the letters forming as if they swallowed the paper’s grain. When she finished, Thea slid the diary’s cover over the fresh script, the coat sleeve brushing the wood as she sealed the entry. The ink would fade unless the room stayed dark, a hidden confession locked in shadow. Old Man Well shuffled in, his wiry frame bent under the weight of years, eyes fixed on the shelf of town records that lined the wall. He spoke in a low, measured tone, mentioning a rumor that had drifted through the archives about a nurse scandal that once shook the town. He said the whispers spoke of donor fraud that ruined families and forced a young nurse to flee the town. Edgar felt the weight of the words settle like soot in a chimney, the suggestion that the past scandal still lingered in memory. The ticking of a distant clock marked the fragile pause between secret and revelation. The room held its breath, the ink waiting for sunrise, the rumor echoing in the dark, and Edgar sensed the decision that would follow.
