The council hall smelled of cold iron and cracked pine. Elite council members sat rigid, frowning as they exchanged terse glances. Their stare settled on Deputy Harriet Wellman, whose badge caught the dim light. The weight of their disapproval pressed against her department, a silent demand for tighter control.

Mayor Caldwell rose to speak, his gold tie immaculate and his voice steady at first. He argued for order, his polished veneer beginning to crack as a council member challenged his handling of the union protest. His smile slipped, his hand trembled slightly on the podium, and the room sensed the fracture.

The debate sealed, the mayor lowered his papers and the murmurs faded. An uneasy calm rippled through Grayhaven, the crowd outside holding its breath as the echo of heated words settled.

After the council doors closed, Harriet walked toward the stone bridge that arched over the canal. Marjorie followed, hearing the distant chant of the farmer’s march rising behind her. The bridge's wooden planks creaked under their steps, the rhythm matching the muted drums from the square.

In a side alcove of the chamber, Lucy Frost leaned against the polished bar, her sleeve moving in small circles as she polished the surface. An Ornate Knife rested on a shadowed shelf nearby, its ivory handle catching a faint glint. Lucy glanced at the knife, her expression unreadable as the metal gleamed.

Beyond the hall, Old Man Well's workshop lay dimly lit, pistons ticking in the background. He muttered to himself about a ledger hidden in the old mill, his voice low enough that only Marjorie caught the clue. The words hung in the stale air, hinting at debts buried beneath stone.

The council chamber emptied, the city holding its breath, and the three paths lay before her.
