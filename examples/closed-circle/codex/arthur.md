title: Arthur Pendelton
---

Arthur Pendelton exists in a state of perpetual, preemptive apology, a man who navigates high society with the sweaty desperation of a cornered rodent. He clings to his rigidly structured, brass-reinforced leather briefcase not merely as a receptacle for fiscal ledgers, but as a tactile shield against impending social and physical hostilities. His pacing is a study in neurotic geometry, characterized by sharp, militant pivots executed precisely upon reaching the fringed border of any area rug. When traversing hardwood flooring, Pendelton’s heavily soled, immaculately polished oxfords produce an unseemly, percussive volume that announces his frantic anxiety to the entire manor. During indoor climate failures—which he inevitably exacerbates by drastically overestimating the thermal efficiency of his seasonal tailoring—Arthur seeks solace in deeply held, irrational oenological superstitions. He firmly maintains that a vintage Bordeaux possesses the lamentable ability to physically absorb atmospheric indelicacies, and will be utterly ruined by the mere proximity of unseemly domestic crises.
