title: The Glass Conservatory
---

The Glass Conservatory stands as a tragic monument to systematic staff neglect and architectural hubris. Following a catastrophic failure of its central heating apparatus three winters prior—an oversight that remains stubbornly unrectified—the botanical annex functions effectively as a hostile outdoor environment contained within a fragile glass enclosure. The space is a graveyard of domestic horticulture, featuring wrought-iron plant stands that exhibit advanced, unsightly oxidation born of chronic wintertime condensation. In the far corner, a remarkably sturdy, albeit thoroughly abandoned, potting bench squats beneath a layer of frost. Resting upon this bench is a heavy, rust-spotted pair of antique botanical shears. These shears possess an unevenly distributed weight, an ergonomic nightmare that forces the operator to apply downward pressure strictly from the shoulder to achieve any cutting force. To traverse this freezing, structurally questionable space is to engage in a silent, desperate battle against both the plummeting barometric pressure and the lingering ghosts of neglected flora.
