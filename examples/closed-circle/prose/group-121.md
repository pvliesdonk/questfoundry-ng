The kinetic energy imparted by the brass candlestick translates into an extraordinarily vulgar display of projectile debris, sending a multitude of jagged polymer fragments cascading across the meticulously hand-woven fibers of the antique Persian rug. This dispersal of deeply inferior synthetic materials constitutes an egregious affront to the room’s otherwise pristine aesthetic, scattering with a disorganized velocity that completely disregards the geometric symmetry of the floral medallion woven into the center of the carpet. Each distinct shard of shattered casing embeds itself within the dense, silk-blended pile, establishing a profoundly hazardous navigational terrain for anyone attempting to cross the drawing room to procure a post-dinner sherry. It is universally understood among persons of appropriate breeding that introducing unregulated debris to an imported textile borders on an act of unforgivable social hostility, rivaling the severe impropriety of the adjacent gentleman who continues to bleed quietly behind the protective facade of his folded newspaper. The harsh, reflective edges of the dismantled electronics gleam unpleasantly under the warm glow of the crystal chandelier, a stark and distasteful reminder of the telecommunications apparatus that so recently threatened to broadcast the entire evening’s intimate financial embarrassments to an unvetted public. Beaumont meticulously surveys the ruination of his flooring with a perfectly neutral countenance, finding the presence of the pulverized microchips almost as distressing as the lingering potential that the severed digital connection might have concluded its transmission on a highly unflattering angle of the supposed sleeper. The absolute stillness of the room settles once more over the pulverized hardware, affording the remaining attendees a necessary pause to properly condemn the profound lack of decorum inherent in manufacturing recording equipment out of such brittle, unapologetically inexpensive plastics.
