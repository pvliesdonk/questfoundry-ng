Before Beaumont can implement a secure extraction of the financial tome from its undignified position beneath the dining table, Arthur precipitates an unseemly physical dispute. The accountant lunges for the leather-bound volume, instinctively clutching both it and his brass-reinforced briefcase tightly against his sternum. This defensive posture proves woefully inadequate against Cecily. Thoroughly insulated against the evening’s mounting tragedies by an escalating consumption of the host's finest vintage, she initiates a shockingly vigorous contest for the ledger. Arthur’s heavily soled oxfords shuffle indecorously against the floorboards as he inevitably yields the catalog of communal ruin.

Armed with the absolute destruction of her peers' reputations, Cecily commences a robust, highly amplified recitation of the ledger's contents. She adopts an alarmingly precarious posture against the study’s intricate plaster molding, blaming the historic masonry's unacceptable thermal retention as she publicly dissects the catastrophic debts of her associates. To append a theatrical gravity to her performance, she embarks upon an unsteady promenade across the Persian rug, her empty crystal glass swaying precariously in one hand. This meandering path brings her into severe proximity with the deeply compromised gentleman currently occupying the central armchair, whose perpetual, forcefully induced silence is thinly veiled by the strategic placement of a financial broadsheet. A single misstep threatens to dislodge his paper disguise and openly broadcast a profound breach of residential safety.

The relentless dictation proceeds down the register until Cecily announces a sequence of figures that permanently dismantles the monetary supremacy of the wealthiest woman on the estate. Sylvia Vance, having evidently subjugated her earlier gastric distress through sheer, uncompromising willpower to maintain her strict oversight of the gathering, receives the news of her utter insolvency. The revelation demands a reaction entirely beyond the capacity of her customary two-finger temple massage.

In an unpardonable lapse of personal standards, Sylvia abandons her repertoire of polite, withering inquiries. She abruptly pivots into a sustained, high-decibel vocalization of absolute hostility, a vulgarity fundamentally incompatible with her pearl drapery and formal silk gloves. To compound this catastrophic failure of decorum, she weaponizes her chosen stemware. Sylvia propels her wine glass across the breadth of the study with a ferocious, entirely uncalculated trajectory. The delicate vessel sails with alarming velocity toward the deceased guest in the armchair, bypassing his carefully folded newspaper by mere millimeters before terminating violently against the mahogany panelling. It is an egregious display of property damage that definitively elevates the evening's transgressions beyond mere homicide.
