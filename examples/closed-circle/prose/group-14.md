This immediate assessment yields highly unsatisfactory results. Lowering his gaze, Beaumont discovers a secondary catastrophe of equal magnitude currently affixing itself to his left oxford. The polished patent leather, previously maintaining a flawless mirror finish suitable for evening entertainment, now bears a distinct, viscous tackiness along its welt. Such a grotesque breach of personal grooming standards threatens to track an unmistakable trail of deep crimson across the inlaid parquet flooring, an offense that would surely prompt unparalleled scrutiny from any passing guest.

With the measured, deliberate motions of a gentleman inspecting a minor scuff, Beaumont approaches the fringed perimeter of the hallway runner. This particular length of woven silk, boasting an intricate medallion motif, must now serve an entirely unsanctioned purpose. He anchors his right heel with absolute precision, executing a sharp, lateral scrape of the soiled oxford against the rug's decorative border. The friction yields a most disagreeable resistance, the congealing organic matter clinging stubbornly to the leather before finally surrendering to the silk fibers.

He repeats the scraping motion thrice, maintaining a posture of profound, architectural stiffness to disguise the highly irregular nature of the maneuver. The hallway rug dutifully accepts the crimson deposit, absorbing the moisture until the expanding stain mimics a mere trick of the low evening light upon the medallion pattern. Beaumont studies the subtle, spreading dampness at the fringe, tendering a rigidly composed, silent petition to whatever patron saint oversees the maintenance of immaculate domestic appearances. Should a discerning eye detect this aggressive alteration to the floor coverings, the ensuing conversational difficulties would undoubtedly prove insurmountable. He pivots sharply away from the compromised textile, presenting an impeccable profile to the empty corridor while firmly anticipating that the ambient metallic odor will shortly correct itself.
