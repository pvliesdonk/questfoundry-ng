Beaumont receives Sylvia's uncouth demand with the serene, glassy indulgence reserved for a wayward toddler or a particularly flatulent spaniel. To acknowledge the vulgarity of her outburst would legitimize it, a concession his breeding simply does not permit. Instead, he pivots gracefully toward the adjacent hallway, removing himself from the epicenter of her incivility under the guise of an urgent hostly duty. His gaze, seeking a focal point of absolute control amidst the mounting social disintegration, lands upon an ancestral oil painting. The gilt-framed portrait of a dour patriarch hangs at an imperceptible, yet entirely unpardonable, slant.

This microscopic deviation from the perpendicular is a profound affront to the architectural harmony of the estate. Beaumont approaches the canvas with the solemnity of a high priest tending to a sacred altar. He extends a manicured hand, applying a calibrated pressure to the lower quadrant of the ornate frame. It yields with a muted scrape against the silk wallpaper, settling into a state of flawless symmetry. For a fleeting second, the universe aligns into its proper configuration. The ambient tension radiating from the drawing room, where financial ruins and unmentionable grudges marinate beneath tailored eveningwear, is temporarily eclipsed by the triumph of a perfectly leveled frame.

The portrait's restorative geometry offers merely a brief reprieve from the overarching decline of the evening. The faint aroma of culinary preparations beckons, intertwining with the palpable hostility festering among his guests. A proper host does not linger in the corridor, regardless of the staggering indelicacies occurring within the principal chambers. Beaumont smooths an invisible wrinkle from his lapel, squares his shoulders against the encroaching chaos, and prepares to shepherd his deeply flawed acquaintances toward the dining room. The scheduled procession of courses must commence exactly on time, even if the gathering threatens to collapse entirely before the appetizer.
