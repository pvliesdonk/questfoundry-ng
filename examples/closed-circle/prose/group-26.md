The strident mechanical summons from the conservatory insists upon a response, vibrating with an appalling lack of consideration for the delicate social equilibrium currently maintained within the study. Arthur’s reaction to this auditory intrusion constitutes a severe lapse in drawing-room composure. He engages in a pattern of rapid, irregular ambulation across the length of the rug, his brogues striking the floorboards with a frequency that suggests a highly unseemly absence of internal fortitude.

With each sharp pivot near the mantlepiece, he issues an uninterrupted sequence of low-volume vocalizations, directing his protestations toward the general assembly rather than any specific interlocutor. He insists, with a distinctly defensive cadence, that the telephonic apparatus had been definitively severed from the exterior exchange. He posits that a complete cessation of signal was the documented and promised state of affairs, framing the current auditory disturbance not merely as a mechanical impossibility, but as a deliberate and profound affront to his administrative expectations. 

The rhythmic jangling of the bell entirely disregards his rapid verbal assurances, escalating in perceived volume as it underscores the utter impropriety of an incoming communication during an active meteorological crisis. This relentless ringing forcibly draws the collective gaze toward the botanical extension, directing attention precisely toward the frost-encrusted glass panes currently buckling under the immense pressure of the howling tempest. Arthur’s restless pacing carries his person perilously close to the archway separating the relatively secure parlor from the structurally compromised greenhouse.

It is at this precise juncture that the architectural integrity of the conservatory ceiling begins to broadcast its final, catastrophic capitulation. A rapid succession of sharp, percussive fractures echoes over the gale, indicating that the immediate vicinity of the demanding telephone is poised to become entirely inhospitable to any guest wishing to maintain an upright, unperforated posture.
