The shrill ringing of the landline presents an intolerable disruption to the evening's delicate auditory ambiance. Beaumont carefully navigates the severely frost-heaved tiles of the glass conservatory, adjusting his cuffs to assert control over the dreadful circumstances of the unheated botanical room. Approaching an abandoned potting bench, he acquires a heavy, rust-spotted pair of botanical shears. With a decisive and entirely unhurried application of leverage, he severs the telephone cord. He announces to the adjacent study that answering the device would place a guest precariously beneath the structurally suspect roof, framing the snipping as a profound courtesy of hostly preservation. In truth, the absolute silence guarantees no one shall summon the constabulary to inquire about the profoundly unmannered condition of the deceased individual currently occupying the study's central armchair.

Hardly has the severed wire struck the floor when a massive pane of overhead glass surrenders to the blizzard. It plummets inward, burying the silenced telephone apparatus beneath a lethally sharp arrangement of shards and an unforgiving cascade of winter snow. The sudden architectural failure prompts several guests to flinch with inexcusable physical vigor. This collective spasm of poor composure creates a chaotic jostle near the doorway, resulting in a severe impact against the dead guest's seating arrangement. Beaumont locks his jaw into a rigid line, observing with intense scrutiny as the corpse sways precariously. Should the body topple onto the Persian rug, it would constitute an aesthetic tragedy of the highest order, completely dismantling the painstaking illusion of a peaceful post-dinner nap.

The deceased slumps backward, safely remaining within the upholstery. Beaumont's left eye develops a minute, rhythmic twitch as the crisis averts itself. The surviving guests stare in quiet contemplation at the sheared cord and the mounting snowdrift within the conservatory. It is eminently clear that the manor is now entirely untethered from polite society. Beaumont systematically smooths invisible wrinkles from the narrow lapels of his velvet smoking jacket and adopts the wide, lateral stance of a classical fencer to subliminally command the room. Without the prospect of immediate rescue, he recognizes he must enforce this exceedingly tiresome fiction of an extended slumber for an indeterminate duration.
