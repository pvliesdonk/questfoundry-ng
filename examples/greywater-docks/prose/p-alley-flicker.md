A weak streetlamp sputtered, casting a thin halo over the rain‑slicked alley. The light fell on the wet cobbles, briefly recalling the city’s faded glories. Elliot March pulled his coat tighter around his gaunt shoulders, the chill seeping through the fabric. He felt the familiar ache rise in his chest and a dry cough escaped him, rattling the thin silence. The alley stretched ahead, its walls slick with oil‑smeared graffiti, the distant clang of the harbor muffled by mist. He glanced toward the pier, where lanterns trembled on the wharf.

A flicker of green caught his eye—a jade figurine, perhaps cradled in Lydia Vane’s hand, or merely a trick of the oil‑lamp’s glow. He lingered, the decision weighing against his failing breath. The promise of experimental medicine lingered in his thoughts, a cold fire that urged him forward. He stepped onto the dock, the slick boards slapping against his boots, the harbor’s steel scent filling his lungs. Behind him the alley disappeared into shadow, the sputtering lamp finally dying.
