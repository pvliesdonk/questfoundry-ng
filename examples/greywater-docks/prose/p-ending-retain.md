Elliot pressed the broken jade shards to his chest, the jagged edges biting his palm. Rain hammered the dock, and the oil lantern flickered on the slick wood. Lydia Vane stood a few paces away, her emerald dress clinging to her skin, silver hair pinned against the wind. He heard his own hoarse voice say, “No,” and he turned away, the promise of payment falling into the night.

Two men in crimson coats lingered in the shadows, rifles resting on their shoulders. Their leader scanned the empty space where the jade had been, eyes narrowing. He whispered a command to a courier who slipped away, the outline of a raid on Elliot’s safe house forming in the gloom.

Word of the missing jade traveled through the Syndicate’s circles, slipping from dockside taverns to hidden warehouses. Lydia vanished down the pier, the envelope of cash she carried disappearing into the tide.

Her carriage rolled away, the wind scattering the promised payment like ash. Elliot felt the hollow of his pockets, the cure forever out of reach.

Fever seized his lungs, each ragged breath scraping the iron of his throat. The rain grew louder, a drum over the harbor, and then it swallowed his final gasp. The dock settled into its indifferent rhythm, the night indifferent to his loss.
