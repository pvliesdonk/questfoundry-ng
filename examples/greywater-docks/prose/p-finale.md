Elliot gripped the cold steel wrench, its weight pulling his fingers down. The jade dragon glinted in the gutter, green veins catching the dim lamplight. Elliot lifted the wrench and slammed it into the figure. The impact sent shards scattering, silvered jade sinking into the black water of the dock. Two Crimson Syndicate thugs lingered in the shadows, their rifles trained on the broken relic. Their eyes flickered like dying lanterns, tightening the danger around him. Gunfire cracked the night as the thugs opened fire. Elliot fell beneath a hail of lead, the iron biting into his chest. Rain mixed with blood, tracing rivulets down the wooden planks. Elliot's breath halted, a final gasp lost to the pounding surf. The tide surged, washing the crimson from the dock as the harbor settled into its indifferent rhythm. The dock cleared, the shattered jade forgotten among the churned water. The city's undercurrents shifted, yet Greywater Docks carried on, its shadows unchanged.
