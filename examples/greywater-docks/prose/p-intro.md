Rain poured over Greywater’s neon‑slick streets, the harbor mist swallowing the night, and the rain turned steel signs into blurred ghosts.

He pushed open the office door, dim glow seeping from the cramped space. The city’s damp night bled into the stale air of his desk, paper yellowed by neglect. A single oil lamp sputtered on the desk, casting a jaundiced halo over scattered case files.

He coughed, a harsh rasp that rattled the thin walls. The terminal diagnosis sat like a ticking clock on his weary shoulders. He stared at the medical notice pinned to the wall, the ink smeared by his own tears.

The sound drifted out, carried by the wind toward the pier where shadows gathered. Fog rolled off the water, muting the clank of distant cranes, while gulls cried above the slick. Lydia Vane stepped onto the wet planks, a jade dragon catching the lantern’s oily glow.

She held the figurine up, green veins gleaming, and said, “I’ll give you the Jade Dragon for cash, and I’ll keep it from worse hands.” His hand trembled, the cheap cigar on the ashtray burning down to ash. He stared at the smooth surface, the weight of the offer pressing against his failing breath.
