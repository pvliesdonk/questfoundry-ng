Elliot slammed his fist onto the scarred oak table, the sound ringing through the dim room. The vial glinted in the sputtering lantern, a promise of salvation he could not swallow. He stared at the jade figurine, its green veins catching the weak light, and said, 'No cure for a price,' his voice hoarse. The emissary's thin smile faded; he lifted the glass and stepped toward the doorway, his eyes flat as the harbor night. Elliot watched him disappear into the rain‑slick alley, the scent of oil and salt following. A cold knot tightened around his chest as the disease pressed harder, each breath a ragged rasp that stole his focus. Darkness edged the corners of his vision, the world narrowing to the steady clang of his own heartbeat. Within two days the fever rose, his hands trembling as rain pattered against the cracked windowpane, a hollow rhythm matching the faltering pulse in his throat. He lay back in his chair, the lantern sputtering out, and felt the final beat of his heart echo the distant storm.
