A wail of distant sirens cut through the storm, echoing the city’s hidden panic. He shut the office door, rain splashing against the cracked wood, and stepped onto the slick pavement. The cold wind slammed him as he crossed, his coat clinging to his thin frame. A ragged cough rose from his chest, each hack sounding louder against the metal street lamps. Across the harbor, Lydia Vane stood beneath a flickering lantern. A jade figurine lay near her hand, its green veins catching the oily glow, though who truly possessed it remained unclear. An envelope sat on his desk, its seal unbroken, hinting at the promised payment that could fund the experimental medicine he needed. He watched the dock’s shadows gather, silhouettes that suggested the Crimson Syndicate was already closing in. The sirens grew louder with each breath he drew, a reminder that the clock was ticking faster than his weakened heart. He turned toward the water’s edge, every step a thin strand between life and the inevitable darkness waiting beyond the pier.
