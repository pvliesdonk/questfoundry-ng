Elliot watched as rain hammered the metal rails of Greywater Docks, each strike a cold echo against the night.
Lydia Vane's car slipped onto the pier, tires shrieking on the slick boards, its emerald dress a flash under the trembling oil lanterns.
From the darkness beside the cargo crates, three men in Crimson Syndicate coats emerged, rifles glinting as they took aim at the crate that held the jade.
The driver eased the vehicle into the shadows, and Lydia stepped out, her silver hair catching a stray spark of light.

The emissary, a lean figure with a scar across his cheek, placed a sealed glass vial on the battered table between them.
His voice was low, firm. 'Medicine, for a share of the jade,' he said, eyes flicking to the green veins of the figurine.

Elliot felt his cough rise, a ragged rasp that tore through the cold air, a thin wind of pain threading through his chest.
It reminded him of the deadline he could not ignore.

Rain hammered the metal rails again, each drop a staccato that matched the gunmen's steady breathing.
Elliot stared at the standoff, the jade glowing faintly beneath the lanterns, and understood that if the figurine left this dock, the city would drown in blood.
