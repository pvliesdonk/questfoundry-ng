Rain fell in a thin sheet, turning the harbor's stone into a mirror of darkness. Elliot stood beneath the flickering oil lantern, its oily glow catching the dull green of broken jade scattered in the gutter. The shards lay like dead fireflies, a cold reminder that the Crimson Syndicate now squeezed the city's pulse. He felt the experimental serum warm his veins, a steady pulse replacing the ragged cough that had haunted his nights. The medicine settled deep, buying him months he could count in quiet breaths. Around him, the Syndicate men lifted a heavy crate, their boots splashing through puddles as they secured the fragment for the carriage waiting beyond the fog. Their leader, scarred and silent, nodded once, sealing the pact that gave the Syndicate a share of the Jade Dragon's power. Elliot watched the carriage wheels roll away, the rain hissing off the steel rails, and knew the city's trade routes would now bend to their will. He tucked the vial into his coat, the heat in his chest a thin shield against the night’s chill.
