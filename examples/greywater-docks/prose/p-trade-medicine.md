The rain hammered the cracked planks as Elliot stepped onto the dock, his coat swaying with each gust. He slid the jade fragment across the oil‑slick table toward the Syndicate emissary, the green veins glinting in the lantern light. The man in the scarred coat took the shard without a word, his fingers brushing the surface as if confirming its weight. He lifted the glass vial, its contents glowing faintly, and pressed the needle into his forearm. He felt a warm surge flood his veins, pushing the disease back into the shadows of his ribs. The coughing that had haunted his nights fell to a ragged gasp, steadied by the serum’s heat. The night deepened, the lanterns sputtering, and a pair of couriers slipped a sealed crate toward the dark carriage waiting beyond the dock. Elliot watched the men lift the jade shard into the carriage, their shoulders tense under the rain‑slick sky. The couriers disappeared into the fog, the carriage’s wheels turning slowly as they vanished. He knew the pact was sealed; the Syndicate now held a share of the jade, a vice tightening around the trade guilds. The warmth in his blood bought him months, enough to see the tide turn at least once more.
