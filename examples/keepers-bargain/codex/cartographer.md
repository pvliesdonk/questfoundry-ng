title: 'Elias Wren, Cartographer'
---

Elias Wren arrived at the Stilt Light aboard the coastal survey's boat,
chart cases in hand and ink already at his cuffs, hired to put the shoals
on paper at last — a stretch of water that has resisted accurate charting
for as long as anyone has tried. He is curious to a fault, the sort of
visitor who asks one question more than manners strictly allow, and
somehow means every one of them kindly. His soundings are, by his own
account, instruments he trusts more than his own instincts. What he makes
of the figures the shoals have lately been giving him — depths where the
charts say there should be none — he has not yet decided how to explain,
even to himself.
