title: The Keeper of the Stilt Light
---

Maren Voss is the third of her family to keep the Stilt Light, having taken
up the post the way her mother and her grandmother did before her: by
inheritance, by long habit, and by a ledger she reads more often than she
lets on. She is economical with words and exact with rope and wick, a
keeper's keeper, unsurprised by the shoals' long storm season or by the
loneliness of the work. Visitors to the light find her plainspoken and
unhurried, weathered hands quick at the lamp's small daily tasks, her eyes
the grey of the harbor water she has watched longer than she has watched
anything else. What the ledger asks of her, and what she means to give it,
is not a question she answers for strangers — nor, some nights, for
herself.
