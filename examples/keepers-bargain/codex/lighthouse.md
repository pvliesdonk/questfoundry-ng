title: The Stilt Light
---

Raised on rust-streaked iron stilts above the outer shoals, the Stilt
Light has warned ships off the reef for three generations of keepers, each
trained to the same exacting rhythm: trim the wick, feed the lamp
whale-oil by the cupful, and hold the flame steady through whatever the
season sends. The lamp room itself is brass gone soft with salt-bloom,
polished bright in patches by hands long since passed on. The post keeps a
ledger of its own — terms, debts, and duties recorded keeper to keeper in
a careful hand — though what exactly binds each Keeper to the work is a
matter the coast prefers not to ask aloud. Sailors navigating the shoals
watch for its light before anything else; what the light is truly meant to
hold steady, they leave for the keepers themselves to know.
