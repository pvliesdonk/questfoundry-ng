title: What the Shoals Hide
---

Sailors who work the water off the Stilt Light tell of a shape beneath the
waves too large for any sounding line — patient, they say, as the tide
itself, and never seen whole by living eyes. Keepers who have held the
light across three generations speak of it only sideways, in old ledger
terms passed hand to hand, and no chart has ever fixed its edges. On still
nights the iron stilts are said to hum faintly, as though something below
has only shifted in its rest. Whether the thing is myth, memory, or the
plain truth of deep water, no surveyor's instrument has yet resolved —
though more than one visiting cartographer has come away from the shoals
with soundings he could not explain, and depths that argued with every
chart he owned.
