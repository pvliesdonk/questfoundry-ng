## lighthouse illustration single light source composition

### craft/illustration.md#Working a single working light source
When a story commits to one working light source — a lamp, a lantern, a
single window — let every plate obey it: shadows fall away from that one
point, and anything the light doesn't reach goes to flat dark rather than
ambient fill. The discipline reads as control, which suits a keeper who
has spent a life tending exactly one flame; it also means the lamp itself
can carry the plate's only warmth, with everything else — coat, rope,
water — left to cool iron greys. Resist the temptation to backlight a
second source "for readability": a folk-horror plate earns its dread by
letting the dark stay dark right up to the edge of what the lamp claims.
