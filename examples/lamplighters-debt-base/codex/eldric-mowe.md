title: Councilman Eldric Mowe
---

Tall and stooped, as though the years had bent him toward the water he serves. You hear him before you see him: the silver watch-chain worried between two fingers, a small metal weather that precedes his gentle voice. He speaks softly and never straight, and he has come to believe that a kindness withheld is a kindness given. Now he sets his weight behind the wiring of the ring — copper and current to replace the old flames, to close some account he never names aloud. He calls this mercy. He calls the ending of a long payment relief. Watch the chain in his hand; it tells the truth his mouth walks around. What debt an elder pays without being asked, and to whom, he keeps folded in his coat like a receipt he means to burn.
