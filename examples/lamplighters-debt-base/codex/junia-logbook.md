title: Junia's Logbook
---

A route-ledger of swollen, waterlogged leather, its pages breathing the smell of algae. Front to back it records the ordinary sacraments of the trade: columns numbered, oil measured, nights kept. Then the neat hand begins to lean and hurry, and the last pages give themselves over to a map — something vast, drawn circling beneath the canals in strokes that trail off mid-line, as though the drawing had been interrupted rather than finished. Its final stroke of ink stays wet. Not smeared, not spreading, not drying with the years — simply never setting, wet as the moment the pen was lifted, whatever the heat or air around it. It sat sealed inside the dark lamp until it came again into a Veil's hands. To open it is to hold an unfinished sentence in one's lap and feel it waiting for a hand to complete.
