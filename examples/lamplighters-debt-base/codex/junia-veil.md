title: Junia Veil
---

Drowned, and known now only by the marks her hand left behind. There is no portrait, no grave that keeps her; there is a logbook swollen with canal-water, a map drawn in ink that will not dry, and the shape of a work broken off partway. Read her early pages and find a lamplighter meticulous to the letter — every column numbered, every oil-measure kept. Read toward the end and feel the hand quicken, the letters lean and crowd, the neat rows fraying into something reaching. Whatever she was following down beneath the ring, she followed it past the edge of her caution. She was mother to Tamsin, keeper of a route, and she left the last line unfinished. The town remembers her the way it remembers the drowned: gently, and from a careful distance.
