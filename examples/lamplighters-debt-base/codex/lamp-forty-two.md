title: The Dark Lamp (Lamp Forty-Two)
---

Forty-two of the ring's columns; the one that is never lit. Its iron has gone black and corroded green, salt-crusted to the touch, and where the others hold flame this one holds hollow. Lay a palm against it and feel a faint hum before you hear a sound at all — a tremor that climbs the stone and iron up into the soles of anyone who stands too near. From within comes the knocking: slow, patient, keeping a rhythm older than the town's memory, carried up through the tower even at distance. Here, they say, Junia sealed her logbook away. Here the lamplighters walk softest, and count fastest, and do not linger. It waits. It has waited a long time. Whatever it counts by that steady knock, it has never once lost the number.
