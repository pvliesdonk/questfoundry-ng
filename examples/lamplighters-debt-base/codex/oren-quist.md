title: Oren Quist
---

Brought over from the mainland to wire the canal ring, young and untroubled by dark water. His forearms carry the copper burns of his trade, and his grin comes easy — at first. He is practical and kind, an outsider who asks aloud the questions the town has learned to swallow: why the lamp is left unlit, why the count matters, what the humming is. Hand him a thing he does not understand and he touches it with a single finger, testing, before he lets his whole hand commit. He does not fear the dark. But the lamps of this place do not stay silent for strangers, and night by night his grin grows thin. He came to bring light and found the columns had something to say to him first. Whether he stays to listen is not a thing the almanac can promise.
