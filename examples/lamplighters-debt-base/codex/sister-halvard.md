title: Sister Halvard
---

Last keeper of the old lamplighter creed, she wears an ash-grey habit that smells of lamp-oil no washing lifts, and her left eye has gone milky as tallow. She is serene beyond argument. Where the town lights the ring against the thing below, she lights it for the thing below — an offering, a tenderness owed, a small warmth held out nightly to what waits in the dark water. She speaks of the knocking the way one speaks of an old friend who has grown patient in waiting. To her the flames are not a wall but a kindness, and to let them go out is not danger but discourtesy. She will teach anyone who asks, and unsettle everyone who listens. Comfort and cage, she would say, are only two words for the same faithful fire.
