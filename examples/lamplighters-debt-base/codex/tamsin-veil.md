title: Tamsin Veil
---

Nineteen, and newly sworn to the route her mother walked and drowned upon. She goes cloaked in Junia's oversized oilskin, hands already brine-chapped, hair the colour of wet rope. Quiet by habit, she reads a room before she opens her mouth, and reads the water the same way. Each night she lights the ring and counts the lamps — every column, without fail — for to lose the count frightens her worse than any dark. She has inherited the oil-tins, the ledger-keys, and a map her mother left half-drawn, its last line still wet. Before her lies the plainest and cruellest of questions: whether to take up the pen and finish what Junia was reaching for, or leave the ink unfollowed and the silence whole. She is the last of the Veils to carry the flame. What she does with it, no almanac can foretell.
