She takes the pen from where it has always been, tucked in the ledger's spine, cold as a nail. She opens the book against her forearm. The last page turns up its wet line, that single stroke trailing off into blank grain, waiting the way a held breath waits.

Tamsin sets the nib to the ink.

It takes. Her mother's line and her own line are the same line now, no seam between them, and she draws where the wet wants to go. The pen does not lead her and she does not lead the pen. They agree. The stroke curves out from where Junia stopped, long and slow, the way water curves around a piling, and Tamsin follows it because there is nowhere else the ink will let it end.

The shape comes up under her hand. Not fast. Nothing here is fast.

She draws its edge and the edge does not close for a long time, and that is the first thing the map tells her: how far it reaches. She had thought it something beneath a lamp. It is beneath all the lamps. It is beneath the whole ring and the water the ring holds and the town the water carries, and the strokes she is laying down are not its body but its orbit, the patient circle it has worn into the dark of two hundred years, the slow turning of a thing that has all the time there is.

The last stroke meets the first.

The map closes.

And she knows it now, the whole of it, the size and the slowness and the terrible evenness of its going round, and the knowing settles into her the way cold settles into iron, all through, nothing left dry. She cannot blot it. She cannot let it dry and flake away. It is in the grain of her.

Beneath her the knock changes.

Not louder. The pitch of it shifts, drops, opens, the way a voice changes when the door it has been knocking at finally gives. Two hundred years of the same slow reaching stroke, and now the stroke has been answered in kind, drawn to its close, met. The column hums against her hip through the oilskin. Halvard's breath goes out of her, soft, glad, a woman hearing the latch lift.

Tamsin does not move. The pen is still wet in her fingers. Below the green iron and the black water the thing she has drawn turns toward the answer it was owed.
