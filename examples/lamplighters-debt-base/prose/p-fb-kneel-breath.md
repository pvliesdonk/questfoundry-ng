There is a way of breathing her mother taught her without words. Tamsin knows this now. Down and slow, the chest kept still, the count carried not in the head but somewhere lower, in the place where the cold has settled. She lets the air out until there is nothing left to let, and then a little more, and in that emptied moment the knock comes up through the iron into her cheekbone and she takes the next breath on its heels, timing herself to it, folding her want into its patience.

At first she chases it. She is always a half-beat behind, breathing after the knock instead of with it, the way a mourner comes late to a hymn and stumbles through the line already sung. She stops chasing. She waits at the bottom of the breath in the dark under herself and lets the knock find her there. Then the two things touch. Her lungs fill as the sound rises through the stone and empty as it settles, and the seam between her breath and its knocking closes so quietly she does not feel it close.

The hum is steady in her teeth. Her pulse has already slackened to meet it, and now the breath comes down to lie alongside them both, three slow things keeping one time at the water's edge. She does not think of Oren, who may still be up above with the truth of it heavy in him, or may already be gone into the fog she sent him toward. She does not think of the map left half-drawn and its wet ink going dry. She does not think. There is only the going down and the coming up, and the cold iron against her face, and the tide that is not hers pulling her breath along with the tenderness of a hand at the back of the neck.

One and then the next. She loses the edges of herself. She is nineteen and she is two hundred years old and she is neither, she is only the listening, breathing what the drowned thing breathes if it breathes at all, or breathing for it, giving it the rhythm of a living body the way you might warm a cold thing against your own skin because there is nothing else to give.

The knock does not hurry her. It never has. It has waited this long and it can wait through one more breath, and she gives it that breath, and the next, and does not lift her head. She is with it now. Wholly. Whatever it was owed, she is here to pay it, slow and steady, in the only coin she has.
