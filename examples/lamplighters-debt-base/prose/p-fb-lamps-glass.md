She does not answer him. There is nothing to say that would not make it worse, and she has spent her whole life learning the shape of that silence. Instead she crosses the last stretch of stone and lays her palm flat against the black column.

The glass is warm. This is the wrong thing about it, the thing no one who has not touched it can be made to understand. The lamp is unlit. It has been unlit for two hundred years, its iron gone green with the canal's slow breath, and still the glass holds a warmth like a fever held under skin. She has felt it before. She feels it now through the brine-chap of her hand, and beneath the warmth the knock comes up to meet her.

Here. Here. Here.

It travels the bone of her wrist. It is patient the way the tide is patient, not hurrying, never once hurrying, and that is the part that undoes her. A thing that hurries can be refused. This only waits. It has always only waited. She keeps her hand where it is and lets the rhythm walk up her arm and into the small dark of her chest, and there, pressed under the plain knock, is the other thing. The shape inside it. A sound that leans against the knock the way a mouth leans against a wall to be heard through it.

Almost a word. Almost her own name, spoken too softly, or a name older than hers worn down to nearly the same syllables. She holds her breath to catch it and it slides just under the surface of hearing, the way a face slides under water and keeps its features a moment longer than it should. She tells herself she does not know it. She goes on not knowing it with her palm still flat to the glass, long past the point where knowing or not knowing makes any difference to her feet, which have not moved.

Oren says her name. She hears it as if from the far bank of something. The green flame at her back keeps its small breath, kind and useless, holding back what it has always held back or holding it close, she has never once been sure which. The knock answers her hand. She answers nothing. She only stays, and stays, because leaving now would mean deciding what the sound is, and she is not yet ready to have decided.

Her mother stood here. Her mother's hand knew this warmth. And somewhere in the oilskin, folded against her ribs, the map her mother left waits with its line running out into wet ink and open water.
