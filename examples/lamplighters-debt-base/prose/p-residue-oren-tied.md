The wire comes apart under their hands in the same motion, his stripping the sheath while she holds the copper true against the terminal. She has done none of this before. She knows it anyway, the way she knows the count, from the shape his fingers make and the small pressures he gives her wrist when he wants a thing held or let go. They do not speak the work. The work speaks itself between them, and the flames burn green down the wall, and the knock keeps time under it all as though it has always kept time for this.

He pauses. He does not set the pliers down so much as let them rest, and she feels the pause before she hears it, the way the tide goes still before it turns.

"I came here to wire a ring," he says. His voice is low, pitched under the flames. "That was the whole of it. Do the work, take the pay, go back to the mainland where the lamps stay quiet."

She waits. She has learned to leave a room the space to finish itself.

"I don't know where my end of it is anymore." He turns the copper over in his fingers, that single-finger testing gone now, his whole hand committed to something he cannot name. "Where mine stops and yours starts. I can't find the seam. I've looked for it."

The salt sits on her lip. She does not tell him there is no seam. He knows. He has strung his fear along the same copper as hers, and now the fear is doubled, two sets of listening feet where there was always one, and the doubling should be worse. It is worse. It is also the first thing this season that has not been hers to carry alone in the dark.

"Then we're afraid of the same thing," she says. Her breath fogs a little against the cold iron. "Both of us. All the way down."

He nods. He does not reach for a comfort-word either. He takes up the pliers again, and she takes up her end, and their hands move over the same wiring as though they were one pair, working a thing too heavy for either to hold.

The coil rests against her knee. The wet ink will not dry. Below them the knock waits, patient, owed something neither of them will name, and the wicks are still there to be tended before the night finishes its measure.

The dark asks to be answered. She reaches for the wicks.
