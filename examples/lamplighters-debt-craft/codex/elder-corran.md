title: Elder Corran Mow
---

Longest-serving of the council, Elder Corran speaks softly and apologizes often, his cuffs stained dark with ink from a lifetime of signing what others would not. He keeps one hand folded into his coat pocket, where a tremor he does not name lives out its days. For forty years his signature has closed the ledger that pays the family to keep a certain house and a certain silence. Now, wearied, he argues that the town should run current through the ring and be done with the old labor. When he offers a bargain he turns both palms open and tilts them forward, laying the terms flat on the air, so that a frightened person might take what is offered without seeming to reach for it. He is gentle in everything but what he will not say aloud.
