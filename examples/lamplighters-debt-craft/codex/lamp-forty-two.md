title: The Dark Lamp
---

The forty-second lamp stands above the deepest bend of the canal, a cold iron column blistered with old paint, its glass housing empty and stained green though no flame has burned there in two hundred years. On a route of hissing lights it is the one silent thing. In Marren Veil's logbook a single word stands beside its number: don't. From below the water comes a knock — one patient sound, never doubled, always followed by stillness, as of a thing keeping count. A stair descends from its base toward the water, worn hollow in the middle by generations of lamplighters' boots, though the logbook gives no reason any of them should have gone down. Marren scored a mark into the column's foot before she died. What it seals, and what it warns, the almanac cannot say.
