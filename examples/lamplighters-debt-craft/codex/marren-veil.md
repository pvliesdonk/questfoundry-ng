title: Marren Veil
---

Marren Veil tended the ring before her daughter, and drowned in the black water below it. Those who remember her recall a woman crouched over a lamp in the green light, always writing. She reveals herself now only through her handwriting: her corrections, her instructions, and the things she took care to leave unsaid. Her logbook lays down a lamplighter's first law — never leave a flame you have not watched settle. She was exact in this, exact in all things, patient as a woman teaching a child to pinch a wick without flinching. Her last work is a map, its ink still wet, showing the shape of something that circles beneath the canals. What she meant by it she did not stay to say. She is a voice from under the water, and reading her is the only way to hear it.
