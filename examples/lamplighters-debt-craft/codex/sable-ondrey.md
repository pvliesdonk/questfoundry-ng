title: Sable Ondrey
---

Sable Ondrey is the last of the family that the town has quietly paid, generation upon generation, never to ask why the forty-second lamp stays dark. In a town of mended coats her clothes go unmended, whole and unfrayed, and the difference shows at a glance. She wears the key to a locked house on a chain, and when she is uncertain she worries it against her collarbone until the habit has worn her thin there. She is defensive, brittle, and aching to be forgiven for a silence she was born into rather than chose. The comfort that raised her was bought with a question she was forbidden to ask, and she has never decided whether she wants the answer or only the peace of never learning it.
