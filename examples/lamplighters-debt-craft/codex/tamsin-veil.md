title: Tamsin Veil
---

Nineteen years old and newly sworn to her drowned mother's route, Tamsin walks the ring in an oilskin coat cut for a larger woman. Her hands are salt-chapped, her hair kept short so it will not catch the flame. She carries her mother's wick-hook, worn to the shape of a grip that is not hers, and a striker no other hand was ever permitted to touch. She is quiet and watchful, and she speaks to the dead through the things they left behind, as if the tools might answer. Her mother taught her to pinch a wick between wet fingers, quick and sure, without flinching from the heat. As a sleepless child she would call up the stairs and wait for the boots on the boards and the green light bending under the door. Now she must weigh the duty she inherited against the truth it was built to hide.
