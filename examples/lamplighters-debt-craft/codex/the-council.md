title: The Veilsmeer Council
---

The Veilsmeer Council governs the town and its ring of green lamps, and tonight it meets to vote on whether to electrify the columns and retire the old flames. Its members are cordial and tired, and by most accounts they are of one mind: they would like the past to switch off cleanly, the labor ended, the expense closed, the strange green light replaced with something ordinary. They speak of progress and of thrift. They do not, in open session, speak of what the flames were kept for, nor of the ledger that has been paid year upon year to a certain family. It is a body that wishes to solve a thing by ceasing to look at it, and it approaches that wish with the mild, weary courtesy of people who have decided already and only want it over.
