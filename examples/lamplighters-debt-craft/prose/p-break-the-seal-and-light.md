You take up the seal first, because the seal must come away before anything else can begin. It is old lead, poured two centuries deep into the housing seam, gone the grey-white of a drowned thing. Your mother's hook fits the groove as if it had waited for this hand and no other. You work it slow. The lead gives with a sound like ice letting go of a stone, and it drops into your palm, still holding the shape of the mold that made it, still holding the intent of whoever pressed it there and wrote the small word beside its number that meant only this. You set it on the ledge beside the ledger you will not open. Two things kept shut. One of them opening now.

The glass housing is empty and stained the color it will soon burn. You feed the wick through. Your fingers know the motion; she taught them, the pinch and the pull, quick and sure, without flinching from the heat. Sable watches. She does not offer her hands and you do not want them. This is not a thing done together. This is a thing done in front of a witness so that later there will have been one.

You lift the striker. No one else was ever let to hold it. You hold it now the way she held it, and you strike.

The flame catches green and climbs. It does not gutter or test the air. It roars up full and certain above the deepest bend, and the light bends under nothing, spills across the black water and the crooked stone and the hollow the boots wore down, and for one long breath the whole of it is lit and living and yours. Below, the knock does not come. The water waits, patient, keeping its count, and then it lets the count go. It settles. It holds its line. It goes quiet, and you understand that the quiet is not peace but distance, the small mercy of one more generation put between the town and the thing you are keeping in the dark.

You know now what the light is for. You know it is a wall and not a prayer. You stand in your mother's coat that is too large and will fit you soon enough, and you feel the vigil close around you the way the oilskin closes around your shoulders, warm and heavy and shaped by a woman who did this until it drowned her. The ring endures. Veilsmeer sleeps above you, spared the knowing, safe in the dark it voted to switch off and does not have. You pinch nothing out tonight. You leave forty-two burning. You take up the hook, and you climb the hollow stair, and you go on.
