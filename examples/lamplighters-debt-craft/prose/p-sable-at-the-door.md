You open the door and the night comes in with her. Sable stands on the step in a coat that fits, in shoes that have not walked the canal edge at low tide, the key on its chain caught against her collar where she has worried it thin. She does not come in. She waits at the threshold the way the water waits below, on the far side of a line she was raised never to cross.

"You're still up," she says. It is not what she came to say.

You stand aside. She looks past you at the room, at the tin, at the ledger lying grey against the green leather, and something in her face goes still. She has seen the seal before. You watch her see it and pretend she has not. Her hand rises toward the key at her throat and stops.

"My mother said your mother was fond of me," she says. "When I was small. She lit the lamps and I followed her once, down as far as the bend, and she carried me back and she never told me why we don't go past it." She stops. The words are too old for her mouth and she knows it. "Nobody ever told me why lamp forty-two stays dark. I stopped asking. You learn to stop."

You say nothing. On the table between you the seal holds its plain closed ring, the line drawn through, the same mark scored into the iron below the town, and you understand now what her house was paid to keep and what her name has been buying quiet for two hundred years, and she does not understand it yet. She reads your silence and mistakes it for the ordinary kind.

You keep your hand on the wick-hook. The map runs on under your thumb, the circle that does not close, and you hold the whole weight of it without a word because you have finished reading and cannot unread. To speak it is to hand it to her, and she will carry it after, into her fitted coat, into her dry house, forever. To let it burn is to let it die with the woman who drowned learning it, and take from Sable the answer she was forbidden and still, somehow, came here for.

Below you the water knocks once, and waits. Sable does not hear it. She is listening only to you.

"Tamsin," she says. "What did she find?"
