You hold still and you let the hush hold you.

It is not the silence of an empty room. It is the silence of a thing waited on. Down the row the flames go on breathing, small and green, each one a soft hiss you have walked past all your life without hearing, the way you never hear your own name until a stranger says it. Here there is no hiss. Here the iron has nothing to say. Lamp forty-two keeps a quiet that the other lamps are only pretending at, and now that you kneel inside it you understand the whole route has been keeping this quiet company, humming to cover the place where the humming stops.

Two hundred years of hands have knelt where you kneel. You feel them in the hollow of the stair, in the shape the wick-hook has taken, worn to a grip that was not yours first. They knelt and they said nothing. Not to the town, not to their children, not to the water. They came up the boards and they hung the coat on the peg and they let the quiet close over the day like a hand closing over a coin. That is what the hush is. Not peace. A kept thing. A word held so long it went hard as the iron it was written beside.

Your mother knelt here. She set her palm where your palm rests and she felt the seam and she wrote one word and she went back up and she did not tell you. She let you call her name up the stairs and she answered with boots on the boards and green light under the door and she never once let the silence slip and show you its underside. She carried this alone so you would not have to know its weight, and the carrying is why the water has her name in it now.

The hush is asking you to do the same. To keep it the way she kept it. To hang the coat on the peg and say nothing and let another child call up a dark stair one day and get boots in answer and never the truth.

You are done being kept from things. Whatever comes of the striker in your hand, whatever comes of the seal warm under your fingers, you will not add your silence to the pile. The knowing is yours now. You gathered it in the cold with your own counting hands, and no ledger, no stipend, no held word takes it back out of you.

You keep the truth in your own hands.
