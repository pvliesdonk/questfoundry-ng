You look, and the lamp looks back the way iron does, without any face to it. The green stands low in the housing. It has always stood low. Two hundred years of hands have kept it standing, striker to wick, wick to flame, a small unbroken tending passed down the stair from grip to grip until it reached yours.

You thought it was a keeping. You stand here now and it turns in you, slow, the way the ash turns when the dish shifts.

A lamp is a light. But a lamp is also a thing set in a row, and you have walked the row all your life, and you have never once thought of a row of lights as anything but a road home. You think of it differently now. You think of the struts. You think of the cold columns standing at their measured distance, one after another along the black water, evenly spaced, patient, and never moved.

Bars are set at a measured distance too.

The knock comes once from below. It does not double. It never doubles. It waits, and the water holds still after it, and you understand the stillness now as the stillness of something counting, something that has counted for a very long time and has nowhere to be.

Not worship. Not grief laid down in light for a thing to be honored. You feel the wrongness of that reading go out of you the way the map went out of the page. The flames were never given to it. The flames were given against it. Every hand that ever climbed the hollow stair came down to feed a fire, and the fire held the dark shut, and the thing in the dark has been kept from the light, not offered it, kept low and green and starving for two hundred years while lamplighters called this devotion.

You cannot prove it. The page is ash in the dish. You will never lay her map beside the shape below and see the two agree. You have only the ring of struts and the single patient knock and the certainty settling in you like the flame she taught you to wait on.

The not-knowing does not lift the weight. It presses it down. If you were only afraid you could doubt yourself. You are not afraid the way a person is afraid of the dark. You are afraid the way you are afraid of a thing you have already decided is true and cannot take back.

The green burns. The stone at your feet is wet, and the light lies across it in long divided lines. You have stood in those lines every night of your working life and called them the safe part of the path.
