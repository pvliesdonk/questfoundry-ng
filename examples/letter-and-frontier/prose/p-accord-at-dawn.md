A thin wind brushed the stone threshold as Elara stepped into the vaulted chamber.
The guards lowered the portcullis without delay, their faces set in the calm of a tired watch.
Elara placed the scarlet‑waxed letter on the cold marble altar and felt the seal press a familiar chill against her palm.
A voice rose from the dais, the queen’s envoy, and he read the opening line aloud, his tone steady and unadorned.
Courtiers leaned forward, their eyes brightened as the proposal unfolded, and the weight of war slipped from their shoulders for a moment.
One noble whispered that the river borders could be shared, and another nodded, saying the fields would feed both peoples.
Elara listened to each argument, noting how the words seemed to carve a path toward peace rather than further enmity.
The discussion moved quickly, the council refusing to linger on doubts, and the envoys exchanged glances of cautious hope.
At last the chief councilor raised his hand and declared that a tentative accord would be sealed before sunrise, binding the two courts in a fragile truce.
Elara felt the scarlet wax warm under the torchlight, a reminder that her oath to the River Clan still lingered, but the new promise steadied her resolve.
The hall opened to a muted dawn, the stone walls catching the first light as the parties stepped toward the outer terrace.
