Elara stepped into the low vaulted alcove, the crimson seal radiating a faint heat against her palm. She reached across the stone table and placed the sealed parchment into Vara's waiting hand, a brief smile flickering across her mouth as the notion of an emerging pact brushed her thoughts. Vara's thin mask caught the torchlight, their voice low as they said, “The frontier will shift when oaths change hands.” A River Clan rider stood near a battered banner, the spear he rested in the dust striking a faint arc in the stone. He tipped his head toward a shadowed opening in the wall, a narrow throat that slipped away from the crowded courtyard. The air carried a hint of pine resin, and the distant river murmur seemed further behind the stone. Elara felt the weight of the seal pressing against her cheek, a reminder of the queen’s command and the promise looming in that hidden route. She gripped the letter tighter, aware that the next step would bind her either to the concealed passage the clan guarded or to the open dunes beyond the fort.
