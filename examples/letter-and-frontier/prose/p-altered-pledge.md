Elara slipped the quill beneath the rim of the scarlet wax, its nib whispering against the parchment as she traced the clause that bound the kingdoms. The ink formed the words “mutual stewardship” where the original had spoken of a single ruler’s dominion, the new phrase settling into the margin like cold stone set beside a fire. She pressed the tip of the quill to the seal, feeling the familiar pulse of the queen’s emblem under her thumb.

Lord Varian took the treaty from the table, his fingers still stained with the fort’s iron stamp. He read the altered line aloud, his voice steady in the muted hall. He lifted his chin, nodded once, and said, “This is a step toward lasting peace.”

Elara watched his eyes flick over the line she had changed. A tightness grew in her chest as the realization settled: the queen’s words now bore her hand. She pressed her thumb against the wax, feeling the calm certainty that had steadied her earlier.

She straightened her posture, sensing the lords’ gazes turning toward her. Then she bowed low, listening as Meros, the court’s advisor, said in a voice barely above a whisper, “Your counsel has been a boon.”
