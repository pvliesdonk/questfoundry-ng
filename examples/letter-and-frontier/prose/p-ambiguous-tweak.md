Elara stood in the vaulted hall, the sealed parchment pressed to her breast. The draft of the peace proposal mentioned a dawn of shared fields, its language stripped of borders or dates, leaving the meaning open. The courtiers in dark cloaks watched her with thin eyes.

The ambiguous lines lingered, and Elara felt the weight of each syllable settle against her ribs like a low drumbeat. She ran a fingertip over the curve of the phrase, sensing how a single word might shift the sense of the whole.

She knew that a subtle tweak could nudge the council toward acceptance, yet the thought settled in her mind like cold wind against the queen’s oath, a betrayal she could not overlook.

She turned toward the courtiers, letting her words unfurl as their gazes sharpened. Her voice steadied, the seal cool against her palm.

She stepped toward the window, the draft stirring the leaves on the sill and sending rust‑colored dust swirling in the muted dusk. A few amber fireflies hovered near the wax, tracing the ink for a brief moment. Elara tightened her grip on the sealed letter, aware that any alteration would become a secret she alone would bear across the border.
