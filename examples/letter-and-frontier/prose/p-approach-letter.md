Elara felt the wax seal press into her palm as the messenger slipped the parchment to her. She tucked the sealed missive beneath her travel worn cloak, the faint scent of parchment mingling with the ember glow of the camp fire behind her. She mounted her horse and rode the southern road toward Stonegate Fort, the border wind biting her cheeks. Ahead the stone walls rose, and Malrik Stonehand stood by the gate, his scar catching the dim light as he watched each traveler. When she reached the gate, she lifted the envelope and pressed it into Malrik's rough palm. On the far side of the entrance a forest clerk leaned against a moss covered stone, his eyes flicking between the gate and her. She turned and offered the sealed letter to the clerk, his fingers brushing the wax as he examined it. She turned the seal over, its scarlet wax dulled by dust, and traced the royal emblem with a fingertip. A memory rose of the queen’s brother, a cartographer who hid maps inside such sealed missives, sealing routes beneath layers of wax.
