The clan’s banner fluttered, a thin promise threading through the dust that clung to the plain. Elara stepped beneath the ragged cloth, feeling the weather‑worn fabric brush the side of her face. The River Clan rider stood before her, his cloak still speckled with rust‑colored sand, his eyes fixed on the satchel she had opened. “We know a passage hidden from the patrols,” he said, “and the price will be settled later.” The chief’s scarred cheek caught the lantern’s dim light as he echoed the bargain, his voice as hard as stone. Elara watched the dried meat and cracked water skin she had laid out, each piece a lonely mound on the rough table. She ran her fingers over the scant rations, counting them one by one, the numbers a stark reminder of how thin the journey would be. The banner snapped in the cold wind, a reminder that the open plain stretched beyond the camp, and the seal on the letter remained intact beneath her palm. The offer settled in the space between her duty and the debt she might owe.
