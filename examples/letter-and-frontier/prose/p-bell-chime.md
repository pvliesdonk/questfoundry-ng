A low knell rolled from the tower beyond the courtyard, its vibration threading through the stone. Elara pressed the sealed letter against her breast, the scarlet wax warm beneath her palm. The sound cut the quiet that had settled over the hall, and she felt the queen’s oath pulse in tandem with the bell. Around her, the lords’ murmurs faltered, and the elder’s eyes lingered on the crimson seal. She inhaled the faint scent of pine resin that clung to the stone floor, a reminder of the forest outside. The distant chime steadied her thoughts, narrowing the turmoil that had swirled since she first lifted the seal. Elara tightened the strap of her satchel, the weight of the parchment steady as the ringing tone. The elder raised his hand, his beard catching a stray ember glow, and spoke again, his voice low. "Patience will preserve what the seal protects," he said, and the hall held its breath. The bell’s reverberation lingered, anchoring her resolve like stone against the wind. Elara stood firm, the seal unblemished, the echo sealing her decision as the final toll faded into the dusk.
