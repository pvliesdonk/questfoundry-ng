The stone markers that lined the road ahead softened, their edges merging into faint smudges of gray. Elara watched as the rows dissolved like breath on cold stone, each outline becoming a suggestion rather than a path. The wind carried a thin rust‑colored dust that settled on her cloak, and the distant murmur of the river seemed farther away, as if the world were pulling apart at the seams. She felt the sealed wax against her cheek, a steady pressure that contrasted with the loose certainty of the fading stones. Around her, the border camp’s green‑tabarded envoy lingered near a low wooden gate, his eyes still scanning the horizon. The soldiers at the wall shifted, their spears catching the last amber light. Elara’s breath came shallow, the frontier’s weight settling heavier on her shoulders, while the blurred markers whispered of roads that might split—one toward the fortified stone of Stonegate, another across the open plain where the River Clan rode unseen. A quiet resolve hardened within her; she would keep the letter hidden, however the road ahead unfurled.
