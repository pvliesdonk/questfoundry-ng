The southern road unfolded toward the border camp, flags snapping in the high wind. The camp rose on the edge of the plain, canvas tents huddled like a reluctant flock, lanterns casting amber circles on rust‑colored dust. Elara guided her horse to the sanded stone circle, unshod the animal, and stepped onto the ground, the scarlet wax of the sealed letter pressed against the side of her cloak.

From the doorway of a roughly hewn tent a figure emerged, silvery hair catching the firelight, a half‑mask framing a faint wisp of incense. It was Vara Shadowmantle, the envoy of the rival queen, and they gestured to an empty seat beside the fire. “Take a moment, courier,” they said, eyes measuring. “Your swiftness has earned the respect of many borders.” Elara lowered herself onto the bench, keeping the letter close, and watched the flames lurch upward, ember glow catching the dust.

The weight of the oath she had carried since youth pressed against her shoulders like a stone burden. Each breath seemed to pull the frontier tighter around her, a border she could no longer cross without leaving the life that had defined her. She lifted the sealed letter, fingers tightening the wax, then set her feet down and strode away from the fire, the scarlet seal steady against her breast.
