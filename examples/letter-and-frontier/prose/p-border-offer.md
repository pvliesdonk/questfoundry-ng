The southern road rolled onward toward the border camp, dust rising in thin clouds as flags on wooden poles caught the wan light. Elara felt the wind tug at her cloak and the scarlet wax of the queen’s seal press cold against her cheek. A low wooden gate marked the camp’s entrance, and a figure in a dull green tabard stood beside it, his eyes scanning the horizon. He raised a hand as she approached, and his voice carried over the dull murmur of soldiers.

“I am the queen’s envoy,” he said. “Your reputation reaches even the court’s walls. The queen would welcome you among her counselors, a seat at her throne where your skill could shape the realm.”

Elara tightened her grip on the sealed letter, the wax still intact beneath her cloak. The offer landed heavy on her shoulders. She watched the seal glow faintly in the fading light and sensed the weight of abandoning the identity she had known since youth. The road beyond the camp waited, indifferent to her decision, while the envoy’s invitation lingered like a distant drumbeat.

Clench the sealed letter and stride onward, refusing the throne’s promise.
