Elara stood in the stone corridor as the chief lifted his gaze. His nod settled like a stone, a quiet affirmation that bound her to the River Clan. The rust‑stained banner hung limp against the cold walls, and the spear he had planted caught a thin slice of dusk. Riders shifted, their hooves whispering over the floor, and the concealed arch opened further into the depths. Elara bowed, offering the debt she owed to the chief, and his glinted eyes accepted. The queen’s red wax warmed against the inside of her cloak, a reminder of the oath she still carried. The riders seemed ready to escort her across the plain, though the path ahead remained uncertain. The favor she had promised might shape the fragile peace of the frontier, an outcome she could not yet predict. She tightened the straps of her satchel, feeling the weight of the pact settle across her shoulder as the clan’s spearmen took up their positions.
