The clan chief's words rose like a thin thread in the cold dusk, a promise that could bind her to the River Clan's tangled politics. The echo of that promise lingered as she laid the meager rations on the rough table, counting each piece of dried meat and cracked water skin one by one. The numbers stared back at her, a stark ledger of how thin the journey ahead would be, and the future debt they implied pressed against the scarlet seal in her palm. She felt the weight of the oath and the subtle pull of the clan's loyalty, each bite of the dry meat a reminder of what she might owe for safe passage. The distant war drums rolled over the plain, a low thunder that hinted at the looming conflict beyond the ridge. In the flickering lantern light she imagined a quiet vow, a pact sealed by her whispered agreement, and the thought of calling the riders to stand against that war trembled in her mind. The banner above rusted in the wind, and the night held its breath as she balanced duty with the cost of a favor.
