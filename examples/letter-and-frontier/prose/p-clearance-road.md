Elara stepped into the clearing of the rival court’s hilltop hall, the sealed parchment trembling in her clasped hands. The massive doors thudded shut behind her, and the forest trail beyond merged into a rough road that cut across the plain. The road turned to uneven stone and loose soil; as she shifted the cloak aside she saw a faint glint beneath the bark of a twisted tree—a map, its lines barely visible, promising shortcuts through the open land. A thin slice of moon slipped through a cracked window, bathing the parchment in cold light; the wax seal caught the silver gleam, its edge crisp as bone. The scent of damp stone mingled with the distant rust of wind‑swept dust. Far on the horizon a ceremonial drum echo rolled over the plain, a low heartbeat that seemed to pulse in time with her own doubts. She pressed the letter to her chest, feeling the parchment’s weight against her spine, and let the drum’s rhythm guide her thoughts. The hidden map stared back beneath the bark, its promise tugging at the edge of her duty. She could study the moonlit parchment now, or follow the drum’s call toward the unknown.
