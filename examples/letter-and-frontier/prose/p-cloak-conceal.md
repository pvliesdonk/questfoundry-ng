Elara felt the heavy weave of her cloak settle against her back, a quiet barrier that concealed the scarlet wax from prying eyes. The stone floor beneath her boots was cold, and the muted dusk light filtered through the high windows, casting a thin strip of shadow across the hall. From the dim alcove, Vara Shadowmantle emerged, half‑mask catching the torch’s flicker. "The letter could secure the trade we need," Vara said, voice low as the border wind. Their hand hovered near the seam of Elara’s cloak, waiting. Elara’s thumb brushed the warm wax, still fresh, the imprint bearing a stamp that might have been Malrik’s, and a faint ember of doubt rose in her chest. She recalled a promise she might have made to the River Clan, the queen’s oath pressing against her resolve. The courtiers’ gazes drifted, but the cloth kept the danger concealed. She adjusted the knot at her collar, aware that her next movement would bind her fate. The sealed letter seemed hidden beneath the folds as she slipped it toward Vara’s outstretched hand.
