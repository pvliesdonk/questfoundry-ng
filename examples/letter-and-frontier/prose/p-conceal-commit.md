Elara felt the weight of the scarlet wax against her thumb as she drew the folded parchment back beneath the inner lining of her cloak. The fabric pressed cold against her skin, a quiet barrier that would not betray the queen’s seal. She glanced at Vara, whose mask caught the flicker of torchlight, and saw the thin line of doubt creep across the spymaster’s jaw. Without a word, Elara slipped the letter deeper, pressing the hem of the cloak over the seal until only the faint scent of pine resin lingered at the edge of her breath. The hall’s muted dusk light fell in a thin strip across the stone floor, and the distant murmur of the river beyond seemed to carry the weight of the oath she bore. Vara stepped forward, hand hovering near the seam, but Elara’s shoulders tightened, and she turned her back to the offer. The courtiers whispered, their gazes shifting like rust‑colored dust raised by a passing breeze, but the cloth held firm. A moment stretched, the border wind outside whispering through the narrow windows, and Elara’s resolve settled like an ember in her chest. She tightened the knot at her collar, feeling the promise of the River Clan pull at the edge of her thoughts, yet the sealed letter remained hidden, untouched. She tucked the sealed letter deeper into her cloak.
