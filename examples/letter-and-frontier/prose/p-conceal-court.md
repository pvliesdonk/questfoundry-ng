The council gathered in the great hall, the air thick with rust‑colored dust. Elara kept the sealed letter hidden beneath her cloak, the scarlet wax a cold pulse against her skin. Murmurs rose as the lords argued the prospect of peace, their words built on the rumor of a "dawn of shared fields" that had drifted through the border wind. Without the messenger’s parchment, the council could only grasp at half‑heard reports, their resolve wavering like a candle flame in a draft. Vara Shadowmantle watched Elara closely, his eyes narrowing as if reading a hidden script on her sleeve. He said, "Without proof, the queen’s oath is hollow," and the courtyard outside seemed to echo his doubt. The chief of the River Clan shifted his spear, a silent reminder of the pact Elara had sworn, but the weight of the concealed missive pressed harder. She felt the wind curl around the edge of her cloak, a fleeting chill that brushed the hidden truth she bore. The clamor of debate faded for a moment as she tightened the knot of her cloak, the sealed letter pressed to her chest, a secret she feared would drown the queen’s voice if it ever left her grasp.
