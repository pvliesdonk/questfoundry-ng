Elara stood in the shadow of the stone gateway, the faint ember of the queen’s seal still pulsing beneath her thumb. A guard’s low murmur faded as the parchment slipped into his hand, the ink settling into its subtly altered phrasing. The iron stamp’s imprint glimmered on the wax, a faint hum rising from the seal as if it remembered a promise. She glanced at the new line and saw the wording now hinted at concessions the queen had never meant to grant. The guard lifted the letter, his eyes flicking over the altered passage before tucking the missive into his satchel. Elara felt the weight of that hidden promise settle like cold stone against her spine. At the edge of the parchment the seal quivered, a soft vibration that beckoned her hand. The distant river’s murmur drifted beyond the fort, and the scent of pine resin mixed with the rust‑colored dust that clung to the stones. The moment lingered, a thin thread of possibility in the muted dusk, and her fingers hovered over the humming seal.
