The great hall opened onto a sea of stone benches, courtiers gathered, eyes like owls fixed on the doorway where Elara lingered. She felt the cool wax of the seal pressed against her palm, a mute reminder of the queen’s command. A low murmur rolled across the floor as the courtiers steadied their gaze, each breath measured in the stale air. Elara lifted the lid of the sealed letter just enough to glimpse the faded emblem, then closed it, the weight of promise settling in her chest. At the far wall an alcove of darker stone recessed beneath an arched column, its mouth a shadow that swallowed the torchlight. A figure stepped from the gloom, the hem of a muted cloak brushing the stone. The figure turned, revealing herself as Vara, the spymaster whose whisper had carried rumors of the frontier’s plight. A courtier said, "The seal remains unbroken," and his words fell like a soft drumbeat against the stone. Elara stepped forward, the floorboards sighing under her boots, and paused at the threshold of the alcove, aware that the choice she made now could tilt the fragile hope of peace.
