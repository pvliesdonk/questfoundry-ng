Elara stood before the great hall’s heavy doors, the sealed parchment still pressed to her chest. The wax seal bore the queen’s faded emblem, and the cramped script on the inner flap whispered of a dawn of shared fields, its meaning as thin as mist. The words lingered, each syllable settling on her like dew upon pine leaves. She breathed in the cold stone and felt the weight of the promise settle in her ribs. A distant wind sighed against the hall’s stone, threading through the narrow windows and stirring the dust that clung to the threshold. Elara listened to the wind, its mournful rush echoing the uncertainty that curled around the ambiguous lines. She knew that a subtle tweak to the phrasing could nudge the rival court toward acceptance, yet any alteration would betray the dying queen’s exacting command. The hallway beyond the doors waited, the courtiers’ murmurs already humming like low drums. Elara tightened her grip on the letter, the wax cool against her palm, and steadied herself for the choice that lay ahead.
