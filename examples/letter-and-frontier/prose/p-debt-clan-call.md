Elara moved deeper into the stone corridor, the weight of the queen’s scarlet seal pressing against the inner lining of her cloak. The River Clan chief stepped forward, his gloved hand resting briefly on her shoulder, and said, "Your debt summons our aid." Riders gathered, their spears catching the thin slice of dusk that fell through the archway. An ember smoldered on a low stone slab, its glow stirring rust‑colored dust that settled on the floor. Around it the clan formed a loose circle, voices low as they repeated the pledge that bound them to her. Elara slipped onto a low stone step and settled beside the ember, feeling its modest warmth while the clan renewed its oath. She drew the satchel strap tighter, the knot biting her wrist like the promise she had just uttered. The chief lifted his hand, signaling the riders to move. Their horses’ hooves whispered over the cold flagstones, keeping any shadow at bay. The ember’s light flickered against the rust‑stained banner as night deepened, a small fire around which the clan affirmed its vow to protect the messenger. Elara kept the sealed missive hidden beneath her cloak, the promise of a future favor heavy in her thoughts as the riders prepared to escort her across the plain.
