Elara stood on the stone platform outside the fort, the sealed letter warm against her palm. Malrik held the iron stamp in his gauntleted hand, the garrison emblem pressed into the scarlet wax. She felt the queen’s seal press into the skin of her thumb. The drumbeat rose from the hills, low and steady as distant thunder, and the vibration traveled through the pine‑cloaked ridge. It pulled her thoughts from the favor she might have whispered to the River Clan to the armies gathering beyond the border. The rhythm reminded her that the stakes stretched far beyond any private bargain. She glanced at the unblemished wax, aware that the iron stamp could alter the phrasing she guarded. The cold pine scent filled her lungs, and rust‑colored dust settled on her boots. She tightened her grip on the parchment, weighing the moment. She could pull back, refusing the stamp and leaving the queen’s seal untouched. Or she could press the iron seal onto the letter, binding the message to the fort’s authority. The echo of the drums grew louder, each thump a reminder that war waited beyond the frontier. She waited, poised between defiance and compliance, ready to decide.
