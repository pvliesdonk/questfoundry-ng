Elara stood in the shadow of the stone gate, the guard's spear angled toward her chest. The cold draft brushed the sealed parchment, echoing her hesitation. She could feel the scarlet wax against her thumb, unmoved, the queen's emblem a silent promise. The guard's eyes lingered on the seal, his breath forming thin clouds in the dusk air. She lifted the letter just enough for the metal clasp to catch the light, the wax catching the faint ember glow from a nearby lantern. A rust‑colored dust settled on the stone, the scent of pine resin mixing with the iron tang of spears. Her thoughts drifted between duty and the hidden map that might lie beneath the wax, a rumor she had heard in the forest clerk's mutterings. The weight of the oath pressed against her cheek, a pressure she could not ignore. She sensed the patrol's watchful stance, each step recorded in the garrison's silent ledger. The guard waited, his hand resting on the hilt, ready for her next move.
