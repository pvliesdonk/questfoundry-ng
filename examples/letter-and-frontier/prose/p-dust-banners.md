Elara stood where the rust‑stained banner rose from the post, its cloth catching the border wind and stirring dust like a thin thread of promise. The fabric rippled, pale colors slicing through the amber haze that settled over the clearing. She felt the coarse rope under her palm as she lifted the banner higher, the weave cool against her skin. The chief's silhouette loomed beside her, his spear still planted in the soft earth. He watched her hands, then inclined his head, voice low as river mud. "You bear the oath now," he said, and the words settled into the silent air. Elara lowered her gaze, the weight of his promise settling on her shoulder as solid as the sealed wax she still held. Around them, a small pack of supplies lay open: a leather satchel spilling a few cracked biscuits and a half‑full water skin, their edges frayed from the journey. The dwindling provisions reminded her of the road ahead, of the debt she now carried. She bent slightly, bowing to the chief, the banner's faint rustle echoing the oath she heard in the wind.
