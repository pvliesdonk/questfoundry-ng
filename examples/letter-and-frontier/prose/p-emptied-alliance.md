Elara’s shoulders tightened, the empty space where a River Clan shield should have rested pressing against her spine. The cold wind over the Silver Plain lifted rust‑colored dust, chilling the skin beneath her cloak. She urged her horse forward, the hooves striking the hard stone of the road with a dull thud. Behind her, the echo of the raiders’ clatter faded, leaving only the muted dusk light to outline the ridge ahead. The scarlet wax seal slipped against her cheek, a reminder of the queen’s oath that still bound her fingers.

She felt the weight of the sealed parchment hidden beneath the lining of her cloak. The thought of handing it to Vara, the spymaster who lingered at the council’s threshold, sparked a thin line of doubt. Elara tightened the knot of her satchel strap, sealing the secret as tightly as the wax sealed the queen’s words. With each breath, the resolve hardened.

At the far end of the plain, a low stone doorway marked the entrance to the council hall, its iron hinges worn by countless petitions. A guard stood beside it, his hand resting on the doorframe. Elara stepped toward the doorway, her eyes fixed on the dark interior, ready to demand an answer.
