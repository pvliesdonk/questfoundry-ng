The messenger’s oath rose above the low chatter of the market traders, a clear note that tied distant battlefields to the present courtyard. Elara stood beside the fire, the crimson seal of the queen’s parchment cool against the inside of her cloak. When the envoy offered her a seat at the throne, his words fell like dust on the wind. Elara lifted the missive, her grip firm, and said, “I bear only the queen’s word.” She turned from the gilded promise, the seal untouched, and felt the oath settle heavier upon her shoulders, a stone weight matching the stubborn wind. A small company of River Clan riders, spears tipped with rust‑colored metal, fell into step behind her, their eyes scanning the horizon. Elara stepped away from the fire, the ember glow fading behind her, and guided the party toward the western edge of the plain. The exposed Silver Plain stretched under a mute sky, dust whispering across the cracked earth. Elara tightened the strap of her satchel, the sealed letter still intact, and set her mount ahead, the riders matching her pace. The road ahead lay uncertain, but the unbroken seal reminded her that she kept her own course.
