Elara tightened the cord around her shoulder, the fibers biting her skin. A River Clan rider lingered near her, his spear upright in the rust‑stained dust, eyes scanning the pine silhouettes. From the hills a drumbeat rose, its pulse steady as distant thunder, pulling her thoughts outward. High in the canopy an owl issued a lone hoot, sharp enough to slice the cold air and mark the clan’s final judgment of the promise she bore. The sound settled over her like fine ash, each vibration reminding her of the scarcity she felt in the cracked biscuit and the thin ripple of the half‑full water skin. She lifted the skin, feeling the cool weight of the remaining water, and the biscuit crumbled between her fingers, a tactile tally of her dwindling supplies. The owl’s call lingered, a quiet tally of trust held in balance, while the drum’s echo grew louder. The night deepened as she steadied her breath, aware that each decision would shape the road ahead.
