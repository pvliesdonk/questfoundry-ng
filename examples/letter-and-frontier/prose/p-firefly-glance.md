The fireflies gathered above the sealed parchment, their soft amber light kissing the edges of the wax and outlining the faint ink. Their glow lingered on the words, a quiet promise that the quiet lines might still bend.

Elara pressed the letter tighter against her chest, feeling the scarlet seal press cold into her palm. The vague promise of peace weighed against the practical need for safe passage, a rope tightening around her ribs. She traced the wording with a fingertip, following the curve of each ambiguous phrase as if the light could reveal a hidden meaning. The rust‑colored dust of the plain swirled around her boots, and the distant murmur of the river seemed farther than ever. Yet the fireflies held the paper steady, their illumination a fragile hope that a subtle shift could steer the rival court toward acceptance without betraying the queen’s intent. She steadied her breath, aware that any alteration would be a secret she alone would carry across the border.
