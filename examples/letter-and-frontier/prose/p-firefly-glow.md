Elara stood in the clearing, the sealed parchment pressed against her chest. A soft glow of fireflies drifted down, their amber light settling on the wax seal and the faint ink beneath. The insects illuminated a pale sketch of a sunrise that traced a winding ridge and a river bend. She felt the cool wax against her palm, a reminder of the queen’s dying command. The map’s lines called to her, each curve promising a shared horizon beyond the forest. With trembling fingers she traced the promise, her touch lingering over the ink as the fireflies flickered in response. The sensation was light, yet the weight of duty pressed at her ribs. The pine wind whispered, carrying the scent of damp bark, while a distant river murmured faintly. Shadows shifted among the trunks, and the fireflies hovered, steady as if waiting for her next move. The night pressed cool against the stone cairn beyond the trees, its surface dark as the sealed secret she guarded.
