A thin flame trembled, throwing uneven shadows over the crimson wax that sealed the queen’s parchment. Elara pressed the sealed letter to her palm, the cold imprint of the emblem lingering against her skin. A River Clan rider emerged from the pine mist, his horse’s breath curling into a pale plume before disappearing into the chill. He halted a few paces away, spear tip catching the dim light as his eyes fixed on the red seal. Elara lifted the parchment and placed the waxed seal directly into the rider’s outstretched hand. His fingers brushed the emblem briefly, then closed around it, his gaze narrowing as he weighed the promise it bore. The candle sputtered, a soft whisper of wax against flame, and a speck of dust settled on the edge of the letter. In that hush Elara sensed the oath’s weight turning, a silent balance she could not name. The flame steadied, the seal held firm, and her resolve hardened as cold stone beneath the forest canopy.
