Dusk settled among the towering pines, the air crisp and cold. Malrik watched the forest clerk standing by a moss‑covered stone, waiting, when Elara approached. The sealed letter glinted faintly in the leaf‑filtered light, and she placed the parchment in the clerk’s palm. He noted how she turned the missive over, tilting it toward the dimming light that filtered through the canopy. Her fingertip traced the queen’s emblem, and her gaze lingered on the wax as if weighing a challenge to its authority. From the shadows a forest ranger emerged, his coat stained with pine sap, a metal stamp hanging from his belt. “I can press an official sigil onto it,” he said, his tone practical. “It will guarantee safe passage, though the wording may shift.” Malrik considered the offer, his mind cataloguing the trade: a guarantee of travel for a possible alteration of the queen’s words. He felt the weight of his duty to order over sentiment. Beyond the clearing that opened toward the hilltop hall, a narrow path disappeared into the dim forest, and a lone pine brushed its needles against the wind, a soft rustle that seemed to echo the rider’s doubt.
