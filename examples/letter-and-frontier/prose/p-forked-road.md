Elara moved along the stone‑marked road, the ancient markers softening into a gray smear beneath her boots. The blur suggested the path could fork, each indistinct slab a promise of a different road ahead. She tightened the satchel strap, feeling the scarlet wax seal thrum against her shoulder. A cold wind slipped over the ridge, carrying pine resin and the distant murmur of the river. Ahead, a border camp rose from the plain, canvas tents huddled like a flock of birds, lanterns throwing amber circles on the dust. A lone rider tended a fire, the ember throwing a rust‑colored glow onto the stones. She recalled the oath she had given the River Clan, its weight pressing against the resolve she held for the queen’s seal. With a steady breath she chose to keep the letter hidden, trusting the seal alone to guard its contents. The road ahead lay unfixed, the blurred stones a silent invitation to the choices that waited.
