Malrik stood beside the moss‑grown stone as the gate creaked open. The soldiers formed a line, their spears tipped with dull iron, and one of them stepped forward to greet the rider. He nodded at her, his voice low, and the courier lowered her head in return. The wax seal on the parchment caught the fading light, the queen’s emblem embossed in scarlet. Malrik traced the edge of the seal with his thumb, noting the official stamp pressed into the wax. It matched the inked crest he kept catalogued in the warder’s ledger, a sign that the letter bore the crown’s authority. He lifted the iron‑ed stamp from his belt, pressed it gently onto the seal, and felt the cool press of metal against stone. The imprint settled with a faint hum, confirming the document’s clearance for travel. He handed the letter to the guard beside the doorway, his grip firm as the stone at his shoulder. The guard bowed, slipped the parchment into his satchel, and stepped aside, allowing Elara to pass. Behind the gate, a wooden door stood ajar, its interior lit by a flickering torch, leading toward the council chamber where the fort’s leaders awaited. The courtyard fell quiet as the messenger rode forward, the garrison’s eyes following her steady stride.
