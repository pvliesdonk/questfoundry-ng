Elara entered the council chamber through the heavy oak doors. The room smelled of pine resin and rust‑colored dust, the torchlight throwing thin ribbons of amber across cold stone walls. A circle of councilors sat on a raised platform, their faces half‑lit, spears leaned against the stone. The gaunt chief lifted an iron stamp, its head bearing the fort’s crest. He motioned for her to step forward.

"State your purpose," he said.

Elara lifted the sealed parchment, the scarlet wax cool against her palm. "I carry a missive from the queen, sealed for peace," she said. The chief’s eyes narrowed, and a councilwoman leaned forward, her fingertip tracing the wax. "What does the letter contain that concerns us?" she said.

Elara’s throat tightened. She eased the flap just enough to reveal a single line of ink, the words trembling in the firelight. "The dawn of shared fields," she read. The council’s murmurs rose, a ripple of calculation across the stone.

The chief placed his hand on the parchment, the iron stamp pressing into the wax with a muted crack. "We will use this fragment to negotiate with the rival court," he said. Elara felt the queen’s oath bend, the seal pressing against her skin like a promise she could not fully keep. She nodded, accepting their leverage in exchange for safe passage along the southern road.

The guards recorded each of her movements, their eyes following her every step. She turned toward the narrow passage that led out of the chamber. The stone walls rose up around her, their height swallowing the torchlight. She pressed her back against the cold stone corridor, feeling the weight of the council’s scrutiny settle behind her.
