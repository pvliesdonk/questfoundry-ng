The border wind slipped through the great hall’s cold stone, scattering a veil of muted dusk light across the flagstones. Elara stepped inside, the sealed letter trembling in her grasp as the scarlet wax pressed cool against the inside of her cloak. The massive doors thudded shut behind her, sealing the courtyard and exposing the rough road that wound across the silver plain. A low drumbeat rose on the horizon, faint as a pulse that chased the wind over the open steppe.

The road ahead grew uneven, its surface cracked and strewn with rust‑colored dust that rose with each of her boots. She lifted the parchment, felt the seal give a faint crack, and turned the sealed parchment over, letting the wax edge fall away to read the queen’s vague wording. In the margin a faint impression hinted at a map, a shortcut through the mountain passes that the dying queen had once whispered to a trusted courier.

The thought of that hidden route tugged at the weight of her oath, pulling curiosity into balance with the duty she bore. She tightened her cloak against the wind, aware that every step on the plain drew her farther from the hall’s stone safety and deeper into the frontier’s uncertain chill.
