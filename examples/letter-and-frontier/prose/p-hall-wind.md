A sigh of wind through the leaves outside echoed against the stone, mirroring the tension in the hall. Elara lingered on the threshold, the sealed parchment pressed to her breast, its wax cool against her palm. She lifted the lid of the letter, the queen’s faded emblem catching the dim torchlight, and saw a faint ridge beneath the seal that did not belong to any heraldic line. The ridge hinted at a folded parchment tucked within, a hidden map the queen’s brother had been known to tuck into such deliveries. Curiosity tugged at her breath while the oath she bore pressed heavy on her ribs. She could feel the promise of duty and the lure of secret routes pressing against each other, each pulse a quiet drum in her chest. The courtyard beyond the great doors thrummed with the low murmur of courtiers, their eyes already fixed on the doorway like owls awaiting prey. Elara’s fingers tightened around the clasp, the wax imprint a reminder of the queen’s exact command, and she steadied herself for what would come when she stepped forward.
