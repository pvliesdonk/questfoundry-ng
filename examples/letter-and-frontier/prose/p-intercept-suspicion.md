The thin veil of suspicion lingered in the vaulted hall, the untouched seal pressing against Elara’s shoulders like cold stone. Torchlight fell in muted pools across a long oak table where a crisp treaty waited, a quill tucked beside a half‑filled inkpot. Across the room a stone altar rose, its shallow depression already cradling the sealed letter. She lifted the parchment from her cloak and placed the sealed letter onto the altar’s hollow, the scarlet wax catching the flame’s flicker. Elara brushed her thumb over the queen’s emblem, feeling the faint heat beneath the chill of the stone. The duty to deliver the queen’s oath sat beside a growing doubt that the rival court might twist the words she bore. A distant drumbeat echoed beyond the walls, a reminder that the frontier watched each breath she took. Her mind listed the risk of rewriting the treaty, the hope that altered phrasing could steer peace, and the possibility of leaving the letter untouched, allowing its ambiguous wording to speak for itself. The choice settled like dust on cold stone, waiting for her hand to move.
