The queen’s peace proposal lay untouched on the cold stone slab, the parchment pristine beneath the scarlet wax that still sealed its secret. Elara pressed her palm against the stone, feeling the faint chill that rose from the surface and the weight of the oath it represented. The sun slid behind the ridge, casting a thin line of muted dusk across the barren plain. She turned her gaze outward, her thoughts shifting to the stone‑lined road that led south toward Stonegate Fort. The southern road stretched ahead, a ribbon of packed earth bordered by low stone walls that rose toward the fortified gate. Soldiers marched in steady columns along the wall, their armor clinking softly against the stone, eyes scanning the horizon. Beyond the soldiers, a small border camp perched at the edge of the road, tents huddled together like a reluctant herd of birds. The road itself bore a faint markings of ancient stones, each set of stones a guide for travelers who dared the frontier. Elara felt the frontier’s weight settle deeper around her shoulders as the wind brushed the dust from the stone, carrying the distant murmur of the river and the sharp scent of pine resin. She tightened the strap of her satchel, the sealed letter pressed against her chest, and stepped forward toward the choices that lay before her.
