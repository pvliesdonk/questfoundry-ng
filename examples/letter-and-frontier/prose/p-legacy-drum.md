The drumbeat drifted over the open plain, a thin line of sound that traced the border wind. Its distant echo lingered, a pulse of family legacy that had followed her father's rides across these lands. Elara pressed the sealed parchment against her chest, the scarlet wax cool against the inside of her cloak, and felt a faint crack that might conceal a hidden margin in the queen’s wording. Dust rose at her boots, fine and cold, as the muted dusk dimmed the sky. She tightened the cloak tighter around the shivering air and steadied the parchment in the fading light. A memory of the drum’s steady thrum whispered of duty inherited, of paths once marked in secret. With a steady hand she turned the sealed parchment over, the wax edge catching the weak glow and revealing the vague phrasing beneath. The words were ambiguous, the ink smudged where the queen had dictated peace. The rhythm of the drum pressed against her thoughts, tightening the knot between oath and curiosity. She lingered, the weight of the seal heavy in her palm, as the frontier wind carried the promise of a hidden route beyond the plain.
