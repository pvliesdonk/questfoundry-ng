Elara tightened the strap of her satchel and felt the scarlet wax press against the inside of her cloak. The sealed letter lay hidden beneath her breastplate, its crimson seal unbroken, a weight she kept close. She turned eastward, stepping onto the scarred passes of the Silver Plain, the ground littered with rust‑colored dust and the broken ribs of abandoned caravans. The wind rose, tugging at the hem of her cloak and catching a loose strip of parchment that slipped from the satchel, drifting like a ghost toward the muted sky. She watched it flutter, a reminder of the unread map that lay sealed beneath the wax, and she pressed the parchment back into her cloak without a second glance. The sun slipped low, casting a dim amber across the flat horizon as she walked alone, the path ahead uncharted without the hidden chart. In the distance, a line of soldiers marched beside a low stone wall that marked the road leading to Stonegate Fort, their armor clanking faintly against the quiet of the plain. The sight of the disciplined column steadied her resolve; the letter would remain sealed, its secrets safe from prying eyes. She continued forward, each step echoing against the cold stone of the wall and the open expanse of the plain, the promise of the sealed missive pressing against her skin like a silent oath. The wind whispered across the dunes, carrying the scent of pine resin and distant river murmur, and Elara felt the frontier’s weight settle around her shoulders as she pressed onward.
