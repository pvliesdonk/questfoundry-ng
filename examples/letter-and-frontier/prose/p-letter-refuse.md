Elara's palm rested on the scarlet wax, the emblem of the queen pressing into her skin. Malrik lifted the iron stamp, the garrison emblem catching the wan light, and his gauntleted hand hovered over the seal. She felt the weight of the oath in the heat of the wax and the cold of the stone beneath her boots. With a breath that tasted of pine resin, she drew her hand back, the letter trembling slightly in the cloak. “I cannot,” she said, and the words fell like a stone onto the stagnant air.

Malrik's eyes narrowed, the stamp still suspended between them. The patrol that had been marching along the outer wall stepped forward, spears angled toward the gate, forming a barrier that blocked any further movement. Their commander barked an order, the voice echoing off the ancient stones, and the soldiers positioned themselves as if the very ground might shift under the letter's weight. Elara sensed their suspicion, a thin thread of doubt winding around the sealed parchment.

The drums from the hills grew louder, each beat a reminder of the war waiting beyond the frontier. She clutched the cloak tighter, feeling the queen's seal unmarked, untouched, a silent promise that refused to be altered. The patrol's presence pressed against the gate, the iron stamp hovering in the space between duty and conscience. Elara stood firm, the sealed letter still whole, the gate barred, the cold wind stirring dust at her boots as the decision lingered.
