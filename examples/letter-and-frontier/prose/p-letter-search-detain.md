Malrik narrowed his eyes, the scar across his cheek deepening, and he barked the order. “Search her pack,” he said. The patrol moved forward, boots thudding on the stone, and they pried open the leather satchel. Cloth and rope fell onto the flagstones, rust‑colored dust catching the wan light that filtered through the slit above the gate. He saw the sealed letter tucked beneath a folded map, its scarlet wax untouched, the queen’s emblem faint against the parchment. The men detained her at the threshold, spears angled as if the very air might betray her. A guard stepped forward, his voice low. “What is your purpose here?” he said. She answered in measured tones, the words carrying the weight of a queen’s oath. Malrik felt a cold draft slip over the sealed parchment, the chill stirring the wax’s edge. He watched as she stepped through the gate while the guard’s wary gaze lingered on the seal, suspicion hanging in the stale air.
