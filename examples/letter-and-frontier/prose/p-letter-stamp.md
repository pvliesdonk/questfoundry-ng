Elara felt the scarlet wax still warm against her palm as Malrik lifted the iron stamp from its leather sheath. She nodded once, a quiet assent, and watched his gauntleted hand bring the emblem of Stonegate down onto the seal. The metal pressed clean, a crisp line cutting into the queen’s mark, and a thin shiver of cold brushed her thumb. A muted crack sounded when the stamp struck, and the vibration traveled up her arm. Behind Malrik, a stone‑clad guard stood with spear planted, eyes fixed on the exchange. Elara slipped the letter from her grip and placed it in the guard’s palm, the wax still soft. The guard gave a brief nod, his helmet catching the fading light, and stepped aside, allowing her to move forward. From the ridge, drums rolled onward, a low thrum that seemed to shake the stone beneath her feet. The sound blended with the rust‑colored dust that drifted in the wind, settling on her boots. She tightened her hold on the satchel, aware that the stamp might have altered the phrasing she guarded, and waited for the next command.
