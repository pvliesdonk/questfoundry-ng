Malrik stood beside the moss‑blackened gate, the scar across his left cheek catching the dim lantern glow. He watched Elara approach, the sealed parchment pressed against her cloak, the scarlet wax a thin ember in the gloom. He lifted the iron stamp, its weight steady in his hand, and asked, “Purpose?” said Malrik. Her answer fell in measured tones, each word landing on the stone as if weighed by the queen’s oath. He felt the pull of duty and a thread of sympathy for the rider’s plight. He lowered the stamp, his palm hovering above the wax, and then pressed his hand lightly to the seal, feeling the faint heat beneath the scarlet. He stepped aside, spears of his patrol forming a thin wall that receded as she passed. Elara placed her palm on the wax, the queen’s emblem warm against her skin, and moved forward through the doorway. Malrik’s eyes stayed on the untouched seal, a thin veil of suspicion settling like dust on the cold stone. The distant river’s murmur drifted beyond the fort, and a cold wind sighed through border pines, reminding him of the frontier’s endless watch.
