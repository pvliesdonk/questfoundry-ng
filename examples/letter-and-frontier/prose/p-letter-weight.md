Elara felt the satchel press tighter against her side as the hidden letter shifted, its weight a cold pressure that seemed to bind her ribs. The scarlet wax seal lay against the fabric, unmoving, yet the paper beneath it pushed forward as if urging release. She glanced at the River Clan rider, his spear planted, eyes scanning the dim corridor. His hand rested on his own leather strap, a silent promise of hidden routes across the plain. A murmur rose from the council beyond the stone doors, but Elara heard only the faint echo of her own breath. The secret in her cloak tightened the knot of trust she had held with the rival court, each step now a wary tread. She thought of Vara’s calculating stare, of the alliance that might crumble if the letter fell into the wrong hands. The weight in the satchel grew, a reminder that the queen’s oath rested on her silence. With the rider waiting, she could take the concealed escort through hidden trails, or she could turn away and walk alone toward the rival court's distant hall.
