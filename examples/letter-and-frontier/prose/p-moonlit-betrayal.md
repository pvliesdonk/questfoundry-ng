A chilling wind lifted dust among the pines, its breath whispering of betrayal among couriers. Elara pressed the sealed letter tighter to her chest, the wax seal cool against her palm, and she weighed the oath she had sworn to the dying queen against the practical need for security; the pine scent grew thick, clinging to her throat. She steadied herself, turned the parchment over once more, and a narrow beam of moonlight fell upon the seal, highlighting the crisp edge of the wax. On the reverse of the parchment a faint sketch of a sunrise traced a route through the hidden map, its pale line promising a shared dawn beyond the trees. Small fireflies rose from the underbrush, their tiny lights alighting on the parchment, flickering like soft embers against the night. Elara watched the fireflies settle, their glow drawing her eye to the concealed lines, and felt the pull of curiosity tug at the threads of her duty. She recalled the queen’s warning that any breach of the seal could unravel the fragile peace, and a cold dread settled in her ribs. The moonlight traced the letters, but the map’s lines remained vague, each curve a question. She lowered the parchment, listening to the night wind, ready to choose what to do.
