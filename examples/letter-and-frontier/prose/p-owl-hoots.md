An owl hooted far over the hollow of the plain, its thin call slicing the twilight. The sound arrived just as the River Clan chief lowered his hand, the metal clasp of his reins catching the fading light. Elara felt the moment settle like cold stone under her palm; the riders ahead formed a silent line, each eye a measure of the trust she had promised. The chief's gaze lingered on the scarlet seal she kept in her satchel, then moved to the horizon where the ridge cut the sky. Around her, the thin dust rose in small eddies, the wind humming across the open stretch. A low roll of war drums rose from the ridge, a muted thunder that threaded through the night and tugged at the edges of her resolve. The drums seemed to carry the weight of the clan's pledge, a reminder that the favor she owed could echo far beyond the plain. She tightened the strap of her cloak, the letter pressed against her side, and listened to the distant rhythm that marked the final weigh of the clan's trust.
