Elara felt the cold wind slip through the high windows, the stone floor sending a thin tremor up through her boots. The elder's voice rose above the murmurs, urging that the council hold its words and let the dispute settle before any oath was broken. He argued that a measured consent would grant the negotiators the hours needed to trace the ambiguous phrasing, lest a hasty decree spark war.

The lords shifted, their eyes flickering to the scarlet seal that rested on the altar. A faint ember glow from a distant torch caught the edge of the wax, casting a brief light across Elara's palm. She sensed the queen’s promise lingering, a quiet pressure that steadied her breath.

Elara watched the elder’s steady cadence, his beard catching the torchlight as he reminded them that haste could betray the fragile accord. She felt the stone’s chill against her skin and the rust‑colored dust that settled on the floor, reminders of the frontier beyond the hall.

She lifted her hand, the seal warm against her skin, and extended the sealed letter toward the chief marshal, offering the still‑intact promise as a token of trust.
