Elara felt the cold stone of the hall floor under her boots as Meros stepped forward, his cloak brushing the tapestries. He lowered his voice to a whisper, "Your insight has eased the tension among the lords." The words hung in the muted dusk light, thin as the border wind that slipped through the narrow windows. He pressed a hand to the scarlet wax still warm on her thumb and added, "When the river clan asks for aid, you will have a place in their council." The promise settled like a faint ember glow in the back of her mind. Elara watched the courtiers' eyes flicker, the faint ember of curiosity in their gazes, but she kept her posture steady. The scent of pine resin rose from the distant torches, sharp against the rust‑colored dust that clung to the hem of her cloak. She drew a slow breath, feeling the fabric press against her shoulders, a silent guard against the scrutiny of the room. The weight of the promise settled deeper, a quiet thrum beneath the murmur of the river outside, and Elara knew the favor would bind her future steps. The hall fell back into its low hum, and she lifted her chin, ready to bear the quiet debt.
