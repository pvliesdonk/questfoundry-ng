Elara stood on the cold stone floor of the vaulted hall, the sealed letter pressed to her breast. The courtiers in dark cloaks stared, thin eyes fixed on her as if waiting for a tide to turn. Their gazes were sharp, the muted dusk filtering through the high windows catching the dust that floated like faint ember sparks. She inhaled the dry breath of stone and the faint scent of burning wood from the hearth beyond, feeling each clause of the queen’s proposal pulse against her ribs. The words were a low drum, each syllable a weight she could not set down. She knew that a single alteration could shift the council’s resolve, yet the thought settled in her throat like a cold wind against a sworn oath. She tightened her grip on the wax‑sealed parchment, aware that any change would become a burden she alone would carry beyond these walls. At the far end of the hall a shadowed alcove lay behind a tapestry of muted banners. There, Spymaster Vara leaned against the stone, his cloak half drawn, a faint smile curving his lips. He lifted a gloved hand, an invitation to speak. Elara stepped toward the alcove, the letter still sealed, the decision knotting tighter at her throat.
