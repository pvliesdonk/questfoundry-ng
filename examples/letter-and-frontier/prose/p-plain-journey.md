Elara turned west, the path opening onto the exposed Silver Plain. She spotted a line of River Clan riders beside weather‑worn stones. She let a rider in mottled cloak tie her saddle rope to his, the rust‑tipped spear brushing her flank as they bound together. The plain stretched under a mute sky, wind whispering over bone‑white dust. A ragged band of plain bandits emerged from a shallow hollow, dust rolling. The Clan riders surged forward, spears flashing. It seemed they might keep the raiders at bay, and a brief, tentative shield flickered around her as the riders pressed the enemies toward the dunes. Their horses veered onto a narrow gullied track hidden beneath the sand, guiding her away from the open road. Suddenly the sky swallowed the horizon, sand and wind roaring. The riders lifted their heads, eyes narrowed, and urged their mounts toward a shallow valley where an ancient stone cairn rose. Rain hammered the stone, gusts howling like a beast. Inside the cairn they kept watch, spears planted at the entrance, the wind howling against the stone walls. Elara pressed her cloak tighter, feeling the scarlet seal of the queen press cool against her shoulder. When the storm spent its fury, clouds thinned and the stone chambers of the rival court came into view. The River Clan escort rode past the slow‑moving convoy of armored wagons that lagged behind, and Elara arrived at the court’s great doors ahead of the fortified column. She stepped through the heavy doors into the echoing chambers, the air scented with damp stone and distant ember glow. The wind tore across the plain outside, pulling at her cloak which she lifted, the fabric snapping as she raised it against the gust.
