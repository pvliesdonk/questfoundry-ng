The distant echo of a drum beat, a pulse of family legacy lingering, rolled over the plain. Elara pressed the sealed letter to her chest, the scarlet wax catching a thin slice of moonlight that slipped through the cracked window. Cold stone beneath her boots creaked as she shifted, and a gust stirred rust‑colored dust among the pine roots. She glanced at the map hidden beneath the bark of the twisted tree; faint lines traced routes through the open land, barely discernible in the gloom. The scent of damp stone mixed with the dry wind, grounding her to the moment. The drum’s rhythm throbbed in her ears, a steady heartbeat that seemed to sync with the tug of duty and the pull of curiosity. She tightened the strap of her cloak, feeling the weight of the queen’s seal against her spine. The hidden map lay waiting, its promise silent, while the drum called her forward toward the unknown road beyond the trees.
