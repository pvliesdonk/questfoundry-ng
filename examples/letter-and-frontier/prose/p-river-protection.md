Elara felt the scarlet wax of the queen's seal press against her thumb, the faint sigil unbroken, a solid reminder of the oath she carried. In the dim candlelight the hardened wax caught a thin flash, a brief whisper of light that seemed to test her resolve. A rider of the River Clan entered the hall, his leather cloak smeared with rust‑colored dust from the dunes, his gaze steady as stone. He halted before her, a weather‑worn banner hanging from his lance, its torn colors snapping in the stale air, and Elara stepped beneath it, the fabric brushing her cheek. "We know a hidden path through the dunes, safe from the patrols," he said, "but the debt will be yours later." The clan chief, scar across his cheek still fresh, repeated the terms in a firmer tone, offering a concealed crossing of the plain in exchange for a future service. Elara opened her satchel, laid out the dried meat and cracked water skin, and counted the meager rations, the numbers echoing the scarcity of her journey. The weight of the queen's seal pulsed at the base of her palm, the frontier's fragile line humming beneath her fingertips.
