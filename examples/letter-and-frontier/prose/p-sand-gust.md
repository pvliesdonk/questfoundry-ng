Elara felt the wind climb from the western ridge, its edge biting the exposed stone. A sudden gust lifted pale sand, whispering of the plain’s unforgiving breath, and the grains formed a thin veil around her shoulders. The howl pressed against the stone walls of the rival court, rattling the iron hinges still damp from the storm. The scarlet wax of the queen’s seal pressed cold against her cheek, slick with condensation. A River Clan rider beside her shouted a warning, his voice swallowed by the gale. Elara tightened her cloak, the fabric snapping, and cupped a hand over her eyes as grit stung. She spurred her mount, urging it hard toward the great doors of the rival court, the distant arch looming like a stone mouth. Sand struck her face, but she held the seal steady, the emblem a weight she would not relinquish. Behind her the clan spears clanged as the riders pressed forward, trying to keep the bandits at bay, and the plain seemed to roar in defiance.
