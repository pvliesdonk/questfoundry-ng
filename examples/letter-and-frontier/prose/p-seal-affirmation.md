Elara stood in the cool shadow of the stone arch, the scarlet wax still warm under her thumb. The stamp of Malrik’s fort had pressed into the queen’s seal, and a faint crack had split the surface, but the wax clung to the parchment. She pressed the edge of the seal against her cheek, feeling the weight settle like a quiet promise. The rough texture of the wax pressed into skin, a silent affirmation that the oath now lived in her flesh as much as on the parchment. A guard in muted mail shifted his weight and opened his palm, waiting. The river‑clan rider she had spoken with earlier lingered near the doorway, spear lowered, eyes on the sealed missive. Elara’s breath rose in the cold wind, scented with pine resin and dust from the plain beyond. She lifted the letter, its crimson seal catching the dim light, and placed it in the guard’s hand. The guard’s fingers closed around the parchment, the seal intact, and his low murmur faded as the missive moved forward.
