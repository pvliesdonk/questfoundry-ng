The scarlet wax of the queen’s seal caught the waning dusk, a small gleam that steadied Elara’s hand. A rider of the River Clan stepped out from the line of pines, his horse exhaling a fleeting breath that vanished in the cold air. He lowered the spear he carried and spoke, offering to hide her from the bandits that prowled the open Silver Plain. "We will shield you," he said, his voice low as the river’s murmur. From the shadowed edge of the clearing the clan chief emerged, broad‑shouldered and crowned with a knot of dark hair. He placed a firm hand on the rust‑stained banner that fluttered from a weather‑worn post, then rested it on Elara’s shoulder. He promised a covert shield across the plain, asking only that she owe him a favor when the time came. The promise settled into the silence like the cold imprint of the seal against her palm, a weight she could feel as clearly as the banner’s fabric trembling in the wind.
