A sigh of wind slipped through the leaves outside, its rust‑colored breath echoing the tightness inside the vaulted hall. Elara stood beneath the high arches, the sealed parchment pressed against her breast, the scarlet wax cool beneath her fingers. The courtiers in dark cloaks watched, their thin eyes flickering like shadows in the dim light. The draft seeped through the stone, carrying the dry scent of dust and a faint ember glow from a hearth beyond the doors. Each clause of the peace proposal settled in her ribs like a low drumbeat, steady and relentless. Her fingertip lingered on a phrase that might tip the council toward truce, and a question rose within her, weighed against the queen’s oath. She tightened her grip, aware that any alteration would become a weight she would bear alone across the frontier. The wind pushed the dust across the sill, scattering rust‑colored specks in the muted dusk, and for a moment the hall seemed to hold its breath. She stepped forward, voice steadied, the fragile truth trembling on her tongue.
