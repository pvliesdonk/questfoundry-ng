She turned west and the trail opened onto the exposed Silver Plain, a stretch she would have to cross, where River Clan riders were whispered about. The plain stretched beneath a mute sky, flat and bone‑white, and rumors said it was defended by swift River Clan riders who rode the wind as a shield. A rider in the River Clan’s mottled cloak fell into step beside her, his spear tipped with rust‑colored metal. "We will ride with you," he said. Her satchel pressed against her side, the cracked biscuits inside felt hard, the half‑filled water skin sloshed a faint, sour scent of pine resin. A thin wind carried a faint ember glow from a distant fire, and the horizon darkened as fine sand began to swirl, promising a storm. To her left the road veered south toward Stonegate Fort, its stone walls rising like a stern silhouette against the dusk. The fort promised shelter, but its gate would scrutinize every traveler, measuring each seal and word. She felt the queen’s scarlet wax seal cold against her cloak, a reminder of the oath she bore while the wind slipped over the steppe.
