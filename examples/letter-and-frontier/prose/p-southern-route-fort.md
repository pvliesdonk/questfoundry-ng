Elara turned the horse toward the southern gate of Stonegate Fort, the decision to travel the southern road set like a line in the dusk. The iron bars of the gate caught the wan light, and the garrison stood in quiet rows, spears lowered but eyes sharp. She lifted her hand and handed the sealed letter to the forward guard. He took the parchment, pressed the scarlet wax to his thumb, and felt the chill of the queen’s oath against the fabric of her cloak. “The seal is intact,” he said, and raised his hand to signal that the gate would open. The heavy wooden doors swung inward on squeaking hinges, and Elara guided her mount into the courtyard. A River Clan rider in a mottled cloak lingered near the entrance, his rust‑tipped spear resting against his shoulder. “We will watch the road as you pass,” he said. Elara returned the nod, feeling the cold edge of the wax on her thumb. The gate slammed shut behind her with a muted thud, and the night air carried a faint glow from a distant fire. The courtyard echoed with the footfalls of the garrison as Elara steadied her horse, ready to move onward.
