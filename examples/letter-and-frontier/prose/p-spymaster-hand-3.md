Elara stood beside the stone slab, the scarlet wax still warm to her touch. Vara Shadowmantle lingered in the dim alcove, silvery strands catching the torchlight. The spymaster's thin smile stretched, then widened, as the sealed letter lay between them. Elara watched the change, the curve of the mouth spelling a quiet calculation. In that instant she understood the letter's new role: no longer a simple messenger of the queen's oath, but a piece that could be shifted between hands for advantage. The cold draft that slipped through the high arches brushed her cheek, carrying the faint scent of damp stone. The distant murmur of the river beyond the walls seemed farther away now, as if the hall itself held its breath. She tightened her grip on the cloak, feeling the weight of the seal against her palm, a reminder of the oath she carried. The council's earlier resolve faded into the background; all that mattered was the decision forming in her mind, the knowledge that handing the parchment to Vara would set a different path into motion.
