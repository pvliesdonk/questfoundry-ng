A cold draft slipped through the high windows, stirring the thin curtain by the doorway. Elara felt the scarlet wax warm beneath her thumb as she turned toward the shadowed alcove. Vara Shadowmantle stood there, half‑mask catching the torchlight, his gauntlet hand resting lightly on his sword's pommel. He watched the seal, then said, "Stability bears a price," his voice low as the border wind. Elara lifted the parchment, the thin paper trembling in her grip, and moved it across the stone floor. The wax‑sealed edge brushed the rim of Vara's palm, the scarlet imprint catching the firelight. She placed the letter fully in his hand, the weight of the queen’s oath pressing against her skin. For a moment the hall held only the distant murmur of the river and the faint crackle of the torch. Vara pressed a cold steel tip to the seal and snapped it cleanly; a small shard of wax fell onto the stone. He turned the broken flap back, eyes scanning the hidden words before he folded the paper. Elara watched, breath held, as the spymaster tucked the letter into his cloak, his smile hidden behind the mask. The choice of trust settled like cooled ember in her chest.
