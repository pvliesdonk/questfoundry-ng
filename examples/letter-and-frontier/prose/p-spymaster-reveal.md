The stone chamber held a thin column of torchlight. Vara stood beside the low table, the sealed parchment resting on the polished slab. Elara stepped forward, her cloak brushing the dust, and placed the letter into Vara's grasp. The scarlet wax felt warm against a gloved fingertip. Vara pressed a sharpened steel tip to the wax and snapped it cleanly; a shard fell onto the flagstones. The broken seal revealed a thin veil of ink, the hidden script still faint in the ember glow. Vara traced the cursive with a careful finger, noting a subtle shift in phrasing that could steer the treaty toward the border trade they coveted. Around the table, council members in muted dusk robes watched in silence, their eyes fixed on the spymaster. When the ink settled, Vara lifted the parchment and held it aloft, the torn edge catching the firelight. "The queen's oath remains, yet our accord must reflect the needs of the frontier," they said, their voice low and measured. A senior elder raised a hand, signaling the council to consider. Vara stepped toward the dais, the letter now a bargaining chip, and placed it on the stone slab before the assembly. The room held its breath as the distant river murmur faded behind the stone walls. With the missive poised, Vara waited for the council's response.
