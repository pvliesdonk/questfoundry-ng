Elara's palm pressed against the scarlet wax, the heat of the seal faint on her skin. A steadiness settled in her grip as she lifted the parchment, the promise of the queen's oath humming beneath the distant murmur of the river outside. She turned toward the marshal, the sealed letter resting on the palm like a thin bridge between courts. The marshal's eyes lingered on the wax, then drifted to her face, and he said nothing, his gauntlet hand resting on the pommel of his sword. From the shadowed alcove Vara stepped forward, the half‑mask catching the torchlight; their voice slipped low, "Stability bears a price." Elara's gaze held the seal, the faint scent of pine resin rising from her cloak as she thought of the promise she carried. She could slide the letter deeper within the inner lining of her cloak, shielding it from all eyes, or she could press the waxed missive into Vara's waiting hand. The hall fell quiet, the wind outside sighing through the high windows, and her resolve hardened like cooled embers in her chest.
