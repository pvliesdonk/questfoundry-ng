After Vara's low offer, the great hall fell silent. Elara's eyes lingered on the spymaster's mask, searching the slight tilt of the brow for a thread of deceit. The incense around them smelled of burnt sandalwood, and the stone floor seemed to hold its breath.

She remembered the rumors that clung to Vara's name in trading posts—stories of contracts twisted after the ink dried. Those whispers rose in her mind like a cold draft, sharpening the edge of her scrutiny.

Her gaze slipped to the scarlet wax that sealed the queen's parchment. In the dim glow of a single candle, the seal caught a thin flash of light, as if daring her to break it. She felt the parchment's rough edge press against her palm, a reminder of duty.

The candle trembled, its flame wavering against the stone walls, casting brief shadows that danced across the letter. A rider from the River Clan stepped forward, his cloak dusted with rust‑colored sand, eyes fixed on the courier's satchel.

The waxglint held the weight of the queen's trust, and Elara felt the frontier's fragile line pulse beneath her fingertips.
