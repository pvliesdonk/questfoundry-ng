Elara entered the council chamber, the stone floor cold beneath her boots. The scarlet wax of the queen’s seal pressed against her cheek, a faint weight she could feel through the cloak. Captain Malrik placed the iron stamp on the table and spoke. “Our messenger arrived late,” he said, “and the rival court grows restless.” A grey‑haired lord lifted his chin. “If we wait any longer, the treaty will lose its force,” he said. Lady Mirela leaned forward. “We could send other envoys now, but that will signal doubt,” she said. Murmurs rose as each voice weighed the danger of dispatching substitutes. Elara listened, the council’s eyes flicking toward the doorway that opened onto the wind‑swept plain beyond the walls. The plain stretched, barren and cold, a path that offered no escort. She felt the absence of the River Clan’s shield as a hollow pressure against her back. With a steady breath, she stepped onto the wind‑swept plain, the dust kicking up around her boots. The wind brushed her face, and the distant river’s murmur seemed far away. She knew the council’s indecision threatened the peace proposal, but she could not wait for another envoy.
