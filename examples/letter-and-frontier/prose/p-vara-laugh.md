The hall's stone walls held a chill that settled in Elara's spine. Light filtered through a narrow slit, painting a pale stripe across the floor where the courier stood, the wax‑sealed letter pressed against her breast. Vara entered, the half‑mask catching the dim glow, incense curling from a lingering coil. Their boots clicked on the flagstones, each step measured, voice low as a border wind when they spoke. “I can offer you a pact in exchange for that letter,” they said, the promise hanging between them like a thin thread. A laugh rose, thin as ember, drifting across the room and masking a calculation beneath. It fell over Vara's grin, a fleeting curve that did not reach the eyes hidden behind the mask. Elara felt the weight of the seal shift in her hand, the scarlet wax still unmarred, while the spymaster's gaze lingered on the surface of the parchment. The moment stretched, the rust‑colored shadows flickering on the walls, and Elara steadied herself, aware that a single decision would ripple beyond the stone.
