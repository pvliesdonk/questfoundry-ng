In the shadowed alcove that crouched beneath the hill’s ridge, Vara stepped from the gloom, the half‑mask catching a thin strip of dusk. Their silvery hair brushed the stone as they moved, and their eyes glittered with intrigue.

Vara held the crimson‑sealed parchment in one gloved hand, the wax smooth and cold against their palm. “A pact could steady the trade routes that howl with bandit‑flames,” they said, voice low, measuring each syllable. They placed the letter on a rough stone altar that rose from the floor, its surface mottled with age. The seal caught the faint ember glow, a sharp glint that split the dimness. “Give me that seal, and I would bind the frontier to a promise of safe passage,” Vara offered, their tone steady as the wind that rattled the distant river murmur beyond the alcove. Their mind weighed the advantage, the fragile hope of peace that trembled like rust‑colored dust in the air. The offer hung between them, a bargain that could steady volatile routes or shatter the queen’s intent, and the stone altar waited, smooth beneath the sealed letter.
