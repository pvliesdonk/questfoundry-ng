The wind brushed the ridge, carrying a low roll of war drums that skimmed the plain. Elara stood on the edge of the Silver Plain, the scarlet wax of the queen’s seal pressed against her palm. Malrik leaned against the cold stone of the fort’s outer wall, the iron stamp of Stonegate glinting in the fading light. He lifted the heavy seal and said, “It will mark you as sanctioned.” Elara felt the metal press against the wax, the imprint forming a cold brand on the surface. A gust lifted rust‑colored dust, settling like ash on the ground, and the drumbeat deepened, echoing against the distant ridge. She tightened her grip on the satchel, refusing to let the iron stamp seal her fate, her fingers digging into the worn leather. The stamp hovered for a breath, and her mind returned to the River Clan chief’s promise, a thin thread of owed loyalty that could become a line of battle. The drums rolled on, a reminder that the favor she owed might cost more than a single word. The choice lingered as the dusk deepened, the seal waiting for her hand.
