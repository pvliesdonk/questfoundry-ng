The candle's flame fluttered, throwing jittery shadows that made the scarlet wax tremble, stirring a doubt about the queen's promise. Elara watched the wavering light chase the seal, each flicker outlining a thin line of uncertainty. The stone walls chilled her fingers, and the air carried the faint scent of burnt sandalwood. She felt the rough parchment pressed against her palm, the wax hard and unmoving beneath her thumb. From the doorway a River Clan rider entered, his cloak streaked with dust from the plain, and he halted a step away. The clan chief, scar across his cheek, drew closer and said, “We can guide you across the plain, but the favor will be yours later.” His words rose like a low ember against the weight of the queen's seal. Elara's eyes lingered on the sealed letter, the crimson wax a stark reminder of the trust she bore. The rider's gaze settled on the satchel, waiting for her response.
