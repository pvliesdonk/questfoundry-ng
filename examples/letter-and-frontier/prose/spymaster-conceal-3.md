Elara stood at the stone threshold, the hall's dust settling on her boots. The scarlet wax of the queen's seal pressed cold against the inside of her cloak, a thin thrum she could feel through the leather. Around her, the council's voices rose and fell, but each syllable seemed to drift farther from her ear, muffled by the weight she carried. She adjusted the strap of her satchel, the worn leather creaking beneath her fingers; the pouch rested against her hip, its contents a reminder of the road ahead. The hidden letter lay folded beneath the fold of her cloak, the seal unmarred, its words unread. A gust slipped through the high windows, brushing the edge of the fabric like a whispered warning. Elara's palm closed over the wax, and a sharp pang of fear settled in her chest—the queen's voice, if ever spoken, might be silenced by her own silence. She inhaled the faint scent of pine resin that lingered from the courtyard, letting it steady the tremor in her hand. The River Clan rider at her side shifted his spear, eyes scanning the dim torchlight, but Elara's thoughts fixed on the secret she guarded.
