Sam slipped the forged dossier into the inner pocket of his coat, the paper cold against his palm. He had told Lieutenant Ramos he would owe a favor when the time came, and the promise settled like a weight in his gut. The ledger scribbled on the back listed a dockside warehouse as the next point of interest. Rain hammered the narrow alleys of the harbor district, turning the concrete to black glass. He followed the faint neon pulse that marked the freight terminal, turning onto a street where the fog hung like a low ceiling.

The warehouse loomed, its doors yawning shut, rusted hinges screaming as he forced them. Inside, the air smelled of salt and rusted steel, the only sound the drip of water from a broken pipe. A body lay crumpled on the concrete, a police badge glinting dull under a flickering bulb. The officer's uniform was torn, his eyes open but empty, a silent indictment of the corrupt chain Sam had just tangled with. Sam knelt, feeling the pulse of a still‑beating heart stop, and read the name etched on the badge—Ramos's own men. The realization hit hard; the evidence he carried was a lie, a tool to bind him tighter.

Later, the street phone buzzed, a thin voice cutting through the night. "Sam," Ramos said, his tone flat, "the witness on the dock knows the jade's true location. You will make sure she never talks." The words landed like a brick in Sam's chest. He stared at the rain‑soaked pavement, the neon signs above casting a cold wash over his clenched fists. The choice was already made; the debt had a price, and the city’s shadows grew deeper.
