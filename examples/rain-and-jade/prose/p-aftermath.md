The thugs slipped back into the shadows, their guns clattering on the wet cobbles as Sam's shots rang out. He hauled Clara by the elbow, pulling her through the narrow side door and into the driving rain. The street was a river of neon reflected on slick stone, and the city’s din fell to a muffled hiss behind them. A gaunt figure leaned against a rusted barrel, his breath misting in the cold air. He whispered that Victor "Iron Fist" Dario intended to use the jade in a ritual that would bind the docks to his will. The words hit Sam like a cold splash, and the rain soaked through his coat, heavy as lead. He counted the drops on his shoulders, each one a reminder that a single misstep could drown him in blood or a debt he could not repay. From the alley’s mouth a guttural roar echoed, Victor’s voice booming over the storm. "This betrayal will cost you dearly," he growled, the sound trailing off as the downpour washed away the last chance Sam had at anonymity. Sam stood still, the rain drumming his hat, his mind a steel trap of vows and threats, knowing the night had only deepened.
