Sam saw the rusted crate wobble against the brick wall, its dented lid trembling in the drizzle. He lunged, seized Clara's arm, and yanked her behind the metal shallow shield. He drew the pistol from his coat and fired a single warning shot; the crack echoed off wet stone and the thugs staggered, lowering their weapons and ducking behind overturned pallets.

Later, the precinct hallway smelled of stale coffee and cold steel. Lieutenant Ramos stood by a flickering fluorescent, his hand sliding a thin file across the desk. The papers were forged, signatures immaculate, the edges crisp as new bills.

Ramos kept his voice low. “This could keep the trail to the jade from freezing over, but taking it might bind you to our side. Declining could let the case go cold; accepting could tie you to a favor we’ll collect later.” Sam watched the file, the weight of the possible bargain settling like a stone in his gut.

Rain hammered the tin roof above the precinct, each patter matching the steady tick he heard in his mind, the clock that drove the case forward.
