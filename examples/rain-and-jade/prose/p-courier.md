Rain hammered the harbor district, turning cobblestones into mirrors. A courier burst from the gloom, his coat flaring like a banner of desperation against the neon glare. He slipped past the broken doorway where Sam had taken refuge, shoulders hunched, eyes fixed on a thin envelope clutched to his chest. Sam watched the man's boots splash through puddles, heard the soft thunk of a dropped package as the courier jerked his head, scanning the street for pursuers. The figure vanished into the maze of alleyways, leaving a trail of damp fabric and a faint scent of seaweed. Sam felt the heavy weight of the moment settle, the promise of a lead humming beneath the din of distant sirens. He crouched low, the rusted crate offering a narrow shield from the drizzle and any eyes that might follow. The rain drummed a steady rhythm on the metal, and Sam's mind slipped into the familiar calculus of risk and reward. He slipped behind the crate, ready to chase the whisper of a clue into the night.
