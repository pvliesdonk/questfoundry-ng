Sam slid the jade figurine across the scarred table and pressed his palm against Clara's in a firm handshake. The green stone felt cold against his fingertips, its weight a reminder of the promise he had made. Clara's lips curled as she lifted the figurine, her eyes flashing for a moment before she leaned in. She whispered, "The jade holds leverage to blackmail the heads of rival syndicates, tightening my grip on the underworld," and the words settled like a pact in the stale air.

The door slammed open and Victor's men surged into the room, rifles raised and faces set. Before they could fire, two silhouettes slipped from the shadows behind a stack of crates. Guns barked, and the thugs went down before they could aim. Clara moved like a conductor, signaling the hidden enforcers who cleared the space with practiced efficiency. Sam watched the bodies hit the floor, the metallic scent of blood mixing with the diesel fumes.

When the gunfire faded, Sam stepped back through the doorway into the relentless rain. The streetlights smeared orange across puddles as the storm hammered the cobblestones. He felt the weight of the jade in his coat pocket and the tighter knot of his own thoughts. For a moment he understood that the city’s tides were no longer his to navigate; he was a pawn in Clara's expanding empire.
