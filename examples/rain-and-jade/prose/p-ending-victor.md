The harbor office smelled of oil and rust. Rain slicked the iron‑gray cobbles outside, and a flickering neon sign threw a thin red line across the cracked window. Sam stepped inside, coat dripping, and placed the jade into Victor's palm. The bulky boss gave a short nod, the gesture sealing a promise of protection.

Victor slid the figurine into a steel chest that sat like a coffin and muttered about the ritual that would bind the city's smuggling routes.

A low hum rose from the chest as the jade's green surface began to pulse, and the ledger inside the stone thrummed to life. Victor's iron ring caught a stray light, his eyes narrowing as control settled over him.

Sam turned back toward the wet dock, his coat soaked through, the ember in his cigarette guttering against the cold. He knew every deal now ran through Victor's iron fist, and the weight of the stone lingered in his mind like a second scar. He walked away, rain hissing on the pavement, aware that his fate had been sealed.
