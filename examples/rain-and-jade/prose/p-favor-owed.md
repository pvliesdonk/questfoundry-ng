Neon lights splashed across Sam's coat, a thin pulse that marked the debt he now carried. He felt the forged file press cold against his spine, the paper a contract he could not tear away from. The backroom smelled of rust and salt, the old warehouse humming with the drip of a cracked pipe. On the scarred metal table sat a jade figurine, its green glaze catching the flicker of the busted bulb; whether Clara had placed it there was a question he did not need to answer. Victor's men lingered in the doorway, their silhouettes cutting the dim light. Sam's scar brushed the edge of the figurine as he lifted it, the weight of the stone matching the weight of the promise in his gut. He slipped the relic into his inner pocket, the ember of his cigarette guttering in the damp air. The neon outside pulsed again, a silent metronome as he turned toward the exit, knowing the favor owed would find him the moment the night slipped into morning.
