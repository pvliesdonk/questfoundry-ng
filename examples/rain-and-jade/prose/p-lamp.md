The streetlamp guttered, a sputtering orange halo over the rain‑slick cobbles. It threw light on a cluster of discarded items: a half‑torn envelope, a cracked bottle, a glint of metal half‑buried in the puddle. Sam knelt, the cold steel of his pistol against his thigh, and brushed the water aside. The envelope was still sealed, the wax seal cracked but intact, the emblem of a dragon faintly pressed into the paper. A quick glance at the imprint showed the same jade dragon that Clara had spoken of, its claws curling around a stylized ledger. His breath fogged in the thin air; the city’s fog pressed close, as if trying to hide the truth. Behind the lamp, a shadow moved, a figure disappearing into an alley as quickly as the rain fell. Sam lifted the envelope, feeling the weight of a decision that could bind him to men he hated or leave him chasing ghosts. He slipped the clue into his coat and stalked onward, the halo fading as the bulb died.
