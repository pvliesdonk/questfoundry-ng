The harbor wind bit the flesh of his neck, a cold reminder that he had walked away from the darkness. He pulled the coat tighter, the rain slicking the cobbles beneath his boots. The ember in his cigarette wavered, casting a thin orange flicker between the narrow alley walls. He slipped the jade figurine into the inner lining of his coat, feeling its smooth chill against the scar on his cheek. Behind him the dockside warehouses exhaled steam, neon signs sputtering red into the iron‑gray fog. He heard the distant clank of a crane loading a cargo ship, the rhythm matching the pulse in his ears. A lone police siren wailed far off, a reminder of the deal he had spurned. Sam scanned the quay, eyes tracing the silhouettes of men in dark coats moving like rusted iron. He knew Victor’s crew would be waiting, but the weight of the stone gave him a purpose he could not ignore. With a steady breath he turned toward the back alley where Clara’s shadow lingered, the wind pressing against his back as if urging him forward.
