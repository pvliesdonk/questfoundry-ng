He slammed the cobblestones of the Harbor District, the rain hissing at his boots, and lit a cigarette beneath the flicker of a neon sign. The ember glowed brief against the damp, casting a thin amber halo on his trench coat. A silhouette slipped through the cracked doorway, silk trailing like water. Clara Vale entered the office, her emerald dress clinging to the drizzle, and placed a sealed envelope on the desk. She told him she needed the jade dragon figurine, rumored to hide a ledger of smuggled goods. He flicked ash into the gutter and watched the smoke curl into the fog. A crash shattered the night as Victor's thugs battered the warehouse door, pistols flashing. Sam grabbed a metal table and pulled Clara behind it as they scrambled for cover, bullets spraying the room. They slipped into a dim backroom where a lone bulb flickered overhead. Clara lifted the jade figurine, its green eyes catching the trembling light. She whispered that the payout would drown the city in gold if he delivered it. Clara's eyes darted to Sam, a silent plea for protection amid the gunfire. Sam felt the weight of the moment settle like a wet stone in his palm.
