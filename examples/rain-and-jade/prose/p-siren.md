The siren cracked the night, a thin blade of sound slicing through rain‑slick alleys. It rose from the dock, far enough to be a warning, close enough to make the blood in Sam's ears thrum. He pressed his back against the cold brick, eyes flicking to the jade figurine cradled in Clara's trembling hands. Her dress clung to her skin, dark as the puddles reflecting the flickering bulb. Outside, the gutted warehouse shuddered with the echo of gunfire, the muzzle flashes painting brief ghosts on the wet stone. Sam inhaled, the metallic taste of seawater filling his lungs, and exhaled a plume that curled into the fog. He knew the siren meant the city was already moving, that the lieutenant's offer would come with a price. He tightened his grip on the metal table, the only cover between them and the doorway. Clara's eyes searched his, begging without words. He thought of the favor owed, the forged papers that might disappear, and the ledger hidden in the green dragon. The wail peaked, then fell, leaving a hollow throb that matched his own pulse.
