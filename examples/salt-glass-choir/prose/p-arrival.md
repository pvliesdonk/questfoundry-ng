Your splintering oars bite into the black swells, tearing through the heavy foam as the Drowned Cathedral groans upward from the deep. Its jagged, shadow-drenched spires pierce the surface, weeping waterfalls of brine and tangled weed into the swirling sea. Bitter salt. Numbing cold. You drag the skiff against the weeping stones, clutching a sputtering storm lantern to your chest, your oilskin coat stiff with frozen spray. The great doors tower in the gloom, encrusted with razor-sharp barnacles that slice at your palms as you press against the petrified wood. You strain against the weight of a thousand sunken years. The hinges scream. The doors break inward.

Inside, the cavernous nave breathes. Tide waters violently pour through shattered, kelp-strangled rose windows, vomiting the churning ocean down the sanctuary walls. The air hangs thick, vibrating with a physical weight that constricts your throat, preparing the flesh for the terrible strain of a severed tongue. Thousands of figures stand shoulder to shoulder in the gloom. Statues forged of translucent, weeping salt-glass. From their crystalline mouths pours a continuous, mournful note of their drowning, an overlapping chorus of whispered sorrow echoing against the vaulted ceiling. Enduring grief. Shattered glass. Your breath catches in your chest, hitched on a sharp, jagged inhale that tastes of wet sand and a brother's faded laugh. The overwhelming chill gnaws at your veins, a deep, marrow-scraping ache that demands an offering of hot blood or precious, irretrievable memory just to survive the dark.

The lantern casts fractured, amber light across the waterlogged stones. The central aisle plunges straight ahead into a pitch-black, flooded abyss, its dark waters churning with unseen currents. To either side, the colossal, barnacle-encrusted pillars lining the perimeter offer a narrow, jagged ledge away from the deepest surge.
