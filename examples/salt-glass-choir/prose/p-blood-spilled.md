You slide the rusted edge of a gutting blade across your palm, parting the salt-chapped skin with a slow, deliberate pressure that bites deep into the muscle. Better to bleed than to surrender the precious, irreplaceable echoes of the past to the grasping kelp. The pain is a sharp, grounding hum amid the deafening, crashing chimes of the flooded sanctuary. Warmth pools in the hollow of your hand, heavy and thick with the iron-tang of life, before spilling in fat droplets onto the rime-encrusted slab. Red falling. Sizzling against the ancient frost. The scarlet drops strike the pale, tooth-like barnacles, melting through the crystallized salt with an agonizing hiss that fills your lungs with the scent of copper and stagnant tide.

The blood does not wash away in the relentless black water pouring through the shattered rose windows above. Instead, it crawls. A pulsing, crimson vein of light cuts across the submerged stone floor, tracing a winding, glowing fracture through the deep gloom. A living map, carved in wet red, pointing the way through the thousands of overlapping, whispered songs.

But the altar demands its absolute due. A hollow, black frost climbs the open wound, sinking jagged fangs into your exposed flesh and racing up the bone of your arm. Necrotic rot. Winter in the marrow. The crushing cold seizes your limbs, turning the heavy oilskin coat into a shroud of lead that drags your shivering shoulders downward. Your breath shatters in your throat, a ragged gasp tearing at the raw cords of your voice as a profound, crippling weakness settles deep within your chest. You grip the sputtering lantern handle with fingers that no longer bend. Numb. Pale. Starving for the hearth.
