Following the path secured by your toll, you finally locate Elias's Effigy in the crushing gloom. Its fragile glass frame vibrates continuously, humming a mournful, baritone melody that rattles the teeth in your skull and tightens the raw cords of your throat. A familiar, heartbreaking song. Shivering flames. You clutch the sputtering storm lantern, your thumbnail compulsively tapping the wet glass in the exact rhythm of his old bedroom knock. *Tap. Tap-tap. Tap.*

Before you can reach for the pale, cold light of the statue, the black waters heave. The Choirmaster rises from a tangle of rotting kelp, a towering conductor forged of fused sea-rime and translucent shadows. His massive salt-glass body scrapes together with the grinding wail of shifting ice, filling the damp air with overlapping, oceanic chimes as he demands a toll to guide you through the deafening chorus. Suffocating pressure. Crushing deep.

The rusted handle of your lantern slips against your sweating palm as the ancient entity gestures toward a rime-encrusted altar. The sunken stone is entirely choked with pale, smooth barnacles, their shells eerily resembling thousands of tiny, broken teeth waiting to feed. Through a cascade of harmonic, crashing chimes, the Choirmaster explains the terrible design of this ruined sanctuary. You must give a piece of your living self upon that frozen slab to attune the cathedral's acoustics to your brother's specific resonance. Wet iron pooling on the tongue. Jagged fragments. The whispered songs of the drowned press against your ears, starving for a sacrifice.
