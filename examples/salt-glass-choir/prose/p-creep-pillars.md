The ledge beneath your boots is a treacherous ribbon of slick, rotting kelp, sloping dangerously toward the abyssal drop. Black water. Shallow, jagged breaths. The barnacles clinging to the ancient columns bite through your soaked oilskin, scraping raw furrows into your shoulder as you drag yourself forward inch by agonizing inch.

Just beyond the shelter of the petrified rock, the flooded sanctuary floor heaves in a violent, hungry vortex, swirling with a force that threatens to drag the last vestiges of warmth down into the crushing deep. The roar of the central current batters the ears, a heavy, suffocating thrum that rattles the teeth in your skull and strains the tender cartilage of your throat. To speak here is to invite the tide. To whisper is to drown. You flatten your hands against the calcified shells of the pillar, their razor edges slicing deep into your palms as a surge of heavy brine crashes over the narrow ledge. Wet iron pooling on the tongue. Bone-deep ache. You cling to the unforgiving stone, letting the vast column break the force of the swell while the shadows wrap tightly around your shivering frame.

The mist hanging between these peripheral spires carries the thick, salt-heavy scent of lost things, pressing upon your chest with the undeniable weight of an inevitable toll. Somewhere further in the gloom, an overlapping chorus of delicate, crystalline sorrow weaves through the deafening rush of the water, pulling at the fraying edges of your mind to demand a sacrifice of hot, living blood or irreplaceable childhood joy. You peel your torn hands from the shell-encrusted safety of the wall, turning your face toward the deeper sanctuary. A sudden gasp. Wet, broken glass.
