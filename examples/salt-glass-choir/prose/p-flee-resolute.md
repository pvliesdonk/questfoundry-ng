The pale dust dissolves into the churning dark, carrying the final, fragile echoes of his baritone down into the lightless abyss. You swallow hard against a throat choked with bitter brine, forcing down the ragged, tearing lump of an unsung dirge that scrapes the tender lining of your windpipe. A heavy gulp. Crushed glass in your gullet.

You pivot your sodden weight against the undertow, tearing your face from the empty altar, and throw your trembling shoulders into the current. The water rises around your thighs, a heavy, freezing shroud woven from the grief you refuse to speak. Your stiffened legs drive forward, forcing heavy oilskin boots to strike the shifting flagstones in a frantic, stumbling cadence that reverberates up through the marrow of your bones. Sluggish. Desperate. Running from the ruin.

Ahead, the grand coral archway groans under the immense, crushing pressure of the ocean above, shedding massive slabs of salt-rimed stone into the gloom. You push toward the narrowing aperture, your lungs burning with the raw friction of labored breaths, your eyes stinging with the salt of a sea that consumes all things. The rushing tide roars in your ears, a deafening surge drowning out the violent splintering of the sanctuary tearing itself apart at your back. The floor bucks beneath your heels. Falling pillars. Cracking shell. You hurl yourself into the collapsing mouth of the passage. Blindly forward. Nothing left behind.
