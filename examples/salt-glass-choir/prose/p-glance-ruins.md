The swirling silt settles into the jagged grooves of the drowned altar, laying a fine, pale sediment where the music used to breathe. You stare at the empty stone, your vision blurring as the freezing tide washes over your stinging eyes. Your pulse beats against the bruised cartilage of your throat, a heavy, rhythmic thud demanding the very breath you hoarded from the dark. A bitter, iron tang coats the back of your tongue, a harsh reminder of the blood kept warm within your veins, mingling with the briny tear that spills across your frozen cheek. A hollow chest. A deafening silence.

The boy of salt is gone, swept into the lightless trenches where neither sacrificed childhoods nor surrendered voices can ever reach him. Only a solitary, jagged fragment of crystalline glass remains wedged in the dark basalt, catching a final glimmer of spectral luminescence before succumbing to the sediment. Your numbed fingers brush the razor edge, drawing a sharp, immediate ache that shoots up your wrist and lodges deep behind your ribs. Cold glass. Empty hands.

Above, the vaulted ceiling of the sunken cathedral groans beneath the crushing mass of the sea, shedding heavy, decaying flakes of limestone into the churning water. You drag your gaze away from the ruined altar, a physical, wrenching strain that pulls the taut muscles of your neck. The shadow of the Choirmaster drifts back into the weaving kelp, an indifferent silhouette fading into its choir of the damned. Ahead, past the fractured pillars, the crumbling archway shudders under the weight of the deep, a narrow fracture leading back to the punishing air of the shore. You swallow the sharp sting of the sea, tasting the faint, dead echo of his baritone, and force your heavy boots toward the exit. A solitary step. A hoarded breath. The unbroken dark.
