Your teeth grind together, trapping the bitter taste of rime against your tongue as you lock your jaw against the abyssal dark. You shake your head, a violent, tearing motion that flings wet strands of hair across your cheeks. The ache at the base of your throat flares into a burning spasm, a fierce hoarding of your own ragged breath, refusing to yield the living muscle of your voice to the freezing tide. You drag your heavy oilskin boots backward across the slick, submerged stones. Retreating from the altar. Retreating from the trade.

The crushing weight of the suffocating deep eases by a mere fraction, only to be overtaken by a churning, harmonic dissonance that wrings the remaining air from your lungs. Upward, above the altar, the Choirmaster looms in a towering mass of rotting kelp and weeping brine. A jagged, translucent arm lifts high into the current, brandishing a baton of fused salt. Beneath the shadow of that eyeless monstrosity, the fragile effigy continues to vibrate, humming its mournful baritone blindly into the void. A perfect likeness. A boy abandoned.

The baton descends in a singular, devastating sweep, tearing the water with the violent roar of a collapsing wave. It strikes the pale, glowing crown. The impact erupts in a deafening crescendo of overlapping chimes, a sound that shreds the familiar baritone melody into ten thousand weeping splinters. The crystalline boy shatters. A burst of cold light. Coarse white dust. The rising tide rushes over the stones, sweeping the powdered remnants of his song off the altar and dragging them into the eternal, silent black. Gone. Swallowed.
