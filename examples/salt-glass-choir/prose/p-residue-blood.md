Every breath rattles in your chest, tearing through raw, salt-chapped tissue like sea foam dragging over cold stones at the lowest tide. A jagged, broken rhythm. Brine and iron coat your tongue. Each inhale brings the graveyard chill of the flooded cathedral deeper into your marrow, suffocating the last embers of your native warmth.

The unnatural frost crawls further up your shoulder, turning the heavy, wet oilskin of your coat into a carapace of unyielding ice. Heavy. Impossible to bear. You drag your boots against the submerged flagstones, following the pulsing crimson path illuminated by your dying lantern, but your legs move with a sluggish, agonizing resistance. Dead wood waterlogged in the abyss. The red vein on the floor demands pursuit, a glowing wound mirroring the aching split in your palm, winding a bright, terrible map through the endless aisles of singing, weeping statues.

A low groan escapes your lips, a frayed vibration humming in your throat as the sheer weight of the black water presses down on your shivering spine. A fragile sound. Glass fracturing under pressure. To take even a single step requires tearing the frozen fibers of your own muscles, breaking the rime that binds your knees to the flooded earth. The copper stench of your spilled blood mingles with the ancient rot of the ocean floor, a thick perfume settling over the rusted altar and the dark tide rising around your calves.

The crimson trail ends at a solitary pedestal, haloed in the watery gloom, where a single effigy waits in the crushing dark. Your pulse thuds slow and thick in your ears. Sinking stones. The freezing depths pull at your flesh, begging for a final surrender, demanding you slip beneath the black water and become just another silent monument. You brace your numb, unbending fingers against your ribs and lean into the punishing, relentless chill.
