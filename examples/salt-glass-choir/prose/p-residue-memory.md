An oceanic vastness settles over the eroded landscape of your mind, a tide pulling out to leave only smooth, unmarked flats of gray silt stretching into an endless, quiet fog. Nothing. Dead calm. You drag your gaze upward along the sweeping ribs of the sunken cathedral, tracing the colossal pillars of petrified coral that weep thick, viscous streams of brine into the shadowy pools at your feet. The rhythmic plash of falling water echoes against the inside of your skull, ringing out into a cavernous expanse where a brilliant warmth ought to burn. White noise. A phantom ache. You swallow hard, but the muscles of your throat grind against an unnatural dryness, physically grasping for the familiar shape of a loss that slips through your raw fingers like sea-foam.

Water cascades from the vaulted, encrusted ceiling in a heavy, continuous curtain, washing over the rows of frozen figures locked in the salt-rimed pews, their silent hymns vibrating through the marrow of your shinbones. You stand amidst the choral ruins without a tether, abruptly severed from the origin of your own sorrow, staring at the weeping architecture with an immense, suffocating detachment. A hollowed chest. Scraped shells. The crushing weight of the deep presses against your eardrums, a relentless drone that fills the fresh void behind your eyes with the heavy taste of rusted iron and dead algae.

A dense physical pressure settles into the base of your throat, a shadow of the terrible stillness waiting to take root within the raw flesh of your vocal cords, thick with the metallic tang of unsung grief. Muted shores. You lift your heavy oilskin boot, pulling it free from the sucking mud with a wet, tearing sound, and step deeper into the nave. The sputtering amber glow of your storm lantern throws long, jagged silhouettes across the aisle, carving a path through the suffocating gloom toward the altar of black basalt at the far end of the sanctuary. A singular destination. Cold inevitability. You drag your exhausted body forward into the numbing quiet.
