You abandon the narrow sanctuary of the stone lip and plunge directly into the swollen belly of the central nave, the black water surging past your knees to swallow the heavy leather of your boots. The tide drags at your calves with a savage, numbing ache, carrying the taste of ancient brine and the slick, rotting tang of kelp straight into the back of your throat. A relentless undertow. Crushing dark. You press your thighs against the invisible, churning force of the aisle, each dragging step a labor that burns the muscles and tightens the chest, preparing your mouth for the terrible strain of a wordless plea.

The water rises higher as the stone floor slopes down into the abyssal heart of the cathedral, slapping against your oilskin coat, slicking the stiff fabric with freezing foam. Swells of shadow roll through the sunken pews, catching the fractured amber light of your storm lantern and twisting it into dying, jagged reflections that dance on the violent crests. Wet glass. Shivering flames. The chorus of the salt-glass statues vibrates through the dark surge, a mournful, continuous hum that thrums against your ribs and claws at the tightening cords of your throat.

You lean your weight into the unyielding current, your hands gripping the rusted handle of the lantern until your knuckles ache, your breath whistling through chapped lips in short, ragged gasps. The black waters resist your passage, swirling with the phantom weight of forgotten childhoods and the heavy, metallic scent of unspilled blood. Bitter fatigue. Sunken grief. Every submerged step forward pulls you deeper into the freezing grip of the tide, dragging your weary bones through the flooded ruins as the sorrowful, overlapping song saturates the heavy air.
