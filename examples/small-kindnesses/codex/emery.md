title: Emery, Keeper of the Wet-Weather Stall
---

You will know her by the lantern-warmth of her face, though the market-goers say the edges of her have begun to go thin, as if the rain could see through her to the awning behind. Her fingers are stained with ink, her shawl carries the smell of a kitchen not her own. She gives easily — a kind word, a joke to warm you through the downpour — and takes nothing back. On her table she keeps small worn things: a cracked cup, a pressed flower, a ribbon that answers to no name. She wraps them, unwraps them, wraps them again. When she is unsure, her thumb finds the frayed hem of whatever cloth lies nearest and worries it soft. To those she loves she says only: you always come through the wet. She is gentle, and teasing, and guards her own losses close, as though naming them might make them true.
