title: The Night Market of Vellamere
---

It comes only with the rain and folds away when the last drop falls. Between those hours it sells what the city has lost and never claimed — grief, faces, afternoons, the sound of a name. It is not cruel. It is systemic, patient, courteous; it does not hate, it only balances its ledgers. At the claiming-table a clerk keeps a brass scale, and upon it debts are weighed and accounts made even. When a debt is settled, its ledger closes somewhere out of sight with the soft finality of a book reaching an ending it always held. The one who pays feels no hand upon them, only a loosening — a knot given slack, a weight set gently down. The rain runs in the gutters and the stalls glisten, and the market keeps its books, and asks for nothing more than what is owed.
