title: Wren, Courier of the Vellamere Rounds
---

A courier in a rain-darkened canvas coat, satchel strap worn to velvet at the shoulder. Wren knows the alleys of Vellamere by feel, could walk them blind and never miss a turning. For years they have carried the market's parcels — other people's lost memory, bundled in damp paper — and delivered each without once opening one for themselves. It is said Wren can find anything set down within the market's reach, and undo its knot in the dark, so long have their hands practiced the trade. They are quiet, watchful, forever deflecting; more at ease holding another's longing than admitting their own. When unsure, Wren does not know what to do with their hands, and so they keep walking, forward always, because standing still asks a question they would rather not answer.
