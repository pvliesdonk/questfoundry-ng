title: The Parcel with Wren's Name
---

It lies among the market's wares like any other: brown paper gone soft and dark with damp, string drawn into the market's exact knot — the one a certain courier could undo blind. But this one is addressed. The name upon it is Wren's, written in a hand they almost recognize and cannot quite place, as though they had penned it themselves in some season they've since misplaced. Inside is a loss of their own, set down long ago and never called back. It waits the way all unclaimed things wait here: without impatience, without judgment, only the quiet fact of the string still tied, the paper still folded, the name still legible under the rain. To carry a parcel is one thing. To be the one it is meant for is another, and heavier.
