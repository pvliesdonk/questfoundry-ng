The rain thins to almost nothing now, a drop, a long wait, a drop. You stand where the last light was, in the emptied dark, and the counting goes on without you.

Somewhere against your coat the small squared weight has not moved. You had carried it so long you stopped feeling it, the way you stop feeling the strap of the satchel, the way you stopped feeling the seam of your own days passing. String-tied. Patient. Yours, though you never let the thought sit long enough to warm. Your name is on it. You know that without looking. You have known it for a while and kept moving forward rather than stand still under the knowing.

Your hands open at last. There is nothing in them. There has never been anything in them but other people's wanting, delivered and set down and walked away from. And now the aisle is bare and no one is left to hand a thing to, and the weight against your coat is the only parcel in the market that stays unclaimed.

You think of her thumb at a frayed hem. You think of the crooked lantern she straightened on her way past and how it goes out crooked now. You think of how she waved you through the wet like she had been expecting you, every time, like your coming was the one certain thing in a place that kept nothing. That was the shape of it. That was what you carried and would not name because naming meant it could be taken, and you had spent years learning that whatever is named gets weighed, gets owed, gets collected.

So you never named it. And it was collected anyway.

The last drop finds the tarp. The rain has finished. In the after-silence you stand with your empty hands open and hold the ache up into it, not hiding it in a closed fist, not folding it away, just holding it out where the stopping rain can see. Here. This is what I lost. This is what she was.

You do not touch the parcel. Not yet. But you no longer pretend the weight is not there. You turn toward the exit, toward the night that has never known her, and you carry your name against your chest into it, owning the loss the way you never let yourself own the love. It is heavier than anything you ever delivered. You take it anyway.
