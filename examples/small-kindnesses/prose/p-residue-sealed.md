The aisles close over you the way water closes over a stone dropped in the dark, without ceremony, without the smallest ring to mark the place. Canvas leans in on either side. The lamps have burned down to their patient middles, and the light they give is the color of old paper, laid soft across the emptying tables. You walk into it and it takes you and holds nothing back for you, and this is the mercy of it, or the cruelty. You cannot tell them apart anymore.

The rain keeps its old rhythm against the tarps overhead. It does not hurry. It has been falling on this market longer than you have carried anything through it, and it will fall the same when you are gone, the same faint irregular counting on the stretched canvas, drop and pause and drop. It asks you nothing. It remembers nothing. There is a kind of peace in a thing that will not hold you to account, and there is a loneliness in it too, folded so close to the peace that your hands cannot separate the two.

Behind you a parcel sits square to the edge of a table with your name gone quiet on it. You do not carry it. You carry the shape of not carrying it, which is heavier, which presses where the strap crosses your shoulder.

You come to stand where the aisle opens a little, where a stall waits with its lamp still lit. The quiet here is the quiet of a room no one enters, dust settling on what was set down and never taken up again, the air gone still with keeping. You breathe it. It tastes of wet rope and cold tin and the long patience of things left.

And then there is a warmth in front of you that the market has not accounted for. Hands, near yours. A face turned toward you in the low light, waiting, the one face that has ever found you in all this. The rain is fewer beads now, thinning toward its last. The market is drawing its ledger closed somewhere you cannot see, and it will want its due, and you feel the wanting of it in the stilled air like a held breath. You have set your own loss down. What you have not yet set down stands here, warm, within reach, and the choosing has narrowed to the space between your two open hands.
