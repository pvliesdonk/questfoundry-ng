Greyrock rises from the churning ocean like a jagged spine, its stone cliffs slick with perpetual mist and the sighing breath of the sea. The lighthouse that crowns its summit struggles against wind that never ceases, its beam a thin thread of defiance against the encroaching fog. The air here is thick, claustrophobic, and every step echoes with the memory of generations who have stood watch over the relentless waves. The rock's narrow ledges and cavernous hollows amplify the sense of isolation, as if the world beyond is a distant rumor unable to breach the stone‑bound horizon that holds the tempest at bay.
