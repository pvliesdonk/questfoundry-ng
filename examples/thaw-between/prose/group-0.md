Greyrock lay shrouded, the mist smothered the world. Elara lifted her weather-worn eyes to the massive sea‑glass lens, feeling the weight of generations settled upon her shoulders like a tide of stone that remained. The lantern sputtered, its oil‑light trembling until it faded. Beneath the stone stack the tide rose in restless swells, seeping into cracks where the wind‑howl lingered, and the great lens pulsed a fragile rhythm that promised both salvation and threatened the world, its promise ruined. Night settled, the stone walls devoured remaining warmth. She moved to the battered wooden table, her fingers tracing the ledgers as she counted the dwindling supplies, each tally a reminder that the flame she tended might soon sputter out, its life soon expired. The sigil on the wall glowed faintly, its lines hummed. Elara paused, the weight of the lighthouse and the logbook heavy in her hands, and she chose whether to study the fragile rune or to catalog the meager stores, each decision a tide that could drown or rescue her, and the choice sealed her fate.
