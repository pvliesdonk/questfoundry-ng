The rune glimmered. Elara's gaze traced the faint sigil etched into the basalt, its lines wavering like a tide caught in iron, and the cold stone seemed to breathe a warning as the glyph throbbed against the lighthouse's dying flame, then vanished. The light guttered. She turned her back toward the iron‑bound logbook, the weight of its secret anchoring her to stone, while the wind‑howl curled through the corridor, and it pressed. She shivered. The lock's rusted teeth bit her palm, each turn of the key echoing the sea's relentless push, and the sigil's last glow sputtered out as the metal sealed. The stone sighed. She placed her palm against the chilled iron, feeling the grain of ancient duty carve itself into her skin, and the lighthouse's pulse throbbed in tandem with her heartbeat, bound. The wind moaned. She hesitated, the locked logbook whispering of the sea’s sleep while the sigil’s ember faded into darkness, and she chose to keep the beacon's guard, steadied.
