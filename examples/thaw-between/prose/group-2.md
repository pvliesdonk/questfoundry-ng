The wind hammered the lighthouse stones. Inside, the cold seeped through the basalt walls, each breath of frost stealing warmth while the dwindling stores of salted pork and oil shrank to a skeletal few, a hollow promise that hunger would soon have crushed. A Coastguard patrol clanged onto the cliffs. The officer stepped forward, his ledger in hand, demanding the iron‑bound logbook in exchange for the rations the next ship would bring, and Elara, with steady fingers, placed the weather‑worn tome upon the stone slab, sealing the pact, and the exchange sealed. Marek entered the lantern room. The great sea‑glass lens, etched with ancient runes, shivered in its cradle, a low hum escaping its facets as if the stone itself warned that the fragile barrier it upheld would shatter should it be lifted. Marek argued. He claimed the coast's safety depended on the lens's power, and that his sturdy brig could bear its weight across the shoals to calmer seas, urging Elara to trust that the light would safeguard the shore and his vessel would have secured.
