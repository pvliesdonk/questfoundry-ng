A low chant rolled from the tunnel, a hollow resonance echoed. Elara's breath rose in the chill as the stone walls seemed to pulse with the rhythm, the lantern's oil flickering against the encroaching tide, and she felt the weight of the unseen doorway press against her resolve, urging her forward. Marek's ink‑stained coat flared under the lantern's sputter. He spread his charts across the cold stone, his finger tracing treacherous shoals as veins, and his voice rose steady, pleading that the great lens could guide his brig to safe waters, a salvation he was insisting. The chant deepened, a cadence of stone and water rumbling. She pressed a gloved hand to the cold basalt, feeling the vibration travel up her arm, the echo threading through the lighthouse's veins like a warning, and the knowledge that every choice tethered her to the lingering light she held. Marek leaned closer, eyes like storm clouds fixed. He gestured toward the glowing lens, its rune‑etched surface trembling as the tide's breath pressed against the walls, and whispered that the path beyond the hidden mouth would lead to a shore where his brig could bear the weight of salvation, a promise the night itself seemed to have awaited.
