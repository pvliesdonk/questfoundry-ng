She turned her weather‑worn hand to the flame, noting how the oil‑light trembled, and the weight of the Coastguard’s promise pressed upon her palm like a cold stone. Marek lifted the lens, its runes hummed. The envoys traced the rune markings, their fingers lingering over a sigil that whispered of a sealed sea, and they recorded that the great glass could bind the tide itself, a leverage the Authority coveted. Elara held the logbook, its iron clasp cold, and felt duty rose within her.
