She climbed the sheer cliff. Above, Marek's weather-worn brig bobbed like a toothless shark in the oil-lit mist, its deck slick with churned foam, and she vaulted across the yawning gap, landing hard on the splintered rail as the lantern's glow guttered. The flame sputtered and died. The great lens, now empty, stopped casting its sea-glass beam, and the tower's stone throat exhaled a low, mournful moan that rose from the abyss, sealing the night with a sound that cracked the wind. The ocean surged. From the black depths, an ancient terror rose, its tide hammering the hull until timber splintered, water gorging the deck as the ship bowed beneath a wave that devoured hull and hope alike, and Elara's oath crumbled as the sinking light shattered. She grasped the rail. The brig lurched as the sea's maw widened, crushing the wooden ribs into splinters while the stone walls of Greyrock shivered under the onslaught, and the last whisper of oil-light flickered out, leaving the world drowned. Silence settled. In the choking dark, the ocean's ancient horror rose to claim the tower, the ruined lens sinking into the abyss as the promise Elara once swore dissolved beneath the crushing weight of tide, and the night claimed.
