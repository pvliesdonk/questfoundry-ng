Elara steadied her resolve and poured oil, refusing to abandon the tower, and stayed. The flame steadied, its amber shaft thrusting a clean, unwavering line through the roiling mist as if to push the restless tide back into the abyss, and it held. Decades slipped past as the light thinned, and Elara watched. She traced the slow dimming of the wick, saw shadows crawl over the weather‑worn cliffs of Greyrock, felt each passing night carve deeper lines into the stone, and understood that her name would rise on the wind while her freedom remained forever bound. Storms battered the stone, and she endured. The heavy glass of the Great Lens rested upon the lantern’s crown, its runes humming low against her spine as the sea’s restless breath strained against the barrier, and the light sustained. Generations whispered their thanks, and Elara listened. Each night the beam carved fresh furrows of darkness across the crag, the wind’s howl folding into the stone like a cold sigh, and the lighthouse endured.
