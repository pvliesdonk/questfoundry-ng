Shutters blink. One after another in a domino hush, wood tongues find their grooves and swallow the street, the light thinning to needles that stitch and bind. Watchlights wheel and cinch the dark; your shadow draws short, a neat wedge pinned at your boots while the tower keeps time through your arms.

You keep the Wind‑Key braced. The coupling dogs lie obedient in their beds, and the siphon’s throat holds, tight as a law. Click. A small, sure tooth of sound, and another debt shelves itself inside the drum. Iron and rosemary rise again at the back of your tongue, and the hum in the girders travels the ladder of your ribs until you can read the city by pressure alone. Calder’s palm is still on your shoulder. Warm. Heavy. The kind of weight that lends steadiness and takes its due.

Boots outside take the measure the bells set and multiply it into lanes where songs once wandered uncounted. The shutters finish their run and the hush that follows is organized, precise, a lid clapped on a kettle you cannot afford to let boil. Your apron tugs against your sternum. The lark chit noses the ledger’s ink in your thoughts, tasting a circle on a name you do not say, and your mouth salts with a choice you keep folding and unfolding in the dark of your coat. You picture a seam failing at someone’s temple, stitches slurring, a laugh you know turning thin, while the block’s talk grows clipped around you like shears. Somewhere, a quill drags. Somewhere, the page grows interested in your gait.

Calder shifts. Stone breathes its slow cold against the tower skin, and the lamps below braid their cones into a single, watchful eye. You count three rungs. You set your hand. You climb to the wall and hold fast beside Calder.
