The Clockkeepers split. Belts slap and keys flash as some rush the coupling winches, teeth poised to bite again, while others throw their bodies across the release chain, cursing through dry mouths that taste the new slack and swear to keep it. Calder calls for order. His voice lands without its old metronome and you read the burn in his eyes, betrayal bright as a fresh score, pumice grit bright on his thumb where he grips the rail and finds no purchase in faith, while hands that once moved to his count peel away toward winch and chain. Rook holds the line beside you. Her coat soughs like canvas in a lull and her salt-cold hand steadies your elbow with a clean chill as she turns her face from the bitten doorframe and nods toward the gates where the wind has begun to count.

You shoulder the Sovereign Wind-Key. Its butt-heavy pull settles into your wrist, the worn thumb crescent finding your skin as if it remembers you, iron-and-rosemary breath rising from its grip while the chain gives a single low chime that folds the room into one slow thought. The city breathes slower. Bells pause like held breath between heartbeats, shutters unstick with soft complaints, and the gates yawn their dark-lipped hinge to the Halt’s clean draft that pries the taste of brass from your teeth. You choose the open sum. Counting in paired whispers, catching the fifth at your ribs, you step through the mouth the walls have made and meet the quiet as a craftsman meets torque, with hands ready to pay in effort instead of theft. Behind, the factions lean and settle. Their shouts dull to a carpentry of compromise as bodies ease from chains and the brave and the frightened begin to divide the watches, a balance struck by need rather than decree. Wonder arrives with peril. Grass ticks at the seams and far off a field of still air waits with its tuned absence, and you walk toward it with the Key a season on your shoulder, Rook’s chill steady at your sleeve, and the city’s honest slow moving through your bones like a kept promise.
