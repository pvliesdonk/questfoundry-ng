The ache is soft. It glows under your skin where the seal bit, a warm ember tucked behind the ribs, as the mainspring’s hum seems to lean close enough that you could take it into your lungs and let it out as steadier breath. Steps count themselves without you asking, one-two, one-two, the fifth stuttering as if a tooth were sheared, and still the city holds you in its teeth and turns. Gazes snag on you like burrs. The blue of authority rides the corner of every pane and puddle, a cold pigment that will not wash.

You carry the trade with both hands. Light on her face, tight in your bones. Mira’s hands were true again when you left, color brightening at her cheeks like a lamp brought back to wick, the tremor in your own fingers settling only when she set her palm to them and hummed the loom tune she half-remembers. Your recall frays in small threads that catch on nails and vanish, a street name lifted from your tongue, the exact pitch of a chime gone thin, and each loss clicks into place as part of the cost. The ledger’s red tab has already told your measure to the Keepers, and the notice walks ahead of you like a shadow with a ruler. You feel it check your pace at each crossing, neat as a scribe’s line.

At corners, blue coats lean as if by design. Their eyes are careful. Their cuffs shine like wet enamel where the light finds them, and you keep the rhythm of an apprentice who has learned to move while weighed. A storefront clock coughs a minute it did not have. A gate you have never seen open sits a thumb’s width ajar, and a draft from the older cold whistles over stone as if the city took a long, slow breath between ticks. You square to the tower and let the hum come through your ribs. You follow the tower’s seam toward what Calder hides.
