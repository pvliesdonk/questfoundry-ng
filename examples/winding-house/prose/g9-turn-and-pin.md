You grunt. The Sovereign Wind‑Key lifts like a season unhooked, chain paying a single note, iron and rosemary touching your tongue as the man‑length shaft eases down and the teeth like small stars seek their pattern in the waiting collar. You find the worn crescent with your thumb. Torque lines up through your shoulder and ribs as the coupling dogs nose and seat, a snug, animal click telegraphed into bone, the tower’s belly attentive to your grip. Pins wait. You mouth numbers in pairs and catch the breath on five as you set the first, then the second, the steel shivering once before it goes true and the siphon’s throat stiffens, its path tight as a valve you dare not leave half‑turned. The bells answer. Brass flares a bright beat that rattles dust from the beam and declares the bite; deep in the drum of the mainspring, a held hunger turns obedient, and the barrel locks its hoard with a sure, cold patience. Your apron tugs. The borrowed lark chit in its pocket leans toward the ledger’s ink as if to kiss the circle around Mira’s name, and a thin suction gathers behind your eyes where mornings cost.

A hand closes. Calder’s palm finds your shoulder through leather, heat from the lamp stored in his skin, pumice grit faint as salt where his thumb rests, and the weight of it steadies and claims in the same press. He is certain. Outside, bootfalls take the tower’s tempo, measured and multiplying, shutters shave light to slits, and somewhere a quill begins its dry rain across a registry that will not forget who slowed or spoke. You keep the Key braced. The dogs hum settled in their beds, torque running into the spine of the city, every tooth bright with other people’s mornings, while the hatch’s black seam breathes cold along your knuckles like a rule reminding you what edge you held and turned. You stand with his warmth on you and the mainspring holding, the whole city wound hard against your palm.
